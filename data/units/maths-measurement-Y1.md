6. Circle the heavier: a chair / a book

# WEEK 1 — ENGAGE

**Focus:** Cold Task + Measuring with informal units
**5E Phase:** Engage

---

## LESSON 1.2 — End-to-End Without Gaps or Overlaps

**Duration:** 50 minutes
**Type:** Concept Introduction

---

### Learning Intentions
- I am learning to place informal units end-to-end with no gaps or overlaps
- I am learning why this is important for accurate measurement

### Success Criteria
- I can measure a line correctly using blocks

---

### I DO (15 min)

> "Measuring properly means placing units END-TO-END. No GAPS between them. No OVERLAPS — they don't stack on top."

> "Watch: I place each block right next to the last one. End to end."

> "This desk is 12 blocks long."

---

### YOU DO (15 min)

1. Measure this line using counters (no gaps, no overlaps):
2. Draw a line. Measure it in blocks.
3. "What would happen if there were gaps between the blocks?"
4. EXTENSION: "Measure the same line with pencils. Why might you get a different answer?"

# WEEK 2 — EXPLORE

**Focus:** Consistent units and comparing
**5E Phase:** Explore

---

## LESSON 2.1 — Does Unit Size Affect the Answer?

**Duration:** 50 minutes
**Type:** Explore

---

### I DO (15 min)

> "Same desk. Measured in pencils: 6 pencils. Measured in books: 3 books."

> "The desk didn't change — but the NUMBER changed because the UNIT changed."

> "Smaller unit → MORE units needed. Larger unit → FEWER units needed."

---

### YOU DO (15 min)

1. "If I measure a table in paper clips, will I get a bigger or smaller number than measuring in pencils? Why?"
2. Measure a pencil in cm cubes. About ___ cubes.
3. Measure the same pencil in cm rods (2cm each). About ___ rods. Which is more accurate?
4. EXTENSION: "What happens if you use mixed-size units?"

---

## LESSON 2.2 — Comparing Lengths

**Duration:** 50 minutes
**Type:** Explore

---

### YOU DO (30 min)

1. "Who has the longer pencil — you or your partner? How can you prove it?"
2. Order your desk items from shortest to longest. Measure to check.
3. "Can you compare two objects without measuring? How?"
4. EXTENSION: "Can you estimate the length of the room in giant steps?"

---

# WEEK 3 — EXPLORE

**Focus:** Mass and capacity
**5E Phase:** Explain

---

## LESSON 3.1 — Comparing Mass

**Duration:** 50 minutes
**Type:** Explain

---

### I DO (15 min)

> "We compare mass using a BALANCE SCALE."

> "If the scale tips down on one side, that object is HEAVIER."

> "If the scale balances, the objects have the SAME mass."

---

### YOU DO (15 min)

1. "If object A balances object B, what does that mean?"
2. "If object C tips the scale down lower than object D, which is heavier?"
3. Order: a book, a rubber, a pair of scissors. (Estimate first!)
4. EXTENSION: "Is the mass of an object the same on the moon?"

---

## LESSON 3.2 — Comparing Capacity

**Duration:** 50 minutes
**Type:** Explain

---

### I DO (12 min)

> "To compare capacity: fill one container, pour into another. If there's space left over, the second is bigger."

> "Or: fill both to the same level and compare."

---

### YOU DO (15 min)

1. "A cup holds less than a bowl. True or false?"
2. Find two containers. Which has greater capacity? Prove it.
3. "Can you order 3 containers from smallest to largest capacity?"
4. EXTENSION: "What holds more — 5 cups or 1 jug?"

---

---

# WEEK 4 — ELABORATE

**Focus:** Choosing appropriate units
**5E Phase:** Elaborate

---

## LESSON 4.1 — Choosing the Right Unit

**Duration:** 50 minutes
**Type:** Elaborate

---

### I DO (12 min)

> "Would you measure your desk in paper clips? It would take a LONG time — lots of paper clips!"

> "Would you measure your room in rulers? You'd get a tiny number — maybe 10 rulers!"

> "We choose a unit that makes sense for the SIZE of what we're measuring."

---

### YOU DO (15 min)

1. Which unit for a pencil: paper clip / book / desk?
2. Which unit for a room: pencil / ruler / desk?
3. Estimate, then measure: the length of the room in giant steps.
4. EXTENSION: "What is an appropriate unit for measuring the length of a football field?"

---

---

# WEEK 5 — ELABORATE (Part 2)

**Focus:** Estimation and problem-solving
**5E Phase:** Elaborate

---

## LESSON 5.1 — Estimation

**Duration:** 50 minutes
**Type:** Elaborate

---

### I DO (12 min)

> "Before measuring, ESTIMATE. Have a guess. Then measure to check."

> "My desk: I estimate about 8 pencils. Actual measurement: 7 pencils. Close!"

---

### YOU DO (30 min)

1. Estimate then measure: your book (in pencils).
2. Estimate then measure: your chair (in hands).
3. Estimate then measure: the door (in footsteps).
4. Record: Estimate / Actual / Difference.
