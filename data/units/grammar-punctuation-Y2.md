# Grammar and Punctuation Unit — Year 2

**Unit Duration:** 8 weeks × 3 lessons per week = 24 lessons
**Year Level:** Year 2
**Curriculum Link:** AC9E2LY01, AC9E2LY02, AC9E2LY03, AC9E2SP01, AC9E2SP02
**Focus:** Compound sentences, pronouns, possessive nouns, verb tenses, adjectives and adverbs, advanced punctuation
**Mentor Text:** *Matilda* by Roald Dahl (1988)
**Cold Task:** Week 1, Lesson 1 — Write sentences demonstrating grammar skills
**Hot Task:** Week 8, Lesson 1 — Same task, measure growth

---

## Unit Overview

**Learning Intention:**
We are learning to construct compound sentences, use pronouns correctly, apply verb tenses accurately, and use advanced punctuation to enhance meaning.

**Success Criteria:**
- I can write compound sentences using conjunctions (and, but, or, so, because).
- I can use pronouns (I, me, he, she, it, they, we) correctly to replace nouns.
- I can use possessive nouns (Matilda's, the book's cover).
- I can use past, present, and future verb tenses accurately.
- I can use adjectives and adverbs to add detail.
- I can use apostrophes for contraction and possession.

## WEEK 1 — COMPOUND SENTENCES

### Lesson 1: Cold Task (Assessment)
**Time:** 40 minutes | **Type:** Assessment

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Hook (5 min) | "Matilda is a brilliant, bold girl who outwits cruel adults. Today you will show your grammar skills through writing about Matilda." | Engage |
| Read (15 min) | Read Chapter 1 and 2 of *Matilda* aloud. Notice Dahl's sentence structures — some are short, some are long and linked. | Listen |
| Task (20 min) | Cold Task (same as Hot Task): Write 8 sentences about Matilda. Include: 2 simple sentences, 2 compound sentences (joined with AND, BUT, OR), 2 sentences with pronouns, 2 sentences showing you understand verb tenses. Underline your conjunctions and circle your pronouns. Year 2: 8+ sentences. | Independent sentence writing |

**Differentiation:** Sentence starter cards. Conjunction word bank. Example sentences displayed.

---

### Lesson 2: Simple + Simple (I Do)
**Time:** 40 minutes | **Type:** Explicit Teaching

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Mini-Lesson (15 min) | Compound sentence = two simple sentences joined into one longer sentence. "Matilda loved books. She was very clever." → "Matilda loved books AND she was very clever." Dahl uses this technique to create flow. Show: TWO SENTENCES joined with a conjunction = ONE COMPOUND SENTENCE. | Learn |
| Activity (15 min) | Join sentence pairs using AND: "Matilda read books. She learned new words." → "Matilda read books AND she learned new words." Do 8 pairs together. Notice how the comma can stay or go with AND. | Practice |
| Share (10 min) | "When do we NOT put a comma before AND?" (When sentences are short and flow directly: "Matilda laughed and played.") | Discussion |

**Success Criteria Check:** Can identify simple vs compound sentences.

---

### Lesson 3: Using Conjunctions (We Do)
**Time:** 40 minutes | **Type:** Guided Practice

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| I Do (5 min) | Five key conjunctions: AND (adds), BUT (contrasts), OR (chooses), SO (result), BECAUSE (reason). Each creates different meaning. | Learn |
| We Do (25 min) | Join sentence pairs using different conjunctions. Discuss which conjunction creates the right meaning: "The school was boring OR it was exciting." vs "The school was boring SO Matilda read books." vs "Matilda was clever BECAUSE she loved learning." Choose the right one each time. | Guided joining |
| You Do (10 min) | Write 4 compound sentences about Matilda — use each conjunction at least once. Circle each conjunction. | Written practice |

## WEEK 2 — PRONOUNS

### Lesson 1: Replacing Nouns (I Do)
**Time:** 40 minutes | **Type:** Explicit Teaching

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Mini-Lesson (15 min) | Pronouns replace nouns to avoid repetition. "Matilda read a book. Matilda learned quickly." → "Matilda read a book. She learned quickly." Replace: Matilda → She/Her. Mr. Wormwood → He/His. The book → It. This avoids repetition and makes writing flow. | Learn |
| Activity (15 min) | Replace nouns with pronouns in sentences from Chapter 3: "Matilda whispered to Lavender." → "She whispered to her." "The children watched the film." → "They watched it." Do 8 transformations. | Practice |
| You Do (10 min) | Write 4 sentences about Matilda. Replace 2 nouns with pronouns. Circle the pronouns. | Written practice |

---

### Lesson 2: Pronoun Types (I Do → We Do)
**Time:** 40 minutes | **Type:** Explicit Teaching + Guided

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| I Do (10 min) | Three types: SUBJECT pronouns (I, you, he, she, it, we, they) — do the action. OBJECT pronouns (me, you, him, her, it, us, them) — receive the action. POSSESSIVE pronouns (my, your, his, her, its, our, their) — show ownership. Model: "She read her book." She (subject), her (possessive). | Learn |
| We Do (20 min) | Sort pronouns into 3 columns: Subject | Object | Possessive. Use sentences from *Matilda*: "Miss Honey gave Matilda a book. She loved it. Her teacher was kind." Sort: She (subject), it (object), Her (possessive). | Guided sorting |
| You Do (10 min) | Write one sentence using each type of pronoun. Label each pronoun with its type. | Written practice |

---

### Lesson 3: Pronoun Hunt (You Do)
**Time:** 40 minutes | **Type:** Independent

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| You Do (40 min) | Read Chapter 4. Find all pronouns. Sort them: Subject | Object | Possessive. Count each type. Write a paragraph about Matilda using at least 3 subject, 3 object, and 3 possessive pronouns. Circle all pronouns. | Pronoun hunt |

## WEEK 3 — POSSESSIVE NOUNS

### Lesson 1: Whose Is It? (I Do)
**Time:** 40 minutes | **Type:** Explicit Teaching

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Mini-Lesson (15 min) | Possessive nouns show OWNERSHIP. Add 'S (apostrophe + s) to show something belongs to someone. "The book belongs to Matilda" → "Matilda's book." "The hat belongs to the boy" → "The boy's hat." One thing belongs to one person = 's. Dahl uses this: "Matilda's father," "Miss Honey's classroom." | Learn |
| Activity (15 min) | Transform: "The father of Matilda" → "Matilda's father." "The room of Miss Honey" → "Miss Honey's room." "The desk of the headmistress" → "The headmistress's desk." Do 8 together. Notice the pattern. | Practice |
| You Do (10 min) | Write 4 possessive noun phrases about the story. Circle the noun that owns something. | Written practice |

---

### Lesson 2: Apostrophes for Possession (I Do → We Do)
**Time:** 40 minutes | **Type:** Explicit Teaching + Guided

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| I Do (10 min) | Apostrophe for possession vs apostrophe for contraction: "Matilda's book" (book belongs to Matilda — possession). "Matilda is" → "Matilda's" is also a contraction of "Matilda is." But in "Matilda's book," the 'S shows possession. Key: Possessive apostrophes come AFTER the noun. | Learn |
| We Do (20 min) | Sort: "The children's playground" (children → plural possessive — apostrophe after S, no extra S) vs "Matilda's book" (singular name → add 's). Note: Plural nouns ending in S only add apostrophe (the dogs' bowls). Do practice sentences. | Guided distinction |
| You Do (10 min) | Write sentences with possessive nouns: "Matilda's teacher" "The children's books" "Mr. Wormwood's car" "Miss Honey's kindness" "The teachers' room." | Written practice |

---

### Lesson 3: Possessive Practice (You Do)
**Time:** 40 minutes | **Type:** Independent

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| You Do (40 min) | Write 8 sentences using possessive nouns from *Matilda*. Include at least: 2 singular possessives (Matilda's), 2 plural possessives (the children's), 2 proper name possessives. Circle each possessive noun. Check: Is there an apostrophe? Whose noun is it? | Independent practice |

## WEEK 4 — VERB TENSES

### Lesson 1: Past, Present, Future (I Do)
**Time:** 40 minutes | **Type:** Explicit Teaching

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Mini-Lesson (15 min) | Tense = when the action happens. PAST (already happened): Matilda read many books. PRESENT (happening now): Matilda reads quickly. FUTURE (will happen): Matilda will start school. Three time frames, three verb forms. Dahl uses past tense for storytelling. | Learn |
| Activity (15 min) | Sort verbs by tense: read, eats, will study, played, watches, will win. Make sentences with each: "Matilda read..." / "Matilda reads..." / "Matilda will read..." Do 10 verbs. | Sorting |
| You Do (10 min) | Write 3 sentences in each tense about Matilda. Label each: PAST / PRESENT / FUTURE. | Written practice |

---

### Lesson 2: Regular and Irregular Verbs (I Do → We Do)
**Time:** 40 minutes | **Type:** Explicit Teaching + Guided

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| I Do (10 min) | REGULAR verbs: add -ED for past tense (walk → walked, play → played, want → wanted). IRREGULAR verbs: change form in different ways (go → went, eat → ate, read → read, see → saw, think → thought). Find examples from *Matilda*. | Learn |
| We Do (20 min) | Irregular verb hunt: Find 10 irregular verbs in Chapter 5. Write them in PAST form. Check: Do they follow the -ED rule? (no — they are irregular). Create a class irregular verb chart. | Group hunt |
| You Do (10 min) | Write 5 sentences using irregular past tense verbs from the story: ate, went, saw, thought, spoke, gave, knew. | Written practice |

---

### Lesson 3: Tense Consistency (You Do)
**Time:** 40 minutes | **Type:** Independent

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| You Do (40 min) | Write a paragraph (8+ sentences) about Matilda. Check: Have you stayed in the SAME tense throughout? Circle all your verbs. Underline any tense changes. Revise to keep consistent tense. Read aloud to check. | Paragraph writing with tense check |

## WEEK 5 — ADJECTIVES AND ADVERBS

### Lesson 1: Describing Words (I Do)
**Time:** 40 minutes | **Type:** Explicit Teaching

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Mini-Lesson (15 min) | ADJECTIVES describe NOUNS (the brilliant girl, the cruel headmistress, the enormous library). ADVERBS describe VERBS (she spoke quietly, he ran quickly, Matilda worked hard). Dahl uses both: "the extraordinarily clever child" (adjective) vs "she walked slowly" (adverb). | Learn |
| Activity (15 min) | Distinguish: "The school was boring." (boring = adjective) vs "The school bored Matilda." (bored = verb). "She sang beautifully." (beautifully = adverb, describes sang). "A terrifying storm." (terrifying = adjective). Sort examples. | Sorting |
| You Do (10 min) | Write 4 sentences with adjectives and 4 sentences with adverbs. Circle adjectives, underline adverbs. | Written practice |

---

### Lesson 2: How, When, Where (I Do → We Do)
**Time:** 40 minutes | **Type:** Explicit Teaching + Guided

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| I Do (10 min) | Adverbs answer questions: HOW? (quickly, quietly, brilliantly) WHEN? (yesterday, tomorrow, later) WHERE? (here, there, upstairs, outside). "Matilda worked quietly." (how) "Matilda would read later." (when) "Matilda hid the mouse there." (where) | Learn |
| We Do (20 min) | Transform sentences to add adverbs: "Matilda wrote." → "Matilda wrote neatly." "The children waited." → "The children waited patiently." "She sat." → "She sat quietly in the corner." Add HOW/WHEN/WHERE adverbs to sentences from the story. | Guided transformation |
| You Do (10 min) | Write 6 sentences. Add an adverb to each: HOW, WHEN, or WHERE. Label each adverb with its type. | Written practice |

## WEEK 6 — APOSTROPHES

### Lesson 1: Contractions (I Do)
**Time:** 40 minutes | **Type:** Explicit Teaching

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Mini-Lesson (15 min) | Contraction = two words joined with an apostrophe taking the place of missing letters. "do not" → "don't" (the apostrophe replaces the "o" in "do not"). "I am" → "I'm." "She is" → "She's." "Will not" → "won't" (irregular contraction). Dahl uses: "I'm," "that's," "wouldn't," "couldn't." | Learn |
| Activity (15 min) | Expand contractions: "Matilda couldn't wait." → "Matilda could not wait." "I'm reading." → "I am reading." "It's amazing." → "It is amazing." "We wouldn't go." → "We would not go." Do 10. Explain which letters are missing. | Practice |
| You Do (10 min) | Write 5 sentences about the story using contractions. Write the expanded form next to each. | Written practice |

---

### Lesson 2: Possession (I Do → We Do)
**Time:** 40 minutes | **Type:** Explicit Teaching + Guided

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| I Do (10 min) | Apostrophe for possession: Singular nouns add 'S (Matilda's book, the school's roof). Plural nouns ending in S just add apostrophe (the children's toys, the dogs' bowls). Apostrophe comes AFTER the owner noun. | Learn |
| We Do (20 min) | Possessive practice: Write the possessive form of: the book belonging to Matilda / the room belonging to Miss Honey / the toys belonging to the children / the hats belonging to the teachers. Check each. | Guided practice |
| You Do (10 min) | Write 6 sentences with possessive apostrophes. Circle each possessive. Check: Whose is it? Is the apostrophe in the right place? | Written practice |

---

## ADAPTATIONS

### EAL Students
- Provide a pronoun chart with visual examples
- Sentence frames for compound sentences
- Possessive noun form chart with examples
- Regular/irregular verb list with past forms displayed
- Allow L1 sentence construction first, then translate
- Visual grammar posters for reference
- Pair with supportive partner

### Gifted Students
- Introduce relative pronouns (who, which, that)
- Explore double negatives and grammar rules about them
- Write complex sentences with multiple clauses
- Create a grammar challenge for the class
- Explore passive voice (was written by)
- Introduce collective nouns (a flock of birds)

### Students with NEPs
- Reduce to 4–5 sentences per task
- Focus on one skill at a time (compound OR pronouns OR tense)
- Provide sentence starters and word banks
- Extended time for all tasks
- One-on-one support for editing
- Use large print and visual supports
- Simplify: Focus on compound sentences and pronoun use only
