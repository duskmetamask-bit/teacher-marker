# Grammar and Punctuation Unit — Year 3

**Unit Duration:** 8 weeks × 3 lessons per week = 24 lessons
**Year Level:** Year 3
**Curriculum Link:** AC9E3LY01, AC9E3LY02, AC9E3LY03, AC9E3SP01, AC9E3SP02
**Focus:** Complex sentences, conjunctions, adverbials, modal verbs, punctuation for clarity
**Mentor Text:** *The BFG* by Roald Dahl (1982)
**Cold Task:** Week 1, Lesson 1 — Write sentences demonstrating complex grammar structures
**Hot Task:** Week 8, Lesson 1 — Same task, measure growth

---

## Unit Overview

**Learning Intention:**
We are learning to construct complex sentences with subordinate clauses, use adverbial phrases to add detail, apply modal verbs appropriately, and punctuate accurately for clarity.

**Success Criteria:**
- I can write complex sentences with a main clause and a subordinate clause.
- I can use adverbial phrases (time, place, manner, reason) to add detail.
- I can use modal verbs (can, could, may, might, must, should, will, would) to show possibility and obligation.
- I can use relative clauses (who, which, that) to add information.
- I can use commas to separate clauses and items in lists.
- I can edit my own work for grammar and punctuation accuracy.

## WEEK 1 — SENTENCE COMPLEXITY

### Lesson 1: Cold Task (Assessment)
**Time:** 45 minutes | **Type:** Assessment

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Hook (5 min) | "The BFG is a big, friendly giant who catches dreams and gives them to children. Today you will show your grammar skills through writing about the BFG." | Engage |
| Read (20 min) | Read Chapters 1–4 of *The BFG* aloud. Notice Dahl's sentence structures — he uses short sentences for effect, but also long, winding sentences full of clauses. | Listen |
| Task (20 min) | Cold Task (same as Hot Task): Write 8 sentences about the BFG and the dream-catching world. Include: 2 complex sentences with subordinate clauses, 2 sentences with adverbial phrases, 2 sentences with modal verbs, 2 sentences with relative clauses. Underline subordinate clauses and circle modal verbs. Year 3: 200–300 words. | Independent sentence writing |

**Differentiation:** Clause structure examples displayed. Sentence starters for complex sentences.

### Lesson 2: Simple vs Complex (I Do)
**Time:** 45 minutes | **Type:** Explicit Teaching

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Mini-Lesson (20 min) | SIMPLE SENTENCE = one clause (The BFG caught a dream). COMPLEX SENTENCE = one main clause + one or more subordinate clauses. "The BFG caught a dream while he was floating in the night sky." MAIN = The BFG caught a dream. SUBORDINATE = while he was floating in the night sky. Dahl uses complex sentences to add detail and description. | Learn |
| Activity (15 min) | Identify main and subordinate clauses in sentences from *The BFG*: "The BFG was enormous because he had been drinking frobscottle for hundreds of years." Identify both parts. Do 6 examples. | Practice |
| Share (10 min) | "How do we know which clause is the main one?" (The main clause can stand alone as a complete sentence. The subordinate clause cannot.) | Discussion |

**Success Criteria Check:** Can identify main clause vs subordinate clause.

### Lesson 3: Subordinate Clauses (We Do)
**Time:** 45 minutes | **Type:** Guided Practice

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|

Create a clause bank: While the giant was sleeping / Because the BFG was kind / If you eat snozzcumber. Use each type in a sentence about the story. | Guided construction |
| You Do (15 min) | Write 6 complex sentences about the BFG — use each type of subordinate clause (time, reason, condition) at least twice. Underline the subordinate clause in each. | Written practice |

## WEEK 2 — SUBORDINATE CLAUSES

### Lesson 1: When, Because, If (I Do)
**Time:** 45 minutes | **Type:** Explicit Teaching

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Mini-Lesson (15 min) | Three key subordinators: WHEN (time) — "The BFG appeared when the other giants were sleeping." BECAUSE (reason) — "Sophie's hair turned white because the BFG touched it." IF (condition) — "If you drink frobscottle, you will burrow up through the ground." Each creates different meaning. | Learn |
| Activity (20 min) | Complete sentence starters with subordinate clauses: "The BFG moved quietly when..." "Because..." "If the other giants found Sophie..." Do 8 completions. Discuss which creates the most interesting sentence. | Practice |
| You Do (10 min) | Write 3 sentences — one using each subordinator. Underline the subordinate clause. | Written practice |

| You Do (10 min) | Build 4 complex sentences on your own. Use different subordinators each time. | Independent building |

## WEEK 3 — ADVERBIAL PHRASES

### Lesson 1: How, When, Where, Why (I Do)
**Time:** 45 minutes | **Type:** Explicit Teaching

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Mini-Lesson (15 min) | Adverbial phrase = a group of words that acts as an adverb (answers how, when, where, why). "The BFG moved quietly" → quietly = adverb. "The BFG moved in complete silence" → in complete silence = adverbial phrase (more detail). ADVERBIAL PHRASES: Manner (how) — with great care, in the darkness / Time (when) — in the middle of the night, before dawn / Place (where) — in the cave, above the clouds / Reason (why) — because of the cold, for warmth. | Learn |
| Activity (20 min) | Add adverbial phrases to simple sentences: "The BFG ate." → "The BFG ate in the middle of the night with enormous bites." "Sophie was frightened." → "Sophie was frightened because of the strange giant." Add to 8 sentences. | Practice |
| You Do (10 min) | Write 4 sentences with adverbial phrases. Label each: HOW / WHEN / WHERE / WHY. | Written practice |

---

### Lesson 2: Fronted Adverbials (I Do → We Do)
**Time:** 45 minutes | **Type:** Explicit Teaching + Guided

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| I Do (10 min) | Fronted adverbial = adverbial phrase at the START of the sentence for emphasis. "The BFG sneezed." → "In the dead of night, the BFG sneezed." "He collected dreams." → "Every night, he collected dreams from the world's children." Fronting creates variety and emphasis. Dahl uses this for effect. | Learn |
| We Do (25 min) | Front 8 sentences: "The BFG waited." → "In the darkness, the BFG waited." "The giants fought." → "During the day, the giants fought each other." Practice fronting with time adverbials. | Guided fronting |
| You Do (10 min) | Write 4 sentences with fronted adverbials. Underline the fronted adverbial. | Written practice |

---

### Lesson 3: Adverbial Practice (You Do)
**Time:** 45 minutes | **Type:** Independent

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| You Do (45 min) | Write a descriptive paragraph about the BFG's cave. Use at least 8 adverbial phrases. Mix: HOW, WHEN, WHERE, WHY. Mix: fronted and mid-sentence positions. Underline all adverbial phrases. Label each one. | Full paragraph |

## WEEK 4 — MODAL VERBS

### Lesson 1: Can, Could, May, Might (I Do)
**Time:** 45 minutes | **Type:** Explicit Teaching

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Mini-Lesson (15 min) | Modal verbs show POSSIBILITY and ABILITY. CAN = ability (I can catch dreams). COULD = past ability or polite request (I could fly). MAY = permission or possibility (You may enter). MIGHT = possibility (It might rain tonight). In *The BFG*: "I could hear the giants from my cave." "The BFG might be angry." Dahl uses modals to show possibility and uncertainty. | Learn |
| Activity (20 min) | Transform sentences using modals: "The BFG catches dreams." → "The BFG can catch dreams." "He heard the giant." → "He could hear the giant." "It will rain." → "It might rain." Use each modal in context about the story. Do 10 transformations. | Practice |
| You Do (10 min) | Write 4 sentences with modal verbs (can, could, may, might) about the BFG. Circle each modal verb. | Written practice |

---

### Lesson 2: Must, Should, Will, Would (I Do → We Do)
**Time:** 45 minutes | **Type:** Explicit Teaching + Guided

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| I Do (10 min) | MUST = obligation (You must go to bed). SHOULD = advice/recommendation (You should eat vegetables). WILL = future intent (I will help you). WOULD = polite request or imaginary (Would you help me?). "The BFG must protect Sophie." "The other giants should be destroyed." "The dream would always visit her." | Learn |
| We Do (25 min) | Transform: "The BFG has to stay hidden." → "The BFG must stay hidden." "It is a good idea to drink frobscottle." → "You should drink frobscottle." "The BFG will give the dream to Sophie." → "The BFG would give the dream to Sophie." Practice all modals. | Guided transformation |
| You Do (10 min) | Write 4 sentences with modal verbs (must, should, will, would) about the story. Circle each modal. | Written practice |

---

### Lesson 3: Modal Verb Writing (You Do)
**Time:** 45 minutes | **Type:** Independent

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| You Do (45 min) | Write a persuasive paragraph about whether the BFG should catch more dreams. Use at least 6 modal verbs. Must, should, can, could, will, would, may, might — use at least 4 different ones. Circle each modal. | Persuasive writing |

## WEEK 5 — RELATIVE CLAUSES

### Lesson 1: Who and Which (I Do)
**Time:** 45 minutes | **Type:** Explicit Teaching

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Mini-Lesson (15 min) | Relative clause = a clause starting with who, which, or that, that adds information about a noun. "The BFG was kind." "He caught dreams." → "The BFG who caught dreams was kind." WHO = for people/giants. WHICH = for things. THAT = for people or things. The relative clause is embedded in the sentence. Dahl uses: "The BFG, who was enormous, caught dreams." | Learn |
| Activity (20 min) | Combine sentences using who/which: "The giant was tall. He ate snozzcumber." → "The giant who ate snozzcumber was tall." "The cave was dark. It sat in the mountain." → "The cave which sat in the mountain was dark." Do 8 combinations. | Practice |
| You Do (10 min) | Write 4 sentences with relative clauses using who or which. Underline the relative clause. | Written practice |

---

### Lesson 2: That (I Do → We Do)
**Time:** 45 minutes | **Type:** Explicit Teaching + Guided

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| I Do (10 min) | THAT can replace WHO or WHICH in most cases. "The BFG who caught dreams" = "The BFG that caught dreams." "The dream that Sophie liked best" = "The dream which Sophie liked best." THAT is more general and often used in speech. Dahl uses which more often in formal writing. | Learn |
| We Do (25 min) | Replace who/which with that in sentences: "The giants who were cruel" → "The giants that were cruel." "The dreams which came at night" → "The dreams that came at night." When is it better to keep who/which vs use that? Discuss style and effect. | Guided replacement |
| You Do (10 min) | Write 4 sentences with relative clauses using that. Underline each relative clause. | Written practice |

---

### Lesson 3: Relative Clause Practice (You Do)
**Time:** 45 minutes | **Type:** Independent

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| You Do (45 min) | Write a description of the BFG and his world. Include at least 6 relative clauses. Use who, which, and that. Mix embedded and sentence-final positions. Underline each relative clause. Check: Does the relative clause add useful information? | Descriptive writing |

## WEEK 6 — PUNCTUATION FOR CLARITY

### Lesson 1: Commas in Sentences (I Do)
**Time:** 45 minutes | **Type:** Explicit Teaching

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Mini-Lesson (15 min) | Commas in complex sentences: Use a comma BEFORE the subordinate clause if it comes FIRST: "Before the sun rose, the BFG collected dreams." But NOT if the subordinate clause comes second: "The BFG collected dreams before the sun rose." Commas also separate items in lists: "The cave contained rocks, sticks, bottles, and old bones." Dahl uses commas for rhythm and clarity. | Learn |
| Activity (20 min) | Add commas where needed: "Before breakfast the BFG ate snozzcumber." "The BFG who was kind and gentle protected Sophie." "The giants ate human beans肉, cabbage and snozzcumber." Check: Is there a subordinate clause at the start? Is there a list? Correct 10 sentences. | Practice |
| You Do (10 min) | Write 4 sentences with commas. At least one must have a subordinate clause with a comma. At least one must have a list with commas. | Written practice |

---

### Lesson 2: Commas in Lists (I Do → We Do)
**Time:** 45 minutes | **Type:** Explicit Teaching + Guided

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| I Do (10 min) | Oxford comma: In a list, use a comma before AND at the end for the last item if there are more than two items in a complex list. "The BFG ate beans, carrots, and potatoes." Without the comma before AND: "The BFG ate beans, carrots and potatoes." (Could mean beans and carrots, or beans and potatoes). The Oxford comma prevents confusion. Use it for clarity. | Learn |
| We Do (25 min) | Correct comma placement in lists: "The giants ate human beans肉 and cabbage and potatoes." (add commas) "Sophie's hair was white and long and wavy." (correct punctuation). Practice with 10 lists. Add the Oxford comma where needed. | Guided correction |
| You Do (10 min) | Write 5 sentences with lists. Use commas correctly including the Oxford comma in complex lists. | Written practice |

---

### Lesson 3: Apostrophes and Hyphens (You Do)
**Time:** 45 minutes | **Type:** Independent

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| Mini-Lesson (5 min) | Apostrophe recap: contractions (can't, won't, I'd), possession (the BFG's cave, the children's dreams). Hyphen: compound adjectives (big-hearted giant, well-known dream). One rule: hyphenate when two or more words work together to describe a noun before the noun. | Learn |
| You Do (40 min) | Write 10 sentences: 5 with apostrophes (explain each), 5 with hyphens (explain each). Check punctuation carefully. | Written practice |

## ADAPTATIONS

### EAL Students
- Provide clause structure templates (Main clause + SUBORDINATE CLAUSE)
- Sentence frames for adverbial phrases
- Modal verb reference chart with meaning explanations
- Allow L1 sentence construction first, then translate
- Visual grammar posters
- Pair with supportive partner
- Focus on complex sentences and modal verbs first

### Gifted Students
- Explore concessive clauses (although, even though)
- Introduce conditional perfect (if I had known)
- Write sentences with multiple subordinate clauses
- Analyse Dahl's sentence structures in depth
- Explore embedded relative clauses
- Introduce passive voice constructions

### Students with NEPs
- Reduce to 4–5 sentences per task
- Focus on complex sentences with ONE subordinate clause type
- Provide sentence starters for complex sentences
- Extended time for all tasks
- One-on-one support for editing
- Simplify: Focus on complex sentence + adverbial phrases only
