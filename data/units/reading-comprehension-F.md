# Reading Comprehension Unit — Foundation (F)

**Unit Duration:** 8 weeks × 3 lessons per week = 24 lessons
**Year Level:** Foundation (F)
**Curriculum Link:** AC9EFLY01, AC9EFLY02, AC9E1RE01
**Focus:** Retelling stories, literal comprehension, making connections
**Mentor Text:** *The Gruffalo* by Julia Donaldson (illustrated by Axel Scheffler, 1999)
**Cold Task:** Week 1, Lesson 1 — Retell *The Gruffalo* in sequence
**Hot Task:** Week 8, Lesson 1 — Same retell task, measure growth

---

## Unit Overview

**Learning Intention:**
We are learning to retell a familiar story in the correct sequence and answer questions about what we have read.

**Success Criteria:**
- I can retell the beginning, middle and end of a story.
- I can name the main character and where the story happens.
- I can answer questions about a story using pictures and words.
- I can talk about what happened in a story.
- I can make a connection between the story and my own life.

## WEEK 1 — GETTING TO KNOW THE STORY

### Lesson 1: Cold Task (Assessment)
**Time:** 30 minutes | **Type:** Assessment

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Hook (5 min) | Show the cover of *The Gruffalo*. Ask: What do you think this story might be about? | Share predictions |
| Read (10 min) | Read *The Gruffalo* aloud with expression. Show illustrations. | Listen, look, follow |
| Task (15 min) | Cold Task: Draw and write about the story of *The Gruffalo*. Show the beginning, middle and end. (Same task as Hot Task.) | Independent drawing and writing |

**Differentiation:** Sentence starters available. Visual sequence cards for support.

---

### Lesson 2: Reading The Gruffalo (I Do)
**Time:** 30 minutes | **Type:** Explicit Teaching

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Read (10 min) | Reread *The Gruffalo* pp. 1–10. Stop at key moments: "What do you notice about the little mouse?" | Point and share observations |
| Mini-Lesson (10 min) | Introduce story vocabulary: Gruffalo, deep, dark, forest, terrified, screeches | Repeat and mime new words |
| Share (10 min) | "What was your favourite part so far?" Turn to a partner and share. | Partner talk |

**Success Criteria Check:** Can identify main character (little mouse).

---

### Lesson 3: Who, What, Where? (We Do)
**Time:** 30 minutes | **Type:** Guided Practice

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| I Do (5 min) | Model: "In *The Gruffalo*, the WHO is the little mouse. The WHERE is the deep dark forest." Write on board. | Observe |
| We Do (10 min) | Together, identify WHO and WHERE using picture cards from the story. Add WHAT (mouse is scared, wants to walk). | Contribute answers |
| You Do (15 min) | Complete Who-What-Where worksheet with pictures and words | Independent or supported work |

| You Do (10 min) | Draw the mouse's face at the beginning and end — how does it change? | Compare emotions through drawing |

## RUBRIC — Reading Comprehension (Foundation)

**AC9EFLY01:** Recognise that texts have different purposes depending on their context and about different types of texts. | **AC9EFLY02:** Identify some features of texts including events and characters and represent events in sequence, using pictures and actions. | **AC9E1RE01:** Understand that texts can have many purposes and recognise、目的に従ってテキストを使い分ける。 Retell events in sequence.

| Criterion | A (Excellent) | B (Strong) | C (Satisfactory) | D (Developing) | E (Beginning) |
|-----------|---------------|------------|-----------------|-----------------|---------------|
| **Story Sequence** | Retells beginning, middle and end in correct order with detailed events. | Retells B-M-E in correct order with main events included. | Retells B-M-E with some support. Events mostly in order. | Retells with teacher prompting. Sequence may be incomplete. | Unable to retell story sequence without significant support. |
| **Character Identification** | Names all characters correctly and describes their traits. | Names main characters and one trait. | Names the main character. | Identifies characters with picture support. | Cannot identify story characters. |
| **Text Understanding** | Answers comprehension questions with relevant evidence from text and illustrations. | Answers most comprehension questions correctly. | Answers literal questions about the story. | Needs support to answer comprehension questions. | Cannot answer comprehension questions. |
| **Making Connections** | Makes clear, relevant personal connections and explains how they relate to the story. | Makes a personal connection to the story. | Identifies a basic connection with prompting. | Struggles to make connections to the text. | No connection made. |
| **Recall and Detail** | Recalls and includes specific and relevant details from the story. | Recalls main events with some details. | Recalls main events. | Recalls limited details from the story. | Cannot recall story details. |

## ADAPTATIONS

### EAL Students
- Use home language for initial story retell
- Provide picture cards for all vocabulary items
- Allow oral retelling as alternative to written
- Visual word wall for story language (deep, dark, scary, clever, brave)
- Allow L1 transliteration for Cold/Hot Task
- Preview story with L1 version when available
- Use picture sequence cards during retelling

### Gifted Students
- Retell from the Gruffalo's perspective
- Write a sequel to *The Gruffalo*
- Compare three Julia Donaldson stories
- Create a new character who enters the forest
- Write a "wanted" poster for the Gruffalo
- Design a map of the deep dark forest

### Students with NEPs
- Reduce expectations to 2-part retell (beginning/end)
- Provide a visual story map template to complete
- Allow single-word or picture-based responses
- Extended time for all tasks
- One-on-one reading support
- Use physical picture card sequences for retelling

## ASSESSMENT NOTES

### Formative Assessment (Ongoing)
- Story map drawings
- Partner retell observations
- Character feeling charts
- Exit tickets: "Draw the problem and the solution"

### Summative Assessment
- Cold Task → Hot Task comparison (same rubric, measure growth)
- Reader's Theatre performance checklist

### Tracking Progress
1. Cold Task score → Hot Task score
2. Specific skills improved (sequence, character, connections)
3. Student self-assessment (thumbs up/half/down for each success criterion)
4. Next semester reading goals
