# WEEK 1 — ENGAGE

**Focus:** Cold Task + Naming 2D shapes
**5E Phase:** Engage

---

## LESSON 1.2 — Circles, Squares, Triangles, Rectangles

**Duration:** 40 minutes
**Type:** Concept Introduction

---

### Learning Intentions
- I am learning to name and recognise circles, squares, triangles, and rectangles
- I am learning the difference between shapes

### Success Criteria
- I can name and draw all four basic shapes

---

### I DO (15 min)

> " CIRCLE: round, like a ball. No corners. (Show examples)"

> " TRIANGLE: three sides, three corners. (Show examples)"

> " SQUARE: four equal sides, four corners. All sides the same. (Show examples)"

> " RECTANGLE: four sides, four corners. Like a square but not all sides equal. (Show examples)"

---

### WE DO (15 min)

**Shape hunt:** Find shapes in the room.
**Shape sort:** Cards showing shapes. Name each one.

---

### YOU DO (10 min)

1. Colour circles red, triangles blue, squares green, rectangles yellow.
2. Draw: a circle, a triangle, a square, a rectangle.
3. "Name two things in this room that are circles."

---

## LESSON 1.3 — Shapes in Different Orientations

**Duration:** 40 minutes
**Type:** Guided Practice

---

### I DO (12 min)

> "A triangle can point up, down, sideways — it's still a triangle!"

> "A square rotated is still a square."

> "The SHAPE doesn't change even when we turn it."

---

### YOU DO (15 min)

1. Draw a triangle pointing DOWN.
2. Draw a square turned sideways.
3. "Can you find a rectangle that is standing on its short end? Draw it."
4. EXTENSION: "What happens to a rectangle if you rotate it 90 degrees?"

# WEEK 2 — EXPLORE

**Focus:** Sorting shapes
**5E Phase:** Explore

---

## LESSON 2.1 — Sorting by Properties

**Duration:** 40 minutes
**Type:** Explore

---

### Learning Intentions
- I am learning to sort shapes into groups
- I am learning to explain HOW I sorted them

### Success Criteria
- I can sort shapes and explain my sorting rule

---

### I DO (15 min)

> "I sorted shapes into two groups: STRAIGHT SIDES and CURVED SIDES."

> "Circle has curved sides only. Square has straight sides only. Triangle has straight sides only. Rectangle has straight sides."

> "Some shapes have CORNERS. Corners are where two sides meet."

---

### YOU DO (15 min)

1. Sort by: CURVED SIDES / STRAIGHT SIDES
2. Sort by: HAS CORNERS / NO CORNERS
3. "Sort the shapes two different ways. Tell your partner the rule."
4. EXTENSION: "Can a shape have both curved and straight sides?"

---

# WEEK 3 — EXPLORE

**Focus:** Positional language
**5E Phase:** Explore

---

## LESSON 3.1 — Above, Below, Beside

**Duration:** 40 minutes
**Type:** Explore

---

### Learning Intentions
- I am learning to use positional words: above, below, beside
- I am learning to follow positional directions

### Success Criteria
- I can draw a shape above, below, and beside other shapes

---

### I DO (15 min)

> "ABOVE means: on top of, higher than."
> "BELOW means: under, lower than."
> "BESIDE means: next to, on the side."

Draw on board: ☐ above ○ → ○ below ☐

---

### YOU DO (15 min)

1. Draw a star ABOVE a moon.
2. Draw a fish BELOW a boat.
3. Draw a cat BESIDE a tree.
4. "Point to something above your head. Point to something below your desk."

---

## LESSON 3.2 — In Front, Behind, Between

**Duration:** 40 minutes
**Type:** Guided Practice

---

### I DO (12 min)

> "IN FRONT: before, at the front."
> "BEHIND: after, at the back."
> "BETWEEN: in the middle of two things."

---

### YOU DO (15 min)

1. "In front of you is ___. Behind you is ___."
2. Draw: A dog BETWEEN two cats.
3. "Sit BETWEEN two people in your group."
4. EXTENSION: "Can you stand BETWEEN the door and the window?"

---

# WEEK 4 — EXPLORE

**Focus:** Describing shapes
**5E Phase:** Explain

---

## LESSON 4.1 — What Makes a Shape?

**Duration:** 40 minutes
**Type:** Explain

---

### YOU DO (30 min)

1. Circle the shapes that have exactly 3 corners.
2. "A shape has 4 straight sides. What could it be?"
3. "A shape has no straight sides. What is it?"
4. "Draw a shape that has 4 corners. Name it."

# WEEK 5 — EXPLORE

**Focus:** Flips and slides
**5E Phase:** Elaborate

---

## LESSON 5.1 — Flips and Slides

**Duration:** 40 minutes
**Type:** Elaborate

---

### Learning Intentions
- I am learning to identify when a shape has flipped or slid
- I am learning the words: flip, slide, turn

### Success Criteria
- I can tell if a shape has been flipped or slid

---

### I DO (15 min)

> "SLIDE: move it without turning. The shape looks the same."

> "FLIP: turn it over. It looks like a mirror image."

> "TURN: rotate it. Point a different direction."

---

### YOU DO (15 min)

1. (Show shape in different positions) Is this a slide or a flip?
2. Draw a slide and a flip of a triangle.
3. "Can you flip a circle? Does it look different?"
4. EXTENSION: "If you flip a square twice, does it look the same?"
