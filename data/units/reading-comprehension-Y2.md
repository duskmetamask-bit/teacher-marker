# Reading Comprehension Unit — Year 2

**Unit Duration:** 8 weeks × 3 lessons per week = 24 lessons
**Year Level:** Year 2
**Curriculum Link:** AC9E2LY01, AC9E2LY02, AC9E2RE01, AC9E2RE02, AC9E2RE03
**Focus:** Sequence, main ideas, inference, author's purpose, text connections
**Mentor Text:** *The Twits* by Roald Dahl (illustrated by Quentin Blake, 1980)
**Cold Task:** Week 1, Lesson 1 — Retell and interpret a chapter from *The Twits*
**Hot Task:** Week 8, Lesson 1 — Same task, measure growth

---

## Unit Overview

**Learning Intention:**
We are learning to retell and interpret chapter fiction texts, making inferences and connections to deepen understanding.

**Success Criteria:**
- I can retell a chapter with key events in correct sequence.
- I can identify the main idea and important details.
- I can make inferences using text evidence and prior knowledge.
- I can identify the author's purpose (entertain, inform, persuade).
- I can connect texts to self, other texts, and the world.

## WEEK 1 — GETTING STARTED WITH CHAPTERS

### Lesson 1: Cold Task (Assessment)
**Time:** 40 minutes | **Type:** Assessment

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Hook (5 min) | Show cover of *The Twits*. "These are the most horrible people in the world. Why might an author write about horrible characters?" | Predict and discuss |
| Read (15 min) | Read Chapter 1 ("The Twits") and Chapter 2 ("The Muggle-Wump") aloud. Show illustrations. | Listen, follow, observe |
| Task (20 min) | Cold Task (same as Hot Task): Read the chapter again or listen to it. Tell me the story: What happened? Who are the characters? What do you think about them? Why did the author write this book? Write and draw to show your understanding. | Independent comprehension |

**Differentiation:** Chapter excerpts available in simplified form. Picture support for character identification.

---

### Lesson 2: Meet the Twits (I Do)
**Time:** 40 minutes | **Type:** Explicit Teaching

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Read (15 min) | Reread Chapter 1. Identify: Mr. Twit (nasty), Mrs. Twit (nasty). What does "twit" mean? Look at context clues. | Track character descriptions |
| Mini-Lesson (10 min) | Chapter book features: chapters are sections, each has a heading, chapters continue a story, may have illustrations. Compare to picture books. | Note-taking |
| Share (15 min) | "What do you think will happen in this book?" Make predictions with evidence. Record in reading journals. | Written predictions |

**Success Criteria Check:** Can explain what a chapter book is and name the main characters.

---

### Lesson 3: Chapter Structure (We Do)
**Time:** 40 minutes | **Type:** Guided Practice

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| I Do (10 min) | Model looking at a chapter: heading, first paragraph, key events, ending. Show how chapters often end with a hook. | Observe |
| We Do (20 min) | Examine Chapter 2 together: What happened? Where did it happen? Who was involved? What will happen next? | Guided analysis |
| You Do (10 min) | Draw a chapter map showing the structure of Chapter 2 | Independent work |

## WEEK 2 — RETELLING AND SEQUENCE

### Lesson 1: Retelling a Chapter (I Do)
**Time:** 40 minutes | **Type:** Explicit Teaching

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Read (10 min) | Read Chapter 3 ("The Glass Eye") aloud. Track events. | Follow text |
| Mini-Lesson (10 min) | Retell strategy: WHO → DID WHAT → WHERE → WHEN → HOW DID IT END. Use sequence words: First, Next, Then, After that, Finally. | Model strategy |
| You Do (20 min) | Use the WHO-DID-WHAT-WHERE-WHEN strategy to retell Chapter 3 in writing | Written retell |

---

### Lesson 2: Key Events and Details (I Do → We Do)
**Time:** 40 minutes | **Type:** Explicit Teaching + Guided

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| I Do (10 min) | "Not everything is equal. Some things are KEY EVENTS (the story can't work without them). Some things are DETAILS (interesting but not essential)." Model with Chapter 3. | Learn distinction |
| We Do (20 min) | Together, sort events from Chapter 3 into KEY EVENTS and DETAILS using a T-chart | Group sorting |
| You Do (10 min) | Complete your own KEY EVENTS / DETAILS chart for Chapter 3 | Independent sorting |

---

### Lesson 3: Chapter Summary (We Do → You Do)
**Time:** 40 minutes | **Type:** Guided → Independent

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| I Do (5 min) | Model writing a chapter summary in 3–5 sentences: WHO and WHERE, WHAT happened, HOW it ended. | Observe |
| We Do (15 min) | Together, write a class summary for Chapter 3 using the 3-sentence frame | Group writing |
| You Do (20 min) | Write a 3–5 sentence summary for Chapter 3 | Independent writing |

## WEEK 3 — MAIN IDEAS AND DETAILS

### Lesson 1: Finding the Main Idea (I Do)
**Time:** 40 minutes | **Type:** Explicit Teaching

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Read (10 min) | Read Chapter 4 ("The Bird Patch") aloud. | Listen |
| Mini-Lesson (10 min) | MAIN IDEA = what the whole chapter is mostly about. DETAILS = the facts and events that support it. Model: "This chapter is mostly about... The details that prove this are..." | Learn concept |
| You Do (20 min) | Identify the main idea of Chapter 4 and list 3 supporting details | Written analysis |

---

### Lesson 2: Important vs Interesting (I Do → We Do)
**Time:** 40 minutes | **Type:** Explicit Teaching + Guided

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| I Do (10 min) | "A fact can be INTERESTING but not IMPORTANT to the main idea." Example from Chapter 4: The Twits are disgusting (interesting, also important for main idea). Distinguish with examples. | Analyze distinction |
| We Do (20 min) | List 5 facts from Chapter 4. Together, sort into IMPORTANT (connects to main idea) vs INTERESTING (fun but not essential). | Sort and discuss |
| You Do (10 min) | Complete the sort independently and write one sentence about the main idea | Application |

---

### Lesson 3: Note-Taking (We Do)
**Time:** 40 minutes | **Type:** Guided Practice

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| I Do (5 min) | Show a simple note-taking format: Heading | Key Points | Important Details. Model with Chapter 4. | Observe format |
| We Do (20 min) | Together, take notes on Chapter 5 ("The Rude Robot") using the format | Group note-taking |
| You Do (15 min) | Practice note-taking on Chapter 6 ("The Trick") | Independent practice |

## WEEK 4 — INFERENTIAL COMPREHENSION

### Lesson 1: Reading Between the Lines (I Do)
**Time:** 40 minutes | **Type:** Explicit Teaching

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Read (10 min) | Read Chapter 7 ("The Great Mouse Plan"). "What isn't the author saying directly?" | Identify unsaid |
| Mini-Lesson (10 min) | Inference formula: WHAT THE TEXT SAYS + WHAT I KNOW + MY THINKING = MY INFERENCE. Example: "Mrs. Twit laughs at Mr. Twit's tricks" → "Mrs. Twit enjoys being cruel" → Inference: These characters enjoy hurting others. | Model strategy |
| You Do (20 min) | Make 3 inferences from Chapter 7 with evidence | Written inference |

---

### Lesson 2: Author's Clues (I Do → We Do)
**Time:** 40 minutes | **Type:** Explicit Teaching + Guided

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| I Do (10 min) | Find clues Dahl uses: word choices (nasty, horrible), illustrations, character actions. These are author's clues for inferences. | Identify clues |
| We Do (20 min) | Hunt for author's clues in Chapter 8. Make an inference. Write: The clue was... I inferred... Because... | Guided inference |
| You Do (10 min) | Make 2 more inferences using author's clues | Independent |

---

### Lesson 3: Inferring Character (You Do)
**Time:** 40 minutes | **Type:** Independent

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| You Do (40 min) | Create a character inference chart for Mr. Twit and Mrs. Twit: What they say | What they do | What others say about them | My inference about their personality | Written character analysis |

## WEEK 5 — AUTHOR'S PURPOSE

### Lesson 1: Why Did the Author Write This? (I Do)
**Time:** 40 minutes | **Type:** Explicit Teaching

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Read (10 min) | Read Chapter 9 ("The Upside-Down upside-Down Cake"). Is this real? Could this happen? | Discuss |
| Mini-Lesson (10 min) | AUTHOR'S PURPOSE: TO ENTERTAIN (make us laugh, enjoy, feel excited) / TO INFORM (teach us something) / TO PERSUADE (make us think or do something). Which purpose fits *The Twits*? Why? | Identify purpose |
| You Do (20 min) | Write a response: What do you think is Roald Dahl's purpose for *The Twits*? Give 3 pieces of evidence. | Written purpose analysis |

---

### Lesson 2: Genre and Purpose (I Do → We Do)
**Time:** 40 minutes | **Type:** Explicit Teaching + Guided

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| I Do (10 min) | *The Twits* is a comic novel — fiction designed to entertain and make us laugh. Show how Dahl uses: gross details, dramatic irony, funny names, exaggerated situations. | Analyze genre |
| We Do (20 min) | Find 5 examples of comic techniques in Chapters 1–5. Categorise: Slapstick (physical) / Verbal irony (words) / Exaggeration / Nonsense words. | Categorise examples |
| You Do (10 min) | Choose your favourite comic moment and explain why it is funny | Written response |

---

### Lesson 3: Evaluating the Text (We Do)
**Time:** 40 minutes | **Type:** Guided Practice

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| We Do (20 min) | Evaluate *The Twits*: Is it a good book? What works? What is effective? Share opinions and back them up with evidence from the text. | Class discussion |
| You Do (20 min) | Write a book review: 1. What is the book about? 2. What did you think of it? 3. Would you recommend it? Why or why not? Give a star rating (1–5). | Written review |

## WEEK 6 — TEXT CONNECTIONS

### Lesson 1: Text to Self (We Do)
**Time:** 40 minutes | **Type:** Guided Practice

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| I Do (5 min) | "Good readers connect texts to their own lives. Have you ever met someone like the Twits? Have you ever played a trick on someone?" Share examples. | Listen |
| We Do (25 min) | Write 3 text-to-self connections for *The Twits*: 1. This reminded me of... because... 2. I have felt like... when... 3. I can relate to... because... | Personal connection writing |
| Share (10 min) | Share connections with a partner | Partner discussion |

---

### Lesson 2: Text to Text (I Do)
**Time:** 40 minutes | **Type:** Explicit Teaching

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Read (10 min) | Recall *Matilda* or *Charlie and the Chocolate Factory*. What is the same about Roald Dahl's books? | Compare texts |
| Mini-Lesson (10 min) | Text-to-text connections: BOTH have... / NEITHER has... / THE WAY THEY HANDLE ___ IS SIMILAR/DIFFERENT. Both Roald Dahl books have horrible adults, clever children, happy endings. | Note-taking |
| You Do (20 min) | Compare *The Twits* to another Roald Dahl book or another funny book you know. Complete a Venn diagram. | Comparison |

---

### Lesson 3: Text to World (I Do → We Do)
**Time:** 40 minutes | **Type:** Explicit Teaching + Guided

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| I Do (10 min) | "Do horrible people like the Twits exist in the real world?" Discuss. Connect to news, history, current events. | Real-world connection |
| We Do (20 min) | Research or discuss: Are there real people who are cruel to animals? What happens to them? How does this connect to Chapter 10? | Group research |
| You Do (10 min) | Write: "Reading *The Twits* made me think about..." Connect the fiction to the real world. | Written connection |

## ADAPTATIONS

### EAL Students
- Provide chapter summaries in home language
- Allow L1 book comparison when available
- Sentence starters for all written responses
- Pair with reading buddy for oral retell practice
- Use picture dictionaries for vocabulary
- Allow oral response as alternative to written
- Preview key vocabulary before each chapter

### Gifted Students
- Compare Roald Dahl's style across 3 books
- Analyse a chapter's structure (setup, complication, resolution)
- Research Roald Dahl's life and writing process
- Write a new chapter for *The Twits*
- Create a documentary-style text about fictional "real" Twits
- Design a movie trailer for *The Twits*

### Students with NEPs
- Reduce to one chapter per session
- Provide a completed chapter summary template
- Allow picture-based retell with single-word labels
- Extended time for all tasks
- One-on-one reading support
- Provide peer helper
- Simplify success criteria to 3 items
