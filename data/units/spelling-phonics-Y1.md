# Spelling and Phonics Unit — Year 1

**Unit Duration:** 8 weeks × 3 lessons per week = 24 lessons
**Year Level:** Year 1
**Curriculum Link:** AC9E1SP01, AC9E1SP02, AC9E1LY03
**Focus:** CVC word patterns, consonant blends, digraphs, common spelling patterns, high-frequency words
**Mentor Text:** *Room on the Broom* by Julia Donaldson (2002)
**Cold Task:** Week 1, Lesson 1 — Spell CVC words and identify sound patterns
**Hot Task:** Week 8, Lesson 1 — Same task, measure growth

---

## Unit Overview

**Learning Intention:**
We are learning to decode and encode words using letter-sound knowledge, identify spelling patterns, and spell high-frequency words accurately.

**Success Criteria:**
- I can read and spell CVC words with short vowels confidently.
- I can identify and use common consonant digraphs (sh, ch, th, wh).
- I can identify and use initial and final consonant blends (bl, cl, tr, st, nd, mp).
- I can read and spell words with common spelling patterns (-ight, -ake, -ell).
- I can read and spell 20+ high-frequency sight words.
- I can use sound-spelling knowledge to attempt unknown words.

## WEEK 1 — CVC WORD PATTERNS

### Lesson 1: Cold Task (Assessment)
**Time:** 35 minutes | **Type:** Assessment

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Hook (5 min) | "Room on the Broom is about a kind witch who makes room for friends. Today we will show how well we can read and spell words with different sounds." | Engage |
| Read (10 min) | Read *Room on the Broom* aloud. Stop to notice spelling patterns: "flying" has -y making /ie/ sound. "Broom" has -oom. "Splendid" has -ent. | Listen |
| Task (20 min) | Cold Task (same as Hot Task): 1. Write these CVC words: cat, sit, dog, run, cup, hen. 2. Write the missing vowel: c_t, s_t, d_g, r_n, c_p, h_n. 3. Write a word that rhymes with: hat → ___, sit → ___, dog → ___. Year 1: All correct spelling expected. | Independent spelling |

**Differentiation:** Word wall support. CVC word cards. Allow verbal spelling.

---

### Lesson 2: Short Vowel Review (I Do)
**Time:** 35 minutes | **Type:** Explicit Teaching

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Mini-Lesson (15 min) | Short vowels in CVC words: A says /a/ (cat), E says /e/ (hen), I says /i/ (sit), O says /o/ (dog), U says /u/ (cup). The consonant surrounds the vowel. The vowel is SHORT — not its name, its SOUND. Review each sound with actions: A = open mouth wide, E = teeth together, I = fingers on jaw, O = round lips, U = open mouth. | Learn |
| Activity (15 min) | Vowel sort: Mix up CVC word cards. Sort into vowels: _at (cat, hat, mat, sat) vs _et (pet, met, let, set). Say each word. Sort into 5 columns: A | E | I | O | U. | Sorting |
| Share (5 min) | "How do you know which vowel to use?" The sound tells you. | Discussion |

**Success Criteria Check:** Can sort CVC words by short vowel sound.

---

### Lesson 3: CVC Spelling (We Do)
**Time:** 35 minutes | **Type:** Guided Practice

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| We Do (20 min) | Build CVC words with letter tiles: Show a picture. Say the word. Segment the sounds. Build the word. Check: hat → h-a-t. Do 12 words (3 per vowel). Write each word after building. | Guided word building |
| You Do (15 min) | Write 8 CVC words. Circle the vowel in each word. Write the vowel sound under each. | Written practice |

## WEEK 2 — CONSONANT DIGRAPHS

### Lesson 1: sh and ch (I Do)
**Time:** 35 minutes | **Type:** Explicit Teaching

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Mini-Lesson (15 min) | Digraph = TWO letters making ONE sound. SH says /sh/ (ship, shell, wish). CH says /ch/ (chip, chair, much). From *Room on the Broom*: "splendid" (ch), "wish" (sh). Show words. Say the digraph sound. Notice the two letters but one sound. | Learn |
| Activity (15 min) | Digraph sort: Lay out SH and CH letter cards. Show picture cards (ship, fish, chair, chin, shoe, bench). Say each word. Sort under the correct digraph. Write each word. | Sorting |
| You Do (5 min) | Write 4 sh words and 4 ch words. Underline the digraph. | Written practice |

---

### Lesson 2: th and wh (I Do → We Do)
**Time:** 35 minutes | **Type:** Explicit Teaching + Guided

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| I Do (10 min) | TH says /th/ (the, this, that, with). WH says /wh/ (when, what, where, wheel). TH is at the start of most words (the, this, that). WH is at the start of question words (what, when, where). | Learn |
| We Do (20 min) | Sort picture cards: thumb, three, whale, wheel, thing, whiskers. Sort under TH vs WH. Write each word. Check: Does the word start with /th/ or /wh/? | Sorting |
| You Do (5 min) | Write 3 th words and 3 wh words. Underline the digraph. | Written practice |

---

### Lesson 3: Digraph Spelling (You Do)
**Time:** 35 minutes | **Type:** Independent

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| You Do (35 min) | Digraph spelling: 1. Complete the digraph: sh_p, ch_ip, th_ee, wh_at, sh_ll, ch_ck. 2. Write the digraph word: /sh/ + /i/ + /p/ → ship. /ch/ + /a/ + /t/ → chat. 3. Write a sentence using one digraph word: "The fish swims in the water." Underline digraphs. | Spelling practice |

## WEEK 3 — INITIAL BLENDS

### Lesson 1: bl, cl, fl, gl (I Do)
**Time:** 35 minutes | **Type:** Explicit Teaching

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Mini-Lesson (15 min) | Initial blend = TWO consonants at the START of a word that BOTH make their sound. BL says /bl/ (black, blast, blue). CL says /cl/ (clap, clock, cloud). FL says /fl/ (flag, flash, float). GL says /gl/ (glow, glass, glitter). From *Room on the Broom*: "flew" (fl-), "splendid" (spl-). The two sounds blend together but you hear both. | Learn |
| Activity (15 min) | Blend walk: I say a word with initial blend. Students walk to the correct blend bucket (BL, CL, FL, GL). Say the word, identify the blend, write the word. Words: black, clap, flag, glow, blast, clock, flash, glitter, plane, dragon (different — not a blend). | Game |
| You Do (5 min) | Write the blend: bl_ck → black, cl_ap → clap, fl_ag → flag, gl_w → glow. | Written practice |

---

### Lesson 2: tr, dr, cr, fr (I Do → We Do)
**Time:** 35 minutes | **Type:** Explicit Teaching + Guided

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| I Do (10 min) | TR says /tr/ (trap, tree, truck). DR says /dr/ (drop, dress, drum). CR says /cr/ (crab, crown, crash). FR says /fr/ (frog, fresh, frame). Practice: Say the blend first, then the full word. | Learn |
| We Do (20 min) | Blend building: Add letters to make words: tr_ → tree, tr_ap → trap, dr_g → drug, dr_op → drop, cr_sh → crash, fr_og → frog, cr_own → crown, fr_sh → fresh. Build and write. | Guided building |
| You Do (5 min) | Write 4 words with tr, dr, cr, fr blends. Underline the blend. | Written practice |

---

### Lesson 3: Initial Blend Practice (You Do)
**Time:** 35 minutes | **Type:** Independent

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| You Do (35 min) | 1. Complete the blend: bl_de, cl_ud, fl_ower, gl_ove, tr_ee, dr_gon, cr_b, fr_g. 2. Write words from a blend dictation: "The frog can jump. The crab has claws. The tree is tall." 3. Sort 12 words into BL, CL, FL, GL, TR, DR, CR, FR columns. | Full spelling practice |

## WEEK 4 — FINAL BLENDS

### Lesson 1: nd, nt, mp, st (I Do)
**Time:** 35 minutes | **Type:** Explicit Teaching

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Mini-Lesson (15 min) | Final blend = TWO consonants at the END of a word that BOTH make their sound. ND says /nd/ (hand, find, and). NT says /nt/ (tent, went, sent). MP says /mp/ (jump, camp, lamp). ST says /st/ (best, last, fast). From *Room on the Broom*: "wand" (-nd), "friend" (-nd). | Learn |
| Activity (15 min) | Final blend sort: Lay out final blend cards (-nd, -nt, -mp, -st). Show picture cards (hand, tent, jump, last, find, sent, camp, best). Say each word, identify the final blend, sort under the correct ending. | Sorting |
| You Do (5 min) | Write words with final blends: ha_d → hand, te_t → tent, ju_p → jump, la_t → last. | Written practice |

## WEEK 5 — COMMON SPELLING PATTERNS

### Lesson 1: -ake, -ate, -ame (I Do)
**Time:** 35 minutes | **Type:** Explicit Teaching

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Mini-Lesson (15 min) | Spelling patterns = letters that always spell the same sound together. -AKE says /ake/ (cake, make, take, snake). -ATE says /ate/ (late, gate, date, plate). -AME says /ame/ (game, name, flame, same). These are pattern words — once you know the pattern, you can spell many words. | Learn |
| Activity (15 min) | Pattern completion: c_se, m_ke, l_te, g_te, n_me, fl_me, sn_ke, pl_te, t_ke, w_ke. Complete with the right vowel pattern. Sort into -ake, -ate, -ame columns. | Pattern practice |
| You Do (5 min) | Write 4 words from each pattern. Underline the pattern. | Written practice |

---

### Lesson 2: -ight, -ite, -ine (I Do → We Do)
**Time:** 35 minutes | **Type:** Explicit Teaching + Guided

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| I Do (10 min) | -IGHT says /ite/ (light, night, right, bright, flight, eight). -ITE says /ite/ (kite, white, write, bite). -INE says /ine/ (line, mine, fine, nine, time). Notice: -ight, -ite, and -ine can all make the /ite/ sound. They are different spellings for similar sounds. | Learn |
| We Do (20 min) | Complete: l_ght, n_ght, k_te, wh_te, l_ne, m_ne, fl_ght, b_te. Discuss why each uses the spelling it does (pattern rules). | Guided completion |
| You Do (5 min) | Write 3 words for each pattern: -ight, -ite, -ine. Sort into columns. | Written practice |

## WEEK 6 — LONG VOWEL PATTERNS

### Lesson 1: a-e, i-e, o-e (I Do)
**Time:** 35 minutes | **Type:** Explicit Teaching

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Mini-Lesson (15 min) | Long vowel with magic E (a-e): cap → cape, mad → made, hat → hate. The E is SILENT and it makes the vowel LONG. Same with i-e: kit → kite, pin → pine. Same with o-e: hop → hope, not → note. One silent E changes the vowel from short to long! | Learn |
| Activity (15 min) | Magic E transformation: Change the word by adding E. Tap → tape, kit → kite, hop → hope, can → cane, kit → kite, not → note, cub → cube, mad → made. Write both forms. Circle the changed vowel. | Transformation |
| You Do (5 min) | Write the magic E words: c_p, h_t, n_t, h_pe, c_ne, k_te, t_pe, l_ke. | Written practice |

---

### Lesson 2: ee, ea (I Do → We Do)
**Time:** 35 minutes | **Type:** Explicit Teaching + Guided

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| I Do (10 min) | Vowel teams = two vowels working together to make one sound. EE says /ee/ (sheep, feet, sleep, green, deep, street). EA says /ee/ (read, bread, lead, team, dream, stream). Both EE and EA can say /ee/. From *Room on the Broom*: "flew" (ew), "green" (ee). | Learn |
| We Do (20 min) | Sort words: sheep, feet, green, read, bread, team, sleep, dream, deep, stream. Sort into EE vs EA columns. Some words: reach (ea says /ee/), good (oo not ee). Discuss. | Guided sorting |
| You Do (5 min) | Write 4 EE words and 4 EA words. Underline the vowel team. | Written practice |

---

### Lesson 3: Long Vowel Spelling (You Do)
**Time:** 35 minutes | **Type:** Independent

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| You Do (35 min) | Long vowel spelling: 1. Write the word: /green/ → green, /dream/ → dream, /hope/ → hope, /kite/ → kite. 2. Complete: sh__p, sl__p, cr__m, l__g, m__d, n__te. 3. Write a sentence using long vowel words. 4. Circle all long vowel spellings. | Full practice |

## ADAPTATIONS

### EAL Students
- Provide word family charts in home language
- Allow verbal spelling before written
- Use picture-word association for patterns
- Focus on one pattern at a time (introduce -ake before -ate)
- Allow phonetic spelling even if irregular
- Provide bilingual word lists

### Gifted Students
- Introduce vowel digraphs (ai, ay, oa, ow, ou)
- Explore -ough, -augh spelling patterns
- Introduce silent letter patterns (kn-, wr-, gn-)
- Read and spell multisyllabic words
- Explore homophones (their/there/they're)
- Introduce basic etymology (Latin roots)

### Students with NEPs
- Focus on 3 CVC vowel patterns (a, i, o) before introducing e and u
- Use multisensory spelling (write in sand, use letter tiles)
- Allow verbal spelling responses
- Extended time for dictation
- Provide word banks for all activities
- Simplify to: CVC words + 2 digraphs + 10 sight words
