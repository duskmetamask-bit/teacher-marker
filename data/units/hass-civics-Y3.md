# Our Community and Democracy — Civics and Citizenship Unit Plan

**Unit Duration:** 8 weeks × 3 lessons per week = 24 lessons
**Year Level:** Year 3
**Curriculum Link:** Australian Curriculum HASS v9 — Civics and Citizenship (Years 3–4)
**Unit Big Idea:** Democracy means more than just voting — it means everyone having a voice in their community. Citizens can participate in democracy at the local level, and every person's participation matters.
**Cold Task:** Week 1, Lesson 1 — Describe how you participate in your community and explain one way you could participate more in local democracy
**Hot Task:** Week 8, Lesson 1 — Investigate one community issue, explain how citizens can participate in the decision, and propose a specific action you could take

---

## Unit Overview

**Learning Intention:**
We are learning to understand local democracy, identify how citizens can participate in their community, and recognise that everyone has a voice in democratic processes.

**Success Criteria:**
- I can describe what democracy means and why it is important.
- I can identify at least 3 ways citizens can participate in their community.
- I can describe the role of local government in my community.
- I can identify a community issue and explain how citizens can have a voice on it.
- I can use sources to find out about local democracy.
- I can propose a specific action a citizen could take to participate in local democracy.

## WEEK 1 — ENGAGE: What Is Democracy?

### Lesson 1: Cold Task — Our Community and Democracy
**Time:** 40 minutes | **Type:** Assessment (Formative Pre-Assessment)

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Hook (5 min) | Show two images: citizens voting in an election / a community meeting. "What do these images have in common? What do they tell us about democracy? What do you think democracy means?" | Observe and discuss |
| Introduction (5 min) | "In this unit, we will learn about DEMOCRACY — what it means, how it works, and how YOU can participate. Democracy is not just about voting — it is about having a voice in your community. Every person matters in a democracy." | Listen |
| Cold Task (30 min) | "Part 1: How do you participate in your community? (e.g., at school, in your neighbourhood, in your family) Part 2: Describe one way you could participate MORE in local democracy. Part 3: Write 3 sentences explaining why it is important for citizens to have a voice in their community." Year 3 expectation: 2–4 sentences, clear examples, reason given. | Independent |

**Differentiation:** Sentence starters: "I participate in my community by..." / "One way I could participate more is..." / "It is important for citizens to have a voice because..."

**Success Criteria Check:** Student describes at least 1 way they participate and explains why democratic participation matters.

### Lesson 2: Democracy Around the World
**Time:** 40 minutes | **Type:** Engage (Hook)

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| What Is Democracy? (10 min) | "DEMOCRACY means 'rule by the people.' In a democracy: citizens have a say in decisions / people can vote for their leaders / everyone is equal under the law / there is freedom of speech and assembly / the majority decides, but minority rights are protected." | Learn |
| Different Types of Democracy (10 min) | "There are different types: DIRECT DEMOCRACY (citizens vote directly on every issue — used in some cantons of Switzerland) / REPRESENTATIVE DEMOCRACY (citizens vote for representatives who make decisions — used in Australia) / MONARCHY (a king or queen rules — some monarchies are also democratic, like Australia) / DICTATORSHIP (one person or group rules without citizen input — NOT democratic)." | Learn |
| Democracy Around the World (15 min) | "Democratic countries: Australia, USA, Canada, UK, Japan, India, Germany — most of the Western world / NOT democratic: North Korea, Cuba, some countries in Africa and the Middle East / Some countries are PARTIALLY democratic (contested elections, limited freedom of speech). About 50 countries are genuinely democratic." | Learn |
| Why Is Democracy Important? (5 min) | "Write 2 sentences: Why is democracy important? What would happen if people didn't have a say in how they were governed?" | Write |

**Success Criteria Check:** Can describe what democracy means and name at least 2 types.

## WEEK 2 — EXPLORE: Citizens and Government

### Lesson 1: What Is a Citizen?
**Time:** 40 minutes | **Type:** Explore (Investigation)

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| What Is Citizenship? (10 min) | "A CITIZEN is a person who belongs to a country. In Australia, citizens have RIGHTS (what they are entitled to) and RESPONSIBILITIES (what they should do). Most people in this room are Australian citizens." | Learn |
| Rights of Citizens (10 min) | "Australian citizens have rights: vote in elections (compulsory) / receive protection from the Australian government abroad / access to healthcare (Medicare) and education / freedom of speech and assembly / access to courts for justice / right to a passport." | Learn |
| How Do You Become a Citizen? | What If You Are Not a Citizen? (10 min) | "People who are not Australian citizens (permanent residents, visa holders, international students) have fewer rights: they cannot vote / cannot get an Australian passport / may be refused certain government services / may be deported if they break the law. This is one reason people apply for citizenship." | Learn |
| Write (5 min) | "Write 2 sentences: What rights does an Australian citizen have? Why might someone want to become an Australian citizen?" | Write |

**Success Criteria Check:** Can describe at least 2 rights of Australian citizens and explain at least 1 way to become a citizen.

## WEEK 3 — EXPLORE + EXPLAIN: Our Local Government

### Lesson 1: How Does Local Government Work?
**Time:** 40 minutes | **Type:** Explore (Investigation)

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|

| How Do Decisions Get Made? | Source Analysis: Council Agendas (10 min) | Show a copy of a council meeting agenda (available on council websites). "This is what a council meeting looks like. What do you notice? Who makes decisions? Who can attend?" | Analyse |

## WEEK 6 — ELABORATE: Rights and Responsibilities

### Lesson 1: What Are Rights?
**Time:** 40 minutes | **Type:** Elaborate (Application)

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| What Are Rights? (10 min) | "RIGHTS are entitlements — things you are OWED just because you are a person and/or a citizen. Rights are protected by law and the government must respect them. Rights include: right to vote / right to free speech / right to legal representation / right to education / right to healthcare / freedom from discrimination." | Learn |
| Rights in Australia (10 min) | "Australia has a bill of rights? Actually NO — Australia does NOT have a comprehensive Bill of Rights. Our rights are protected in a MIXED way: through anti-discrimination laws ( Racial Discrimination Act, Sex Discrimination Act) / through the CONSTITUTION (implied rights) / through international agreements we have signed." | Learn |
| Australian Bills of Rights (10 min) | "States and territories: Victoria and the ACT have HUMAN RIGHTS ACTS — these protect specific rights and make government accountable to the courts. Western Australia does NOT have a comprehensive Human Rights Act. This means rights in WA are less protected than in Victoria." | Learn |
| Write (5 min) | "Write 2 sentences: What are rights? Why does Australia need better protection of rights?" | Write |

**Success Criteria Check:** Can describe what rights are and explain at least 1 gap in Australian rights protection.

### Lesson 2: What Are Responsibilities?
**Time:** 40 minutes | **Type:** Elaborate (Application)

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| What Are Responsibilities? (10 min) | "RESPONSIBILITIES are duties — things citizens SHOULD do. Being a citizen comes with responsibilities: OBEY the law / VOTE in elections / SERVE on a jury if called / PAY taxes / PROTECT the environment / RESPECT the rights of others / PARTICIPATE in democracy." | Learn |
| Why Responsibilities Matter (10 min) | "Responsibilities make democracy work: If people don't vote, government doesn't reflect everyone's views / If people don't serve on juries, the justice system fails / If people don't pay taxes, services (schools, hospitals) don't get funded / If people break the law, society breaks down." | Learn |
| Rights and Responsibilities Are Linked (10 min) | "Rights and responsibilities go together: FREEDOM OF SPEECH gives you the right to express opinions, but you have the RESPONSIBILITY not to incite violence / RIGHT TO EDUCATION gives you the right to learn, but you have the RESPONSIBILITY to attend and work / RIGHT TO VOTE gives you the right to choose leaders, but you have the RESPONSIBILITY to inform yourself." | Learn |
| Write (5 min) | "Write 3 sentences: What are responsibilities? Why do they matter? What is one responsibility you have as a citizen?" | Write |

**Success Criteria Check:** Can describe at least 3 citizen responsibilities and explain why they matter.

## WEEK 7 — ELABORATE + EVALUATE: Community Action

### Lesson 1: Plan an Action

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Planning Community Action (15 min) | "Let's PLAN community action. Steps: 1. IDENTIFY the issue clearly / 2. RESEARCH the facts / 3. | Share Plans (5 min) | 3–4 students share their action plans. Class feedback: Is the request specific? Is it reasonable? | Share |
