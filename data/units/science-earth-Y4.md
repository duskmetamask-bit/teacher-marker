# Science Unit: Earth's Resources, Fossils and Geological Timescales

**Unit Duration:** 8 weeks × 3 lessons per week = 24 lessons
**Year Level:** Year 4
**Curriculum Link:** Australian Curriculum Science v9 - Earth and Space Sciences
**AC9S4U01** — Describe how fossils provide evidence of past life and changes in Earth's surface and past environments

**Mentor Text/Context:** Natural history museum, geological formations, environmental science
**Writing Focus:** Scientific observation, evidence-based explanation, factual writing about Earth's history

## Unit Overview

**Learning Intention:**
We are learning about Earth's resources, how fossils form, what they tell us about past life and environments, and how scientists use evidence to understand geological timescales.

**Success Criteria:**
- I can describe what fossils are and how they are formed.
- I can explain how fossils provide evidence of past life and environments.
- I can understand the basic geological timescale — that Earth is very old and life has changed over time.
- I can explain what Earth's resources are and why they are important.
- I can use evidence to support scientific explanations.

## WEEK 1 — INTRODUCTION TO EARTH'S RESOURCES

### Lesson 1: What Is Earth Made Of? (I Do)
**Time:** 50 minutes | **Type:** Explicit Teaching

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| Hook (10 min) | Show images of Earth's layers (crust, mantle, outer core, inner core). "Earth is made of layers — like an onion. The outermost layer, the CRUST, is where we live. It is made of rocks, minerals, and soil. These are Earth's RESOURCES — things we use from the Earth." | Understand layers |
| I Do (15 min) | "Earth's resources include: Minerals (iron, gold, copper) / Rocks (granite, sandstone, limestone) / Soil / Water / Air / Fossil fuels (coal, oil, gas). These resources have formed over BILLIONS of years." | Learn resources |
| You Do (20 min) | Sort: Given 20 images of natural resources, sort into categories (rock, mineral, water, soil, fuel). Write why each fits the category. | Sort |
| Share (5 min) | Which resource do you think is most important for human life? Why? | Discuss |

**Success Criteria Check:** Can name at least 5 of Earth's resources.

**Time:** 50 minutes | **Type:** Guided

## WEEK 2 — HOW FOSSILS FORM

### Lesson 1: The Fossilisation Process (I Do)
**Time:** 50 minutes | **Type:** Explicit Teaching

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| I Do (20 min) | "Step by step: 1. An organism dies in a place where it can be buried — like in mud, sand, or volcanic ash. 2. Layers of sediment (mud, sand) pile on top. 3. Over thousands to millions of years, the sediment hardens into rock — and the organism's body parts (bones, shells) slowly turn to stone. 4. Erosion (wind, water) removes the top layers, exposing the fossil." | Learn process |
| You Do (25 min) | Sequence: Put the 6 steps of fossilisation in order. Write each step. Draw a picture for step 1 and step 6. | Sequence |
| Share (5 min) | Why do most living things NOT become fossils? (They decompose, get eaten, or are not buried) | Discuss |

---

### Lesson 2: Types of Fossils (We Do)
**Time:** 50 minutes | **Type:** Guided

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| I Do (15 min) | "There are several TYPES of fossils. BODY FOSSILS: Actual parts of the organism — bones, shells, teeth, leaves. TRACE FOSSILS: Evidence of activity — footprints, burrows, coprolites (fossilised poo!), tooth marks. IMPRESSION FOSSILS: Prints or impressions left in rock — leaf prints, ripple marks, dinosaur skin prints. MOLD AND CAST: If a shell dissolves leaving a cavity (mold), later minerals can fill it (cast)." | Learn types |
| We Do (20 min) | Match: Fossil type + description + example. Sort 12 fossil examples into categories. | Match |
| You Do (10 min) | Write: "A trace fossil tells us about an organism's ___, not its ___." Complete. | Write |

## WEEK 3 — WHAT FOSSILS TELL US

### Lesson 1: Evidence of Ancient Life (I Do)
**Time:** 50 minutes | **Type:** Explicit Teaching

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| I Do (20 min) | "Fossils tell us: What organisms looked like (body shape, size, structure). What they ate (teeth, digestive system evidence). How they moved (footprints, tail marks). What the environment was like (coral fossils = ancient sea, leaf fossils = ancient forest). How life has changed over time (simple organisms → complex organisms). Why organisms became extinct (climate change, asteroid, disease)." | Learn evidence |
| You Do (25 min) | Analyse fossil data: Given a set of fossil images and descriptions, for each: What does it tell us about the organism? What does it tell us about the environment? Write notes. | Analyse |
| Share (5 min) | What is one thing you learned from a fossil that you couldn't learn any other way? | Share |

### Lesson 2: Extinction and Change (We Do)
**Time:** 50 minutes | **Type:** Guided

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| I Do (10 min) | "EXTINCTION = when a species dies out completely and no longer exists. The fossil record shows that MOST species that have ever lived are now extinct. Extinction happens naturally, but sometimes it happens fast — mass extinction events (5 major ones in Earth's history). The dinosaurs went extinct about 66 million years ago — likely from an asteroid impact." | Learn extinction |
| We Do (25 min) | "Mass extinction events: The 'Big Five' — Ordovician (450 mya), Devonian (375 mya), Permian (250 mya — worst, 90% of species lost), Triassic (200 mya), Cretaceous (66 mya — dinosaurs). For each: What died? What survived? Why?" Research and record. | Research |
| You Do (10 min) | Write: "The Permian extinction was the most devastating because..." Use evidence. | Write |

## WEEK 4 — GEOLOGICAL TIMESCALES

### Lesson 1: How Old Is Earth? (I Do)
**Time:** 50 minutes | **Type:** Explicit Teaching

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| I Do (15 min) | "Earth is approximately 4.54 BILLION years old. This is almost impossible to imagine. If Earth's entire history were compressed into one year: Humans appear on December 31 at 11:59pm. Dinosaurs: June-August. First multicellular life: September. Earth forms: January 1." | Understand age |
| You Do (30 min) | Create a timeline: On a strip of paper (or use a long corridor), mark key events. 4.5 billion years = your timeline. Mark: Earth forms (beginning), first life (3.8 bya), first animals (600 mya), first dinosaurs (245 mya), dinosaurs extinct (66 mya), first humans (300,000 ya). Calculate: If 4.5 billion = 1 year, 1 million years = ? hours? | Create timeline |
| Share (5 min) | What surprises you most about Earth's age? | Discuss |

### Lesson 2: Eras and Periods (We Do)
**Time:** 50 minutes | **Type:** Guided

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| I Do (10 min) | "Scientists divide Earth's history into EONS → ERAS → PERIODS. Phanerozoic Eon (last 540 million years) has three eras: PALEOZOIC (ancient life, fish, amphibians, early reptiles, many extinctions). MESOZOIC (middle life, dinosaurs, mammals small). CENOZOIC (new life, mammals dominate, birds, humans)." | Learn eras |
| We Do (25 min) | Create an era guide: For each era (Paleozoic, Mesozoic, Cenozoic): Write the time range. List key life forms. Write one interesting fact. Illustrate one dominant life form. | Create guide |
| You Do (10 min) | Write: "The dinosaurs lived in the ___ era, which spans from ___ to ___ million years ago." Fill in. | Write |

## WEEK 5 — EARTH'S RESOURCES

### Lesson 1: Minerals and Rocks (I Do)
**Time:** 50 minutes | **Type:** Explicit Teaching

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| I Do (20 min) | "MINERALS are natural, solid substances with a specific chemical structure. ROCKS are made of minerals. Three rock types: IGNEOUS (formed from cooled molten rock — e.g., basalt, granite). SEDIMENTARY (formed from compressed layers — e.g., sandstone, limestone). METAMORPHIC (changed by heat and pressure — e.g., marble from limestone, slate from shale)." | Learn rock types |
| You Do (25 min) | Examine rock samples: Granite (igneous), Sandstone (sedimentary), Marble (metamorphic). For each: Describe appearance, test hardness (scratch with fingernail, nail, knife), identify rock type with reasoning. Record in a table. | Examine |
| Share (5 min) | How can you tell a sedimentary rock from an igneous rock? | Explain |

### Lesson 2: Soil as a Resource (We Do)
**Time:** 50 minutes | **Type:** Guided

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| I Do (10 min) | "SOIL is a crucial resource — without soil, we cannot grow food. Soil forms from rocks breaking down over thousands of years + decomposing organic matter. | We Do (20 min) | "Soil investigation: Take a soil sample. Observe: Colour, texture (sand/silt/clay), moisture, visible organic matter. Test: Put soil in water, stir, let settle — which layers form? (Sand settles fast, clay stays suspended, organic matter floats)." | Investigate soil |

## ADAPTATIONS

### EAL Students
- Visual fossil formation process chart
- Word wall: fossil, sediment, erosion, extinct, mineral, renewable
- Label images with names in home language + English
- Sentence starters for scientific explanations
- Use of visual timelines with images, not just numbers

### Gifted Students
- Research the Burgess Shale and what it tells us about soft-bodied animals
- Investigate the dinosaur-bird link (theropod dinosaurs → modern birds)
- Explore the Younger Dryas impact hypothesis or the Cretaceous-Paleogene extinction in detail
- Research geoengineering as a solution to climate change

### Students with Additional Needs
- Reduce to core concepts: Fossils form / Fossils tell us about past / Earth is old / Some resources can run out
- Provide labelled diagrams for fossil types
- Use hands-on fossil dig with pre-made replicas
- One-on-one support for written tasks
- Extra time for all tasks
