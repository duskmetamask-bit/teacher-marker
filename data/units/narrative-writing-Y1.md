# Narrative Writing Unit: The Snowman

**Unit Duration:** 8 weeks × 3 lessons per week = 24 lessons
**Year Level:** Year 1
**Curriculum Link:** Australian Curriculum English v9 - Narrative Writing
**Mentor Text:** *The Snowman* by Raymond Briggs (1982)
**Writing Focus:** Imaginative narrative — winter wonderland, magical adventures, friendship
**Cold Task:** Pre-assessment (Weeks 1, Lesson 1) - Draw and write about a magical winter adventure
**Hot Task:** Post-assessment (Week 8, Lesson 1) - Same as Cold Task

## Unit Overview

**Learning Intention:**
We are learning to write a simple imaginative story with a beginning, middle and end, using pictures and words.

**Success Criteria:**
- I can draw and write about a character in a story.
- I can tell you the beginning, middle and end of my story.
- I can use pictures to help tell my story.
- I can write sentences that make sense.

## WEEK 1 — INTRODUCTION TO STORIES

### Lesson 1: Cold Task (Assessment)
**Time:** 30 minutes | **Type:** Assessment

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Hook (5 min) | Show cover of *The Snowman*. "What do you think this story is about?" Make a class list of predictions. | Share predictions |
| Task (25 min) | Cold Task: Draw a picture of a magical winter adventure with a snowman. Write labels or sentences underneath. Focus: Can you tell a story with pictures and words? | Independent drawing and writing |

**Differentiation:** Sentence starters available. Visual word wall for winter words.

---

### Lesson 2: Looking at The Snowman (I Do)
**Time:** 30 minutes | **Type:** Explicit Teaching

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| Read (15 min) | Read *The Snowman* wordlessly. Page through carefully. "What do you see? What is happening?" | Observe and share |
| Discuss (10 min) | What happened in the story? Sequence: The boy builds a snowman → The snowman comes alive → They fly together → Snowman melts → Boy wakes up | Sequence events verbally |
| Share (5 min) | "What was your favourite part? Why?" | Share responses |

**Success Criteria Check:** Can name at least 3 events from *The Snowman*.

---

### Lesson 3: What Happened First? (We Do)
**Time:** 30 minutes | **Type:** Guided Practice

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| I Do (10 min) | Put 4 pictures from *The Snowman* on the board. Sequence: 1. Boy builds snowman 2. Snowman comes alive 3. They fly 4. Snowman melts. Model ordering | Observe and discuss |
| We Do (10 min) | Students stand up and act out the 4 events in order | Physical engagement |
| You Do (10 min) | Draw the 4 events from *The Snowman* in order in your books | Independent sequencing |

**Success Criteria Check:** Can put 4 story events in order.

## WEEK 2 — CHARACTERS

### Lesson 1: Who Is in the Story? (I Do)
**Time:** 30 minutes | **Type:** Explicit Teaching

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| Read (10 min) | Revisit *The Snowman*. "Who are the characters?" Boy (protagonist), Snowman (magical friend). "How are they different?" | Identify characters |
| Mini-Lesson (10 min) | Characters are people or creatures in a story. The boy is a child. The snowman is magical. | Discuss |
| You Do (10 min) | Draw the two main characters from *The Snowman*. Label them. | Independent character work |

---

### Lesson 2: How Does My Character Look? (We Do)
**Time:** 30 minutes | **Type:** Guided Practice

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| I Do (10 min) | Model drawing the boy: "He has brown hair, a red scarf, blue coat." Describe each feature. Draw on board. | Observe |
| We Do (10 min) | Together, describe the snowman: "Round white body, black hat, orange carrot nose, stick arms." Describe and draw together | Contribute descriptions |
| You Do (10 min) | Draw YOUR main character for your story. Add 3 labels describing how they look. | Independent creative work |

---

### Lesson 3: Drawing My Character (You Do)
**Time:** 30 minutes | **Type:** Independent

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| You Do (25 min) | Create a character card: Draw your main character. Add their name. Add 3 things about them (what they like, what they look like). | Creative character creation |
| Share (5 min) | 3–4 students share their characters with the class | Present characters |

---

## WEEK 3 — SETTING

### Lesson 1: Where Is the Story? (I Do)
**Time:** 30 minutes | **Type:** Explicit Teaching

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| Read (10 min) | Look at pages of *The Snowman*. "Where does the story happen?" Identify: boy's bedroom, backyard/snow, city/town, sky | Identify settings |
| Mini-Lesson (10 min) | Setting is WHERE a story happens. In The Snowman, the setting changes: inside → outside → flying → city | Understand setting concept |
| You Do (10 min) | Draw the main setting from your story | Independent drawing |

---

### Lesson 2: Drawing Our Setting (We Do)
**Time:** 30 minutes | **Type:** Guided

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| I Do (10 min) | Model drawing a winter setting: snow on ground, trees, cold colours (white, blue, grey). Add a house. | Observe drawing |
| We Do (10 min) | Class creates a shared winter setting on large paper: snow, trees, sky, maybe a snowman | Collaborative creation |
| You Do (10 min) | Draw the setting for YOUR story. Make it look like a winter place. | Independent work |

---

### Lesson 3: Magic Settings (I Do → You Do)
**Time:** 30 minutes | **Type:** Creative

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| I Do (10 min) | "What makes the setting in The Snowman special?" It is snowy, cold, magical, the boy flies! "What makes YOUR setting special?" | Discuss magic elements |
| You Do (20 min) | Add something magical to your setting — could be glowing lights, talking animals, snow that sparkles, a secret door | Creative enhancement |

---

## WEEK 4 — THE MIDDLE

### Lesson 1: What Happens Next? (I Do)
**Time:** 30 minutes | **Type:** Explicit Teaching

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| Read (10 min) | Re-read *The Snowman* focusing on the middle. "What happens after the snowman comes alive?" | Identify middle events |
| Mini-Lesson (10 min) | The MIDDLE of a story is what happens between the beginning and the end. In The Snowman: they play, they fly, they go to the city, the snowman starts to melt | Understand story middle |
| You Do (10 min) | Draw what happens in the MIDDLE of your story | Independent drawing |

---

### Lesson 2: Drawing the Problem (We Do)
**Time:** 30 minutes | **Type:** Guided

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| I Do (10 min) | "In The Snowman, is there a problem?" Discuss: the snowman melts, the boy loses his friend. This is sad but not really a problem they cause. It's just what happens. | Discuss story event |
| We Do (10 min) | "What could be a problem in YOUR story?" Give ideas: character gets lost, something breaks, someone is sad, bad weather comes | Brainstorm problems |
| You Do (10 min) | Draw the problem in your story | Independent work |

---

### Lesson 3: What Goes Wrong? (You Do)
**Time:** 30 minutes | **Type:** Independent

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| You Do (25 min) | Complete the problem section of your story. Draw 2 pictures showing what goes wrong. Write labels. | Independent story development |
| Share (5 min) | Share one problem idea with your partner | Partner discussion |

---

## WEEK 5 — THE END

### Lesson 1: How Does It End? (I Do)
**Time:** 30 minutes | **Type:** Explicit Teaching

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| Read (10 min) | Look at the end of *The Snowman*. "How does the story end?" Boy wakes up, snowman is gone, it's morning. "Is it a happy ending or a sad ending?" | Discuss ending |
| Mini-Lesson (10 min) | Endings can be happy, sad, or a bit of both. The boy is sad the snowman melted BUT he has wonderful memories. | Understand endings |
| You Do (10 min) | Think about the end of YOUR story. Will it be happy, sad, or both? | Think about ending |

---

### Lesson 2: Happy Endings (We Do)
**Time:** 30 minutes | **Type:** Guided

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| I Do (10 min) | List happy ending ideas: friends reunite, lost thing found, problem solved, character learns something | List ideas |
| We Do (10 min) | Class decides on a happy ending for a new class story — draw it together | Collaborative ending |
| You Do (10 min) | Draw how YOUR story ends | Independent ending |

---

### Lesson 3: Story Mapping (You Do)
**Time:** 30 minutes | **Type:** Independent

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| I Do (5 min) | Show a story map: BEGINNING → MIDDLE → END. Put pictures in each box for The Snowman. | Observe |
| You Do (25 min) | Make your own story map: 3 boxes. Draw BEGINNING, MIDDLE, END of YOUR story. | Independent story map |

**Success Criteria Check:** Story map has all 3 parts clearly shown.

## WEEK 6 — WRITING OUR STORY

### Lesson 1: Labelling (I Do → You Do)
**Time:** 30 minutes | **Type:** Writing

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| I Do (10 min) | "We have pictures. Now we add WORDS." Model labelling pictures from *The Snowman*: boy, snowman, scarf, snow, moon, stars | Learn labelling |
| You Do (20 min) | Label all the pictures in your story. Sound out words yourself. | Independent labelling |

---

### Lesson 2: Simple Sentences (We Do)
**Time:** 30 minutes | **Type:** Writing

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| I Do (10 min) | "A sentence is a group of words with a capital letter at the start and a full stop at the end." Model: "The boy built a snowman." | Learn sentence structure |
| We Do (10 min) | Together, write sentences for the BEGINNING of our class story: "Once there was a boy." "He lived in a cold place." | Joint sentence writing |
| You Do (10 min) | Write one sentence for each part of your story: Beginning / Middle / End | Independent sentences |

---

### Lesson 3: Finger Spaces (You Do)
**Time:** 30 minutes | **Type:** Writing

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| I Do (10 min) | "Finger spaces help us read our writing." Demonstrate: put finger between each word. Show good vs bad spacing. | Learn finger spaces |
| You Do (20 min) | Rewrite one page of your story with finger spaces between words. Check: Is each word clear? | Practice spacing |

## WEEK 7 — POLISHING

### Lesson 1: Checking Our Work (We Do)
**Time:** 30 minutes | **Type:** Editing

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| I Do (5 min) | Checklist: 1. Do I have pictures? 2. Do I have labels/sentences? 3. Do my sentences make sense? 4. Do I have a beginning, middle and end? | Read checklist |
| We Do (15 min) | Swap with partner. Partner checks: Are there pictures? Are there words? Is the story in order? | Peer checking |
| You Do (10 min) | Fix anything partner noticed | Independent editing |

**Time:** 30 minutes | **Type:** Art

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| You Do (30 min) | Add colour to your pictures. Use crayons, markers or coloured pencils. Make your pictures clear and neat. | Colour and present |

---

### Lesson 3: Sharing Our Stories (We Do)
**Time:** 30 minutes | **Type:** Sharing

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| We Do (30 min) | Author's chair — each student shows their book to a small group. Say one thing you are proud of. Say one thing to improve next time. | Present and reflect |

## WEEK 8 — HOT TASK AND CELEBRATION

### Lesson 1: Hot Task (Assessment)

| Task (25 min) | SAME TASK: Draw and write about a magical winter adventure with a snowman character. Focus: Beginning → Middle → End. Add labels and sentences. | Independent assessment |

## RUBRIC — Narrative Writing (Year 1)
**AC9E1LY01** — Create short imaginative texts using familiar words and images

| Criterion | A (Excellent) | B (Strong) | C (Satisfactory) | D (Developing) | E (Beginning) |
|-----------|---------------|------------|-----------------|-----------------|---------------|
| **Story Structure** | Clear beginning, middle and end. Events in logical order. Story makes complete sense. | Has beginning, middle and end. Events mostly in order. Story clear. | Beginning, middle and end mostly clear. Some confusion in order. | Attempt at structure. Story unclear or incomplete. | No clear story structure. |
| **Pictures** | Detailed, imaginative pictures that match the story. Shows all key events. | Clear pictures that match the story. Most key events shown. | Pictures match story. Some events shown. | Pictures don't fully match story. | No pictures or unclear. |
| **Writing/Labelling** | Labels and sentences are clear and readable. Sounds correct. | Most labels and sentences readable. Some errors. | Some labels or simple sentences. Partially clear. | Limited writing. Hard to read. | No writing attempted. |
| **Effort and Presentation** | Beautifully presented. Shows great care. Student proud of work. | Good presentation. Shows care. | Adequate presentation. Reasonable effort. | Limited effort in presentation. | No care shown. |

## ADAPTATIONS

### EAL Students
- Allow home language for initial planning
- Visual word wall with winter words and common story words
- Sentence starters: "Once there was...", "Then...", "At the end..."
- Allow verbal retelling (teacher scribes)
- Pair with supportive English-speaking partner
- Use picture-only planning for initial story

### Gifted Students
- Add more details to pictures — background, extra characters
- Write more sentences per page
- Add speech bubbles or thought bubbles
- Create a second story in the same book
- Write from the snowman's perspective

### Students with Additional Needs
- Provide story map template with boxes
- Allow scribe for writing
- Reduce to 3 pictures with labels
- One-on-one support during writing time
- Pre-drawn character and setting templates to colour in
- Extra time for all tasks

## ASSESSMENT NOTES

### Formative Assessment (Ongoing)
- Story maps and sequence drawings
- Character cards
- Labels and sentences during writing time
- Teacher conferences (Weeks 3, 5, 7)
- Peer sharing feedback

### Summative Assessment
- Cold Task → Hot Task comparison (same rubric, measure growth)
- Published book assessed with rubric
- Student self-reflection: "I am proud of..." and "Next time I will..."

### Tracking Progress
Use the comparison template to document:
1. Cold Task score → Hot Task score
2. Number of pictures/sentences
3. Story structure clarity
4. Next writing goal
