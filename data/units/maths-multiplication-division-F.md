## BIG UNDERSTANDINGS

Students will understand that:
1. Equal groups mean each group has the SAME number of objects
2. Multiplication means making equal groups and counting the total
3. Division means sharing equally or finding how many in each group
4. Doubling means making two groups the same size
5. Skip-counting is faster than counting one by one
6. Arrays arrange objects in equal rows and columns

---

## ESSENTIAL QUESTIONS

- What makes groups equal?
- How do we make equal groups?
- How do we share fairly?
- What is doubling?
- How can skip-counting help us?

## LESSON 1.2 — Equal Groups: What Is Multiplication?

**Duration:** 40 minutes
**Type:** Concept Introduction

---

### Learning Intentions
- I am learning that multiplication means making equal groups
- I am learning that equal groups have the SAME number in each group

### Success Criteria
- I can tell if groups are equal or unequal
- I can make equal groups

---

### I DO (12 min)

> "Pete has 4 buttons on each row of his shirt. That's 2 ROWS. 4 and 4. That's GROUPS OF 4."

> "2 groups of 4. Each group has the SAME number. That's called MULTIPLICATION."

> "When we make equal groups, we put the SAME number in EACH group."

---

### WE DO (18 min)

**Equal or unequal sort:**

Cards showing groups. Sort into: EQUAL / UNEQUAL.

Make equal groups:
- Put 4 in each group × 2 groups = 8
- Put 3 in each group × 3 groups = 9

---

### YOU DO (10 min)

1. Circle the equal groups: 3 groups of 4 / 3 groups of 4 and 1 group of 5
2. Make 3 groups of 2 using counters.
3. "Are these groups equal? 4 and 4 and 4? YES / NO"

---

## LESSON 1.3 — Making Equal Groups

**Duration:** 40 minutes
**Type:** Guided Practice

---

### Learning Intentions
- I am learning to make equal groups from a collection
- I am learning to count equal groups

### Success Criteria
- I can make 3 groups of 2 from 6 counters
- I can count equal groups

---

### I DO (12 min)

> "I have 6 counters. I want to make 3 equal groups. How many in each?"

> "6 shared across 3 groups. 2 in each group."

> "2 + 2 + 2 = 6. That's the same as 3 groups of 2."

> "We can say: 3 × 2 = 6. Three groups of two equals six."

> "The × sign means GROUPS OF."

---

### WE DO (18 min)

Make equal groups:

- Make 4 groups of 2: ___ altogether
- Make 3 groups of 3: ___ altogether
- Make 2 groups of 5: ___ altogether
- Make 5 groups of 2: ___ altogether

---

### YOU DO (10 min)

1. Make 4 groups of 3. How many altogether? Draw and write: 4 × 3 = ___
2. Make 2 groups of 6. How many altogether? ___
3. EXTENSION: "Can you make equal groups of 4 from 10 counters? Why or why not?"

## LESSON 2.1 — Sharing Equally Between 2 People

**Duration:** 40 minutes
**Type:** Explore

---

### Learning Intentions
- I am learning to share objects equally between 2 people
- I am learning that fair sharing means each person gets the same

### Success Criteria
- I can share 8 objects equally between 2 people
- I can explain that fair sharing means equal amounts

---

### I DO (12 min)

> "Pete has 8 buttons. He wants to share them equally between himself and his friend. How many does each get?"

Model with counters: give 1 button to Pete, 1 to friend. Repeat until all given.

Pete: 4. Friend: 4. Each gets the SAME.

> "8 shared equally between 2 = 4 each."

> "This is DIVISION. We write: 8 ÷ 2 = 4."

> "The ÷ sign means SHARE EQUALLY."

---

### WE DO (18 min)

**Sharing practice:**

- Share 6 counters between 2 people: ___ each
- Share 10 counters between 2 people: ___ each
- Share 8 counters between 2 people: ___ each
- Share 4 counters between 2 people: ___ each

---

### YOU DO (10 min)

1. Share 12 between 2: ___ ÷ 2 = ___
2. "Sam shares 10 stickers between 2 friends. How many each?"
3. EXTENSION: "Sam shares 9 between 2 friends. Is this fair? What happens?"

---

## LESSON 2.2 — Sharing Equally Between 3 People

**Duration:** 40 minutes
**Type:** Explore

---

### I DO (12 min)

> "12 cookies shared between 3 friends. Each friend gets the same. How many?"

Model: give 1 to each friend. Repeat. Each gets 4.

> "12 ÷ 3 = 4."

---

### YOU DO (10 min)

1. Share 9 between 3: 9 ÷ 3 = ___
2. Share 6 between 3: 6 ÷ 3 = ___
3. Share 15 between 3: ___ ÷ 3 = ___
4. EXTENSION: "Share 10 between 3. Can you share equally? What's left over?"

---

## LESSON 2.3 — Equal Sharing and Taking Away

**Duration:** 40 minutes
**Type:** Guided Practice

---

### I DO (12 min)

> "Division and subtraction are connected. 12 ÷ 3 = 4 means: how many 3s are in 12?"

> "Subtraction version: take away 3 at a time. 12 - 3 = 9, 9 - 3 = 6, 6 - 3 = 3, 3 - 3 = 0. How many 3s did we take away? 4!"

---

### YOU DO (10 min)

1. How many 2s in 8? 8 ÷ 2 = ___ (using subtraction)
2. How many 3s in 9? 9 ÷ 3 = ___
3. EXTENSION: "How many 4s in 12?"

## LESSON 3.2 — Skip-Counting by 2s and 5s

**Duration:** 40 minutes
**Type:** Explore

---

### I DO (12 min)

> "Skip-counting is faster than counting one by one. Count by 2s: 2, 4, 6, 8, 10..."

> "Count by 5s: 5, 10, 15, 20..."

> "Skip-counting by 2s: 2, 4, 6 — that's the 2× table: 1×2, 2×2, 3×2..."

---

### WE DO (18 min)

**Skip-counting practice:**

- 2, 4, 6, ___, 10, ___, 14, ___, ___
- 5, 10, ___, ___, 25, ___, ___, ___, ___

---

### YOU DO (10 min)

1. Count by 2s to 20.
2. Count by 5s to 50.
3. "How many groups of 2 make 12?"
4. EXTENSION: "Count by 3s: 3, 6, ___, 12, ___"

---

## LESSON 3.3 — Skip-Counting to Find Totals

**Duration:** 40 minutes
**Type:** Guided Practice

---

### I DO (12 min)

> "4 groups of 5. Skip-count by 5: 5, 10, 15, 20. Total: 20. That's 4 × 5 = 20."

### YOU DO (10 min)

1. 3 groups of 5 = ___ (skip-count)
2. 5 groups of 2 = ___
3. 4 groups of 3 = ___ (if accessible)
4. EXTENSION: "How many groups of 2 make 20?"

## LESSON 4.1 — Groups Of: The Multiplication Sentence

**Duration:** 40 minutes
**Type:** Explain

---

### Learning Intentions
- I am learning to write multiplication as "groups of"
- I am learning to write 3 × 4 to mean 3 groups of 4

## LESSON 4.2 — Arrays

**Duration:** 40 minutes
**Type:** Explain

---

### Learning Intentions
- I am learning that an array arranges objects in equal rows
- I am learning to count an array using skip-counting

### Success Criteria
- I can create a 3×4 array and count it

---

### I DO (12 min)

> "An ARRAY is objects arranged in EQUAL ROWS."

> "2 rows of 4: ●●●● / ●●●●"

> "That's 2 × 4 = 8."

> "Count by rows: 4, 8. Count by columns: 2, 4, 6, 8."

---

### WE DO (18 min)

Create arrays:
