## BIG UNDERSTANDINGS

Students will understand that:
1. Integers include positive and negative whole numbers and zero; they can be represented on a number line
2. Adding a negative number is equivalent to subtracting the absolute value
3. Subtracting a negative number is equivalent to adding the absolute value
4. Order of operations (BEDMAS/PEMDAS) ensures consistent results
5. Algebraic expressions can be simplified using the distributive law
6. Integer operations follow the same arithmetic properties as whole numbers

## LESSON 2.1 — Adding Integers: Same Sign

- I am learning to add two positive integers

---

### I DO (15 min)

> "3 + 4 = 7. Positive + positive = positive."

> "(-3) + (-5). Start at -3 on the number line. Jump +5. -3 → -2 → -1 → 0 → 1 → 2. Answer: -8."

> "Same sign addition: ADD the absolute values. Keep the sign."

> "(-3) + (-5) = -(3+5) = -8."

---

### YOU DO (15 min)

1. 4 + 7 = ___   (-3) + (-5) = ___
2. (-8) + (-3) = ___   (-10) + (-5) = ___

---

## LESSON 2.2 — Adding Integers: Opposite Signs

**Duration:** 50 minutes
**Type:** Explore

---

### I DO (15 min)

> "3 + (-5). Start at 3. Jump -5. 3 → 2 → 1 → 0 → -1 → -2. Answer: -2."

> "The jumps went past zero! 3 + (-5) = -2."

> "(-3) + 5. Start at -3. Jump +5. -3 → -2 → -1 → 0 → 1 → 2. Answer: 2."

> "Opposite sign addition: SUBTRACT the absolute values. The answer takes the sign of the number with the LARGER absolute value."

> "3 + (-5): |3|=3, |-5|=5. 5-3=2. Answer takes sign of |-5|: -2."

---

### YOU DO (15 min)

1. 4 + (-7) = ___   (-3) + 8 = ___
2. (-10) + 6 = ___   7 + (-4) = ___
3. "For (-10) + 6, which number has larger absolute value? What sign should the answer have?"
4. EXTENSION: "What is 0 + (-5)? What is 0 + 5?"

---

## LESSON 2.3 — Integer Addition Strategies

**Duration:** 50 minutes
**Type:** Guided Practice

---

### I DO (12 min)

**Rule summary:**

| Addition Case | Rule | Example |
|---------------|------|---------|
| Positive + Positive | Add, positive | 3 + 4 = 7 |
| Negative + Negative | Add, negative | (-3) + (-5) = -8 |
| Positive + Negative | Subtract absolute values; sign of larger | 3 + (-5) = -2 |
| Negative + Positive | Subtract absolute values; sign of larger | (-3) + 5 = 2 |
| Any + 0 | Same number | (-7) + 0 = -7 |

---

### YOU DO (15 min)

1. (-12) + (-8) = ___   15 + (-7) = ___   (-9) + 4 = ___
2. "What is the sum of all integers from -5 to +5? Can you find a fast way?"
3. EXTENSION: "Prove that (-a) + a = 0 for any integer a."

## LESSON 3.2 — Subtracting Negative Integers

**Duration:** 50 minutes
**Type:** Explore

---

### Learning Intentions
- I am learning that subtracting a negative is equivalent to adding the positive
- I am learning that 5 - (-3) = 5 + 3 = 8

### Success Criteria
- I can explain: subtracting a negative means adding the opposite

---

### I DO (15 min)

> "5 - (-3). Start at 5. Jump -(-3) = +3. 5 → 6 → 7 → 8. Answer: 8."

> "But wait: 5 + 3 = 8. So 5 - (-3) = 5 + 3 = 8!"

> "This is KEY: subtracting a negative = adding the positive."

> "Why? Because -(-3) means the opposite of -3, which is +3."

> "(-4) - (-6): -4 + 6 = 2."

Write the rule:
**a - (-b) = a + b**

---

### YOU DO (15 min)

1. 5 - (-3) = ___   (-4) - (-6) = ___
2. 7 - (-2) = ___   (-8) - (-3) = ___
3. "For 5 - (-3), why does the answer become 8 (bigger than 5)?"
4. EXTENSION: "If a - b = a + (-b), what is a - (-b)?"

## LESSON 4.1 — BEDMAS

**Duration:** 50 minutes
**Type:** Explain

---

### Learning Intentions
- I am learning the correct order of operations: BEDMAS
- I am learning to solve expressions step by step

### Success Criteria
- I can apply BEDMAS to evaluate expressions
- I can explain why order matters

---

### I DO (15 min)

**BEDMAS = Brackets, Exponents, Division/Multiplication, Addition/Subtraction**

> "3 + 4 × 2. B before DM before AS."
> "Step 1: Brackets? None."
> "Step 2: Exponents? None."
> "Step 3: Division/Multiplication? 4 × 2 = 8."
> "Step 4: Addition/Subtraction? 3 + 8 = 11."
> "Answer: 11. NOT 14 (which is wrong if you add first)."

> "Now: (3 + 4) × 2. Brackets FIRST: (3+4) = 7. Then 7 × 2 = 14. Different answer! That's why order matters."

---

### WE DO (20 min)

**BEDMAS practice:**

1. 8 + 4 ÷ 2 = ___  (8 + 2 = 10)
2. (8 + 4) ÷ 2 = ___  (12 ÷ 2 = 6)
3. 10 - 3 × 2 = ___  (10 - 6 = 4)
4. 20 ÷ 5 + 3 × 2 = ___  (4 + 6 = 10)
5. (20 ÷ 5 + 3) × 2 = ___  (4+3)×2 = 14
6. 15 - (4 + 3) × 2 = ___  (15 - 7 × 2 = 15 - 14 = 1)

---

### YOU DO (15 min)

1. 12 ÷ 3 + 2 × 3 = ___
2. (12 ÷ 3 + 2) × 3 = ___
3. "Why does (8 + 4) ÷ 2 give a different answer than 8 + 4 ÷ 2?"
4. EXTENSION: "Where would you add brackets to make 3 + 6 × 2 - 1 = 17?"

---

## LESSON 4.2 — BEDMAS with Integers

**Duration:** 50 minutes
**Type:** Explain

---

## LESSON 5.1 — Expanding: Distributive Law

**Duration:** 50 minutes
**Type:** Explain

---

### Learning Intentions
- I am learning to expand expressions using the distributive law
- I am learning that 4(x + 3) = 4x + 12

### Success Criteria
- I can expand 4(x + 3) = 4x + 12

---

### I DO (15 min)

> "4(x + 3) means 4 × (x + 3)."

> "Using the distributive law: 4 × x + 4 × 3 = 4x + 12."

> "This is the same as: (x + 3) × 4 = x × 4 + 3 × 4 = 4x + 12."

> "Proof with a number: x = 5. 4(5+3) = 4×8 = 32. 4×5 + 4×3 = 20+12 = 32. Same! ✓"

---

## LESSON 5.2 — Expanding with Integers; Factorising

**Duration:** 50 minutes
**Type:** Explain

---

### I DO (15 min)

**With integers:**

> "(-3)(2x - 5) = -6x + 15."

**Factorising:**
> "4x + 12 = 4(x + 3). Common factor: 4."

> "5x - 10 = 5(x - 2)."

---

### YOU DO (15 min)
