## BIG UNDERSTANDINGS

Students will understand that:
1. Place value extends to millions and to decimal fractions (tenths, hundredths, thousandths)
2. Addition and subtraction of decimals require careful alignment of decimal points
3. Adding and subtracting fractions requires common denominators
4. Efficient strategies and the formal algorithm both require place value understanding
5. Estimation and inverse operations are essential checking tools
6. Generalisations about operations apply universally

---

### Tier 3 (mathematical)
formal algorithm, flexible strategy, inverse operation, reasonableness, partition notation, mixed numeral, terminating decimal

## LESSON 1.2 — Place Value to Thousandths

**Duration:** 50 minutes
**Type:** Concept Introduction

---

### Learning Intentions
- I am learning to read, write, and understand decimals to thousandths
- I am learning that thousandths are 10× smaller than hundredths

### Success Criteria
- I can explain 3.582 = 3 + 5/10 + 8/100 + 2/1000

---

### I DO (15 min)

Write 3.582 on the board.

> "3 ones + 5 tenths + 8 hundredths + 2 thousandths. 3 + 0.5 + 0.08 + 0.002 = 3.582."

> "Each place value is 10× smaller than the one to its left."

> "1 = 10× 0.1. 0.1 = 10× 0.01. 0.01 = 10× 0.001."

---

### YOU DO (15 min)

1. Write 4.582 in expanded form.
2. Write the value of each digit in 7.346.
3. EXTENSION: "What is 0.001 × 10? What is 0.1 × 10? What is 1 × 10?"

---

## LESSON 1.3 — Comparing Decimals

**Duration:** 50 minutes
**Type:** Guided Practice

---

### I DO (15 min)

> "3.582 vs 3.528. Compare ones → same (3). Compare tenths → 5 vs 5 → same. Compare hundredths → 8 vs 2. 8 hundredths > 2 hundredths. So 3.582 > 3.528."

> "Key rule: Compare digit by digit from the left."

---

### YOU DO (15 min)

1. Compare using >, <, or =: 4.356 ○ 4.365
2. Order smallest to largest: 2.345, 2.534, 2.453, 2.543
3. EXTENSION: "Is 0.5 the same as 0.500? Explain."

## LESSON 2.1 — Decimal Addition

**Duration:** 50 minutes
**Type:** Explore

---

### I DO (15 min)

> "4.5 + 3.7. Jump: 4.5 + 3 = 7.5, +0.7 = 8.2."

> "Or column: line up decimal points."
```
  4.5
+ 3.7
 ----
  8.2
```
> "Same for 4.56 + 3.78: line up decimal points."
```
  4.56
+ 3.78
 ------
  8.34
```

---

### YOU DO (15 min)

1. 4.5 + 3.7 = ___   5.6 + 3.8 = ___
2. 4.56 + 3.78 = ___
3. 7.234 + 2.345 = ___
4. EXTENSION: "4.567 + 2.345 = ? Where does the decimal point go?"

---

## LESSON 2.2 — Decimal Subtraction

**Duration:** 50 minutes
**Type:** Explore

---

### I DO (15 min)

> "8.5 - 3.7. Column: line up decimal points."
```
  8.5
- 3.7
 ----
```
> "5 tenths - 7 tenths: can't! Regroup: 8 ones 5 tenths → 7 ones 15 tenths. 15 - 7 = 8 tenths. 7 - 3 = 4 ones. 4.8."

> "Think-addition: 3.7 + ? = 8.5. 3.7 + 4 = 7.7, +0.8 = 8.5. Answer: 4.8."

---

### YOU DO (15 min)

1. 9.5 - 4.7 = ___  (column)
2. 8.00 - 3.67 = ___  (add zeros to fill decimal places)
3. 7.654 - 2.345 = ___
4. EXTENSION: "Why do we add zeros to 8.00 when subtracting 3.67?"

---

## LESSON 2.3 — Estimation for Decimals

**Duration:** 50 minutes
**Type:** Guided Practice

---

### I DO (12 min)

> "4.567 + 2.345. Round to nearest whole: 5 + 2 = 7. Actual: 6.912. Close ✓."

> "Round to nearest tenth: 4.6 + 2.3 = 6.9. Even closer ✓."

---

### YOU DO (15 min)

1. Estimate then calculate: 5.678 + 3.456 = ___
2. Estimate then calculate: 9.000 - 4.567 = ___
3. "Which gives a better estimate — nearest whole or nearest tenth?"
4. EXTENSION: "Estimate: 34.567 + 21.234 ≈ ?"

## LESSON 3.1 — Adding Fractions (Same Denominator)

**Duration:** 50 minutes
**Type:** Explore

---

### I DO (15 min)

> "3/8 + 2/8. Denominators are the same — 8. Add the numerators: 3+2=5. Denominator stays 8. 5/8."

> "Can it be simplified? 5/8 — 5 and 8 have no common factors (other than 1). So 5/8 is in simplest form."

> "What about 3/8 + 7/8 = 10/8 = 8/8 + 2/8 = 1 + 2/8 = 1 2/8 = 1 1/4."

---

### YOU DO (15 min)

1. 2/7 + 3/7 = ___   4/9 + 5/9 = ___
2. 3/8 + 5/8 = ___  (simplify)
3. 5/6 + 5/6 = ___  (convert to mixed numeral)
4. EXTENSION: "What is 3/7 + 4/7 + 2/7?"

---

## LESSON 3.2 — Subtracting Fractions (Same Denominator)

**Duration:** 50 minutes
**Type:** Explore

---

### I DO (15 min)

> "5/8 - 2/8 = 3/8."

> "7/8 - 5/8 = 2/8 = 1/4. Simplified!"

> "What about 1 - 3/5? 1 = 5/5. So 5/5 - 3/5 = 2/5."

---

### YOU DO (15 min)

1. 7/9 - 4/9 = ___   5/8 - 3/8 = ___
2. 1 - 3/7 = ___
3. 5/6 - 4/6 = ___  (simplify)
4. EXTENSION: "1 - 3/5 = 2/5. Check: 2/5 + 3/5 = 5/5 = 1. What does this tell us?"

---

## LESSON 3.3 — Mixed Numerals

**Duration:** 50 minutes
**Type:** Guided Practice

---

### I DO (15 min)

> "2 3/8 + 1 5/8."

> "Method 1 — add whole parts and fraction parts separately."
> "2 + 1 = 3. 3/8 + 5/8 = 8/8 = 1. So 3 + 1 = 4. Answer: 4."

> "Method 2 — convert to improper fractions first."
> "2 3/8 = 19/8. 1 5/8 = 13/8. 19/8 + 13/8 = 32/8 = 4."

---

### YOU DO (15 min)

1. 3 1/4 + 2 1/4 = ___
2. 4 5/8 + 2 3/8 = ___
3. 5 3/5 - 2 1/5 = ___
4. EXTENSION: "3 3/4 + 2 3/4 = ?"

## LESSON 4.1 — Common Denominators

**Duration:** 50 minutes
**Type:** Explain

---

### I DO (15 min)

> "1/2 + 1/4. Denominators: 2 and 4. The LCM of 2 and 4 is 4. Convert: 1/2 = 2/4."

> "1/2 + 1/4 = 2/4 + 1/4 = 3/4."

> "For 2/3 + 1/6: LCM of 3 and 6 is 6. Convert: 2/3 = 4/6. 4/6 + 1/6 = 5/6."

---

### YOU DO (15 min)

1. 1/3 + 1/6 = ___  (LCM of 3,6 = 6 → 2/6+1/6=3/6=1/2)
2. 1/2 + 1/3 = ___  (LCM of 2,3 = 6 → 3/6+2/6=5/6)
3. 2/3 + 1/4 = ___  (LCM of 3,4 = 12 → 8/12+3/12=11/12)
4. EXTENSION: "Find the LCM of 4, 6, and 8."

---

## LESSON 4.2 — Adding and Subtracting with Related Denominators

**Duration:** 50 minutes
**Type:** Explain

---

### I DO (15 min)

> "2/3 - 1/4. LCM of 3 and 4 is 12. 2/3 = 8/12. 1/4 = 3/12. 8/12 - 3/12 = 5/12."

> "3/4 + 2/3. LCM of 4 and 3 is 12. 3/4 = 9/12. 2/3 = 8/12. 9/12 + 8/12 = 17/12 = 1 5/12."

---

### YOU DO (15 min)

1. 2/3 + 1/4 = ___
2. 3/4 - 2/5 = ___
3. 5/6 + 1/3 = ___
