## BIG UNDERSTANDINGS

Students will understand that:
1. Place value extends to thousands and enables flexible partitioning of large numbers
2. Mental strategies (compensation, jump, split) work for numbers of any size
3. The formal column algorithm is efficient but requires understanding of place value
4. Rounding and estimation help check answers before and after calculating
5. Addition and subtraction are inverse operations — always verify with the opposite
6. Real-world problems require selecting the right operation and strategy

---

## ESSENTIAL QUESTIONS

- How do we add and subtract efficiently with numbers to 10,000?
- What is the difference between mental and written strategies?
- How do we round numbers to make estimation useful?
- Why does the column algorithm work?
- How do we know when to add and when to subtract in word problems?

## DIFFERENTIATION FRAMEWORK

### EAL/D Students
- Pre-teach mathematical vocabulary with visual cards (regroup, trade, estimate, round)
- Sentence frames for strategy explanation
- Allow oral explanation in place of written
- Allow additional processing time
- Reduce written output

### Students Identified as Gifted/Advanced
- Explore addition and subtraction with 5-digit numbers
- Investigate algebraic connection: if a+b=c, what is c-b? c-a?
- Challenge: can you add numbers without writing anything down?
- Open investigation: what happens when you add the largest 4-digit and smallest 4-digit number?

### Students Not Yet at Expected Level (NEP)
- Consolidate place value to 1000 before introducing 10,000
- Use MAB blocks for all computation
- Focus on 3-digit + 3-digit without regrouping; extend when solid
- Pre-lesson micro-session: number talk on rounding
- Modified success criteria

- I am learning to read, write, and understand numbers to 10,000
- I am learning that thousands, hundreds, tens, and ones are the building blocks

### Success Criteria
- I can explain the value of each digit in a 4-digit number
- I can build 4-digit numbers in different ways

---

### I DO (15 min)

Write 4,582 on the board.

> "4 thousands + 5 hundreds + 8 tens + 2 ones. 4,000 + 500 + 80 + 2 = 4,582."

Build with MAB: 4 thousand-flats, 5 hundred-flats, 8 rods, 2 cubes.

> "The 4 is not worth 4 — it's worth 4,000. The 5 is worth 500. The 8 is worth 80. The 2 is worth 2."

> "What about 3,049? 3 thousands + 0 hundreds + 4 tens + 9 ones. Notice the 0 — we must write it!"

---

### WE DO (20 min)

**Place value challenge:**

- 5 thousands + 3 hundreds + 7 tens + 6 ones = ___

- 6 thousands + 2 hundreds + 0 tens + 9 ones = ___

- 7 thousands + 5 hundreds + 3 tens + 8 ones → 7,538
- 9 thousands + 0 hundreds + 6 tens + 0 ones → 9,060

## LESSON 5.1 — Column Subtraction: 4-Digit Numbers

### Learning Intentions
- I am learning to subtract 4-digit numbers in column format
- I am learning to trade when the digit being subtracted is larger

### Success Criteria
- I can solve 6,234 - 2,567 in column format
- I can explain each trade

---

### I DO (15 min)

```
  6,234
- 2,567
 --------
```

> "Ones: 4 - 7. Can't! Trade 1 ten → 10 ones: 14 - 7 = 7."
> "Tens: 2 tens - 1 (after trade) = 1 ten remaining, minus 6. Can't! Trade 1 hundred → 10 tens: 12 - 6 = 6."
> "Hundreds: 1 hundred - 1 (after trade) = 0 hundreds remaining, minus 5. Can't! Trade 1 thousand → 10 hundreds: 10 - 5 = 5."
> "Thousands: 5 (after trade) - 2 = 3."

```
  5 12 1 12    ← after all trades: 6000-1000=5000, 200-100=100, 30-10=20, 4+10=14
  6,234
- 2,567
 --------
  3,667
```

> "6,234 - 2,567 = 3,667. Check: 3,667 + 2,567 = 6,234. ✓"

### Learning Intentions

- I am learning that 3,004 means 3 thousands + 0 hundreds + 0 tens + 4 ones

### Success Criteria
- I can solve 4,002 - 1,567 in column format
- I can explain why trading through zeros requires multiple trades

---

### I DO (15 min)

> "4,002 - 1,567."

> "4,002 = 4 thousands + 0 hundreds + 0 tens + 2 ones."
> "To subtract in the ones: 2 - 7. Can't! Need to trade from tens — but tens is 0! Must trade from hundreds — also 0! Must trade from thousands."

```
  3 9 9 12
  4,002
- 1,567
 --------
```

> "Trade 1 thousand → 10 hundreds. Trade 1 hundred → 10 tens. Trade 1 ten → 10 ones."
> "Now: 4 thousands = 3 thousands + 10 hundreds. 0 hundreds = 9 hundreds (after trading 1 to tens). 0 tens = 9 tens (after trading 1 to ones)."
> "Ones: 12 - 7 = 5."
> "Tens: 9 - 6 = 3."
> "Hundreds: 9 - 5 = 4."
> "Thousands: 3 - 1 = 2."
> "Answer: 2,435."

---

### WE DO (20 min)

1. 5,004 - 2,567 = ___
2. 6,020 - 3,456 = ___
3. 7,000 - 2,345 = ___

---

### YOU DO (15 min)

1. 4,003 - 1,567 = ___
2. "For 4,003 - 1,567, how many times did you need to trade? Explain."
3. EXTENSION: "What is 10,000 - 1?"

## LESSON 6.1 — Rounding to Nearest 100 and 1000

**Duration:** 50 minutes
**Type:** Elaborate / Concept Development
**Resources:** Number lines, rounding posters

---

### Learning Intentions
- I am learning to round 4-digit numbers to the nearest 100
- I am learning to round to the nearest 1000

### Success Criteria
- I can round 3,456 to the nearest 100
- I can round 4,567 to the nearest 1000

---

### I DO (15 min)

> "3,456 to the nearest 100."

> "Is 3,456 closer to 3,400 or 3,500?"

> "Look at the tens digit: 5. 5 or more → round UP to 3,500. 4 or less → round DOWN to 3,400."

> "3,456: the tens digit is 5. 5 or more → round UP. So 3,456 ≈ 3,500."

Number line:
|---|---3,450---|---3,460---|
3,400 ← midpoint 3,450 → 3,500

> "3,450 is exactly halfway. Our rule: 5 or more rounds UP."

> "3,567 to the nearest 1000: look at hundreds digit = 5 → round UP to 4,000."

## LESSON 6.2 — Estimation Before Calculating

**Duration:** 50 minutes
**Type:** Elaborate / Guided Practice

---

### Learning Intentions
- I am learning to estimate BEFORE I calculate to help catch errors
- I am learning that estimation gives a "ballpark" answer

### Success Criteria
- I can round numbers to estimate a sum before adding
- I can round numbers to estimate a difference before subtracting

### I DO (15 min)

## LESSON 6.3 — Using Estimation to Check Answers

**Duration:** 50 minutes
**Type:** Elaborate / Guided Practice

---

### I DO (12 min)

> "We estimate BEFORE. But we can also use estimation to CHECK AFTER."

> "I calculated 4,567 + 2,789 = 7,356."

> "But I calculated 4,567 + 2,789 = 7,256. That's 100 off from 7,356. My estimate would catch this error!"

## LESSON 7.1 — Multi-Step Word Problems

### Learning Intentions

- I am learning to write intermediate steps clearly

### Success Criteria
- I can solve two-step and three-step word problems
- I can identify what each step requires

---

### I DO (15 min)

> "A school buys 3,456 maths books and 2,789 reading books. Each book costs $12. How much do they spend?"

> "Step 1: How many books in total? 3,456 + 2,789 = 6,245 books."
> "Step 2: Cost. 6,245 × $12 = ? Wait — this is multiplication. Let's simplify: each book costs $10. 6,245 × $10 = $62,450. Close enough!"

> "Alternative: 3,456 books + 2,789 books = 6,245. School spends $6,245 on maths books and $2,789 on reading books. Total: $6,245 + $2,789 = $9,034."

> "Two steps. Write each step. Don't combine them."

## LESSON 7.2 — Operation Selection

**Duration:** 50 minutes
**Type:** Elaborate / Guided Practice

---

### I DO (12 min)

Operation decision guide:

| Situation | Operation | Signal words |
