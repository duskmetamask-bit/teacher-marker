# Mathematics Unit: Geometry — Angles, Parallel and Perpendicular Lines

**Unit Duration:** 8 weeks × 3 lessons per week = 24 lessons
**Year Level:** Year 3
**Curriculum Link:** Australian Curriculum Mathematics v9 - Geometry
**AC9M3SP01** — Recognise and classify angles as acute, right, obtuse, reflex and straight

**Mentor Text/Context:** Real-world architecture and design — angles in buildings, bridges, and everyday objects
**Writing Focus:** Drawing, constructing, describing and classifying angles and lines

## Unit Overview

**Learning Intention:**
We are learning to identify, describe, and classify angles and lines in two-dimensional shapes and the environment.

**Success Criteria:**
- I can identify angles as acute, right, obtuse, reflex and straight.
- I can identify and describe parallel and perpendicular lines.
- I can use a protractor to measure angles in degrees.
- I can draw angles of a given size.
- I can describe the geometric properties of shapes using correct terminology.

## WEEK 1 — INTRODUCTION TO ANGLES

### Lesson 1: What Is an Angle? (I Do)
**Time:** 50 minutes | **Type:** Explicit Teaching

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| Hook (10 min) | Show pieces of wood or card joined at a hinge. Open and close them. "An angle is formed when two lines or arms meet at a point called the VERTEX. The size of the angle changes as we open or close the arms — it is NOT about how long the arms are, but how wide the opening is." | Understand concept |
| I Do (15 min) | "An angle is measured in DEGREES. A full turn is 360 degrees (°). A straight line is 180°. A right angle is 90°. We can have angles of any size from 0° to 360°." Show with arms. | Learn degrees |
| You Do (20 min) | Create angles using card strips and a split pin. Open to different sizes. Estimate: Is it bigger or smaller than a right angle? | Create angles |
| Share (5 min) | Show your most acute angle. Show your most obtuse. | Share |

**Success Criteria Check:** Can explain what an angle is and that it is measured in degrees.

---

### Lesson 2: Right Angles (We Do)
**Time:** 50 minutes | **Type:** Guided

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| I Do (10 min) | "A RIGHT ANGLE is exactly 90° — like the corner of a square or a book. It is the most important angle in geometry." Show: corner of a book, door frame, window, tile. | Learn right angles |
| We Do (20 min) | Make right angle checkers (square cut from paper). Walk around classroom — find 10 right angles. Record: door frame, book corner, table corner, window, tile. | Find right angles |
| You Do (15 min) | Sort shapes: Which have right angles? Square, rectangle, triangle (some), pentagon (some). Circle the right angles in each shape. | Sort shapes |
| Share (5 min) | Share one right angle you found that surprised you | Share |

---

### Lesson 3: Acute and Obtuse (I Do)
**Time:** 50 minutes | **Type:** Explicit Teaching

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| I Do (15 min) | "An ACUTE angle is LESS than 90°. Think: A-cute = A-small/little. Acute angles are sharp and small — like the tip of a pencil." Model: 30°, 45°, 60°, 80°. "An OBTUSE angle is MORE than 90° but LESS than 180°. Ob-tuse = Ob-wide. Obtuse angles are wide and open — like a book half open." Model: 100°, 120°, 150°. | Learn acute/obtuse |
| You Do (20 min) | Sort angle cards into: Acute / Right / Obtuse. Label each. Check with a right angle checker — is it less than, equal to, or more than 90°? | Sort angles |
| Share (5 min) | Show an acute angle and an obtuse angle. How can you tell the difference? | Explain |

## WEEK 2 — CLASSIFYING ANGLES

### Lesson 1: The Angle Family (I Do)
**Time:** 50 minutes | **Type:** Explicit Teaching

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| I Do (20 min) | "Every angle belongs to a family based on its size. 0° to 90° = ACUTE. 90° = RIGHT. 90° to 180° = OBTUSE. 180° = STRAIGHT. 180° to 360° = REFLEX." Show the angle family on a number line 0–360. "0° is no angle — two lines not meeting. 360° is a full circle." | Learn families |
| You Do (25 min) | Draw angles at these sizes and label them: 30°, 90°, 135°, 45°, 180°, 60°, 120°, 270°. Write the angle type below each. | Draw and label |
| Share (5 min) | Check a partner's work — are the angles labelled correctly? | Check |

---

### Lesson 2: Reflex and Straight (We Do)
**Time:** 50 minutes | **Type:** Guided

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| I Do (10 min) | "A STRAIGHT angle is exactly 180° — it is a straight line. A REFLEX angle is MORE than 180° but LESS than 360° — it is the 'big' angle, the one that goes around the other way." Show straight and reflex angles with card arms. | Learn straight/reflex |
| We Do (20 min) | Find reflex and straight angles in the room. A straight angle: opened book fully flat, the edge of a desk. A reflex angle: the outside corner of a room, a book opened more than flat. | Find reflex/straight |
| You Do (15 min) | Draw: A straight angle (180°). A reflex angle (choose any between 180–360°). Label both with size and type. | Draw angles |
| Share (5 min) | Explain: What is the difference between a straight angle and a reflex angle? | Explain |

---

### Lesson 3: Angle Hunt (You Do)
**Time:** 50 minutes | **Type:** Investigation

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| You Do (40 min) | Angle hunt: Walk around classroom and school (with teacher permission). Find and record: 3 acute angles, 3 right angles, 3 obtuse angles, 1 straight angle, 1 reflex angle. For each: Draw it, describe where it is, estimate the size. | Hunt |
| Share (10 min) | Share your most interesting angle find. What was it? Where was it? What type was it? | Share |

## WEEK 3 — MEASURING ANGLES

### Lesson 1: Using a Protractor (I Do)
**Time:** 50 minutes | **Type:** Explicit Teaching

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| I Do (15 min) | "A PROTACTOR is a tool for measuring angles in degrees. It has a straight edge and a semicircular edge marked from 0° to 180°. The centre point is where you align the vertex." Show and explain each part. | Learn protractor |
| I Do (20 min) | Demonstrate: To measure angle ABC: 1. Place centre of protractor on vertex B. 2. Align one arm (BA) along the 0° line. 3. Read the number where the other arm (BC) crosses the scale. 4. Say: "Angle ABC measures ___°." | Demonstrate |
| You Do (15 min) | Practice: Measure 5 given angles using a protractor. Check each measurement with a partner. | Measure |

---

### Lesson 2: Measuring in Degrees (You Do)
**Time:** 50 minutes | **Type:** Practice

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| You Do (45 min) | Measure and record: 10 angles drawn on cards. For each: Draw a copy, measure with protractor, write the measurement in degrees, label the angle type. Record in a table. | Measure |
| Share (5 min) | What is the smallest angle you measured? The largest? | Share |

---

### Lesson 3: Estimate and Check (We Do)
**Time:** 50 minutes | **Type:** Guided

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| I Do (10 min) | "Before measuring, ESTIMATE. Is it less than 90° (acute)? Is it close to 90° (right)? Is it more than 90° but less than 180° (obtuse)?" Practice estimation with 5 angles before measuring. | Learn estimation |
| We Do (20 min) | Estimate-then-measure: 10 angles. Write estimate, then measure, then calculate difference. Score: Estimate within 5° = 3 points, within 10° = 2 points, within 20° = 1 point. Play in pairs. | Estimation game |
| You Do (15 min) | Reflection: Write: "To estimate an angle, I look for..." / "My best estimation strategy was..." | Reflect |

## WEEK 4 — DRAWING ANGLES

### Lesson 1: Draw a Given Angle (I Do)
**Time:** 50 minutes | **Type:** Explicit Teaching

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| I Do (15 min) | "To draw an angle of a given size: 1. Draw a baseline (one arm). 2. Mark a point at the vertex. 3. Place protractor centre on vertex, 0° on baseline. 4. | You Do (30 min) | Draw these angles: 45°, 90°, 120°, 60°, 135°, 30°, 150°, 75°. Label each with size and type. Use a protractor. | Draw angles |
| Share (5 min) | Show your most accurately drawn angle. How did you ensure accuracy? | Share |

## WEEK 5 — PARALLEL AND PERPENDICULAR

### Lesson 1: Parallel Lines (I Do)
**Time:** 50 minutes | **Type:** Explicit Teaching

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| I Do (15 min) | "PARALLEL lines are lines that NEVER meet — they stay the same distance apart forever. The symbol for parallel is //. Examples: Train tracks (the rails), lines on a notebook paper, opposite edges of a rectangle." | Learn parallel |
| You Do (30 min) | Find parallel lines in the classroom: edges of windows, lines on the floor tiles, sides of doors. Draw 5 examples. Label with the // symbol. | Find and draw |
| Share (5 min) | Show your most interesting example of parallel lines | Share |

**Success Criteria Check:** Can identify parallel lines in the environment.

---

### Lesson 2: Perpendicular Lines (We Do)
**Time:** 50 minutes | **Type:** Guided

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| I Do (15 min) | "PERPENDICULAR lines meet at a RIGHT ANGLE (90°). The symbol is ⟂. When one line is perpendicular to another, they form four right angles at the meeting point. Examples: Corners of a square, wall meeting floor, book spine meeting cover." | Learn perpendicular |
| We Do (20 min) | Find perpendicular lines in the room. Draw 5 examples. Label with ⟂ symbol. Check each with a right angle checker (square corner). | Find perpendicular |
| You Do (10 min) | Draw a rectangle. Mark all pairs of perpendicular sides with ⟂. How many did you mark? | Draw rectangle |

### Lesson 3: Real-World Lines (You Do)

|-------|---------|---------|

| Share (10 min) | Share one example where it was tricky to tell the difference between parallel and perpendicular | Share |

## WEEK 6 — ANGLES IN SHAPES

### Lesson 1: Triangles (I Do)
**Time:** 50 minutes | **Type:** Explicit Teaching

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| I Do (20 min) | "TRIANGLES have 3 angles that add to 180°. RIGHT triangle = 1 right angle (90°) + 2 acute angles. ACUTE triangle = 3 acute angles. OBTUSE triangle = 1 obtuse angle + 2 acute angles." Draw examples of each. Show that all three always sum to 180°. | Learn triangles |
| You Do (25 min) | Draw the three types of triangles. For each: Label the type, measure all 3 angles (use protractor), write the angle sizes, verify they sum to 180°. | Draw and measure |
| Share (5 min) | What do all triangles have in common? (3 sides, 3 angles, sum to 180°) | Discuss |

---

### Lesson 2: Quadrilaterals (We Do)
**Time:** 50 minutes | **Type:** Guided

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| I Do (10 min) | "QUADRILATERALS have 4 sides and 4 angles. The angles ALWAYS add to 360°. Square: 4 right angles (90° each = 360°). Rectangle: 4 right angles. Parallelogram: 2 pairs of parallel sides, opposite angles equal. Trapezium: one pair of parallel sides." | Learn quadrilaterals |
| We Do (20 min) | Measure and record angles of: Square, Rectangle, Parallelogram, Trapezium. For each: Measure 4 angles, calculate sum, record type. | Measure quadrilaterals |
| You Do (15 min) | Draw a quadrilateral of your own choice. Measure all 4 angles. Calculate the sum. Is it 360°? | Draw and measure |

---

### Lesson 3: Polygons (You Do)
**Time:** 50 minutes | **Type:** Practice

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| You Do (45 min) | Explore: Draw a pentagon (5 sides), hexagon (6 sides), octagon (8 sides). Measure all angles. Calculate sum. Find pattern: Number of angles × ? = sum? (Each angle sum = (n-2) × 180° where n = number of sides.) | Explore polygons |
| Share (5 min) | What is the pattern for the sum of angles in any polygon? | Discover |

## ADAPTATIONS

### EAL Students
- Visual charts with angle types and real-world examples
- Angle type words with pictures: acute (sharp), right (square corner), obtuse (wide), reflex (big), straight (flat)
- Physical angle makers to explore by touch
- Sentence starters: "This angle is ___° because..."

### Gifted Students
- Explore angle bisection — if you bisect a 90° angle, what do you get? Prove it.
- Investigate why angles in a triangle always sum to 180°
- Explore exterior angles of polygons
- Create a city design using only right angles (like Rectangles by Jon Scieszka)

### Students with Additional Needs
- Provide right angle checker (square cut-out) for all classification tasks
- Reduce to 3 angle types (acute, right, obtuse) before adding straight and reflex
- Protractor use with guided steps and laminated practice cards
- One-on-one support with protractor alignment
- Extra time for all tasks
