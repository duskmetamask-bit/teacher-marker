# Living Things — Biological Sciences Unit Plan

**Unit Duration:** 6 weeks × 3 lessons per week = 18 lessons
**Year Level:** Foundation
**Curriculum Link:** Australian Curriculum Science v9 — Biological Sciences
**Unit Big Idea:** All living things have needs — they need food, water, air, and a place to live. Living things can be compared by their features and what they need to survive.
**Cold Task:** Week 1, Lesson 1 — Draw yourself as a living thing and label what you need to survive
**Hot Task:** Week 6, Lesson 1 — Compare two living things, explain how they are the same and different, and name what each needs to survive

---

## Unit Overview

**Learning Intention:**
We are learning to identify living things and non-living things, describe the features of living things, and explain what living things need to survive.

**Success Criteria:**
- I can tell the difference between living things and non-living things.
- I can name and describe the features of living things (body parts, coverings, movement).
- I can explain that living things need food, water, air, and a place to live.
- I can compare two living things by how they look and what they need.
- I can use words like living, non-living, needs, survive, grow.

## WEEK 1 — ENGAGE: What Is Living?

### Lesson 1: Cold Task — Draw Yourself as a Living Thing
**Time:** 30 minutes | **Type:** Assessment (Formative Pre-Assessment)

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Hook (5 min) | Show images: a dog, a tree, a rock, a teddy bear. "Which of these is ALIVE? How do you know?" | Observe and discuss |
| Task (20 min) | Cold Task: "Draw yourself. You are a LIVING THING. Label: What does your body look like? What do you need to stay alive? Food? Water? Air? Draw and label what you need to SURVIVE." | Draw and label |
| Share (5 min) | 3–4 students share their drawings | Share |

**Differentiation:** EAL students draw and point to labels. NEP students: draw self-portrait with teacher scribing labels.

**Success Criteria Check:** Student produces a drawing showing themselves with some attempt to identify what they need to survive.

---

### Lesson 2: Living or Not?
**Time:** 30 minutes | **Type:** Engage (Sorting Activity)

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Sorting Time (10 min) | Place items/pictures on mat: feather, leaf, plastic dinosaur, real flower, shell, toy car, dried pasta, photograph of a person, pinecone, balloon. "LIVING or NOT LIVING? How do you know?" | Sort items |
| Rules for Living (10 min) | "Living things: grow (get bigger), breathe (need air), eat food, move, have babies. Non-living things: do not do any of these things by themselves." | Learn rules |
| Sort Again (5 min) | Re-sort with new understanding. "Is the feather living? (No — came from a bird but is not alive itself.) Is a seed alive? (Yes — it can grow into a plant.)" | Re-sort |
| Record (5 min) | Draw one living thing and one non-living thing. Label LIVING or NOT LIVING. | Draw |

**Success Criteria Check:** Correctly sorts at least 8 items as living or non-living and gives a reason.

---

### Lesson 3: What Do Living Things Look Like?
**Time:** 30 minutes | **Type:** Engage (Observation)

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Body Parts (10 min) | Look at images of different animals: dog (legs, tail, ears, eyes), bird (wings, beak, feathers, legs), fish (fins, scales, gills), human (arms, legs, eyes, mouth). "What body parts do they have? How are they the same? How are they different?" | Observe |
| Walk and Look (10 min) | Walk around school looking at living things: trees, grass, insects, birds, classmates. Draw 3 living things you see. Label one body part for each. | Walk and draw |
| Share (10 min) | Students share their drawings. "What body parts did you notice?" | Share |

**Differentiation:** EAL students draw and label. Gifted students: draw a fantasy animal with body parts from different real animals.

**Success Criteria Check:** Observes and draws at least 3 living things, correctly identifying at least 1 body part for each.

## WEEK 2 — EXPLORE: What Do Living Things Need?

### Lesson 1: All Living Things Need...
**Time:** 30 minutes | **Type:** Explore (Investigation)

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| What Do We Need? (10 min) | "You are alive. What do YOU need to stay alive? Food, water, air, a place to sleep, warmth." | Discuss |
| Food Is Fuel (5 min) | "FOOD gives us ENERGY to grow, move, and stay alive. Without food, we get hungry and tired. All living things need food." | Learn |
| Water Is Life (5 min) | "WATER is very important. Without water, plants wilt, animals get thirsty, people get sick. All living things need water." | Learn |
| Air to Breathe (5 min) | "AIR gives us OXYGEN. We breathe air in through our nose/mouth. Fish breathe water through their GILLS. Plants breathe air too! Air is all around us even if we can't see it." | Learn |
| Draw Our Needs (5 min) | Draw: ME → FOOD, WATER, AIR, SHELTER. Label each. | Draw |

**Success Criteria Check:** Identifies food, water, and air as basic needs of all living things.

---

### Lesson 2: Needs Hunt
**Time:** 30 minutes | **Type:** Explore (Investigation)

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Set Up Hunt (5 min) | "We are going on a LIVING THINGS NEEDS HUNT. Find evidence that living things near our school are getting what they need." | Prepare |
| Hunt (15 min) | Students walk school grounds with recording sheet: LIVING THING — WHAT IT IS — WHERE IS IT? — WHAT DOES IT NEED? — IS IT GETTING IT? Look for: bird eating (food), dog drinking water, tree with roots in soil (water, shelter), ant crawling (food), flower in sun (light). | Hunt |
| Share Findings (10 min) | Share 3–4 discoveries. "Did you find evidence of food? Water? Air? Shelter?" | Share |
| Record (5 min) | Draw one living thing you observed and write what it needs. | Record |

**Success Criteria Check:** Identifies at least 3 living things and correctly names at least 2 needs for each.

---

### Lesson 3: How Do We Meet Our Needs?
**Time:** 30 minutes | **Type:** Elaborate (Application)

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Our Needs vs Animal Needs (10 min) | "How do YOU get your needs met? Food: from shops/family growing. Water: from taps. Air: we breathe. Where do animals get their needs? A bird: food = insects/worms, water = puddles/streams, air = breathing, shelter = nest in a tree." | Compare |
| Matching Game (10 min) | Animal cards: dog, fish, butterfly, cow, bee. Need cards: food, water, air, shelter. Match each animal to all its needs. | Match |
| Draw Your Pet or Favourite Animal (10 min) | Draw your favourite animal. Write: What does it eat? Where does it drink? Where does it sleep? | Draw |

**Differentiation:** EAL students draw and verbally explain. NEP students: match 3 animals to 2 needs each.

**Success Criteria Check:** Correctly matches at least 4 animals to their needs.

## WEEK 3 — EXPLORE: Plants Are Living

### Lesson 1: Is a Plant Living?
**Time:** 30 minutes | **Type:** Explore (Investigation)

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Plant Evidence (10 min) | Show a pot plant. "Is this ALIVE? How can we tell?" Evidence: it grows (new leaves), it needs water (leaves wilt when dry), it takes in air (leaves breathe), it can make babies (seeds/flowers). | Observe |
| Seed Observation (10 min) | Give each student a dried bean seed (pinto/lima). Look at it with magnifying glass. "Is this ALIVE? (It's dormant — sleeping — but it IS alive, just waiting for right conditions.) What does it need to wake up? Water, warmth, soil." | Observe seeds |
| Plant an Experiment (10 min) | Plant seeds in clear plastic cups with wet cotton wool. "We will watch our seeds grow over the next few weeks. This will show us that plants are living things — they grow!" | Plant seeds |
| Record (5 min) | Draw the seed. Label: SEED — needs WATER and WARMTH to grow. | Draw |

**Success Criteria Check:** Correctly identifies a plant as a living thing and names at least 2 signs that plants are alive (grow, need water, need air).

---

### Lesson 2: What Does a Plant Need?
**Time:** 30 minutes | **Type:** Explore (Investigation)

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| What Do Plants Need? (10 min) | "We learned that all living things need food, water, air, and shelter. Plants are living! What do they need? WATER from the soil. SUNLIGHT from the sky. AIR from the atmosphere. SOIL for roots to hold them and get water." | Learn |
| Plant Experiment Setup (15 min) | Create 4 groups with 4 identical plants: (1) Plant with water + sunlight. (2) Plant with NO water (cover soil with plastic). (3) Plant with NO sunlight (cover with box). (4) Plant with water but in dark. Make predictions: what will happen to each plant over 1 week? | Set up experiment |
| Predict and Record (5 min) | Draw your predictions for each plant in 3 days and 7 days. | Draw predictions |

**Success Criteria Check:** Sets up a fair test, makes predictions about plant growth, and can name at least 3 things plants need.

---

### Lesson 3: Plant Parts
**Time:** 30 minutes | **Type:** Explain (Explicit Teaching)

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Plant Parts Walk (10 min) | Walk to a garden area. Look at plants. Find: ROOTS (underground, hold plant in soil, suck up water), STEM (holds plant up, carries water from roots to leaves), LEAVES (catch sunlight, make food for the plant), FLOWERS (make seeds for new plants). | Walk and find |
| Parts Sorting (10 min) | Picture cards of plant parts. Sort: What does each part DO? ROOT = get water. STEM = carry water. LEAF = make food. FLOWER = make seeds. | Sort |
| Draw and Label (10 min) | Draw a plant with all four parts: ROOTS, STEM, LEAVES, FLOWER. Label what each part does. | Draw and label |

**Differentiation:** EAL students draw plant with teacher labeling parts. Gifted students: research what a plant makes from sunlight (photosynthesis in simple terms — "plants make their own food using sunlight").

**Success Criteria Check:** Correctly identifies and labels at least 3 plant parts (root, stem, leaf, flower) and names what each part does.

## WEEK 4 — EXPLAIN: Animal Features

### Lesson 1: Animal Bodies
**Time:** 30 minutes | **Type:** Explain (Explicit Teaching)

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Animal Bodies (10 min) | Show images of different animals. "Animals have BODY PARTS that help them live. EYES help them SEE. LEGS help them WALK or RUN. WINGS help them FLY. FINS help them SWIM. EARS help them HEAR." | Learn |
| Matching Body Parts to Jobs (10 min) | Animal cards: frog (legs for jumping), cheetah (legs for running fast), bird (wings for flying), fish (fins for swimming), elephant (big ears for hearing). What does each body part HELP the animal DO? | Match |
| Our Body Parts (5 min) | "What body parts do YOU have? What do they help you do?" | Discuss |
| Draw (5 min) | Draw an animal and label its body parts. | Draw |

**Success Criteria Check:** Identifies at least 3 body parts of animals and explains what each body part helps the animal do.

---

### Lesson 2: Animal Coverings
**Time:** 30 minutes | **Type:** Explain (Explicit Teaching)

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Different Coverings (10 min) | Show images: dog (FUR), fish (SCALES), bird (FEATHERS), snake (SCALY SKIN), turtle (SHELL), insect (HARD OUTSIDE SHELL called EXOSKELETON). "Animals have different COVERINGS that protect them and keep them the right temperature." | Learn coverings |
| Covering Walk (10 min) | Look at images of animals. Match animal to its covering: cat → fur, goldfish → scales, bee → exoskeleton, duck → feathers, crab → shell. Sort into groups: FUR/HAIR, SCALES, FEATHERS, SHELL/EXOSKELETON. | Sort |
| Why Different Coverings? (5 min) | "Fur keeps WARM animals warm. Fish scales help them swim smoothly. Bird feathers help them fly AND stay warm. Turtle shell protects from predators." | Learn purpose |
| Draw (5 min) | Draw two animals with different coverings. Label the coverings. | Draw |

**Success Criteria Check:** Correctly identifies at least 4 types of animal coverings (fur, scales, feathers, shell/exoskeleton).

---

### Lesson 3: How Animals Move
**Time:** 30 minutes | **Type:** Explain (Explicit Teaching)

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| How Do Animals Move? (10 min) | "ANIMALS MOVE to find food, escape danger, and find shelter. WALKING: humans, dogs, cats. RUNNING: cheetahs, horses. FLYING: birds, butterflies, bats. SWIMMING: fish, dolphins, penguins. HOPPING: frogs, rabbits, kangaroos. CRAWLING: snakes, worms, ants." | Learn movements |
| Movement Sort (10 min) | Animal cards. Sort by HOW they move: walk/run, fly, swim, hop/crawl. Record in table. | Sort |
| Animal Movement Hunt (5 min) | "What animals did you see moving today? How did they move?" Draw or write 2 animals and how they move. | Hunt |
| Exit Ticket (5 min) | "Name one way an animal can move. Name the animal." | Write |

**Differentiation:** EAL students draw animals in motion. Gifted students: research how a particular animal moves (e.g., how does a snake crawl without legs?).

**Success Criteria Check:** Correctly categorises at least 5 animals by how they move (walk, run, fly, swim, hop, crawl).

## WEEK 5 — ELABORATE: Comparing Living Things

### Lesson 1: Same and Different
**Time:** 30 minutes | **Type:** Elaborate (Comparison)

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Compare Two Animals (10 min) | Compare dog and cat: Same: four legs, fur, tail, eyes, ears. Different: dogs wag tails to show happiness, cats purr and meow differently, dogs are more likely to fetch. Both are PETS that live with humans. | Compare |
| Partner Comparison (15 min) | Choose two animals to compare. Draw both. Find: 3 things that are the SAME. 3 things that are DIFFERENT. | Compare |
| Share (5 min) | 3–4 pairs share their comparisons. | Share |

**Success Criteria Check:** Compares two animals, identifying at least 2 similarities and 2 differences.

---

### Lesson 2: Living Thing Groups
**Time:** 30 minutes | **Type:** Elaborate (Grouping)

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Animal Groups (10 min) | "Animals can be grouped by what they EAT: HERBIVORES eat only plants (cows, rabbits, horses). CARNIVORES eat other animals (lions, eagles, sharks). OMNIVORES eat plants AND animals (humans, bears, pigs)." | Learn groups |
| Sort by Diet (10 min) | Animal cards: lion, cow, human, eagle, rabbit, shark, bear, horse. Sort into: HERBIVORE / CARNIVORE / OMNIVORE. | Sort |
| Group by Other Features (10 min) | "Animals can also be grouped by their BODY: MAMMALS (have fur/hair and drink milk from mum), BIRDS (have feathers and wings), FISH (have scales and fins), INSECTS (have 6 legs and sometimes wings)." Sort same animals by body type. | Sort |
| Draw (5 min) | Draw one animal from each group: mammal, bird, fish, insect. | Draw |

**Success Criteria Check:** Correctly sorts at least 6 animals into groups by diet (herbivore/carnivore/omnivore) or body type (mammal/bird/fish/insect).

---

### Lesson 3: Where Do Living Things Live?
**Time:** 30 minutes | **Type:** Elaborate (Application)

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Where Do They Live? (10 min) | "LIVING THINGS live in places that give them what they need. FISH live in WATER — they have gills to breathe underwater. BIRDS live in TREES or on CLIFFS — they can fly to find food. RABBITS live in BURROWS underground — safe from predators. TREES live in SOIL — they can get water through their roots." | Learn habitats |
| Habitat Sort (10 min) | Animal cards: fish, parrot, rabbit, frog, monkey, camel. Match to habitat: ocean, rainforest, desert, pond, tree, desert. Explain WHY each animal lives there. | Sort |
| Our School Habitat (5 min) | "What is OUR school habitat like? What living things live here? What do they eat? Where do they get water?" | Discuss |
| Draw a Habitat (5 min) | Draw a habitat (ocean, forest, desert, pond). Draw 2–3 animals that live there. Label what each eats. | Draw |

**Differentiation:** EAL students draw habitat with animal stamps/drawings. Gifted students: draw a food chain in their habitat (plant → herbivore → carnivore).

**Success Criteria Check:** Matches at least 4 animals to their habitat and explains why each animal is suited to that habitat.

## WEEK 6 — HOT TASK

### Lesson 1: Hot Task — Compare Two Living Things
**Time:** 30 minutes | **Type:** Summative Assessment

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Introduce Hot Task (5 min) | "Today you compare two living things. Choose any two: a cat and a dog, a fish and a bird, a flower and a tree. Draw both. Tell: How are they the SAME? How are they DIFFERENT? What does EACH need to survive?" | Instructions |
| Complete Task (20 min) | Students complete independently. | Complete |
| Share (5 min) | 3–4 students share. | Share |

**Differentiation:** EAL students draw and verbally explain with teacher scribing. NEP students: compare 2 living things with teacher support.

## DIFFERENTIATION NOTES

### EAL Learners
- **Visual Word Wall:** Picture-word pairs for living, non-living, food, water, air, shelter, survive, grow.
- **Partner Support:** Paired during activities.
- **Home Language First:** Students use home language to describe living things, then add English.

### Gifted Learners
- **Mini Research:** Research one Australian living thing in depth — what does it look like, where does it live, what does it eat, how does it protect itself?
- **Food Chain Extension:** Draw a simple food chain showing how energy moves from sun → plant → animal.

### Students with NEPs
- **Reduced Sorting:** Sort 5 items instead of 10.
- **Oral Responses:** Verbally explain instead of writing.
- **Partner Support:** Work with supportive partner.
- **Extra Time:** Additional time for all tasks.
