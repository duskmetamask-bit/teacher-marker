# People and Places — Geography Unit Plan

**Unit Duration:** 8 weeks × 3 lessons per week = 24 lessons
**Year Level:** Year 2
**Curriculum Link:** Australian Curriculum HASS v9 — Geography (Year 2)
**Unit Big Idea:** People live in different places around the world. The features of a place affect how people live — what they eat, what they wear, how they travel, and what they do for work and fun. Understanding places helps us understand each other.
**Cold Task:** Week 1, Lesson 1 — Describe two places and how people live differently in each one
**Hot Task:** Week 8, Lesson 1 — Compare how people live in two places, explaining how features affect daily life

---

## Unit Overview

**Learning Intention:**
We are learning to describe how people's lives are shaped by the features of places and how people in different places meet their needs.

**Success Criteria:**
- I can describe how people in at least 2 places meet their basic needs (food, water, shelter, clothing).
- I can explain how the features of a place affect what people eat, wear, and do.
- I can compare how people live in places that are similar and places that are different.
- I can use sources (photos, stories, maps) to find out about places.
- I can use directional language (near/far, north/south/east/west, inside/outside) to describe where places are.
- I can ask and answer questions about places and the people who live there.

## WEEK 1 — ENGAGE: How Do People Live?

### Lesson 1: Cold Task — How Do People Live in Different Places?
**Time:** 40 minutes | **Type:** Assessment (Formative Pre-Assessment)

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Hook (5 min) | Show two contrasting photos: a city in Asia and a rural village in Africa. "What do you see? How are the places different? How might people's lives be different too?" | Observe and compare |
| Introduction (5 min) | "Today we start a new unit about people and places. We will travel around the world without leaving our classroom. We will learn how the place you live in shapes how you live." | Listen |
| Cold Task (30 min) | "Draw two places where people live. Label: What do the houses look like? What is the weather like? How do people get food? How do people travel? Write 2 sentences: How are these two places different?" Year 2 expectation: drawing + 4+ labels + 2 sentences. | Independent |

**Differentiation:** Image word bank. Sentence starters: "People in this place live differently because..." / "Both places have..." / "One difference is..."

**Success Criteria Check:** Student produces a drawing showing 2 places and at least 2 differences in how people live.

### Lesson 2: Needs in Every Place
**Time:** 40 minutes | **Type:** Engage (Hook)

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| What Do We All Need? (10 min) | "Every person in the world needs the same things: food, water, shelter, clothing, safety, and love. No matter where you live — in Australia, Japan, Brazil, or Africa — everyone has these needs." | Listen |
| Needs Sort (10 min) | Show image cards: bread, rain jacket, house, tap, hat, fence. "All people need food, water, shelter, and clothing. But different places have different ways of meeting these needs." | Sort needs |
| Needs Around the World (15 min) | Show three examples: Australia (supermarket, tap water, brick house) / Japan (convenience store, tap water, wooden house) / Rural Kenya (market, river, mud hut). "Same needs, different ways of meeting them." | Observe |
| Connect (5 min) | "How do YOU meet your needs? How is it similar to or different from how people in Japan or Kenya meet their needs?" | Think |

**Success Criteria Check:** Can name at least 3 basic human needs that all people share.

## WEEK 2 — EXPLORE: Places Around the World

### Lesson 1: Visiting 3 Places
**Time:** 40 minutes | **Type:** Explore (Investigation)

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Virtual Visit Introduction (5 min) | "We are going on a virtual field trip today. We will visit 3 places: an island in the Philippines, a city in Canada, and a town in outback Australia. Pack your geo-eyes!" | Prepare |
| Place 1: Island Philippines (10 min) | Show images: tropical island, stilt houses over water, fishing boats, palm trees, market. "This island is hot and near the ocean. People fish for food. Houses are built on stilts over water." | Observe |
| Place 2: City Canada (10 min) | Show images: Toronto skyline, snowy winter, subway, multicultural food markets, brick houses. "Toronto is a cold city with snow in winter. People work in offices. Food comes from farms and stores." | Observe |
| Place 3: Outback Australia (10 min) | Show images: red earth, sparse trees, cattle station, windmill, small community. "The outback is very dry. People live far apart. Some work on cattle stations. Water comes from bores." | Observe |
| Compare (5 min) | "Which of these 3 places would you most like to visit? Why?" | Share |

**Success Criteria Check:** Can describe at least 1 feature of each of the 3 places visited.

### Lesson 2: Hot, Cold, and In Between — Climate Zones
**Time:** 40 minutes | **Type:** Explore (Investigation)

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Hot, Cold, and In Between (10 min) | "The world has different climate zones — hot near the equator, cold near the poles, and temperate in between. Our location on the globe determines our climate." | Listen |
| Climate Zone Map (10 min) | Show a world climate map. Equator marked in red. Identify: tropical (hot and wet), arid (hot and dry), temperate (mild), cold (polar), Mediterranean. Point to Australia (varied), Canada (cold/temperate), Brazil (tropical), Japan (temperate). | Observe |
| What Grows Where? (10 min) | "Climate determines what grows. In hot wet places: mangoes, bananas, rice. In cold places: wheat, potatoes, apples. In between: a mix. Climate shapes food." | Connect climate to food |
| Climate Zone Sort (10 min) | Image sort: palm tree (tropical), wheat field (temperate), cactus (arid), iceberg (polar). Which climate zone does each belong to? | Sort |

**Success Criteria Check:** Can correctly sort at least 3 items into the correct climate zone.

### Lesson 3: What Grows Where and Why?
**Time:** 40 minutes | **Type:** Explain (Explicit Teaching)

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Plants Suit Their Climate (10 min) | "Plants are matched to their climate. Cacti store water for hot dry climates. Rainforest trees have broad leaves for wet tropical climates. Apple trees need cold winters to grow well. Nature matches plants to places." | Listen |
| Food and Climate Connection (15 min) | Show: bananas (tropical countries), wheat (temperate/prairie countries), rice (tropical Asia), potatoes (cold climates). "Where do these foods grow best? What climate do they need?" | Connect food to climate |
| Our Australian Food Map (10 min) | "In Australia, what grows near us? WA produces wheat, barley, wool, lamb, seafood. Tropical north produces mangoes, bananas, sugarcane. Climate shapes what we eat." | Learn local food geography |
| Write (5 min) | "Write 2 sentences: Why does rice grow in hot wet places? Why does wheat grow in temperate places?" | Write |

**Differentiation:** EAL students draw the food-climate connection. Gifted students: map a week's worth of meals to where the food came from.

**Success Criteria Check:** Can explain at least 1 connection between climate and what can be grown in a place.

### Lesson 2: Homes Around the World
**Time:** 40 minutes | **Type:** Explain (Explicit Teaching)

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Homes Are Built for Their Places (10 min) | "Every home is built to suit its place and climate. The materials used, the shape of the roof, the size of windows — all respond to the local environment." | Listen |
| Homes Gallery (15 min) | Show 6 types: Igloo (Arctic — snow insulates, dome shape resists wind) / Moroccan riad (thick stone walls, central courtyard, small windows) / Australian outback homestead (corrugated iron, verandas, thick walls) / Balinese home (open-sided, raised floor, tropical ventilation) / Norwegian stave house (steep roof for snow, wood) / Brazilian favela (informal, recycled materials, densely packed). Describe each. | Observe |
| Why Are They Different? (10 min) | "Homes look different because of CLIMATE, AVAILABLE MATERIALS, CULTURE, and NUMBER OF PEOPLE. In snowy Norway, you need a steep roof. In hot Morocco, you need thick walls. In tropical Bali, you need open airflow." | Understand reasons |
| Draw Your Ideal Home (5 min) | "Design a home suited to our local Perth/Australian climate. Label 2 features that suit our weather." | Design |

**Success Criteria Check:** Can explain why at least 1 home type is suited to its environment.

## ASSESSMENT RUBRIC — Geography: People and Places (Year 2)

### Australian Curriculum v9 Links
- **AC9H2GEO01:** Investigate features and characteristics of places and spaces in Australia and New Zealand and the Pacific, and compare them with elsewhere.

## DIFFERENTIATION NOTES

### EAL Learners
- **Visual Word Wall:** place, feature, climate, urban, rural, compare, transport, shelter, food, clothing, similar, different, needs, natural, built.
- **Sentence Starters:** "In [place], people eat..." / "One similarity is..." / "One difference is..." / "The climate affects what people..."
- **Home Language Research:** Students may investigate their family's country of origin as a place study.
- **Partner Support:** EAL students paired for discussions and activities.

### Gifted Learners
- **Research Extension:** Research a specific culture in depth — include language, food, festivals, and daily life.
- **Comparative Essay:** Write a 5-paragraph essay comparing children's lives in 2 places.
- **Creative Extension:** Write a story from the perspective of a child visiting their place of heritage.

### Students with NEPs
- **Reduced Written Expectation:** 3–5 sentences on written tasks.
- **Word Banks:** Provided for all written tasks.
- **Oral Option:** Students may present verbally.
- **Extra Time:** Additional time for all tasks.
- **Scaffolded Templates:** Pre-drawn Venn diagrams, planners with prompts.
