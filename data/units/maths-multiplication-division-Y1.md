## BIG UNDERSTANDINGS

Students will understand that:
1. Multiplication is adding the same number repeatedly (2+2+2 = 3×2)
2. Multiplication can be shown as equal groups
3. Division is the inverse of multiplication
4. The 2×, 5×, and 10× tables are learnable
5. Arrays show multiplication visually
6. Groups of and sharing are both multiplication and division

---

## LESSON 1.2 — Multiplication as Repeated Addition

**Duration:** 50 minutes
**Type:** Concept Introduction

---

### Learning Intentions
- I am learning that multiplication is adding the same number many times
- I am learning to write repeated addition as multiplication

### Success Criteria
- I can write 4+4+4 as 3×4
- I can explain that multiplication is faster than repeated addition

---

### I DO (15 min)

> "The caterpillar eats 4 apples on Monday, 4 on Tuesday, 4 on Wednesday. How many apples?"

> "4 + 4 + 4 = 12. That's adding 4 THREE times."

> "That's the same as 3 GROUPS OF 4. We write: 3 × 4 = 12."

> "3 × 4 means: 3 groups of 4. Or: add 4 three times."

> "Multiplication is FASTER than repeated addition!"

---

### WE DO (20 min)

Write as multiplication:

- 5 + 5 + 5 = 3 × 5 = 15
- 2 + 2 + 2 + 2 = 4 × 2 = 8
- 10 + 10 + 10 = 3 × 10 = 30
- 3 + 3 + 3 + 3 + 3 = 5 × 3 = 15

---

### YOU DO (15 min)

1. 6 + 6 = 2 × 6 = ___
2. 4 + 4 + 4 + 4 + 4 = 5 × 4 = ___
3. Write 3 × 5 as repeated addition: ___ + ___ + ___ = ___
4. EXTENSION: "3×4 and 4×3 — are they the same? Test with counters."

## LESSON 4.1 — Division as Sharing

**Duration:** 50 minutes
**Type:** Explain

---

### Learning Intentions
- I am learning that division means sharing equally
- I am learning that 12 ÷ 3 = 4 means 12 shared between 3 = 4 each

### Success Criteria
- I can solve 12 ÷ 3 = 4 using sharing
- I can relate division to multiplication

---

### I DO (15 min)

> "Division is the OPPOSITE of multiplication."

> "12 ÷ 3 = 4. This means: 12 shared equally between 3 people = 4 each."

> "12 ÷ 4 = 3. This means: 12 shared equally between 4 people = 3 each."

> "Related facts: 3 × 4 = 12 and 4 × 3 = 12. And: 12 ÷ 3 = 4 and 12 ÷ 4 = 3."

> "The FACT FAMILY: 3, 4, 12."

---

### WE DO (20 min)

Share and solve:

- 10 ÷ 2 = ___ (share 10 between 2)
- 15 ÷ 3 = ___ (share 15 between 3)
- 20 ÷ 5 = ___
- 12 ÷ 4 = ___
- 18 ÷ 2 = ___

---

### YOU DO (15 min)

1. 14 ÷ 2 = ___   20 ÷ 4 = ___   25 ÷ 5 = ___
2. "Share 15 stickers between 3 friends. How many each?"
3. "For 20 ÷ 5 = 4, what multiplication fact is related?"
4. EXTENSION: "Write the fact family for 2, 6, 12."

---

## LESSON 4.2 — Division as Grouping

**Duration:** 50 minutes
**Type:** Explain

---

### Learning Intentions
- I am learning that division can also mean "how many groups of a size fit in?"
- I am learning to solve 12 ÷ 3 by asking "how many 3s in 12?"

### Success Criteria
- I can solve "how many 3s in 12?" = 12 ÷ 3 = 4

---

### I DO (15 min)

> "12 ÷ 3 can also mean: how many groups of 3 fit in 12?"

> "Skip-count by 3: 3, 6, 9, 12. That's 4 groups of 3. So 12 ÷ 3 = 4."

> "15 ÷ 5: skip-count by 5: 5, 10, 15. That's 3 groups of 5. So 15 ÷ 5 = 3."

---

### WE DO (20 min)

How many groups?

- How many 2s in 14? 14 ÷ 2 = ___ (2, 4, 6, 8, 10, 12, 14 → 7)
- How many 5s in 20? 20 ÷ 5 = ___ (5, 10, 15, 20 → 4)
- How many 3s in 15? 15 ÷ 3 = ___ (3, 6, 9, 12, 15 → 5)
- How many 2s in 20? 20 ÷ 2 = ___

---

### YOU DO (15 min)

1. How many 2s in 18? 18 ÷ 2 = ___
2. How many 5s in 30? 30 ÷ 5 = ___
3. "How many 10s in 40? 40 ÷ 10 = ___. How do you know?"
4. EXTENSION: "How many 2s in 30? How many 5s in 30?"

## LESSON 6.2 — Commutativity

**Duration:** 50 minutes
**Type:** Elaborate

---

### I DO (12 min)

> "2 × 5 = 10 and 5 × 2 = 10. The ORDER doesn't matter for multiplication."

> "This is the COMMUTATIVE PROPERTY: a × b = b × a."

> "Division is NOT commutative: 10 ÷ 2 = 5 but 10 ÷ 5 = 2. Different answers!"

---

### YOU DO (15 min)

1. Prove: 3 × 5 = 5 × 3 (draw arrays)
2. "Is 20 ÷ 4 = 20 ÷ 5? YES / NO. Explain."
3. EXTENSION: "Is subtraction commutative? Test: 10 - 2 vs 2 - 10."
