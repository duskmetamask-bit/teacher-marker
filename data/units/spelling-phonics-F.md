# Spelling and Phonics Unit — Foundation (F)

**Unit Duration:** 8 weeks × 3 lessons per week = 24 lessons
**Year Level:** Foundation (F)
**Curriculum Link:** AC9EFSP01, AC9EFSP02, AC9EFLY03
**Focus:** Phonemic awareness, letter-sound relationships, CVC words, initial and final sounds, simple sight words
**Mentor Text:** *Brown Bear, Brown Bear, What Do You See?* by Bill Martin Jr. and Eric Carle (1967)
**Cold Task:** Week 1, Lesson 1 — Identify and write initial sounds in words
**Hot Task:** Week 8, Lesson 1 — Same task, measure growth

---

## Unit Overview

**Learning Intention:**
We are learning to hear, identify, and represent the sounds (phonemes) in spoken words, and to connect letters (graphemes) to their sounds.

**Success Criteria:**
- I can hear and identify the first sound in a word.
- I can hear and identify the last sound in a word.
- I can match letters to their sounds (A-Z).
- I can read and write CVC words (consonant-vowel-consonant).
- I can read and write simple sight words.
- I can blend sounds together to read simple words.

## WEEK 1 — HEARING SOUNDS

### Lesson 1: Cold Task (Assessment)
**Time:** 30 minutes | **Type:** Assessment

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Hook (5 min) | "We are going to play sound games! Listen carefully to my voice. What sound do you hear at the start of 'sun'?" Show and make the /s/ sound. | Listen |
| Read (10 min) | Read *Brown Bear, Brown Bear* aloud. Pause to isolate initial sounds: "Brown... /b/ bear. What sound do you hear?" | Listen and identify |
| Task (15 min) | Cold Task (same as Hot Task): 1. Point to the picture that starts with /b/ (bear, frog, duck). 2. Draw a picture of something that starts with /b/. 3. Write the letter B. (Same task as Week 8 — we are looking for growth.) | Independent sound work |

**Differentiation:** Large picture cards. Physical objects to touch. Allow verbal responses as alternative to written.

---

### Lesson 2: First Sounds (I Do)
**Time:** 30 minutes | **Type:** Explicit Teaching

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Mini-Lesson (15 min) | First sound = the sound at the START of a word. "Ball starts with /b/. Bird starts with /b/. Book starts with /b/." Use actions: "When I say /b/, my lips come together like this." Show how to listen for first sounds. | Learn |
| Activity (10 min) | I Say You Point: I say a word, students point to the picture that starts with that sound. Use pictures from *Brown Bear*: bear, bird, duck, frog, teacher, child. | Game |
| Share (5 min) | "How did you know which picture to choose?" Talk about listening carefully. | Discussion |

**Success Criteria Check:** Can identify the first sound in simple CVC words.

---

### Lesson 3: Sound Walk (We Do)
**Time:** 30 minutes | **Type:** Guided Practice

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| We Do (25 min) | Walk around the classroom (or playground). Find things that start with specific sounds: /b/ (book, bin), /t/ (table, teddy), /s/ (sock, sun). Make a list or draw pictures of what you found. | Sound walk |
| You Do (5 min) | Share one thing you found that starts with a specific sound. | Sharing |

## WEEK 2 — LETTER-SOUND MATCHING

### Lesson 1: A-Z Sounds (I Do)
**Time:** 30 minutes | **Type:** Explicit Teaching

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Mini-Lesson (15 min) | A says /a/ like apple. B says /b/ like ball. C says /k/ like cat. Show the letter and say the sound with an example word. Use the alphabet chart. | Learn |
| Activity (10 min) | Letter hunt: Find letters A, B, C, S, M, T around the room. Say each sound as you find the letter. Match letter cards to pictures that start with that sound. | Game |
| You Do (5 min) | Write the letters that make these sounds: /b/ → B, /s/ → S, /m/ → M. | Written practice |

---

### Lesson 2: Matching Game (We Do)
**Time:** 30 minutes | **Type:** Guided Practice

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| We Do (25 min) | Memory match game: Letter cards (A, B, C, S, T, M, P, L) vs picture cards (apple, ball, cat, sun, tree, moon, pig, log). Turn over two cards. If the picture starts with the letter's sound, they match! Play in pairs. | Game |
| You Do (5 min) | Write 3 letters and the sound each makes. | Written practice |

---

### Lesson 3: Writing Letters (You Do)
**Time:** 30 minutes | **Type:** Independent

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| You Do (30 min) | Practice writing letters A-Z (or focus letters A, B, C, S, M, T). Say each sound as you write. Trace, then write independently. Circle your best letter for each sound. | Letter writing practice |

## WEEK 3 — CVC WORDS

### Lesson 1: Short Vowel Sounds (I Do)
**Time:** 30 minutes | **Type:** Explicit Teaching

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Mini-Lesson (15 min) | CVC = Consonant-Vowel-Consonant. cat, dog, hat, sit, bus. Short vowels: a = /a/ (apple), e = /e/ (egg), i = /i/ (insect), o = /o/ (orange), u = /u/ (umbrella). The vowel is in the MIDDLE of a CVC word and it SHORT. | Learn |
| Activity (10 min) | Sort words by short vowel: cat, hat, sit, bit, dog, log, bus, cup. Sort into columns: A | E | I | O | U. Say each word. | Sorting |
| You Do (5 min) | Write one word from each vowel column. | Written practice |

---

### Lesson 2: Blending (I Do → We Do)
**Time:** 30 minutes | **Type:** Explicit Teaching + Guided

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| I Do (10 min) | Blending = putting sounds together to read a word. "c-a-t" → blend → "cat." Sound buttons: Draw circles under each sound. Say each sound, then blend: "c" + "a" + "t" = "cat." | Learn |
| We Do (15 min) | Blend together: d-o-g → dog, h-a-t → hat, s-i-t → sit, b-u-s → bus. Use sound buttons. Tap each circle as you say the sound, then blend. | Guided blending |
| You Do (5 min) | Blend: c-a-t, m-a-p, r-u-n, f-o-x. Write the word after blending. | Written blending |

---

### Lesson 3: Reading CVC (You Do)
**Time:** 30 minutes | **Type:** Independent

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| You Do (30 min) | Read CVC word cards (cat, dog, hat, sit, bus, men, pin, top, cup). Match to pictures. Write 6 CVC words you can read. Circle the vowel in each word. | Reading practice |

## WEEK 4 — INITIAL SOUNDS

### Lesson 1: Finding First Sounds (I Do)
**Time:** 30 minutes | **Type:** Explicit Teaching

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Mini-Lesson (15 min) | Initial sound hunt: "What sound does this word START with?" Show an object or picture. Say the word clearly. Students identify the first sound. "Bear starts with /b/." "Red starts with /r/." | Learn |
| Activity (10 min) | Initial sound sorting: Lay out letter cards (B, R, S, T, M). Show picture cards (bear, red, sun, tiger, moon). Students say each word and sort under the correct letter. | Sorting game |
| You Do (5 min) | Write the letter that matches the first sound: ball → B, frog → F, dog → D, sun → S. | Written practice |

---

### Lesson 2: Sorting by Sound (We Do)
**Time:** 30 minutes | **Type:** Guided Practice

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| We Do (25 min) | Sort picture cards by initial sound. Create groups for B, D, F, M, S, T. Place pictures under the correct letter. Name each picture and say its first sound. | Group sorting |
| You Do (5 min) | Write 3 pictures from one group. Say the first sound for each. | Written practice |

---

### Lesson 3: Word Building (You Do)
**Time:** 30 minutes | **Type:** Independent

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| You Do (30 min) | Build CVC words with letter tiles: B - A - G → bag, C - A - T → cat, S - U - N → sun. Say each sound. Blend. Write the word. Build 6 words. | Word building |

## WEEK 5 — FINAL SOUNDS

### Lesson 1: Finding Last Sounds (I Do)
**Time:** 30 minutes | **Type:** Explicit Teaching

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Mini-Lesson (15 min) | Final sound = the sound at the END of a word. "Cat ends with /t/." "Dog ends with /g/." "Bus ends with /s/." Practice: Say a word, what sound do you hear at the end? | Learn |
| Activity (10 min) | Final sound game: I say a word, you say the last sound. Cat → /t/, Dog → /g/, Bus → /s/, Hen → /n/, Map → /p/, Bed → /d/. Show the letter that matches. | Game |
| You Do (5 min) | Write the final sound letter for: hat → t, dog → g, bus → s, cup → p. | Written practice |

---

### Lesson 2: Ending Sounds (I Do → We Do)
**Time:** 30 minutes | **Type:** Explicit Teaching + Guided

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| I Do (10 min) | Word families = words that end the same way and make the same sound. -at (cat, hat, mat, rat). -og (dog, log, fog). -en (hen, ten, men). They share the final sound pattern. | Learn |
| We Do (15 min) | Sort words by word family: -at: cat, hat, mat, sat, rat, flat. -og: dog, log, fog, jog. -en: hen, ten, men, pen. Say each word. Notice the ending pattern. | Sorting |
| You Do (5 min) | Write words from one word family (e.g., -at: cat, hat, rat). Circle the ending. | Written practice |

---

### Lesson 3: Word Families (You Do)
**Time:** 30 minutes | **Type:** Independent

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| You Do (30 min) | Word family writing: -at words: cat, hat, rat, mat, sat, bat. Write each word. Draw a picture for one. -og words: dog, log, fog, jog. Write and draw. -ip words: lip, dip, tip, sip, zip, hip. Build your own word family list. | Word family writing |

## WEEK 6 — BLENDING SOUNDS

### Lesson 1: Putting Sounds Together (I Do)
**Time:** 30 minutes | **Type:** Explicit Teaching

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Mini-Lesson (15 min) | Blending practice: Say each sound slowly, then speed up. "s-u-n" → "sun." "c-a-t" → "cat." "m-e-n" → "men." Use sound buttons under each letter. Tap and blend. | Learn |
| Activity (10 min) | Blending chain: h-a-t → hat, f-a-n → fan, r-a-n → ran, b-a-g → bag, p-i-g → pig, b-i-g → big. Students blend and say the word. | Chain game |
| You Do (5 min) | Blend and write: p-e-n, l-e-g, c-u-p, t-i-n. | Written blending |

---

### Lesson 2: Sound Buttons (I Do → We Do)
**Time:** 30 minutes | **Type:** Explicit Teaching + Guided

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| I Do (10 min) | Sound buttons: Draw a circle for each sound. Say the word and push each button. "c-a-t" — push, push, push — "cat." Works for all CVC words. | Learn |
| We Do (15 min) | Practice with word cards: sit, dog, hat, sun, men, pan, tin, hot. Draw sound buttons under each word. Say and blend. | Guided practice |
| You Do (5 min) | Draw sound buttons for these words and blend: bus, cup, map, leg. | Written practice |

---

### Lesson 3: Reading Words (You Do)
**Time:** 30 minutes | **Type:** Independent

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| You Do (30 min) | Reading relay: Read CVC word cards. If you can read it, keep it. Write 10 CVC words you can read from the cards. Circle the vowel in each word. Sort into short vowel families (a, e, i, o, u). | Reading practice |

## WEEK 7 — SIGHT WORDS

### Lesson 1: High-Frequency Words (I Do)
**Time:** 30 minutes | **Type:** Explicit Teaching

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Mini-Lesson (15 min) | Sight words = words you know by SIGHT, without sounding them out. "the, a, I, to, is, it, in, on, at, and, go, we, he, she, be, was." These are common in reading. Show each word on a card. Say it. Show it again. Repeat. | Learn |
| Activity (10 min) | Flashcard race: See the word, say it, keep it. Words: the, a, I, to, is, it, in, on, at, and. Repeat until automatic. | Game |
| You Do (5 min) | Write 5 sight words: the, a, I, to, is. Read them to a partner. | Written practice |

| You Do (5 min) | Write the sight word that matches each sentence: "___ bear is brown." (The) "I can ___ it." (see) "The cat is ___ the mat." (on) | Written practice |

## ADAPTATIONS

### EAL Students
- Use home language for sound clarification
- Provide picture cards with CVC words in L1
- Allow verbal identification of sounds before written
- Use physical objects for sound demonstration
- Allow letter tracing before independent writing
- Provide bilingual alphabet charts
- Focus on one vowel at a time (a, then i, then o)

### Gifted Students
- Introduce initial blends (bl, cr, st)
- Explore ending blends (-ng, -nk)
- Introduce consonant digraphs (sh, th, ch)
- Read simple CVC sentences
- Write CVC sentences independently
- Explore word families beyond -at, -og

### Students with NEPs
- Focus on 3–5 letter-sound correspondences only
- Use tactile letter cards (sandpaper letters)
- Allow verbal responses as alternative to written
- Extended time for all activities
- One-on-one sound identification support
- Simplify to: identify first sound only, write letter only
- Use physical manipulation of letter tiles
