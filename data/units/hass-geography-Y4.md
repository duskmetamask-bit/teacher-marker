# Our Land, Our Future — Geography Unit Plan

**Unit Duration:** 8 weeks × 3 lessons per week = 24 lessons
**Year Level:** Year 4
**Curriculum Link:** Australian Curriculum HASS v9 — Geography (Years 3–4)
**Unit Big Idea:** The world faces significant environmental challenges that require understanding, action, and global cooperation. Young people can make a difference by understanding these challenges and taking action in their own places.
**Cold Task:** Week 1, Lesson 1 — Investigate an environmental challenge in two places and explain how people are responding
**Hot Task:** Week 8, Lesson 1 — Investigate and evaluate a local environmental challenge, propose solutions, and explain how connections between places can help address the challenge

---

## Unit Overview

**Learning Intention:**
We are learning to investigate environmental challenges facing places around the world and in Australia, and to propose and evaluate solutions that consider sustainability and global cooperation.

**Success Criteria:**
- I can describe at least 2 significant environmental challenges facing places today (climate change, biodiversity loss, water scarcity, deforestation).
- I can explain how and why environmental challenges affect different places differently.
- I can evaluate at least 2 solutions to environmental challenges and assess their sustainability.
- I can describe how places are connected and how global cooperation can address environmental challenges.
- I can take action in my local place to contribute to environmental solutions.
- I can use sources (data, maps, reports, photos) to investigate environmental issues.

## WEEK 1 — ENGAGE: Our Planet Under Pressure

### Lesson 1: Cold Task — Environmental Challenges
**Time:** 40 minutes | **Type:** Assessment (Formative Pre-Assessment)

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Hook (5 min) | Show two contrasting images: one pristine natural environment vs one showing environmental damage (e.g., coral bleaching, deforestation, flood aftermath). "What do you notice? What is happening to our planet? What questions do you have?" | Observe and wonder |
| Introduction (5 min) | "In this unit, we will investigate environmental challenges facing our planet. We will look at how places are under pressure, how people are responding, and what we — as young people — can do about it. This is one of the most important topics of our time." | Listen |
| Cold Task (30 min) | "Choose two places facing environmental challenges (from images provided or your knowledge). For each: (1) Name the environmental challenge. (2) Describe how it is affecting the place. (3) Explain how people in the place are responding. (4) Write 2 sentences: What should people do differently? Is the response sustainable?" Year 4 expectation: 6–10 sentences, clear environmental focus, evaluation of responses. | Independent |

**Differentiation:** Image word bank of environmental challenges. Sentence starters: "The environmental challenge in [place] is..." / "People are responding by..." / "This response is [sustainable/unsustainable] because..." / "A better solution would be..."

**Success Criteria Check:** Student identifies at least 2 places with environmental challenges and describes at least 1 response.

## WEEK 2 — EXPLORE: Climate Change and Its Effects

### Lesson 1: Understanding Climate Change
**Time:** 40 minutes | **Type:** Explore (Investigation)

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Weather vs Climate (Again) (5 min) | "Remember: weather is day-to-day conditions. Climate is the usual pattern over long time. Climate CHANGE means the PATTERN is shifting — the baseline is moving." | Review |
| The Greenhouse Effect (15 min) | "Our atmosphere has gases (CO2, methane) that trap heat from the sun — like a blanket. This is natural and keeps Earth warm enough for life. But since the Industrial Revolution (1760s), humans have added MORE gases by burning fossil fuels (coal, oil, gas). More gases → more heat trapped → warming Earth." | Understand mechanism |
| Evidence (10 min) | Show data: CO2 levels (280 ppm pre-industrial to 420+ ppm today) / global temperature rise (~1.1°C above pre-industrial) / sea level rise (ice melting, thermal expansion) / Arctic sea ice decline (satellite data). "Scientists have measured all of this. The evidence is clear." | Observe data |
| Why Should Australia Care? (10 min) | "Australia is warming faster than the global average. We already see: more extreme heat events, longer fire seasons, coral bleaching, drying in the south-west. WA's climate has already shifted." | Local relevance |

**Success Criteria Check:** Can explain the greenhouse effect in simple terms and name at least 2 pieces of evidence for climate change.

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |

## WEEK 3 — EXPLORE + EXPLAIN: Biodiversity Under Threat

### Lesson 1: What is Biodiversity?
**Time:** 40 minutes | **Type:** Explain (Explicit Teaching)

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Biodiversity Defined (10 min) | "Biodiversity is the variety of ALL life on Earth — the DIFFERENT species of plants, animals, fungi, and microorganisms, the GENETIC differences within species, and the ECOSYSTEMS they form. A healthy ecosystem has high biodiversity." | Listen |
| Why Biodiversity Matters (10 min) | "Biodiversity provides: FOOD (crops, livestock, fish) / MEDICINE (many medicines come from nature) / CLEAN AIR AND WATER (ecosystems filter our air and water) / POLLINATION (bees and insects pollinate our crops) / FLOOD AND EROSION CONTROL (wetlands, mangroves). Biodiversity is our LIFE SUPPORT SYSTEM." | Understand importance |
| Web of Life (15 min) | "All species are connected — like a web. Remove one strand and the whole web weakens. If bees disappear, many crops fail. If apex predators disappear, prey species overpopulate and destroy vegetation. EVERYTHING is connected." | Understand interconnection |
| Biodiversity Hotspots (5 min) | "Biodiversity hotspots are places with huge species variety AND high threat of destruction. Australia is one of them — 87% of our mammals are found nowhere else. The Amazon, Madagascar, and Cape Floristic Region (South Africa) are others." | Note |

**Success Criteria Check:** Can define biodiversity and explain at least 2 reasons why it is important.

### Lesson 2: Australian Species Crisis
**Time:** 40 minutes | **Type:** Explain (Explicit Teaching)

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Australia Has a Problem (10 min) | "Australia has the highest rate of MAMMAL extinction in the world. Since European arrival (1788), we have lost over 100 species — more than any other country. We also have one of the highest rates of plant and animal extinction overall." | Listen |
| Why Are Species Disappearing? (15 min) | 5 main pressures: (1) HABITAT LOSS — clearing for farming, cities, mining / (2) INTRODUCED SPECIES — rabbits, foxes, cats, pigs destroy habitat and prey on native animals / (3) CLIMATE CHANGE — changing rainfall, more fires / (4) POLLUTION — chemicals, plastic / (5) OVERHARVESTING — fishing too much, logging too fast. | Learn pressures |
| Case Study: The Thylacine (5 min) | "The thylacine (Tasmanian tiger) is our most famous extinction. Hunted, lost habitat to farming, introduced diseases. Last known living thylacine died in Hobart Zoo in 1936. It is GONE. This happened within 100 years of European arrival." | Note extinction |
| Species We Must Save Now (10 min) | Show images: Koala (threatened by habitat loss and climate) / Black Cockatoo (habitat loss, food scarcity) / Maui's dolphin (New Zealand, critically endangered, < 60 remain) / Leadbeater's Possum (Victoria, critically endangered). "These are the species we are fighting to save right now." | Observe |

**Success Criteria Check:** Can name at least 3 reasons why species in Australia are disappearing and at least 1 threatened species.

## WEEK 4 — EXPLAIN: Water Scarcity and Management

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|

| Write (10 min) | "Write 3–4 sentences: What is causing water scarcity in places like the Middle East? How is it affecting people there?" | Write |

**Success Criteria Check:** Can describe at least 3 causes of global water scarcity and identify regions with high water stress.

### Lesson 2: Water in Australia
**Time:** 40 minutes | **Type:** Explain (Explicit Teaching)

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|

| Water Sources in WA (15 min) | "In Perth and south-west WA: DAM WATER (harvested rainfall, now declining) / GROUNDWATER (underground water, used extensively) / DESALINATION ( seawater, 3 plants in WA including Perth's Southern Seawater Desalination Plant, produces 45% of Perth's water) / WATER RECYCLING (indirect and direct). ## WEEK 5 — EXPLAIN: Land Degradation and Conservation

### Lesson 1: Land Under Pressure
**Time:** 40 minutes | **Type:** Explain (Explicit Teaching)

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| What is Land Degradation? (10 min) | "Land degradation is when land gets worse at supporting life. Types: SOIL EROSION (topsoil blown or washed away) / SALINITY (underground salt rising due to clearing trees) / COMPACTION (soil squashed by heavy machinery) / CONTAMINATION (chemicals, oil, heavy metals) / DESERTIFICATION (land turning to desert due to misuse)." | Listen |
| Causes of Land Degradation (15 min) | "Land is degraded by: DEFORESTATION (trees hold soil together — remove them and soil erodes) / OVERGRAZING (too many animals eat all vegetation, soil exposed) / INTENSIVE FARMING (monocultures deplete specific nutrients) / URBAN SPrawl (concrete replaces soil) / CLIMATE CHANGE (more fires, more drought)." | Learn causes |
| Global Extent (10 min) | "The UN estimates that 40% of all agricultural land is moderately to highly degraded. In Australia: 57% of land shows some degree of degradation. Salinity affects 3–5 million hectares in NSW alone. The equivalent of one cricket pitch of soil is lost every 2 minutes in Australia." | Observe data |
| Why Should We Care? (5 min) | "We care because: soil takes 100s to 1000s of years to form / food security depends on healthy soil / land degradation releases carbon stored in soil (worsening climate change) / degraded land costs farmers billions in lost productivity." | Understand |

**Success Criteria Check:** Can name at least 3 types of land degradation and at least 2 causes.

### Lesson 2: Australian Land Challenges
**Time:** 40 minutes | **Type:** Explain (Explicit Teaching)

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| The Australian Context (10 min) | "Australia has unique land challenges: ancient, weathered, nutrient-poor soils / fragile ecosystems / high fire risk / much of the continent is already arid or semi-arid / introduced species (rabbits, goats, camels) damage land." | Listen |
| Case Study: The Wheat Belt (15 min) | "The WA Wheat Belt was originally diverse native vegetation. Since clearing for farming: (1) SALINITY — clearing native deep-rooted plants allowed water tables to rise, bringing salt up into the root zone / (2) EROSION — exposed topsoil blown away / (3) BIODIVERSITY LOSS — 90%+ of original vegetation cleared / (4) ACIDIFICATION — soils becoming too acidic for crops. This is one of Australia's most significant land degradation stories." | Observe |
| Salinity in Focus (10 min) | Show images: salt-affected farmland in WA / salt-tolerant crops being trialled / revegetation programs. "Solutions: deep-rooted native perennials, salt-tolerant crops, agroforestry, preventing further clearing." | Learn |
| What Can Be Done? (5 min) | "Write 2–3 sentences: What should farmers do to prevent further land degradation in the Wheat Belt?" | Write |

**Success Criteria Check:** Can describe at least 2 land degradation issues in the WA Wheat Belt and at least 1 solution.

## WEEK 7 — ELABORATE + EVALUATE: Solutions and Action

### Lesson 1: Solutions Toolkit
**Time:** 40 minutes | **Type:** Evaluate (Critical Thinking)

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Solutions Framework (15 min) | "When evaluating solutions to environmental challenges, use this TOOLKIT: (1) Does it address the ROOT CAUSE or just the symptoms? (2) Is it SUSTAINABLE long-term? (3) Does it consider SOCIAL JUSTICE — fair for all people? (4) Does it work at SCALE — can it grow to make a big difference? (5) What are the COSTS and who pays?" | Learn |
| Evaluate Solutions (15 min) | Show and evaluate: Electric vehicles (reduces emissions, but battery production has environmental cost, electricity must be clean too) / Renewable energy (solar/wind — sustainable long-term, but intermittency challenges, land use) / Meat reduction (reduces methane and water use, but cultural/economic barriers) / Carbon offset schemes (controversial — some are genuine, some are not). | Evaluate |
| Best Solutions (10 min) | "The best solutions: are systemic (change the system, not just individual behaviour) / are just (fair to everyone) / are long-term (look decades ahead) / are multiple (many actions together, not one silver bullet). There is no single solution — it is all of us, doing many things." | Conclude |
| Exit Ticket (5 min) | "Apply the toolkit to ONE solution from today's lesson. Evaluate it against the 5 criteria." | Write |

**Success Criteria Check:** Can apply the solutions toolkit to evaluate at least 1 proposed solution.

### Lesson 2: Local Environmental Investigation
**Time:** 40 minutes | **Type:** Evaluate (Critical Thinking)

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Local Investigation Brief (5 min) | "Today you will investigate a local environmental challenge — something happening in Perth or WA. You will research: What is the challenge? Where is it happening? Why is it happening? Who is affected? What solutions are proposed? Is the solution sustainable?" | Prepare |
| Investigation Time (20 min) | Students investigate their chosen local issue using provided sources. Options: Urban heat in Perth / Marine debris on WA coast / Groundwater depletion in Perth / Feral cats in WA / Swan-Canning River system health / Fire risk in Perth hills / Local biodiversity loss in Perth. | Investigate |

## DIFFERENTIATION NOTES

### EAL Learners
- **Visual Word Wall:** climate change, biodiversity, sustainability, conservation, degradation, scarcity, mitigation, adaptation, carbon, ecosystem, species, renewable, non-renewable.
- **Sentence Starters:** "The environmental challenge is..." / "This challenge is caused by..." / "People are responding by..." / "A sustainable solution would be..." / "The connection between this place and another is..."
- **Image-Based Research:** Students use image-based sources rather than text-heavy sources where possible.
- **Partner Support:** Paired for research and discussion activities.

### Gifted Learners
- **Research Extension:** Investigate a specific environmental policy (e.g., Australian carbon credit scheme, Great Barrier Reef 2050 Plan).
- **Policy Proposal:** Write a formal proposal to a local council about a specific environmental action.
- **Comparative Study:** Compare environmental challenges in two countries in similar situations.

### Students with NEPs
- **Reduced Written Expectation:** 4–6 sentences on written tasks.
- **Word Banks:** Provided for all written tasks.
- **Oral Option:** Students may present verbally.
- **Extra Time:** Additional time for all tasks.
- **Scaffolded Templates:** Action plan template, solutions toolkit template.
