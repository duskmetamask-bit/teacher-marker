# Earth's Surface and Changes — Earth and Space Sciences Unit Plan

**Unit Duration:** 8 weeks × 3 lessons per week = 24 lessons
**Year Level:** Year 3
**Curriculum Link:** Australian Curriculum Science v9 — Earth and Space Sciences
**Unit Big Idea:** Earth's surface is shaped by slow natural processes operating over millions of years and faster processes we observe every day. Understanding these changes helps us protect Earth's environments.
**Cold Task:** Week 1, Lesson 1 — Investigate and describe how one place on Earth's surface has changed over time
**Hot Task:** Week 8, Lesson 1 — Analyse geological evidence for how Earth's surface has changed and explain how natural and human processes interact to shape landscapes

---

## Unit Overview

**Learning Intention:**
We are learning to describe Earth's surface features and how they form, explain slow and rapid changes to Earth's surface, analyse evidence of past changes from rocks and fossils, evaluate human impacts on Earth's surface, and describe how geological processes shape Australian landscapes.

**Success Criteria:**
- I can describe Earth's surface features (mountains, valleys, coastlines, deserts).
- I can explain slow changes (weathering, erosion, plate tectonics) and rapid changes (earthquakes, volcanoes, floods).
- I can interpret geological evidence (rock layers, fossils, landforms).
- I can evaluate human impacts on Earth's surface.
- I can use science words: weathering, erosion, deposition, tectonic, fossil, geological, landscape, sediment, magma, lava.

## WEEK 2 — EXPLORE: Slow Changes

### Lesson 1: Weathering
**Time:** 50 minutes | **Type:** Explain

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| What Is Weathering? (15 min) | "WEATHERING = breakdown of rocks IN PLACE (they don't move). Unlike erosion, weathered material stays in place. PHYSICAL (MECHANICAL) WEATHERING: freeze-thaw (water in cracks freezes → expands 9% → widens crack). Temperature extremes (rocks expand and contract unevenly → cracks). Biological (roots grow into cracks). CHEMICAL WEATHERING: acid rain dissolves some minerals. Oxygen oxidises iron (rusting). Water + CO2 forms weak carbonic acid." | Learn |
| Weathering Rates (15 min) | "Weathering rates depend on: CLIMATE: hot wet climates → fastest weathering. ROCK TYPE: soft rocks (limestone) weather faster than hard rocks (granite). EXPOSURE: exposed rocks weather faster than sheltered. TIME: weathering takes thousands to millions of years." | Learn |
| Weathering Demonstration (15 min) | Observe: limestone + vinegar (acid) = fizzing (chemical weathering). Sugar cube crushing (physical). Discuss. | Observe |
| Draw (5 min) | Draw the freeze-thaw cycle in a rock crack. Label: water, ice, crack. | Draw |

**Success Criteria Check:** Distinguishes physical from chemical weathering and explains at least 2 weathering mechanisms.

### Lesson 2: Erosion and Deposition
**Time:** 50 minutes | **Type:** Explain

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Erosion (15 min) | "EROSION = MOVEMENT of weathered material by wind, water, or ice. WIND EROSION: removes loose particles, creates sand dunes (e.g., Simpson Desert). WATER EROSION: most significant — rivers carve valleys, rain splashes and washes soil, waves erode coastlines. ICE EROSION: glaciers scrape and carry rock (e.g., Swiss Alps, Patagonian ice fields)." | Learn |
| Deposition (10 min) | "DEPOSITION = dropping of eroded material in new places. Occurs when transport agent slows down: rivers slow at river mouth → deltas form. Wind slows → sand dunes. Waves deposit sand on beaches. Glaciers melt → drop glacial flour (silt)." | Learn |
| Erosion Investigation (20 min) | Test: different slopes (steep vs gentle) affect erosion. Same soil amount, different water volume. Measure runoff sediment. | Test |
| Draw Landform (5 min) | Draw one landform created by erosion or deposition. | Draw |

**Success Criteria Check:** Distinguishes erosion from weathering and correctly identifies deposition in at least 2 landforms.

### Lesson 3: The Rock Cycle
**Time:** 50 minutes | **Type:** Explain

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| The Rock Cycle (20 min) | "ROCK CYCLE: rocks transform between three types over millions of years. IGNEOUS: from magma/lava cooling. SEDIMENTARY: from compressed sediments. METAMORPHIC: from heat/pressure. Any rock type → melting → igneous. Any rock type → weathering/erosion → sediments → sedimentary. Any rock type → heat/pressure → metamorphic. The cycle has no beginning and no end." | Learn |
| Connect to Earth's Surface (15 min) | "The rock cycle explains why old rocks are rare at Earth's surface (they get recycled). But ancient rocks exist where rocks are very old and stable (shields, e.g., Yilgarn Craton in WA — 2.7 billion years old)." | Learn |
| Model the Cycle (10 min) | Draw the rock cycle diagram. Label each arrow with the process: cooling (igneous), burial/cementation (sedimentary), heat/pressure (metamorphic), melting (magma). | Draw |
| Write (5 min) | "The rock cycle explains how rocks transform from one type to another over geological time." | Write |

**Success Criteria Check:** Describes the rock cycle and correctly identifies the processes linking each rock type.

## WEEK 3 — EXPLAIN: Rapid Changes

### Lesson 1: Earthquakes
**Time:** 50 minutes | **Type:** Explain

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| What Causes Earthquakes? (15 min) | "EARTHQUAKE = sudden shaking of Earth's surface. CAUSE: TECTONIC PLATES move past, against, or away from each other. STRESS builds up at plate boundaries. When stress exceeds rock strength → sudden slip → seismic waves radiate outward. FOCUS = where the slip occurs (underground). EPICENTRE = point on surface directly above focus." | Learn |
| Earthquake Effects (15 min) | "Earthquake effects: GROUND SHAKING (most common, most damaging). SURFACE RUPTURE (ground cracks and displaces). LANDSLIDES (especially on steep slopes). TSUNAMIS (if earthquake is under ocean). LIQUEFACTION (water-saturated soil becomes fluid). Buildings can collapse, roads crack." | Learn |
| Measuring Earthquakes (10 min) | "MAGNITUDE: measured on RICHTER or MOMENT MAGNITUDE SCALE (logarithmic: magnitude 6 = 30× more shaking than magnitude 5). AUSTRALIA: not on a plate boundary but experiences intraplate earthquakes (e.g., 2022 Mawson earthquake magnitude 5.9)." | Learn |
| Exit Ticket (5 min) | "What is the difference between the focus and epicentre of an earthquake?" | Written response |

**Success Criteria Check:** Explains the cause of earthquakes and distinguishes between focus and epicentre.

### Lesson 2: Volcanoes
**Time:** 50 minutes | **Type:** Explain

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| How Volcanoes Form (15 min) | "VOLCANO = opening in Earth's surface where magma (molten rock underground) reaches surface as LAVA. FORM at: DIVERGENT boundaries (mid-ocean ridges, e.g., Iceland). CONVERGENT boundaries (subduction zones, e.g., Indonesia's 'Ring of Fire'). HOT SPOTS ( plumes of hot rock rising from deep mantle, e.g., Hawaii)." | Learn |
| Volcanic Landforms (15 min) | "LANDMARKS created by volcanoes: SHIELD VOLCANOES — gentle slopes, fluid lava (Hawaii). STRATOVOLCANOES — steep cones, explosive (Mount Fuji). CALDERAS — collapsed crater (Lake Toba). LAVA PLATEAUS — flood basalts cover vast areas (Deccan Traps, India)." | Learn |
| Australia and Volcanoes (10 min) | "Australia: no active volcanoes today. But the NEWER VOLCANICS province in Victoria (last eruption ~5,000 years ago — Mount Kosciuszko area). Volcanoes have shaped much of eastern Australia (Basaltic plains). OLDEST: some WA rocks formed from ancient volcanic arcs 3.5 billion years ago." | Learn |
| Draw (10 min) | Draw a cross-section of a volcano showing magma chamber, conduit, crater, lava flow. | Draw |

**Success Criteria Check:** Describes how volcanoes form and identifies at least 3 volcanic landform types.

### Lesson 3: Floods and Landslides
**Time:** 50 minutes | **Type:** Explain

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Floods (15 min) | "FLOODS: when water exceeds the capacity of river channels. CAUSES: prolonged rain, rapid snowmelt, dam failure, storm surges. TYPES: FLASH FLOODS — rapid, dangerous, in narrow valleys. RIVERINE FLOODS — slower, cover wider areas. FLOODPLAIN DEPOSITS: floods deposit rich sediment, creating fertile floodplains (Nile Delta, Murray-Darling)." | Learn |
| Landslides (15 min) | "LANDSLIDES: movement of rock, debris, or soil down a slope. TYPES: ROCK SLIDES (sudden, dangerous). DEBRIS FLOWS (mud and rock, fast). MUDFLOWS (saturated soil). TRIGGERS: heavy rain, earthquakes, undercutting of slopes, vegetation removal. LANDSLIDE FREQUENCY increases with human activity." | Learn |
| Australian Flood Events (10 min) | "1974 Brisbane Flood: Brisbane River overflowed. 2011 Brisbane Flood: combination of heavy rain and already saturated catchments. Perth's Forrestfield: flash flooding after heavy rainfall events. FLOOD MANAGEMENT: dams, levees, floodplain zoning." | Learn |
| Write (5 min) | "Floods and landslides are rapid changes that shape Earth's surface and affect human communities." | Write |

**Success Criteria Check:** Distinguishes between floods and landslides and identifies at least 2 causes of each.

## WEEK 4 — ELABORATE: Evidence of Past Changes

### Lesson 1: Fossils
**Time:** 50 minutes | **Type:** Explain

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| How Fossils Form (15 min) | "FOSSIL = preserved remains or traces of ancient organisms. FORMATION: organism dies → quickly buried by sediment → soft parts decay → hard parts (bones, shells) preserved. Over millions of years, sediment becomes rock, organism becomes mineralised (petrified). CONDITIONS needed: rapid burial, low oxygen, undisturbed sediment." | Learn |
| Types of Fossils (15 min) | BODY FOSSILS: actual preserved remains (bones, shells, teeth). CAST/MOULD: impression left in rock after organism dissolves. TRACE FOSSILS: evidence of life without body (footprints, burrows, coprolites — fossilised dung). PERMINERALISATION: minerals fill cell spaces, preserving structure (petrified wood)." | Learn |
| What Fossils Tell Us (15 min) | "FOSSIL EVIDENCE: organisms that lived in the past, ancient environments, evolutionary change over time, climate at time of formation (e.g., coal seams indicate tropical swamps). Geological time scale built from fossil layers." | Learn |
| Draw (5 min) | Draw how a fossil forms in 4 stages. | Draw |

**Success Criteria Check:** Describes how fossils form and identifies at least 3 types of fossils.

---

### Lesson 2: Rock Layers
**Time:** 50 minutes | **Type:** Explain

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Law of Superposition (15 min) | "SEDIMENTARY ROCK LAYERS (strata): OLDEST layers at the bottom (deposited first). YOUNGEST layers at the top (deposited most recently). PRINCIPLE OF SUPERPOSITION: allows geologists to determine relative ages of rock layers. If you find a fossil in layer 3 and another in layer 7, layer 3 is older." | Learn |
| Relative Dating (10 min) | "RELATIVE DATING: determining order of events, not exact age. Methods: SUPERPOSITION (older below younger). ORIGINAL HORIZONTALITY (layers deposited flat). CROSS-CUTTING RELATIONSHIPS (faults cut across layers → fault is younger than layers it cuts). UNCONFORMITIES (gaps in rock record — erosion removed layers)." | Learn |
| Unconformities (10 min) | "UNCONFORMITY = gap in geological record showing erosion or non-deposition. Tells us: uplift occurred → erosion removed layers → subsidence → new layers deposited. Exposes geological history through missing chapters." | Learn |
| Investigate Rock Layers (15 min) | Examine rock strata images (e.g., Grand Canyon). Identify: oldest layer, youngest layer, unconformities, what each layer represents. | Investigate |

**Success Criteria Check:** Applies the law of superposition to determine relative ages of rock layers.

### Lesson 3: Geological Evidence Analysis
**Time:** 50 minutes | **Type:** Elaborate

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Evidence at Uluru (15 min) | "ULURU (Ayers Rock): 550 million years old. Originally on ocean floor (sedimentary). Tectonic forces tilted it vertical. Softer surrounding rock eroded away, leaving Uluru standing. RED COLOUR: iron oxide (rust) in the rock — indicates exposure to oxygen, weathering. FISSURES (vertical cracks): show stress from tectonic movement." | Learn |
| Evidence from Rock Types (15 min) | "Different rocks tell different stories: GRANITE: formed underground from slow-cooling magma — continental rock. BASALT: solidified from lava flows — volcanic rock. SANDSTONE: compressed sand grains — ancient beaches or deserts. LIMESTONE: compressed shells and skeletons — ancient seas. Each rock type reveals past environments." | Learn |
| Interpret Evidence (20 min) | "Given a rock sample or image: What type of rock? What does it tell us about the environment when it formed? How old is it likely? What changes have occurred since?" | Interpret |

**Success Criteria Check:** Analyses geological evidence to reconstruct past environments and geological events.

### Lesson 2: Geological History
**Time:** 50 minutes | **Type:** Explain

## WEEK 6 — EXPLAIN: Human Impact

### Lesson 1: Land Degradation
**Time:** 50 minutes | **Type:** Explain

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Types of Land Degradation (15 min) | "LAND DEGRADATION: decline in land quality. SOIL EROSION: loss of topsoil by wind and water — accelerated by clearing vegetation. SALINITY: water table rises, dissolves salts, surface evaporates, leaves salt deposits. ACID SULFATE SOILS: disturbed → release sulfuric acid, acidifies waterways. CONTAMINATION: chemical spills, mining waste, heavy metals." | Learn |
| Causes of Degradation (15 min) | "HUMAN CAUSES: VEGETATION CLEARING (for agriculture, urbanisation) is biggest cause. OVERGRAZING (livestock remove vegetation). INAPPROPRIATE IRRIGATION (raises water table). INTENSIVE CROPPING (depletes soil nutrients). NATURAL CAUSES: drought, floods, climatic cycles." | Learn |
| Australian Degradation Examples (15 min) | "WHEATBELT SALINITY: clearing deep-rooted native vegetation → water table rose → dissolved salts mobilised → salt appears at surface. Affecting large areas of SW Australia. GULLY EROSION: particularly in Queensland, severe soil loss from overgrazing." | Learn |
| Write (5 min) | "Human activities accelerate land degradation beyond natural rates." | Write |

**Success Criteria Check:** Identifies at least 3 types of land degradation and their causes.

### Lesson 2: Protecting Land
**Time:** 50 minutes | **Type:** Explain

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|

TECHNIQUES: revegetation, erosion control (contour planting, terracing), salinity management (deep-rooted perennials), contaminated site clean-up." | Learn |
