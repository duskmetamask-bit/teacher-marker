# WEEK 1 — ENGAGE

**Focus:** Cold Task + 3D objects
**5E Phase:** Engage

---

## LESSON 1.2 — What Is a 3D Object?

**Duration:** 50 minutes
**Type:** Concept Introduction

---

### Learning Intentions
- I am learning to name and describe 3D objects
- I am learning that 3D objects have faces, edges, and vertices

### Success Criteria
- I can name a cube, cylinder, sphere, cone, pyramid, and prism

---

### I DO (15 min)

> "3D OBJECTS: real things you can hold. They have LENGTH, WIDTH, and HEIGHT."

> "CUBE: 6 square faces, 12 edges, 8 vertices. (Show cube)"
> "SPHERE: round ball. No faces, edges, or vertices. (Show ball)"
> "CYLINDER: 2 circular faces, 1 curved surface. (Show can)"
> "CONE: 1 circular face, 1 point. (Show ice cream cone)"
> "PYRAMID: triangular faces meet at a point. (Show pyramid)"
> "PRISM: same cross-section all along. (Show rectangular prism)"

---

### WE DO (15 min)

**Sort 3D objects:** Cards or real objects. Sort by name.

**Build with blocks:** Build a tower using cylinders and cubes.

---

### YOU DO (10 min)

1. Name: cube, sphere, cylinder, cone, pyramid, prism
2. "What 3D object is a dice? A tennis ball? A tent?"
3. EXTENSION: "What is the difference between a cylinder and a cone?"

---

## LESSON 1.3 — Faces, Edges, Vertices

**Duration:** 50 minutes
**Type:** Guided Practice

---

### I DO (12 min)

> "FACE: flat or curved surface."
> "EDGE: where two faces meet — a line."
> "VERTEX/VERTICES: corners where edges meet."

> "This cube: 6 faces, 12 edges, 8 vertices."

---

### YOU DO (15 min)

1. A cylinder: ___ faces, ___ edges, ___ vertices
2. A triangular pyramid: ___ faces, ___ edges, ___ vertices
3. "Can you find a 3D object with no vertices?"
4. EXTENSION: "How many faces does a rectangular prism have? What shapes are the faces?"

# WEEK 2 — EXPLORE

**Focus:** 2D shapes on 3D objects; nets
**5E Phase:** Explore

---

## LESSON 2.1 — What 2D Shapes Are on 3D Objects?

**Duration:** 50 minutes
**Type:** Explore

---

### I DO (15 min)

> "Faces of a cube: squares."
> "Faces of a cylinder: circles and rectangles."
> "Faces of a triangular prism: triangles and rectangles."

---

### YOU DO (15 min)

1. What 2D shapes are on a pyramid with a square base?
2. "A dice has square faces. What 3D object is it?"
3. Build a 3D object from playdough. Press it onto paper. What 2D shapes do you see?
4. EXTENSION: "Can you find a 3D object that has ONLY triangles on its faces?"

---

## LESSON 2.2 — Nets

**Duration:** 50 minutes
**Type:** Explore

---

### I DO (12 min)

> "A NET is a 2D shape that FOLDS to make a 3D object."

> "This is a net of a cube. Fold it — it becomes a cube."

---

### YOU DO (15 min)

1. Match nets to 3D objects.
2. Draw a net of a cube.
3. "Can a cube have more than one net? YES / NO. Draw another."
4. EXTENSION: "Draw a net of a triangular prism."

---

---

# WEEK 3 — EXPLORE

**Focus:** Maps and coordinates
**5E Phase:** Explain

---

## LESSON 3.1 — Using Coordinates and Grid References

**Duration:** 50 minutes
**Type:** Explain

---

### Learning Intentions
- I am learning to use coordinates (A1, B3) to describe positions on a grid
- I am learning to plot positions on a map

### Success Criteria
- I can find (2,3) on a grid; I can describe where (B,3) is

---

### I DO (15 min)

> "A map grid: rows (1,2,3) going up. Columns (A,B,C) going across."

> "The school is at B2. The park is at D4."

> "To describe the path: Start at B2. Go up 2 squares to D2. Go right 2 squares to D4."

---

### YOU DO (15 min)

1. Plot: A1, B3, C2 on a grid.
2. "Start at A1. Go east 2, north 1. Where are you?"
3. Draw a simple map of your bedroom. Label furniture using coordinates.
4. EXTENSION: "Start at (3,3). Go south 2, west 2, north 1. Where are you?"

---

## LESSON 3.2 — Giving Directions

**Duration:** 50 minutes
**Type:** Explain

---

### YOU DO (30 min)

1. "Give directions from the shop to the park on your map."
2. "My house is at A3. My school is at C1. What direction is the school from my house?"
3. "Draw a treasure map. Give coordinates to find the treasure."
4. EXTENSION: "Can you describe a path using only north/south/east/west?"

# WEEK 4 — EXPLORE

**Focus:** Transformations on grids
**5E Phase:** Explain

---

## LESSON 4.1 — Flips, Slides, Turns on Grids

**Duration:** 50 minutes
**Type:** Explain

---

### I DO (15 min)

> "On a grid, we can track transformations."

> "SLIDE: shift right/left/up/down. The shape points the same way."

> "FLIP: reflect across a line. Left becomes right."

> "TURN: rotate around a point. The shape turns."

---

### YOU DO (15 min)

1. On the grid, slide the shape 3 squares right.
2. On the grid, flip the shape across the line.
3. On the grid, turn the shape 90 degrees clockwise.
4. EXTENSION: "If you slide right 2 and up 3, where does (2,3) end up?"

---

---

# WEEK 5 — ELABORATE

**Focus:** Symmetry and angles
**5E Phase:** Elaborate

---

## LESSON 5.1 — Lines of Symmetry

**Duration:** 50 minutes
**Type:** Elaborate

---

### I DO (12 min)

> "LINE OF SYMMETRY: a line where you can fold the shape and both halves match exactly."

> "Square: 4 lines of symmetry (2 diagonals, 2 through midpoints)."
> "Rectangle: 2 lines of symmetry."
> "Isosceles triangle: 1 line of symmetry."

---

### YOU DO (15 min)

1. How many lines of symmetry does an equilateral triangle have?
2. Draw a shape with 2 lines of symmetry.
3. "Does a regular pentagon have more or fewer lines of symmetry than a square?"
4. EXTENSION: "How many lines of symmetry does a circle have?"

---

## LESSON 5.2 — Right Angles

**Duration:** 50 minutes
**Type:** Elaborate

---

### I DO (12 min)

> "A RIGHT ANGLE is exactly 90 degrees. Like the corner of a piece of paper."

> "A square has 4 right angles. A rectangle has 4 right angles."

> "If an angle is LESS than 90° it is ACUTE. If it is MORE than 90° it is OBTUSE."

---

### YOU DO (15 min)

1. Which shapes in our shapes set have right angles?
2. "Is an acute angle bigger or smaller than a right angle?"
3. "Draw a shape with 4 right angles."
4. EXTENSION: "How many right angles does a rectangular prism have?"
