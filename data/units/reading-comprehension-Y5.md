# Reading Comprehension Unit — Year 5

**Unit Duration:** 8 weeks × 3 lessons per week = 24 lessons
**Year Level:** Year 5
**Curriculum Link:** AC9E5LY01, AC9E5LY02, AC9E5LY03, AC9E5RE01, AC9E5RE02, AC9E5RE03
**Focus:** Multi-layered analysis, evaluating bias, comparing perspectives, synthesising across texts, argumentation
**Mentor Text:** *The Boy Who Fell to Earth* by Kate Champion / *Running with the Bulls* — or use *Holes* by Louis Sachar (1998) as a mentor text for complex plot and thematic analysis
**Cold Task:** Week 1, Lesson 1 — Analyse how an author constructs meaning across layers of a text
**Hot Task:** Week 8, Lesson 1 — Same task, measure growth

*Note: This unit uses Holes by Louis Sachar as the primary mentor text for Years 5–6. It is a complete, complex novel with multiple layers suitable for upper-primary analytical reading.*

---

## Unit Overview

**Learning Intention:**
We are learning to read complex texts with analytical depth, evaluate how authors construct meaning through structure and language, and build supported arguments about texts using evidence from multiple layers.

**Success Criteria:**
- I can analyse how multiple narrative layers (flashback, present-day,命运的 ironic connections) contribute to meaning.
- I can evaluate the author's purpose and explain how structure, language and characterisation serve that purpose.
- I can compare how different texts or perspectives address the same themes.
- I can build a sustained argument about a text using specific, cited evidence.
- I can synthesise information from across a text to form and defend interpretations.

## WEEK 1 — ENTERING THE TEXT

### Lesson 1: Cold Task (Assessment)
**Time:** 55 minutes | **Type:** Assessment

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Hook (5 min) | Show cover of *Holes*. "Stanley Yelnats is wrongfully convicted and sent to a labour camp. This is a story about fate, justice, and finding good in the worst circumstances. Let's read carefully." | Engage |
| Read (25 min) | Read Chapters 1–8 aloud. Introduce: Camp Green Lake, the boys, the digs, Warden and Mr. Sir. Show how the story is simultaneously about the past (Kate Barlow, Sam the Onion Man) and present (Stanley's camp life). | Listen, follow, note |
| Task (25 min) | Cold Task (same as Hot Task): In *Holes*, Louis Sachar weaves together two timelines and multiple character stories. Analyse: How does the structure affect your understanding of the story? What do you think the author wants you to understand about fate, justice, or belonging? Support your ideas with specific details from the text. Year 5: 250–400 words. | Independent analysis |

**Differentiation:** Chapter summary sheet with character names. Timeline visual. Vocabulary support.

---

### Lesson 2: Complex Structure (I Do)
**Time:** 55 minutes | **Type:** Explicit Teaching

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Read (15 min) | Reread Chapters 1–4. Notice how the story moves between present (Camp) and past (old Green Lake, the onion man story). | Notice structure |
| Mini-Lesson (15 min) | Multi-layered narrative: Sachar uses FLASHBACK (past events) and PARALLEL NARRATIVE (two storylines happening at the same time but in different eras). Model mapping the two timelines on a board. Explain that the connections between timelines are the KEY to understanding the story. | Learn structure |
| Share (25 min) | Map the two timelines together: 1800s (Green Lake, Kate Barlow, Sam) vs PRESENT (Camp Green Lake, dig team). Note what we know in each. Note what connects them. | Collaborative mapping |

**Success Criteria Check:** Can identify and describe the two narrative timelines.

### Lesson 3: First Impressions (We Do)
**Time:** 55 minutes | **Type:** Guided Practice

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| I Do (10 min) | Model: "My first impression of Stanley is that he is unlucky but kind. He carries his grandfather's curse but helps other boys at camp. This shows moral complexity." Model finding evidence. | Observe |
| We Do (25 min) | Together, analyse first impressions of 3 key characters: Stanley, Zero, the Warden. Find text evidence for each impression. Discuss: Are first impressions reliable? How do they change? | Group analysis |
| You Do (20 min) | Write first impressions of the camp itself: What is Camp Green Lake really like? What clues does the author give us? | Written observation |

## WEEK 2 — NARRATIVE STRUCTURE

### Lesson 1: Flashback and Backstory (I Do)
**Time:** 55 minutes | **Type:** Explicit Teaching

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Read (15 min) | Read Chapters 9–16 (the backstory of Kate Barlow and the curse). | Follow |
| Mini-Lesson (15 min) | Flashback = a scene set in the past, told to explain the present. In *Holes*, the Kate Barlow story is a flashback. It explains: Why is there no lake? Why is Stanley's family cursed? Why does the Warden want treasure? Model connecting past events to present. | Learn concept |
| You Do (25 min) | Create a cause-effect chain: What happened 100+ years ago in Green Lake → How does this affect Stanley's story NOW? Use specific events from both timelines. | Analysis |

---

### Lesson 2: Interwoven Timelines (I Do → We Do)
**Time:** 55 minutes | **Type:** Explicit Teaching + Guided

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| I Do (10 min) | "Sachar could have told this story in many ways. He chose to weave past and present together. WHY?" Discuss: What is gained by interweaving? (Ironic connections, suspense, theme reinforcement) | Analyse structure choice |
| We Do (30 min) | Track the timeline interweaving through chapters 9–20: In Chapter X, we get Y from the past and Z from the present. How do they connect? What does each add to the other? | Guided tracking |
| You Do (15 min) | Write a paragraph: "Sachar's choice to weave past and present creates [EFFECT] because [REASON]. An example is..." | Written analysis |

---

### Lesson 3: Why Structure Matters (You Do)
**Time:** 55 minutes | **Type:** Independent

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| You Do (55 min) | Write a full structural analysis: 1. Describe the two timelines. 2. Explain how Sachar interweaves them. 3. Choose one specific connection between past and present (e.g., Sam and Mary's story connecting to Stanley and Zero's friendship). 4. Explain why this structure choice matters for meaning. Use quotes and page numbers. | Full essay |

## WEEK 3 — CHARACTER AND MOTIVE

**Time:** 55 minutes | **Type:** Explicit Teaching

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|

|-------|---------|---------|

| We Do (30 min) | Track Stanley's want vs need and Zero's want vs need across the novel so far. How do they change? Where do they overlap? | Group tracking |
| You Do (15 min) | Write: "The friendship between Stanley and Zero matters to the theme because..." Find evidence. | Written connection |

## WEEK 5 — AUTHOR'S PURPOSE AND CRAFT

### Lesson 1: Why Did Sachar Write This? (I Do)
**Time:** 55 minutes | **Type:** Explicit Teaching

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Read (15 min) | Read Chapters 31–36 (resolution, the reveal about Stanley's family, Zero's literacy, fate fulfilled). | Follow |
| Mini-Lesson (15 min) | Author's purpose = the goal the author has in writing. Sachar's purposes in *Holes*: To entertain (funny, suspenseful plot) + To explore justice (innocent boy wrongly convicted) + To show friendship saves + To argue against stereotyping. Model finding multiple purposes. | Learn |
| You Do (25 min) | Analyse: What are the 2–3 most important purposes in *Holes*? For each, find 2 examples of how Sachar achieves this purpose through his craft. | Written purpose analysis |

---

### Lesson 2: Language and Effect (I Do → We Do)
**Time:** 55 minutes | **Type:** Explicit Teaching + Guided

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| I Do (10 min) | Close reading: Find passages where Sachar's language choices create specific effects (humour, suspense, pathos). Examples: Naming "Camp Green Lake" when there is no lake (irony), the Warden's threat about "no lily svc" (foreshadowing), Zero's illiterate speech vs his growing literacy. | Analyse |
| We Do (30 min) | Find 5 specific language choices Sachar makes. For each: Quote it. Name the technique (irony, foreshadowing, contrast, repetition). Explain the effect on the reader. | Group analysis |
| You Do (15 min) | Choose one passage and write a paragraph: "Sachar's use of [TECHNIQUE] in this passage creates [EFFECT] because [REASON]." | Written craft analysis |

---

### Lesson 3: Craft Analysis (We Do → You Do)
**Time:** 55 minutes | **Type:** Guided → Independent

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| We Do (25 min) | Together, write a comprehensive craft analysis paragraph: "Louis Sachar's craft in [CHAPTER/SECTION] creates [EFFECT] through [TECHNIQUE 1], [TECHNIQUE 2], and [TECHNIQUE 3]. For example, he writes '...' (p. X) which... This matters because..." | Group writing |
| You Do (30 min) | Write your own craft analysis of a chapter you have not yet analysed in depth. Include 3 techniques with quotes and page numbers. | Independent writing |

**Time:** 55 minutes | **Type:** Explicit Teaching

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Read (10 min) | Reread key passages about Stanley's innocence and the injustice of his situation. | Review |
| Mini-Lesson (15 min) | Argument structure: CLAIM (what you believe) → EVIDENCE (quotes, events, techniques) → REASONING (why this evidence proves your claim) → COUNTER-ARGUMENT (what someone might say against you) → REBUTTAL (why your argument still stands). Model building a full argument about whether *Holes* is a story about justice or fate. | Learn structure |
| You Do (30 min) | Choose one interpretive claim about *Holes*. Build a full argument using the structure. Include at least 3 pieces of evidence with page numbers. | Written argument |

## ADAPTATIONS

### EAL Students
- Provide comprehensive chapter summaries in home language
- Pre-teach all key vocabulary before each section
- Allow bilingual dictionary use
- Sentence starters for argument and evidence paragraphs
- Provide a timeline template to complete as they read
- Allow oral analysis with teacher transcription
- Visual character web with photos/drawings

### Gifted Students
- Analyse the difference between the book and the film adaptation
- Research Louis Sachar's other works and trace his authorial development
- Compare to other circular/narrative-structure novels (*Cloudy with a Chance of Meatballs*, or short stories)
- Write an additional chapter in Sachar's style
- Create a critical essay on the novel's treatment of justice
- Design a board game based on the story's structure

### Students with NEPs
- Provide pre-digested chapter summaries
- Reduce to key scenes for structural analysis
- Allow focus on one character for full analysis
- Extended time for all tasks
- One-on-one reading support
- Provide a peer helper
- Simplify to one theme and one piece of evidence per paragraph
