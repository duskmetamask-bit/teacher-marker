# Growth and Change — Biological Sciences Unit Plan

**Unit Duration:** 8 weeks × 3 lessons per week = 24 lessons
**Year Level:** Year 2
**Curriculum Link:** Australian Curriculum Science v9 — Biological Sciences
**Unit Big Idea:** All living things grow and change. Some changes happen quickly, some slowly. Living things have different life stages and their needs may change as they grow.
**Cold Task:** Week 1, Lesson 1 — Draw and describe the life cycle of one living thing you know well
**Hot Task:** Week 8, Lesson 1 — Compare the life cycles of two living things and explain how their needs change as they grow

---

## Unit Overview

**Learning Intention:**
We are learning to describe the life stages of living things, explain how living things change as they grow, compare life cycles of different organisms, and describe how the needs of living things change across life stages.

**Success Criteria:**
- I can describe the life stages of at least 3 different organisms.
- I can explain how a living thing changes as it grows.
- I can compare the life cycles of two organisms.
- I can describe how an animal's needs change as it grows (e.g., baby to adult).
- I can use science words: life cycle, stages, grow, change, offspring, parent.

## WEEK 1 — ENGAGE: Life Cycles

### Lesson 1: Cold Task — Draw a Life Cycle
**Time:** 40 minutes | **Type:** Assessment (Formative Pre-Assessment)

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Hook (5 min) | Show images of a baby, a child, a teenager, an adult. "This is a human life cycle. What happens at each stage? What do you look like now? What will you look like as an adult?" | Observe and discuss |
| Task (30 min) | Cold Task: "Choose ONE living thing you know well (a pet, a farm animal, a garden plant, a human). Draw its LIFE CYCLE — all the stages from the beginning of life to making new life. Label each stage. Write 2–3 sentences about what happens at each stage. Year 2: draw 3–4 stages." | Draw life cycle |
| Share (5 min) | 3–4 students share their life cycles | Share |

**Differentiation:** EAL students draw life cycle and point to labels. NEP students: draw 2 stages with teacher support.

**Success Criteria Check:** Student draws a life cycle with at least 3 stages.

---

### Lesson 2: What Is a Life Cycle?
**Time:** 40 minutes | **Type:** Engage (Concept Introduction)

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Life Cycle Definition (15 min) | "A LIFE CYCLE is all the stages a living thing goes through from being born/hatched to becoming an adult that can make new living things. The cycle then CONTINUES — the new living thing will also go through the same stages. This is how living things REPRODUCE and keep their species alive." | Learn |
| All Living Things Have Life Cycles (10 min) | "ALL living things have life cycles — humans, dogs, birds, fish, insects, frogs, plants. The details are different, but the idea is the same: born → grow → become adult → make offspring → repeat." | Learn |
| Identify Life Stages (10 min) | Show a butterfly life cycle poster. Identify stages: egg → caterpillar (larva) → chrysalis (pupa) → adult butterfly. "This is called METAMORPHOSIS — a big change in body form." | Learn metamorphosis |
| Record (5 min) | Write: "A life cycle has stages from birth to adult. Some animals change a lot — this is metamorphosis." | Write |

**Success Criteria Check:** Correctly defines a life cycle and identifies at least 4 stages.

### Lesson 3: Life Stages of Animals
**Time:** 40 minutes | **Type:** Engage (Investigation)

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Human Life Stages (10 min) | "Humans go through: BABY (needs lots of care), CHILD (learns to walk, talk), TEENAGER (body changes, becomes able to reproduce), ADULT (can have children), ELDERLY (slows down, eventually dies). Each stage has different needs." | Learn |
| Compare Two Animals' Stages (15 min) | Compare human to dog: Human: baby → child → teen → adult → elderly. Dog: puppy → juvenile → adolescent → adult → senior. Compare: both are mammals, both go through growth stages, both need care as babies, both eventually age. | Compare |
| Animal Life Stage Hunt (10 min) | Students research one animal's life stages: dog, cat, chicken, frog, butterfly. Draw the stages in order. Label each stage. | Research |
| Share (5 min) | Share 3–4 animal life cycles. | Share |

**Differentiation:** EAL students draw with teacher support. Gifted students: research the life cycle of a uniquely Australian animal (echidna or platypus — they lay eggs like reptiles but are mammals!).

**Success Criteria Check:** Researches one animal's life stages and correctly sequences at least 3 stages.

## WEEK 2 — EXPLORE: Insects and Frogs

### Lesson 1: Butterfly Life Cycle
**Time:** 40 minutes | **Type:** Explore (Investigation)

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Complete Metamorphosis (15 min) | "Butterflies go through COMPLETE METAMORPHOSIS — the body changes COMPLETELY at each stage. STAGE 1: EGG — tiny egg on a leaf (food for hatching caterpillar). STAGE 2: LARVA (caterpillar) — eats and eats, grows, sheds skin multiple times. STAGE 3: PUPA (chrysalis) — body dissolves and reforms into adult inside a protective case. STAGE 4: ADULT — butterfly emerges, feeds on nectar, mates, females lay eggs." | Learn stages |
| Observe Caterpillar (if available) (15 min) | If caterpillars are available, observe daily: What are they eating? How are they moving? How often do they shed? Record observations. | Observe |
| Draw and Label (10 min) | Draw the complete butterfly life cycle: EGG → CATERPILLAR → CHRYSALIS → BUTTERFLY. Label each stage and write one sentence about what happens. | Draw |
| Exit Ticket (5 min) | "Name all 4 stages of a butterfly's life cycle." | Written response |

**Success Criteria Check:** Correctly identifies and sequences all 4 stages of butterfly complete metamorphosis.

### Lesson 2: Frog Life Cycle
**Time:** 40 minutes | **Type:** Explore (Investigation)

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Amphibian Metamorphosis (15 min) | "Frogs go through METAMORPHOSIS too — but different from butterflies. STAGE 1: SPAWN (eggs) laid in water. STAGE 2: TADPOLE (has gills like a fish, swims like a fish, looks like a fish). STAGE 3: FROGLET (back legs grow, tail still present, starts to develop lungs). STAGE 4: ADULT FROG (front legs grow, tail disappears, fully formed frog with lungs for breathing air)." | Learn |
| Compare Tadpole to Adult Frog (10 min) | "How is a tadpole different from an adult frog? Tadpole: has TAIL, has GILLS, breathes UNDERWATER, EATS plants. Adult frog: has NO TAIL, has LUNGS, breathes AIR, EATS insects. These are VERY different animals — but they are the SAME species!" | Compare |
| Draw Frog Life Cycle (10 min) | Draw: SPAWN → TADPOLE → FROGLET → ADULT FROG. Label each stage. | Draw |
| Compare to Butterfly (5 min) | "Butterfly: egg → caterpillar → chrysalis → butterfly. Frog: egg → tadpole → froglet → frog. Same idea (born → grow → change → adult), different details." | Compare |

**Success Criteria Check:** Correctly sequences frog life cycle stages and explains the key changes (gills to lungs, tail to no tail).

### Lesson 3: Compare Insects and Amphibians
**Time:** 40 minutes | **Type:** Elaborate (Comparison)

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Compare Life Cycles (15 min) | Venn diagram: INSECT LIFE CYCLE ←→ AMPHIBIAN LIFE CYCLE. Same: egg stage, metamorphosis, both need water for egg stage, adult is reproductive. Different: insect eggs → larvae (eat lots) → pupa (rest/transformation) → adult. Amphibian eggs → aquatic larvae (gills) → gradual change → adult. Insects: complete metamorphosis. Amphibians: incomplete metamorphosis. | Compare |
| Metamorphosis Investigation (10 min) | Research: Is a ladybird an insect? (Yes.) What are its life stages? (Egg → larva → pupa → adult.) Compare to butterfly. Both complete metamorphosis insects. | Research |
| Sort: Complete vs Incomplete (10 min) | Animals: butterfly, dragonfly, frog, grasshopper, bee, toad, ladybird, mosquito. Which are COMPLETE metamorphosis (egg → larva → pupa → adult) vs INCOMPLETE (egg → nymph/naiad → adult — nymph looks like a small adult)? | Sort |
| Write (5 min) | "Complete metamorphosis: body changes completely. Incomplete metamorphosis: nymph looks like a small adult." | Write |

**Success Criteria Check:** Correctly sorts at least 6 animals by metamorphosis type and explains the difference between complete and incomplete metamorphosis.

## WEEK 3 — EXPLORE: Birds and Mammals

### Lesson 1: Chicken Life Cycle
**Time:** 40 minutes | **Type:** Explain (Explicit Teaching)

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Bird Life Cycle (15 min) | "Birds lay EGGS with hard shells (protect the developing chick). STAGE 1: FERTILISED EGG — laid by mother hen, incubated (kept warm) by mother or in incubator. STAGE 2: EMBRYO develops inside egg — uses yolk for food. STAGE 3: CHICK PIPS out of shell — breaks shell with egg tooth (special bump on beak). STAGE 4: DOWNY CHICK — covered in soft down feathers, unsteady on feet. STAGE 5: JUVENILE — learns to eat, walk, preen feathers. STAGE 6: ADULT — fully feathered, can reproduce." | Learn |
| Incubation Investigation (if possible) (15 min) | If fertilised eggs are available: observe over 21 days (chicken incubation period). Candle eggs (shine light through) to see embryo development. Record daily observations. | Observe |
| Draw and Label (10 min) | Draw the chicken life cycle stages. Label. | Draw |

**Success Criteria Check:** Correctly sequences chicken life cycle stages and explains the role of the egg yolk and shell.

### Lesson 2: Mammal Life Cycle
**Time:** 40 minutes | **Type:** Explain (Explicit Teaching)

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Mammal Life Cycle (15 min) | "Mammals (except monotremes) are born LIVE, not from eggs. STAGE 1: EMBRYO develops inside mother's UTERUS. STAGE 2: BABY is born — mammals are the only animals that feed their babies MILK from mammary glands (this is where the word 'mammal' comes from). STAGE 3: JUVENILE — learns to walk, eat solid food, socialise. STAGE 4: ADULT — sexually mature, can reproduce. STAGE 5: ELDERLY — slows down, eventually dies." | Learn |
| Compare Mammals to Birds (15 min) | Mammals vs Birds: Same: both are warm-blooded, both have stages from birth to adult, both care for young. Different: mammals born alive (mostly), birds hatch from eggs. Mammals drink milk, birds feed insects/seeds to chicks. Bird eggs need incubating, mammal babies don't. | Compare |
| Human Life Cycle (10 min) | "Humans are mammals. Our life cycle: BABY (0–1 year — needs lots of care, milk or formula) → CHILD (2–11 — loses baby teeth, grows steadily) → TEENAGER (12–17 — puberty changes, become able to reproduce) → ADULT (18+ — can have children) → ELDERLY (65+ — may slow down)." | Learn |
| Write (5 min) | "Mammals are born alive and drink milk from their mothers. This is different from birds which hatch from eggs." | Write |

**Success Criteria Check:** Correctly describes mammal life cycle stages and explains the difference between mammal and bird reproduction.

### Lesson 3: Compare Birds and Mammals
**Time:** 40 minutes | **Type:** Elaborate (Comparison)

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Deep Compare (15 min) | Create comparison table: BIRDS vs MAMMALS. Features: body covering (feathers vs fur/hair), reproduction (eggs vs live birth), temperature (warm-blooded both), how they feed young (regurgitated food/seeds vs milk), where they live (both varied), body temperature regulation. | Compare |
| Life Cycle Similarity (10 min) | "Despite different reproduction methods, birds and mammals share the same LIFE CYCLE STRUCTURE: born/hatched → grow → become adult → reproduce → die. Each generation repeats the cycle." | Learn |
| Design an Animal (10 min) | "Design an imaginary animal. Give it a life cycle. Decide: Does it lay eggs or give birth to live young? How many stages does it have? How long is each stage? What does it look like at each stage?" Draw your imaginary animal's life cycle. | Design |
| Share (5 min) | 3–4 students share designs | Share |

**Differentiation:** EAL students draw life cycle with teacher support. Gifted students: research monotremes (platypus and echidna) — they are mammals that lay eggs! How does their life cycle work?

**Success Criteria Check:** Compares bird and mammal life cycles, correctly identifying similarities and differences.

## WEEK 4 — EXPLAIN: Plants Grow Too

### Lesson 1: Seed to Seedling
**Time:** 40 minutes | **Type:** Explore (Investigation)

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Seed Observations (15 min) | Examine seeds: broad bean, sunflower seed, pumpkin seed, acorn. Draw each. What do seeds look like? Inside every seed is a baby plant (embryo) and stored food. When conditions are right (water, warmth), the seed GERMINATES — the embryo starts to grow. | Observe |
| Germination Investigation Setup (15 min) | Set up germination test: bean seeds on damp cotton wool in a clear plastic bag. Observe daily. Record: Day 1: seed. Day 2: seed swells, tiny root emerges. Day 3: root grows, shoot emerges. Day 4+: first leaves appear. | Set up |
| Predict (5 min) | "What do you think will happen? Draw your prediction." | Predict |
| Record (5 min) | Draw and label seed parts: seed coat (outer shell), embryo (baby plant), cotyledon (stored food). | Draw |

**Success Criteria Check:** Sets up a seed germination investigation, makes predictions, and can identify basic seed parts.

---

### Lesson 2: Plant Life Stages
**Time:** 40 minutes | **Type:** Explain (Explicit Teaching)

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Plant Life Stages (15 min) | "Plants also have life stages: STAGE 1: SEED — dormant (sleeping), contains embryo and food. STAGE 2: GERMINATION — seed sprouts, root emerges first (anchors plant), then shoot. STAGE 3: SEEDLING — first leaves appear (cotyledons), starts making its own food via photosynthesis. STAGE 4: MATURE PLANT — adult, produces flowers. STAGE 5: FLOWERING — flowers make seeds (pollination by insects/wind). STAGE 6: SEED DISPERSAL — seeds spread away from parent plant (by wind, animals, water). Cycle repeats." | Learn |
| Check Germination (10 min) | Return to germination experiment. Record observations. Have roots and shoots emerged? Measure them. | Observe |
| Draw Plant Life Cycle (10 min) | Draw: SEED → GERMINATION → SEEDLING → MATURE PLANT → FLOWERS → SEEDS. Label each stage. | Draw |
| Write (5 min) | "Plants grow through stages just like animals. Seeds germinate, seedlings grow into adult plants, and adult plants make new seeds." | Write |

**Success Criteria Check:** Correctly sequences plant life stages and can explain at least 4 stages.

---

### Lesson 3: Growing from Seeds Investigation
**Time:** 40 minutes | **Type:** Explore (Investigation)

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Fair Test — What Do Seeds Need? (15 min) | "We will test what seeds need to germinate. Set up 4 conditions: (1) Seeds with water + warmth + light. (2) Seeds with water + warmth, NO light. (3) Seeds with light + warmth, NO water. (4) Seeds with water, NO warmth (in fridge). Predict: which will germinate?" | Set up test |
| Observe and Record (15 min) | Students observe all 4 conditions over 2 weeks, recording daily: Has the seed germinated? (Yes/No). How big is the shoot? Any leaves? | Record |
| Conclusion (10 min) | After 2 weeks: What did we learn? Seeds need WATER + WARMTH to germinate. Light is not needed for germination (but needed later for photosynthesis). Cold stops germination. Draw conclusions. | Conclude |

**Differentiation:** EAL students draw observations. Gifted students: research what happens if a seed doesn't get light after germination — will the seedling survive?

**Success Criteria Check:** Conducts a fair test investigating seed germination conditions, records observations, and draws conclusions.

## WEEK 6 — ELABORATE: Survival and Life Cycles

### Lesson 1: Life Cycles Help Survival
**Time:** 40 minutes | **Type:** Explain (Explicit Teaching)

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Why Have Life Cycles? (15 min) | "Life cycles help species SURVIVE. Different stages at different times means: (1) PARENTS and OFFSPRING don't compete for the same food — caterpillars eat leaves, butterflies drink nectar. (2) Some stages are better for SURVIVAL in bad conditions — seeds can survive winter, adult frogs may die but eggs survive in pond mud. (3) SPREADING OUT — offspring look different from parents so they can move to different places. (4) GENETIC VARIATION — offspring are not identical to parents, some may be better adapted to changes in environment." | Learn |
| Case Study: Salmon (15 min) | "Salmon life cycle: adult salmon swim upstream to where they were born (homing instinct). Lay eggs in river gravel. Eggs hatch → alevins (baby fish with yolk sac). Fry (young fish) → grow → parr (juvenile). Smolt (adapted to salt water) → migrate to ocean. Adult salmon → grow huge in ocean. Return to river to reproduce. WHY? Freshwater is safer for eggs, ocean has more food for adults." | Learn |
| Write (10 min) | "Life cycles help animals survive by: [reason 1], [reason 2]." Write 2 reasons. | Write |

**Success Criteria Check:** Explains at least 2 ways life cycles help species survive.

### Lesson 2: Australian Life Cycles
**Time:** 40 minutes | **Type:** Explain (Explicit Teaching)

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Koala Life Cycle (15 min) | "Koala life cycle: joey (baby) born tiny and undeveloped (size of a jellybean). Crawls into mother's POUCH (upside down, backward-facing). Drinks milk in pouch for 6–7 months. Starts eating PAPPY (partially digested eucalyptus leaves from mother's caecum) from pouch entrance. Leaves pouch at 8 months, rides on mother's back for 6 more months. Fully independent at 1 year. Adult at 3–4 years." | Learn |
| Emu Life Cycle (15 min) | "Emu life cycle: male emu builds nest, female lays eggs (5–11 eggs, large green eggs). MALE incubates eggs (only male — this is rare!). Male sits on nest for 8 weeks, loses up to 8kg, barely eats/drinks. Chicks hatch and can walk/run within hours. Male protects chicks for 6–8 months. Unusual for birds — male does most childcare." | Learn |
| Compare Australian Animals (10 min) | Venn: KOALA ←→ EMU. Same: both Australian, both care for young. Different: koala in pouch, emu not. Emu dad incubates, koala mum cares. | Compare |

**Success Criteria Check:** Describes at least 2 Australian animals' life cycles and compares them.

### EAL Learners
- **Visual Word Wall:** Picture-word pairs for all vocabulary.
