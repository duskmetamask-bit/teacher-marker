# Global Connections — Geography Unit Plan

**Unit Duration:** 8 weeks × 3 lessons per week = 24 lessons
**Year Level:** Year 5
**Curriculum Link:** Australian Curriculum HASS v9 — Geography (Years 5–6)
**Unit Big Idea:** Our world is deeply interconnected through trade, migration, information, and culture. These connections shape how places develop, how resources are distributed, and how global challenges are shared. Understanding connections helps us understand our place in the world.
**Cold Task:** Week 1, Lesson 1 — Investigate two places and explain how they are connected to other places through trade, migration, or communication
**Hot Task:** Week 8, Lesson 1 — Investigate how a significant global challenge connects multiple places and explain how cooperation between places could address it

---

## Unit Overview

**Learning Intention:**
We are learning to investigate how places are connected through trade, migration, communication, and shared challenges, and to evaluate how these connections affect people's lives and wellbeing.

**Success Criteria:**
- I can describe at least 3 types of connections between places (trade, migration, communication, culture, environment).
- I can explain how trade networks distribute resources and shape economies.
- I can describe how migration shapes both places of origin and destination.
- I can explain how communication technology has changed connections between places.
- I can investigate a global challenge and evaluate how connections between places affect responses.
- I can use sources (data, maps, reports, statistics) to investigate global connections.

## WEEK 1 — ENGAGE: A Connected World

### Lesson 1: Cold Task — Connections Between Places
**Time:** 40 minutes | **Type:** Assessment (Formative Pre-Assessment)

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Hook (5 min) | Show a world map highlighting a smartphone supply chain: minerals from the DRC (cobalt), manufacturing in China, design in California, sales worldwide. "This phone is connected to the whole world. Everything is connected." | Observe and wonder |

| Cold Task (30 min) | "Choose two places. Describe at least 2 ways they are connected to OTHER places (trade, migration, communication, culture). For each connection: WHO is involved? WHAT is being exchanged? HOW does it move? Write 4–6 sentences explaining how these connections affect the places." Year 5 expectation: detailed, multiple connection types, evidence-based. | Independent |

**Differentiation:** Image word bank. Sentence starters: "This place is connected to [place] through..." / "The connection involves..." / "This affects the places by..." / "The connection works because..."

## WEEK 2 — EXPLORE: Trade Networks

### Lesson 1: How Trade Works
**Time:** 40 minutes | **Type:** Explore (Investigation)

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Why Do Places Trade? (10 min) | "Places trade because: no place has EVERYTHING / some places are BETTER at producing certain things / specialisation increases efficiency / comparative advantage (even if not the best, it is cheaper for you to produce what you are relatively better at). Trade makes all places better off than if they tried to be self-sufficient." | Learn |
| Trade Vocabulary (10 min) | "EXPORTS: goods a place SELLS to others / IMPORTS: goods a place BUYS from others / TRADE BALANCE: exports minus imports / TARIFF: a tax on imported goods / FREE TRADE: trade without restrictions / PROTECTIONISM: using tariffs to protect local industries." | Learn |
| Australia's Trade Profile (10 min) | "Australia is a major global trader. Top exports: iron ore (world's largest exporter, ~60% to China) / coal (2nd largest exporter) / LNG (LNG: liquefied natural gas, 4th largest) / beef, lamb, wheat / wool / gold. Top imports: machinery / electronics / vehicles / petroleum / consumer goods. We are a RESOURCE ECONOMY — we sell what comes from the ground." | Observe |
| Trade Partners (10 min) | "Australia's top trade partners: CHINA (our biggest by far — both exports and imports) / JAPAN / SOUTH KOREA / USA / ASEAN countries. China is our largest because: proximity (geography), growing middle class (demand), manufacturing capacity." | Learn |

**Success Criteria Check:** Can define exports and imports and name at least 2 of Australia's top exports and trade partners.

### Lesson 3: Trade and Power
**Time:** 40 minutes | **Type:** Explain (Explicit Teaching)

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Trade Is Not Neutral (10 min) | "Trade is not just about economics — it is about POWER. Countries and companies with more power get better deals. Historical trade patterns often reflect colonial history — some countries are still caught in patterns of exporting raw materials (like ore) and importing finished products (like electronics). This keeps them less wealthy." | Understand |
| The Resource Curse (10 min) | "The RESOURCE CURSE: countries with lots of natural resources (oil, gold, diamonds) often have LOWER economic growth than countries without them. Why? Because: prices are volatile / governments rely on resource revenue, neglect other industries / inequality if wealth not distributed / 'Dutch Disease' (resource wealth makes other exports uncompetitive). Australia largely avoided this because we diversified." | Learn |
| Power in Supply Chains (10 min) | "In supply chains, POWER is held by those with unique resources or unique capabilities. China has power over rare earth refining. Apple has power over design and brand (captures most profits). Foxconn has less power (competes with other manufacturers). Countries negotiate TRADE AGREEMENTS to get better access." | Understand |
| Trade Agreements (10 min) | "Trade agreements are deals between countries to reduce trade barriers. Examples: ASEAN Free Trade Area / RCEP (Regional Comprehensive Economic Partnership — includes Australia, China, Japan, Korea) / USMCA (US-Mexico-Canada) / EU Single Market. Agreements aim to make trade fairer and easier." | Learn |

**Differentiation:** EAL students create a visual power map of one supply chain. Gifted students: research one specific trade agreement and present findings.

**Success Criteria Check:** Can explain the resource curse and describe at least 1 example of power dynamics in trade.

## WEEK 3 — EXPLORE + EXPLAIN: Migration and People

### Lesson 1: Why People Move
**Time:** 40 minutes | **Type:** Explore (Investigation)

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Why Do People Migrate? (15 min) | "People move for many reasons — we call these PUSH and PULL factors. PUSH FACTORS (make you want to leave): war/conflict, persecution, poverty, lack of jobs, natural disasters, climate change effects. PULL FACTORS (attract you to a new place): safety, better jobs, education, family connections, political freedom, better healthcare." | Learn |
| Types of Migration (10 min) | "MIGRATION types: (1) INTERNAL — moving within a country (rural to city) / (2) INTERNATIONAL — moving between countries / (3) PERMANENT — to live permanently / (4) TEMPORARY — work visa, student visa / (5) FORCED — refugees, asylum seekers / (6) VOLUNTARY — by choice." | Learn |
| Australia's Migration History (10 min) | "Australia's migration story: 1788 onwards British colonists / 1850s Gold Rush — Chinese and European / 1901 White Australia Policy (restricted non-European migration until 1973) / Post-WWII: Big Australian migration program — displaced persons from Europe (1945–1970s) / 1970s onwards: multicultural Australia — Asia, Middle East, Africa, Eastern Europe. Now: highest per-capita immigration rate in the world." | Learn |
| Exit Ticket (5 min) | "Write 2 sentences: What is one push factor and one pull factor that might cause someone to migrate?" | Write |

**Success Criteria Check:** Can name at least 2 push and 2 pull factors and identify the type of migration for at least 1 scenario.

For some countries, remittances > foreign investment + foreign aid combined." | Learn |

|-------|----------------|---------|

All of this is infrastructure." | Learn |
| Draw the Internet (5 min) | "Draw a simple diagram showing how a message from Perth travels to London. Label: device → WiFi/cable → server → undersea cable → London server → recipient." | Draw |
