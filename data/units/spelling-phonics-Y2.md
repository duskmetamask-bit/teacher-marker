# Spelling and Phonics Unit — Year 2

**Unit Duration:** 8 weeks × 3 lessons per week = 24 lessons
**Year Level:** Year 2
**Curriculum Link:** AC9E2SP01, AC9E2SP02, AC9E2LY03
**Focus:** Vowel digraphs, r-controlled vowels, common syllable patterns, compound words, common irregular words
**Mentor Text:** *The Magic Finger* by Roald Dahl (1966)
**Cold Task:** Week 1, Lesson 1 — Spell words with vowel digraphs and common patterns
**Hot Task:** Week 8, Lesson 1 — Same task, measure growth

---

## Unit Overview

**Learning Intention:**
We are learning to spell words with vowel digraphs (ai, ay, oa, ow, ou, ie, ee), r-controlled vowels (ar, or, er, ur), common syllable patterns, and compound words.

**Success Criteria:**
- I can spell words with vowel digraphs (ai, ay, oa, ow, ou, ie, ee).
- I can spell words with r-controlled vowels (ar, or, er, ur, ir).
- I can read and spell compound words.
- I can spell common irregular words accurately.
- I can apply syllable-spelling patterns to decode and encode longer words.
- I can use spelling strategies to attempt unknown words.

## WEEK 1 — VOWEL DIGRAPHS REVIEW

### Lesson 1: Cold Task (Assessment)
**Time:** 40 minutes | **Type:** Assessment

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Hook (5 min) | "The Magic Finger is about a girl whose magic finger causes hilarious chaos! Today we will test our spelling skills on words from the story and other patterns." | Engage |
| Read (10 min) | Read selected passages from *The Magic Finger*. Notice spelling patterns: "flying" (y as vowel), "bright" (igh), "change" (ge). | Listen |
| Task (25 min) | Cold Task (same as Hot Task): 1. Spell vowel digraph words: rain, boat, feet, cow, tail, road. 2. Spell r-controlled words: farm, horn, fern, turn, star, storm. 3. Spell compound words: rainbow, farmland, nighttime. 4. Write a sentence using 3 spelling pattern words. Compare to Week 8. | Independent spelling |

**Differentiation:** Word wall. Sound-spelling cards. Allow verbal spelling.

---

### Lesson 2: ai and ay (I Do)
**Time:** 40 minutes | **Type:** Explicit Teaching

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Mini-Lesson (15 min) | AI says /ai/ (rain, train, pain, gain, mail, chain). AY says /ai/ (day, play, say, way, stay, pray). Rule: ai usually in the MIDDLE of words. ay usually at the END of words. From *The Magic Finger*: "flying" (y=/ie/), "change" (ge), but also: "rain" (ai), "stay" (ay). | Learn |
| Activity (15 min) | Complete with ai or ay: r__n, pl__, tr__n, st__, m__l, w__y, ch__n, s__y. Sort: Words with AI (middle) vs AY (end). Write each word. | Practice |
| Share (10 min) | "How do you know when to use ai vs ay?" Rules: middle = ai, end = ay. | Discussion |

**Success Criteria Check:** Can spell ai and ay words correctly.

### Lesson 3: oa and ow (We Do)
**Time:** 40 minutes | **Type:** Guided Practice

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| I Do (10 min) | OA says /oa/ (boat, road, coat, goat, float, snow — wait, snow uses OW!). Two options for /oa/: OA (middle) and OW (end). OA: boat, road, coat, float. OW: show, slow, grow, snow, flow. | Learn |
| We Do (25 min) | Complete with oa or ow: b__t, r__d, sh_w, sl_w, fl__t, gr_w, c__t, sn_w. Sort: OA vs OW. Write sentences with each. | Guided practice |
| You Do (5 min) | Write 4 oa words and 4 ow words. Circle the digraph. | Written practice |

## WEEK 3 — R-CONTROLLED VOWELS

### Lesson 1: ar and or (I Do)
**Time:** 40 minutes | **Type:** Explicit Teaching

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Mini-Lesson (15 min) | Bossy R makes vowels change sound. AR says /ar/ (farm, car, star, hard, dark, start). OR says /or/ (horn, born, storm, short, form, fork). The R "owns" the vowel and changes its sound. From *The Magic Finger*: "farm" (ar), "storm" (or). | Learn |
| Activity (15 min) | Complete with ar or or: f_rm, h rn, st_r, d_rma, h_rd, sh_rt, l_ng, st_rt. Sort: AR vs OR. Say each word and sort. | Practice |
| You Do (10 min) | Write 5 ar words and 5 or words. Circle the r-controlled vowel. | Written practice |

---

### Lesson 2: er, ur, ir (I Do → We Do)
**Time:** 40 minutes | **Type:** Explicit Teaching + Guided

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| I Do (10 min) | ER, UR, IR all say /er/ (her, turn, bird). The R controls the vowel and makes the /er/ sound. ER: her, over, under, after, mother. UR: turn, burn, fur, hurl, Thursday. IR: bird, girl, first, third, birthday. | Learn |
| We Do (25 min) | Complete: h_rd, b_rd, t_rn, b_rn, f_rst, g_rl, th_rd, S_t_rday. Sort: ER vs UR vs IR. Write sentences. | Guided |
| You Do (5 min) | Write 3 words from each group: ER, UR, IR. | Written practice |

### Lesson 3: R-Controlled Practice (You Do)
**Time:** 40 minutes | **Type:** Independent

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| You Do (40 min) | 1. R-controlled dictation: "The bird turned in the storm. The girl was hurt on the farm. Hard work can turn a worm into a bird." 2. Sort 15 words into AR, OR, ER, UR, IR columns. 3. Write a sentence using one word from each group. | Full practice |

## WEEK 4 — COMPOUND WORDS

### Lesson 1: Two-Word Combinations (I Do)
**Time:** 40 minutes | **Type:** Explicit Teaching

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Mini-Lesson (15 min) | Compound word = two smaller words joined to make one new word. rain + bow = rainbow. farm + land = farmland. night + time = nighttime. The meaning COMBINES: rainbow (rain + bow — a bow in the rain). farmland (farm + land — land for farming). | Learn |
| Activity (15 min) | Build compound words with cards: rain + bow → rainbow, sun + shine → sunshine, but + terfly → butterfly, foot + ball → football, bed + room → bedroom, book + shop → bookshelf. Match and write. | Building |
| You Do (10 min) | Draw a line between two words to make compound words: moonlight, toothbrush, starfish, mailbox, waterfall, grandfather. Write all 6 compounds. | Written practice |

---

### Lesson 2: Spelling Compounds (I Do → We Do)
**Time:** 40 minutes | **Type:** Explicit Teaching + Guided

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| I Do (10 min) | When you spell compound words, you spell each part. rainbow = r-a-i-n-b-o-w. Both words keep their spelling — no changes when joining. Exception: sometimes the spelling stays exactly the same (football = foot + ball). | Learn |
| We Do (25 min) | Spell these compounds: rain + bow = rainbow, sun + shine = sunshine, but + terfly = butterfly, grand + father = grandfather, earth + quake = earthquake, home + work = homework. Build and write each. Check spelling of both parts. | Guided |
| You Do (5 min) | Write 4 compound words you know. Underline both parts in each word. | Written practice |

---

### Lesson 3: Compound Sentences (You Do)
**Time:** 40 minutes | **Type:** Independent

| Phase | Teacher | Students |

Write 6 compound words in alphabetical order: sunlight, bedroom, spacecraft, lighthouse, keyboard, firefighter. 3. Write a sentence using one compound word from each letter of the alphabet (if possible — aim for 6). | Full practice |

## WEEK 5 — SYLLABLE PATTERNS

### Lesson 1: Closed Syllables (I Do)
**Time:** 40 minutes | **Type:** Explicit Teaching

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Mini-Lesson (15 min) | Closed syllable = ends with a CONSONANT. The consonant CLOSES the vowel and makes it SHORT. CVC pattern: cat, hat, sit, run, cup, bed. Each syllable is a CLOSED syllable because the vowel is closed by a consonant. This is why CVC vowels are short. | Learn |
| Activity (15 min) | Identify closed syllables in longer words: pic-nic (2 closed syllables), ren-net, bas-ket, but-ton, mag-net, ves-ted, in-sect, com-et. Say each syllable. Notice the vowel sound in each — it should be SHORT. | Practice |
| You Do (10 min) | Break into syllables (write the syllable breaks): pocket, rabbit, carpet, helmet, basket, rocket. Circle the vowels in each syllable — are they short? | Written practice |

---

### Lesson 2: Open Syllables (I Do → We Do)
**Time:** 40 minutes | **Type:** Explicit Teaching + Guided

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| I Do (10 min) | Open syllable = ends with a VOWEL. The vowel is OPEN and says its NAME (long sound). go (open — o says /oe/), he (open — e says /ee/), ba-by (ba is open, by is closed — by ends with consonant Y). Open syllables usually have LONG vowel sounds. | Learn |
| We Do (25 min) | Identify open syllables: ta-ble (ta is open → /tay/), ro-bot (ro is open → /roe/), le-gent (le is open → /lee/). Break these words: robot, data, famous, tiny, over, open, music, unit. Label: open (long vowel) or closed (short vowel). | Guided |
| You Do (5 min) | Write open vs closed: go, he, hi, we, so, no, she, be. Label each syllable as open or closed. | Written practice |

---

### Lesson 3: VCE Pattern (You Do)
**Time:** 40 minutes | **Type:** Independent

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| You Do (40 min) | VCE = Vowel-Consonant-E (magic E). The E is SILENT and makes the preceding vowel LONG. made, kite, home, cute, hope, flame, prize, drive. 1. Write 8 VCE words. 2. Break into syllables: making (mak-ing), hiding (hid-ing), using (us-ing). 3. Dictation: "I made a cake at home. The flame was bright. We drive to the lake on Friday." 4. Sort: VCE vs closed syllables. | Full practice |

## WEEK 6 — COMMON IRREGULAR WORDS

### Lesson 1: Hard Words (I Do)
**Time:** 40 minutes | **Type:** Explicit Teaching

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Mini-Lesson (15 min) | Irregular words = don't follow normal spelling rules. Must be MEMORISED. Common irregular words: said, have, friend, been, because, people, their, would, two, one, once, move, improve, sure, figure. These are common but tricky — no pattern to memorise, just remember. | Learn |
| Activity (15 min) | Mnemonic strategies: said = "s-a-i-d — Sally and I are dancing." friend = "f-r-i-e-n-d — Friendship requires intimacy, everyone needs drama." because = "b-e-c-a-u-s-e — Big elephants can always use small elephants." Create a mnemonic for 5 irregular words. | Mnemonics |
| You Do (10 min) | Write each irregular word 5 times. Use it in a sentence. Circle the hardest part of each word. | Written practice |

### Lesson 2: Mnemonic Strategies (I Do → We Do)

| Phase | Teacher | Students |

| We Do (25 min) | Create personal mnemonics for: friend, because, said, people, been, their, would, two, once, move, improve, sure, figure. Write each mnemonic. Share with partner. Test each other. | Guided creation |

## WEEK 7 — SPELLING STRATEGIES

### Lesson 1: Sound-Spelling (I Do)
**Time:** 40 minutes | **Type:** Explicit Teaching

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Mini-Lesson (15 min) | Spelling strategy 1: SOUND IT OUT. Say the word. Segment the sounds. Try spelling. Example: "flying" → /f-l-ie-ing/ → fl-y-ing → flying. Some sounds are tricky: /f/ can be f or ff, /ie/ can be i, ie, y. Use what you know. | Learn |
| Activity (15 min) | Attempt unknown words using sound-spelling: lightning, surprised, enormous, immediately, beautiful, different, thought, through, caught. Say it. Segment. Try spelling. Check. Note the tricky parts. | Practice |
| You Do (10 min) | Write 5 words by sounding out. Circle the tricky part in each. Write the correct spelling next to it. | Written practice |

---

### Lesson 2: Pattern-Spelling (I Do → We Do)
**Time:** 40 minutes | **Type:** Explicit Teaching + Guided

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| I Do (10 min) | Spelling strategy 2: PATTERN SPOTTING. Find known patterns in unknown words. "Light" has ight pattern. "Catching" has atch pattern. If you know patterns, you can spell more words. | Learn |
| We Do (25 min) | Spot patterns in unknown words: light, right, sight, taught, caught, thought, through. Pattern: ight, augh, ough. Find as many words with these patterns as you can. Write them. | Guided |
| You Do (5 min) | Write 5 words with patterns you know (ai, ay, oa, ar, or, ight, augh, ough). Circle the pattern. | Written practice |

## ADAPTATIONS

### EAL Students
- Provide vowel digraph charts with examples
- Focus on one digraph pair per week (ai/ay, then oa/ow)
- Use mnemonics for irregular words
- Allow verbal spelling for complex words
- Provide compound word word lists

### Gifted Students
- Introduce diphthongs (oi, oy, au, aw)
- Explore silent letter patterns (kn-, wr-, gn-)
- Introduce suffix spelling patterns (-tion, -sion)
- Explore homophones and spelling differences
- Research etymology for spelling patterns
- Create spelling games for the class

### Students with NEPs
- Focus on 2 vowel digraph patterns (ai, oa) + ar/or
- Provide word banks for all activities
- Use multisensory spelling (write in sand, air write)
- Extended time for dictation
- Allow verbal responses for longer words
- Simplify: Focus on digraphs + ar/or + 8 sight words only
