# Spelling and Phonics Unit: Advanced Phonics and Common Patterns

**Unit Duration:** 8 weeks × 3 lessons per week = 24 lessons
**Year Level:** Year 2
**Curriculum Link:** Australian Curriculum English v9 - Phonics and Word Knowledge
**AC9E2LY04** — Understand how to use knowledge of spelling to read and write multisyllabic words with regular patterns

**Writing Focus:** Advanced phonics — vowel digraphs (ai, ay, ea, ee, oa, ow, ou, ie, ue), diphthongs, common spelling patterns, prefix and suffix introduction, syllabification
**Cold Task:** Pre-assessment (Week 1, Lesson 1) - Spell words with vowel digraphs and advanced patterns
**Hot Task:** Post-assessment (Week 8, Lesson 1) - Same as Cold Task

## Unit Overview

**Learning Intention:**
We are learning to read and spell words with vowel digraphs (two vowels together making one sound), common spelling patterns, and simple prefixes and suffixes.

**Success Criteria:**
- I can read and spell words with vowel digraphs: ai/ay, ea/ee, oa/ow, ou/ie, ue.
- I can read and spell words with common patterns: -ight, -ould, -ough.
- I can break longer words into syllables to help reading and spelling.
- I can add prefixes (un-, re-, pre-) and suffixes (-ful, -ly, -ing, -ed) to change word meaning.
- I can use spelling rules to help me read and spell new words.

| Phase | Teacher | Students |

| Task (35 min) | Dictation: Teacher says 20 words. Students write them. Words: rain, day, head, feet, boat, saw, house, tie, blue, light, could, through, jump, fast, trap, spin, step, flag, flat, slip. Focus: What vowel patterns do students know? What needs teaching? | Assessment |

---

### Lesson 2: Vowel Digraphs Introduction (I Do)
**Time:** 35 minutes | **Type:** Explicit Teaching

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| I Do (10 min) | "In Year 1, we learned consonant digraphs — two consonants making one sound (sh, ch). NOW we learn VOWEL DIGRAPHS — two vowels together making ONE SOUND." Show: ai = /ai/ as in rain. "When a and i get together, they make the /ai/ sound." | Learn vowel digraphs |
| Mini-Lesson (15 min) | Vowel digraphs: ai (rain), ay (day), ea (head), ee (feet), oa (boat), ow (show), ou (house), ie (tie), ue (blue), ew (new). Each makes ONE sound, not two. | Note patterns |
| You Do (10 min) | Sort: 10 words into 5 vowel digraph categories. Circle the digraph in each word. | Sort |

**Success Criteria Check:** Can identify vowel digraphs in spoken words.

---

### Lesson 3: ai and ay (We Do)
**Time:** 35 minutes | **Type:** Guided

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| I Do (10 min) | "ai and ay both make the /ai/ sound. ai is usually in the MIDDLE of a word (rain, paint, train). ay is usually at the END (day, play, say)." | Learn ai/ay |
| We Do (15 min) | Sort: Words with ai vs ay. Write 5 ai words and 5 ay words. Read them aloud. | Sort |
| You Do (10 min) | Write sentences with ai and ay words: "It is fun to play in the rain." | Write |

## WEEK 2 — EA, EE, OA

| Phase | Teacher | Students |

## WEEK 5 — SYLLABIFICATION

### Lesson 1: Breaking Words into Syllables (I Do)
**Time:** 35 minutes | **Type:** Explicit Teaching

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| I Do (15 min) | "A SYLLABLE is a beat in a word. Every syllable has a vowel sound. robot = ro-bot (2 syllables). elephant = e-le-phant (3 syllables). To break words: Find the vowel. Count them. That's how many syllables." | Learn syllables |
| You Do (20 min) | Count syllables in: rabbit (2), carpet (2), window (2), butterfly (3), beautiful (3), amazing (3), strawberry (3), fantastic (4). Clap each one. | Count |

---

| You Do (10 min) | Break into syllables, then write: remember, September, November, December, different, important, dangerous, delicious. | Write |

## WEEK 6 — PREFIXES AND SUFFIXES

**Time:** 35 minutes | **Type:** Explicit Teaching

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| I Do (10 min) | "A PREFIX is letters added to the START of a word to change its meaning. un- = not (happy → unhappy). re- = again (do → redo). pre- = before (view → preview)." | Learn prefixes |
| Mini-Lesson (10 min) | Show: un-happy, re-do, pre-view, un-friendly, re-play, pre-heat. What does each prefix mean? | Show |
| You Do (15 min) | Add prefixes: happy → ? / do → ? / write → ? / fill → ? / view → ? / pack → ? / connect → ? / appear → ? | Add prefixes |

**Time:** 35 minutes | **Type:** Guided

|-------|---------|---------|

## WEEK 7 — MORE SUFFIXES AND APPLICATION

### Lesson 1: -ing, -ed, -er (I Do)
**Time:** 35 minutes | **Type:** Explicit Teaching

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| I Do (15 min) | "-ing = ongoing (walk → walking). -ed = past (walk → walked). -er = person who (teach → teacher). Note: When adding -ing or -ed to words ending in e, drop the e: make → making, love → loved. But: hike → hiked (drop e, add ed)." | Learn suffixes |
| You Do (20 min) | Add -ing, -ed, -er to: dance, jump, paint, write, read, speak, learn, teach. Write each new word. | Add suffixes |

## ADAPTATIONS

### EAL Students
- Pre-teach vowel digraph sounds with mouth position videos
- Visual chart of all patterns with example words
- Highlight tricky patterns that differ from home language
- Extra syllable counting practice
- Home language word comparison

### Gifted Students
- Introduce silent letters (kn, wr, gn) and when they appear
- Research etymology — where do these patterns come from?
- Create a spelling rules poster for the classroom
- Challenge with irregular vowel patterns (a_e, i_e, o_e, u_e)

### Students with Additional Needs
- Focus on 3 digraphs at a time before expanding
- Use multi-sensory approach (write in sand, say sounds)
- Provide word bank for all activities
- Reduce to 2-syllable words for syllabification
- Extra time
- One-on-one support
