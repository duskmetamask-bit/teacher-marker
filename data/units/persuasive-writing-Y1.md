# Persuasive Writing Unit: We Want Our Books

**Unit Duration:** 8 weeks × 3 lessons per week = 24 lessons
**Year Level:** Year 1
**Curriculum Link:** Australian Curriculum English v9 - Persuasive Writing
**Mentor Text:** *We Want Our Books* by Sophie Giles and An撕er (2023)
**Writing Focus:** Persuasive writing — making your opinion known, speaking up for what matters
**Cold Task:** Pre-assessment (Week 1, Lesson 1) - Tell someone why they should listen to you about something important
**Hot Task:** Post-assessment (Week 8, Lesson 1) - Same as Cold Task

## Unit Overview

**Learning Intention:**
We are learning to tell people what we think and why they should agree with us, using simple reasons and pictures.

**Success Criteria:**
- I can say what I think about something.
- I can give ONE reason why someone should agree with me.
- I can draw a picture that shows what I am arguing for.
- I can say my idea clearly so people understand me.

## WEEK 1 — WHAT DO YOU THINK?

### Lesson 1: Cold Task (Assessment)
**Time:** 30 minutes | **Type:** Assessment

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Hook (5 min) | "Think of something you really want. It could be more play time, a pet, a special food, or something else. Can you tell someone WHY they should agree with you?" | Think |
| Task (25 min) | Cold Task: Draw a picture of something you want. Write or say WHY someone should agree with you. Year 1: Speaking or drawing, simple words. | Draw, think, share |

**Differentiation:** Sentence starters on board. Visual supports for students who need them.

---

### Lesson 2: We Want Our Books! (I Do)
**Time:** 30 minutes | **Type:** Explicit Teaching

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| Read (15 min) | Read *We Want Our Books* aloud — this book is literally children arguing for what they want (books). It is perfect for Year 1 persuasion. The children say what they want and why. Simple, direct, funny. | Listen and engage |
| Discuss (10 min) | "What did the children in the book want? Did they give a reason? Was it a good reason?" The book shows: Say what you want + Give a reason = Good persuasion | Analyze structure |
| Share (5 min) | "What do YOU want? What is YOUR reason?" | Personal connection |

**Success Criteria Check:** Can identify "I want" and "because" in the book.

---

### Lesson 3: Having an Opinion (We Do)
**Time:** 30 minutes | **Type:** Guided

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| I Do (10 min) | "An opinion is what YOU think. Everyone has opinions. Your opinion is important." Show examples: "I think ice cream is yummy." "I think dogs are better than cats." These are opinions. | Learn concept |
| We Do (10 min) | Class makes an OPINION CHAIN — each student says "I think ____." Go around the room. Everyone says something different. That is okay — opinions are personal. | Participate |
| You Do (10 min) | Think: What is YOUR opinion? Write it on a sticky note: "I think ________________________." | Write opinion |

---

## WEEK 2 — REASONS

### Lesson 1: Why Do You Think That? (I Do)
**Time:** 30 minutes | **Type:** Explicit Teaching

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| Read (10 min) | Re-read *We Want Our Books*. "The children say WHY they want books. What reasons do they give?" They say things like: "Books help us learn" / "Books are fun" / "We want to visit new worlds." | Identify reasons |
| Mini-Lesson (10 min) | A REASON tells WHY someone should agree with you. Good reasons are TRUE (they actually make sense) and CLEAR (people understand them). | Understand reasons |
| You Do (10 min) | Think: What is YOUR reason? Write: "I think __________________ because __________________." | Construct reason |

---

### Lesson 2: Good Reasons (We Do)
**Time:** 30 minutes | **Type:** Guided

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| I Do (10 min) | Show a good reason vs a not-so-good reason. GOOD: "I want a dog because dogs are loyal and make great friends." NOT-SO-GOOD: "I want a dog because my friend has a dog." Both are reasons but one is more convincing. | Compare |
| We Do (10 min) | Class rates 3 reasons: Which is the BEST? Which is the SECOND BEST? Which needs to be stronger? | Rate reasons |
| You Do (10 min) | Write your reason again. Make it the BEST it can be. | Strengthen reason |

---

### Lesson 3: Drawing Your Reason (You Do)
**Time:** 30 minutes | **Type:** Art + Writing

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| You Do (25 min) | Draw a picture that shows your opinion AND your reason. If you want a dog because they are loyal — draw a dog looking loyal. If you want more books because books are fun — draw someone laughing while reading. | Illustrate argument |
| Share (5 min) | Show your drawing to a partner. Can they tell what you want and why? | Peer feedback |

---

## WEEK 3 — WRITING YOUR ARGUMENT

### Lesson 1: I Think... (I Do → You Do)
**Time:** 30 minutes | **Type:** Writing

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| I Do (10 min) | Model writing: "I think we should have more books in our classroom because books help us learn and they are fun." Sound out each word. Put finger spaces between words. | Observe writing |
| You Do (20 min) | Write: "I think __________________________________________________" on your paper. | Write opening |

---

### Lesson 2: Because... (We Do)
**Time:** 30 minutes | **Type:** Writing

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| I Do (10 min) | "Now we add the REASON. This is the most important part." Add: "because __________________________________________________." Model completing the sentence. | Learn structure |
| We Do (10 min) | Together, complete 3 examples on the board. Students call out reasons, teacher writes. | Practice together |
| You Do (10 min) | Write your full argument: "I think [WHAT YOU WANT] because [YOUR REASON]." | Write complete argument |

---

### Lesson 3: Putting It Together (You Do)
**Time:** 30 minutes | **Type:** Writing

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| You Do (25 min) | Write your argument neatly. Draw a picture to go with it. Check: Does it make sense? Did you use finger spaces? | Write and illustrate |
| Share (5 min) | 2–3 students share their writing | Share |

---

## WEEK 4 — MAKING POSTERS

### Lesson 1: Big Letters (I Do)
**Time:** 30 minutes | **Type:** Art

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| I Do (10 min) | "A poster needs big letters so people can read it from far away." Show how to write each letter big and clear. Use upper case letters. | Learn poster style |
| You Do (20 min) | Write the MAIN MESSAGE of your argument in BIG LETTERS on poster paper. "I WANT MORE BOOKS IN OUR CLASS" or "DOGS ARE THE BEST PETS." | Create headline |

---

### Lesson 2: Good Pictures (We Do)
**Time:** 30 minutes | **Type:** Art

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| I Do (10 min) | "The picture should show what you want and why it is good." Show examples of persuasive pictures — bright, clear, simple. | Look at examples |
| We Do (10 min) | Plan your picture: What will you draw? What colours will you use? Draw a small version first to plan. | Plan illustration |
| You Do (10 min) | Draw your picture on your poster. | Draw |

---

### Lesson 3: Planning Your Poster (You Do)
**Time:** 30 minutes | **Type:** Planning

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| You Do (25 min) | Plan your final poster: TOP — Big headline. MIDDLE — Your reason written clearly. BOTTOM — Big, bright picture. | Plan layout |
| Share (5 min) | Show your plan to a partner. Does it make sense? | Feedback |

## WEEK 5 — PRESENTING YOUR IDEAS

### Lesson 1: Standing Up and Speaking (I Do)
**Time:** 30 minutes | **Type:** Oral

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| I Do (10 min) | "When we persuade someone, we can SPEAK to them. Speaking is powerful — you can use your voice to sound confident." Model: Say "I want more books" in a boring voice. Then say it in a confident, excited voice. Which one made you want to agree? | Learn voice |
| Mini-Lesson (10 min) | How to speak confidently: Look at people / Speak clearly / Stand up straight / Use your hands to show importance | Note techniques |
| You Do (10 min) | Practice: Say your argument out loud 3 times. Can you say it without looking at your paper? | Practice |

---

### Lesson 2: Practice Runs (We Do)
**Time:** 30 minutes | **Type:** Guided

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| We Do (15 min) | Practice in pairs: Student A tells their argument. Student B listens, then says: "I agree because..." Did the argument work? | Practice pairs |
| You Do (15 min) | Switch roles. Try again. Did you improve? | Practice again |

---

### Lesson 3: Presenting to the Class (You Do)
**Time:** 30 minutes | **Type:** Presentation

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| You Do (25 min) | Present your argument to the class. Show your poster. Say your argument out loud. Class claps when you finish. | Present |
| Feedback (5 min) | After each presentation, class says one thing the presenter did well | Celebrate |

---

## WEEK 6 — MORE REASONS

### Lesson 1: Adding More Reasons (I Do)
**Time:** 30 minutes | **Type:** Explicit Teaching

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| Read (10 min) | "Sometimes one reason is not enough. We can add MORE reasons to make our argument stronger." Read *We Want Our Books* again — do the children give one reason or many? Many! Books are fun, books help us learn, books take us places. | Analyze |
| Mini-Lesson (10 min) | "One reason is good. TWO reasons are BETTER. Three reasons are STRONGEST." | Understand |
| You Do (10 min) | Add a second reason to your argument. "I think [WHAT] because [REASON 1]. Also, [REASON 2]." | Add second reason |

---

### Lesson 2: Pick the Best Reason (We Do)
**Time:** 30 minutes | **Type:** Guided

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| We Do (20 min) | Each student writes 3 possible reasons for their argument. Then, with a partner, pick the TWO best ones. Cross out the weakest. | Select best reasons |
| You Do (10 min) | Write your two best reasons on your poster. | Update poster |

---

### Lesson 3: Polishing Your Work (You Do)
**Time:** 30 minutes | **Type:** Editing

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| You Do (25 min) | Re-read your poster. Check: Is it neat? Are there finger spaces? Are the letters big enough? Can someone read it from across the room? Fix anything that needs fixing. | Edit |
| Share (5 min) | Share with partner — partner reads it out loud. Can they read every word? | Feedback |

## RUBRIC — Persuasive Writing (Year 1)
**AC9E1LY03** — Understand that people can express their opinion and put their viewpoint in a text

| Criterion | A (Excellent) | B (Strong) | C (Satisfactory) | D (Developing) | E (Beginning) |
|-----------|---------------|------------|-----------------|-----------------|---------------|
| **Opinion** | Clear, strong opinion about something that matters to the writer. | Clear opinion. Writer knows what they want. | Opinion present but may be vague. | Attempt at opinion. Not clear what writer wants. | No clear opinion. |
| **Reason** | Gives a good, clear reason why someone should agree. Reason makes sense and connects to opinion. | Gives a reason. Reason connects to opinion. | Gives a reason but may not be very strong or may not fully connect. | Reason unclear or doesn't connect. | No reason given. |
| **Presentation** | Poster is attractive, clear, readable from a distance. Picture matches argument. | Poster is clear and attractive. Picture fits. | Poster is adequate. Some parts unclear. | Poster is messy or hard to read. | No poster or unreadable. |
| **Speaking** | Speaks confidently, clearly, with enthusiasm. Makes eye contact. | Speaks clearly. Some confidence. | Speaks but may be quiet or nervous. | Hard to hear. Not confident. | Cannot present. |

## ADAPTATIONS

### EAL Students
- Allow home language to develop initial opinion
- Visual sentence starters on desk
- Allow drawing only for initial drafts
- Partner practice before class presentation
- Pre-teach vocabulary: opinion, reason, agree, because

### Gifted Students
- Add a third reason
- Write a short letter to a real audience (e.g., principal about something real)
- Create a class petition based on one argument
- Research real examples of children persuading adults

### Students with Additional Needs
- Reduce to one strong reason
- Provide pre-drawn poster template
- Allow scribe for writing
- One-on-one practice before presenting
- Extra time for all tasks
- Allow presentation to teacher only if class is too stressful

## ASSESSMENT NOTES

### Formative Assessment (Ongoing)
- Opinion sticky notes
- Reason sentences
- Draft posters
- Practice presentations
- Peer feedback

### Summative Assessment
- Cold Task → Hot Task comparison (same rubric, measure growth)
- Final poster assessed with rubric
- Presentation observation

### Tracking Progress
Use the comparison template to document:
1. Cold Task score → Hot Task score
2. Number and quality of reasons
3. Presentation confidence
4. Next persuasive writing goal
