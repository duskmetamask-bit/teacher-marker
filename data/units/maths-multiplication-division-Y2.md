## LESSON 1.2 — The ×3 Table

**Duration:** 50 minutes
**Type:** Concept Introduction

---

### Learning Intentions
- I am learning the 3× multiplication table
- I am learning that 3× connects to doubles: 3×4 = 2×4 + 1×4

### Success Criteria
- I can recall 3×1 through 3×10

---

### I DO (15 min)

> "3× table: skip-count by 3: 3, 6, 9, 12, 15, 18, 21, 24, 27, 30."

> "3×4 = 12. Double 4 is 8. Plus one more 4 = 12."

> "Pattern: each answer is 3 more than the one before."

---

### YOU DO (15 min)

1. Complete ×3: 1×3=___, 2×3=___, 3×3=___, 4×3=___, 5×3=___, 6×3=___, 7×3=___, 8×3=___, 9×3=___, 10×3=___
2. 5×3=___, 7×3=___, 8×3=___, 9×3=___
3. EXTENSION: "What is the 10th number in the 3× table? How do you know?"

---

## LESSON 1.3 — ×3 Patterns

**Duration:** 50 minutes
**Type:** Guided Practice

---

### YOU DO (30 min)

1. "What comes after 18 in the 3× table?"
2. "Is 17 in the 3× table? How do you know?"
3. "If 3×7 = 21, what is 3×8? How do you know without counting?"
4. EXTENSION: "Prove that every 3× answer has digits that add to 3, 6, or 9 (digital root)."

# WEEK 4 — EXPLORE

**Focus:** Division with remainders

---

## LESSON 4.1 — Division with Remainders

**Duration:** 50 minutes
**Type:** Explain

---

### Learning Intentions
- I am learning that division doesn't always work out evenly
- I am learning what a remainder means

### Success Criteria
- I can solve 17 ÷ 3 = 5 remainder 2

---

### I DO (15 min)

> "17 ÷ 3. Can we share 17 between 3 equally?"

> "3×5 = 15. Share 5 each. 2 left over. That's the REMAINDER."

> "17 ÷ 3 = 5 remainder 2."

> "Check: 3×5 + 2 = 17. ✓"

---

### YOU DO (15 min)

1. 14 ÷ 3 = ___ r ___   (3×4=12, remainder 2)
2. 19 ÷ 4 = ___ r ___
3. 23 ÷ 5 = ___ r ___
4. EXTENSION: "What is the largest remainder when dividing by 4?"

---

## LESSON 4.2 — Interpreting Remainders

**Duration:** 50 minutes
**Type:** Guided Practice

---

### I DO (12 min)

> "Remainder in context: 17 cookies shared between 3. 5 each, 2 left over."

> "What happens to the 2? Either: give to teacher, save for later, or cut in half!"

> "Context tells us what the remainder means."

---

### YOU DO (15 min)

1. "23 children go to camp in groups of 4. How many full groups? ___ How many left over? ___"
2. "19 apples packed into bags of 5. How many bags? ___ Any left? ___"
3. "If I have 25 and divide by 6, what is the remainder?"
4. EXTENSION: "How many 3s in 20? What is the remainder?"
