# Informative Writing Unit: Emus

**Unit Duration:** 8 weeks × 3 lessons per week = 24 lessons
**Year Level:** Year 2
**Curriculum Link:** Australian Curriculum English v9 - Informative Writing
**Mentor Text:** *Emus* by Graeme Base (1998)
**Writing Focus:** Informative writing — Australian animals, using descriptive language, diagrams with labels, paragraphs
**Cold Task:** Pre-assessment (Week 1, Lesson 1) - Write an informative text about an Australian animal with facts and a picture
**Hot Task:** Post-assessment (Week 8, Lesson 1) - Same as Cold Task

## Unit Overview

**Learning Intention:**
We are learning to write an informative text about an Australian animal that teaches readers facts using clear sentences, descriptive language, and a labelled diagram.

**Success Criteria:**
- I can write an informative text with a clear topic sentence, facts, and a closing sentence.
- I can include 4–5 facts about my animal.
- I can use descriptive words (adjectives) to make facts interesting.
- I can draw and label a diagram of my animal.
- I can write in paragraphs.

## WEEK 1 — INTRODUCTION TO INFORMATIVE TEXTS

### Lesson 1: Cold Task (Assessment)
**Time:** 35 minutes | **Type:** Assessment

| Phase | Teacher (I Do) | Students |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Hook (5 min) | "Australia has some of the most amazing animals in the world! Today you will write an informative text about one Australian animal — teaching readers facts they might not know." | Think |
| Task (30 min) | Cold Task: Write an informative text about an Australian animal. Include: A title, facts, a picture, labels. Year 2: 60–100 words. | Independent writing |

**Differentiation:** Australian animal fact sheets available. Sentence starters.

---

### Lesson 2: Emus by Graeme Base (I Do)
**Time:** 35 minutes | **Type:** Explicit Teaching

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| Read (15 min) | Read *Emus* by Graeme Base — this picture book is packed with FACTS about emus, presented in an engaging, accessible format for Year 2. Base's illustrations are stunning and the facts are accurate and interesting. | Listen and analyze |
| Discuss (15 min) | "What facts did you learn about emus?" Discuss: Size, diet, nesting, speed, unique features. "What makes Base's text interesting to read?" The language — he uses descriptive words and specific details. | Analyze craft |
| Share (5 min) | "What is the difference between a FACT and an OPINION?" | Review |

**Success Criteria Check:** Can identify at least 4 facts from *Emus* and explain why they are facts.

---

### Lesson 3: Fact-Finding (We Do)
**Time:** 35 minutes | **Type:** Guided

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| We Do (15 min) | From *Emus*, list the key facts on the board: Emus are the second tallest bird / They can't fly / They can run up to 50km/h / They eat plants and insects / The male cares for the eggs. "What makes these good informative facts?" They are specific, true, and teach you something new. | Analyze facts |
| You Do (20 min) | Think of 3 Australian animals you could write about. Write their names and 1 fact about each. You will choose one in Week 2. | Brainstorm |

## WEEK 2 — AUSTRALIAN ANIMALS

### Lesson 1: Our Unique Wildlife (I Do)
**Time:** 35 minutes | **Type:** Explicit Teaching

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| I Do (10 min) | "Australia has animals that live nowhere else on Earth. Kangaroos, koalas, platypuses, wombats, echidnas, emus, cockatoos, goannas — these are found mainly or only in Australia. When we write about Australian animals, we are sharing something special about our home." | Understand |
| Mini-Lesson (10 min) | "To be an informative writer, you need to be an expert. Experts RESEARCH — they find facts from books, websites, and other reliable sources." | Note |
| You Do (15 min) | Research: Look at Australian animal books or websites. Find 3 facts about an animal that you did not know before. Write them in your fact file. | Research |

---

### Lesson 2: Choosing Our Animal (We Do)
**Time:** 35 minutes | **Type:** Guided

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| We Do (15 min) | Make a class list of Australian animals. Vote on the most interesting ones. Discuss: What makes an animal interesting to write about? | List and discuss |
| You Do (20 min) | Choose ONE Australian animal that you will research for the rest of this unit. Write: Animal name / Why you chose it / 3 things you already know about it. | Choose animal |

---

### Lesson 3: Researching (You Do)
**Time:** 35 minutes | **Type:** Independent

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| You Do (30 min) | Use books, websites, or information sheets to research your animal. Find: APPEARANCE (what it looks like) / HOME (where it lives in Australia) / FOOD (what it eats) / YOUNG (how it has babies) / FACTS (any other interesting things). Record in your research notes. | Research |
| Share (5 min) | Share one surprising fact you found | Share |

## WEEK 3 — GATHERING AND ORGANISING FACTS

### Lesson 1: Fact Circles (I Do)
**Time:** 35 minutes | **Type:** Explicit Teaching

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| I Do (10 min) | "We have lots of facts. Now we need to ORGANISE them. Fact circles: Each circle is a DIFFERENT type of fact." Show categories: APPEARANCE / HABITAT / DIET / BEHAVIOUR / YOUNG / UNIQUE FEATURE. | Categorize |
| You Do (25 min) | Sort your facts into the 5 categories. Write at least one fact in each circle. If a circle is empty, research more. | Sort facts |

---

### Lesson 2: Organising Facts (We Do)
**Time:** 35 minutes | **Type:** Guided

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| We Do (15 min) | "We have lots of facts. We can't use them ALL — we choose the BEST facts." Class discussion: What makes a fact the BEST? It's INTERESTING (not boring) / It's UNIQUE (not something everyone knows) / It's ACCURATE (true). | Discuss |
| You Do (20 min) | Choose your 4 BEST facts — one from each of 4 different circles. Number them 1–4 in the order you will write about them. | Select best facts |

---

### Lesson 3: The Topic Sentence (I Do → You Do)
**Time:** 35 minutes | **Type:** Writing

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| I Do (10 min) | "The topic sentence tells the reader what the whole text is about. It should be INTERESTING — make the reader want to read more." Model: "Koalas are cute marsupials that live in Australian trees." vs "Koalas are animals." Which makes you want to read more? | Note |
| You Do (25 min) | Write the topic sentence for your informative text. Try 2 versions. Choose the one that sounds most interesting. Write it at the top of your planning sheet. | Write topic sentence |

## WEEK 4 — DESCRIPTIVE LANGUAGE

### Lesson 1: Adding Adjectives (I Do)
**Time:** 35 minutes | **Type:** Explicit Teaching

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| I Do (10 min) | "Adjectives are describing words. They make our facts more interesting." From *Emus*: "Emus have long, powerful legs." Long and powerful are adjectives. "They live in Australia's scrubland and eucalypt forests." Scrubland and eucalypt are also specific. | Analyze |
| You Do (25 min) | Choose 3 of your facts. Rewrite each one, adding at least one adjective. Example: "Kangaroos hop" → "Large, muscular kangaroos hop powerfully across the Australian outback." | Add adjectives |

---

### Lesson 2: Making Facts Interesting (We Do)
**Time:** 35 minutes | **Type:** Guided

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| I Do (10 min) | "A boring fact vs an interesting fact." BORING: "Emus are tall." INTERESTING: "Emus stand almost two metres tall — as tall as a human adult." The interesting version gives a comparison that helps the reader understand. | Compare |
| We Do (15 min) | Practice: For each of your 4 facts, add a comparison or extra detail that makes it more interesting. Share with class. | Practice |
| You Do (10 min) | Rewrite your 4 facts with comparisons and extra details. | Rewrite facts |

---

### Lesson 3: Drafting (You Do)
**Time:** 35 minutes | **Type:** Writing

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| You Do (30 min) | Write the full draft of your informative text: Title → Topic sentence → 4 facts with descriptive language → Closing sentence. | Write draft |
| Share (5 min) | Share one of your most descriptive sentences | Share |

## WEEK 5 — DIAGRAMS AND LABELS

### Lesson 1: Scientific Diagrams (I Do)
**Time:** 35 minutes | **Type:** Explicit Teaching

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| I Do (10 min) | "Informative texts often have DIAGRAMS — drawings that show the different parts of something. Diagrams help readers understand what something looks like." Show examples: Animal diagrams with labelled parts / Plant diagrams / Body part diagrams. | Learn diagrams |
| You Do (25 min) | Draw a large picture of your animal. Look at real photos to make it accurate. Draw the main body parts clearly. | Draw diagram |

---

### Lesson 2: Writing Labels (We Do)
**Time:** 35 minutes | **Type:** Guided

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| I Do (10 min) | "Labels point to a specific part and tell the reader what it is. Labels are SHORT — just the name of the part." Show: Label lines point to the part, label written clearly at end of line. | Learn labels |
| We Do (15 min) | Label the class animal diagram together. Discuss: Where do the lines go? What do we call each part? | Label together |
| You Do (10 min) | Add labels to your own diagram: Point to at least 5 parts of your animal. Write the name of each part. | Label diagram |

---

### Lesson 3: Adding Captions (You Do)
**Time:** 35 minutes | **Type:** Writing

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| I Do (5 min) | "A caption is a sentence under a picture that tells the reader something important about it. Captions add EXTRA information — a fact that is shown in the picture." | Learn caption |
| You Do (30 min) | Write a caption for your diagram. It should be one sentence that adds an interesting fact. Example: "The emu's strong legs allow it to run at speeds of up to 50 kilometres per hour." | Write caption |

## WEEK 6 — PARAGRAPHS AND STRUCTURE

### Lesson 1: One Fact Per Paragraph (I Do)
**Time:** 35 minutes | **Type:** Explicit Teaching

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| I Do (10 min) | "In Year 2, we start writing in PARAGRAPHS. A paragraph is a group of sentences about ONE thing. Each fact gets its own paragraph." Show: Paragraph 1 = Topic sentence. Paragraph 2 = Fact 1. Paragraph 3 = Fact 2. Paragraph 4 = Fact 3. Paragraph 5 = Fact 4. Paragraph 6 = Closing. | Learn paragraphs |
| You Do (25 min) | Re-write your informative text with PARAGRAPHS. One fact per paragraph. Leave a space between each paragraph. Number your paragraphs to check: 6 paragraphs total? | Format with paragraphs |

---

### Lesson 2: Structure Check (We Do)
**Time:** 35 minutes | **Type:** Guided

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| We Do (20 min) | Check as a class: Title → Topic sentence (Paragraph 1) → 4 fact paragraphs (2–5) → Closing sentence (Paragraph 6) = 6 paragraphs total. Does everyone have 6? | Check structure |
| You Do (15 min) | Check your own text. Do you have a title? Topic sentence? 4 facts? Closing sentence? Fix anything missing. | Self-check |

---

### Lesson 3: Peer Feedback (We Do)
**Time:** 35 minutes | **Type:** Peer Feedback

| Phase | Teacher | Students |
|-------|---------|---------|
| We Do (35 min) | Swap texts with a partner. Partner reads and checks: Title? Topic sentence? 4 facts in 4 paragraphs? Closing sentence? Adjectives in facts? Labels and caption on diagram? Write: 2 stars and 1 wish. | Peer check |

## RUBRIC — Informative Writing (Year 2)
**AC9E2LY02** — Create short informative texts using facts and words from own knowledge

| Criterion | A (Excellent) | B (Strong) | C (Satisfactory) | D (Developing) | E (Beginning) |
|-----------|---------------|------------|-----------------|-----------------|---------------|
| **Facts** | 4+ accurate, well-chosen facts. Each fact is specific, interesting and teaches the reader something new. Facts are from multiple categories. | 4 accurate facts. Most are specific and interesting. | 3–4 facts present. Some may be basic or less interesting. | 2–3 facts. Some may be vague. | No real facts. |
| **Descriptive Language** | Consistent use of adjectives and descriptive language. Facts are vivid and engaging. Comparisons help reader understand. | Good use of adjectives. Most facts have descriptive elements. | Some descriptive words. Facts are adequate but could be more vivid. | Limited descriptive language. Facts are basic. | No descriptive words. |
| **Structure** | Clear paragraphs. Topic sentence, 4 facts, closing sentence. Logical order. Flow is smooth. | Paragraphs mostly clear. Structure present. | Structure present but paragraphs may not be clear or facts may not be in best order. | Attempt at structure but unclear. | No clear structure. |
| **Diagram & Labels** | Accurate, detailed diagram with 5+ clear labels and an informative caption. Adds significant value. | Good diagram with 4+ labels and caption. | Diagram with 2–3 labels. Caption present. | Diagram present but labels/caption minimal or unclear. | No diagram or labels. |
| **Punctuation & Grammar** | Consistent capitals and full stops. Correct spelling. Sentences clear and grammatically correct. | Minor errors. Generally clear. | Some errors. Meaning mostly clear. | Noticeable errors affect clarity. | Significant errors. |

## ADAPTATIONS

### EAL Students
- Allow home language for initial research
- Provide bilingual animal fact sheets
- Sentence starters for topic sentence and closing
- Visual word bank of descriptive words
- Allow oral presentation with scribe

### Gifted Students
- Choose an Australian animal with more complex facts
- Add a comparison section: "How is X different from Y?"
- Include habitat map or range information
- Write about the animal's role in the ecosystem

### Students with Additional Needs
- Provide pre-selected animal with fact sheet
- Reduce to 3 facts in 3 paragraphs
- Provide diagram to label
- Allow scribe or typing
- Extra time
- One-on-one support

## ASSESSMENT NOTES

### Formative Assessment (Ongoing)
- Fact circles and research notes
- Draft writing
- Paragraph structure check
- Peer feedback
- Teacher conferences (Weeks 3, 5, 6)

### Summative Assessment
- Cold Task → Hot Task comparison (same rubric, measure growth)
- Published text assessed with rubric
- Diagram quality assessed

### Tracking Progress
Document: Cold → Hot scores, fact quality, descriptive language use, paragraph structure.
