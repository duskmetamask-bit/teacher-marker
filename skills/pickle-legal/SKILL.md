## MANDATORY REPORTING (WA)

### The Law

**Children and Community Services Act 2004 (WA)** — the primary mandatory reporting law in Western Australia.

### Who Must Report

Mandatory reporters include:
- Teachers and school administrators
- Nurses and doctors
- Police
- Social workers
- Family day care educators

*If you're a teacher in WA, you're a mandatory reporter. Full stop.*

### What Must Be Reported

Three categories only:
1. **Sexual offence** against a child
2. **Serious physical harm** to a child
3. **Sexual exploitation** of a child

Not every concern — only these three. A child being hungry or neglected without immediate physical/sexual harm may be a child protection concern but is NOT a mandatory report category unless it meets the threshold.

### The Threshold

**"Believes on reasonable grounds"** — not certainty, not a suspicion that dances around the edges.

You do NOT need to be sure. You do NOT need to have proof. You need a reasonable belief based on what you've seen, heard, or been told.

> ⚠️ Common mistake: Waiting until you're certain. By the time you're certain, you've often waited too long.

### The Reporting Process

1. **Form the belief** — something you've observed or been told gives you reasonable grounds
2. **Report to your principal** — as soon as practicable (immediately is best)
3. **Report to CP Hub** — Department of Communities, Child Protection Hub: **1800 277 066**
4. **Document** — date, time, what you observed, what you were told, who was present
5. **Follow up** — if no response within 24 hours, follow up again

### Key Distinctions

| Myth | Reality |
|------|---------|
| "I need parent's consent to report" | No. You do NOT need parental knowledge or consent. |
| "I need to investigate first" | No. You report, CP investigates. Your job is to report, not to solve it. |
| "If I report wrongly I'll get in trouble" | If you report in good faith, you're protected. |
| "It's only mandatory reporting if it's certain" | Belief on reasonable grounds is enough. |

### Documentation Template

When you make a report, log:
```
Date: 
Time: 
Student: (code/name, not full name if sensitive)
Year level: 
What I observed / what was disclosed: 
Who was present: 
What I said/did immediately: 
Reported to (principal name): 
Reported to CP Hub (time of call): 
CP Hub reference number: 
Follow-up required: 
```

## DUTY OF CARE

### What It Means

Teachers have a **legal duty to take reasonable care** of students. This isn't a policy — it's law. It applies:
- During school hours
- During any school-organised activity (excursions, camps, sport)
- On school grounds and off

It does NOT only apply during class time.

### What Reasonable Care Looks Like

**Supervision:**
- Adequate supervision ratios by year level (check your school's guidance)
- Primary (F–2): closer, more constant
- Upper primary (3–6): more independent but still within sight/hearing
- Never leave a class unattended (even for "just a minute")
- Know where your students are

**Safe environment:**
- Check equipment before use (PE, science, D&T)
- Report hazards immediately
- Ensure classroom layout is not a risk
- Outdoor activities: risk assess first

**Appropriate instruction:**
- In PE: warm up, correct technique, don't push beyond ability
- In science/D&T: demonstrate equipment, supervise tool use
- In any activity: students must know the risks and how to be safe

**Medical emergencies:**
- Provide first aid (implied consent exists for emergency first aid)
- Call an ambulance if needed — don't wait
- Know your school's first aid and anaphylaxis plans
- Know student medical plans

**Informed consent:**
- Parents must be told of risks before high-risk activities
- Not telling them = you didn't have consent = potential liability

### Negligence

If you fail to take reasonable care, and a student is harmed, you may be personally liable. The school/employer can also be vicariously liable.

**Your best protection:**
- Document everything
- Follow school policy
- Act on concerns quickly
- Don't leave students unsupervised

## SUSPENSION AND EXCLUSION (WA)

### Suspension

- **Who can suspend:** The principal
- **Duration:** Up to 4 weeks
- **Written notice required:** Yes — must notify parents in writing with reasons
- **Work must be provided:** Yes — the student continues to receive school work during suspension
- **Appeals:** To the Regional Executive Director

### Exclusion

- **Who can exclude:** Regional Executive Director (not the principal)
- **Duration:** Up to 6 months
- **Threshold:** More serious — behaviour that threatens safety of others
- **Alternative education:** Must be offered an alternative setting

### In-School Suspension

- Not specifically in WA legislation — but schools use it
- Must still provide work
- Cannot be unlimited duration (unreasonable = procedural fairness issue)
- Cannot deny lunch breaks or bathroom access

### Key Rule

**Suspension and exclusion are NOT expulsion.**

Expulsion is rare and involves a formal panel process. Most behaviour issues result in suspension, not expulsion.

## STUDENT WELFARE AND WELLBEING

### Teacher Obligations

- **Identify** students at risk (signs of abuse, self-harm, mental health crisis)
- **Respond** — take reasonable steps, don't ignore
- **Document** — date, time, what you observed, what you did
- **Refer** — school counsellor, psychologist, support services

### Signs to Watch For

- Unexplained injuries
- Significant behaviour changes
- Withdrawal or suddenly flat
- Self-harm or talk of suicide
- Disclosure of abuse (listen, don't interrogate)
- Student tells you something in confidence — you cannot promise confidentiality if it involves safety

### Critical Incidents

1. **Document immediately** — date, time, who was present, what happened, what you did
2. **Notify principal immediately**
3. **Complete Critical Incident Report (CIR)** — submit to WA Department of Education
4. **Follow up** — ensure the student is supported

## PRIVACY AND INFORMATION SHARING

### When You CAN Share Without Consent

Teachers can share student information WITHOUT consent when:
- Required by law (mandatory reporting, court orders)
- Necessary to lessen or prevent a serious threat to life, health, or safety
- Suspected abuse or neglect (mandatory reporting context)

### What You CANNOT Share Without Consent

- Student addresses or phone numbers to third parties
- Student photos (note: schools typically have blanket consent for general school photos — check your school's policy)
- Medical information to other parents
- Detailed personal information to other teachers outside of need-to-know

### Privacy in Practice

- Lock student files — don't leave them on your desk
- Don't discuss students in public spaces (staffroom, hallways, playground)
- Use school-approved communication channels for parent contact
- Password-protect digital records
- If in doubt, ask your principal before sharing

## DISCRIMINATION AND EQUAL OPPORTUNITY

### The Law

**Equal Opportunity Act 1984 (WA)** — makes it unlawful to discriminate based on protected attributes.

**Protected attributes:** race, sex, disability, age, sexuality, gender history, family status, etc.

### Students With Disability

- Must NOT discriminate in admission, provision of education, or assessment
- Must make **reasonable adjustments** (this is the NCCD framework)
- Refusing to admit a student with disability = potential discrimination
- Failing to provide adjustments = discrimination

### In the Classroom

| Practice | Is it discrimination? |
|----------|---------------------|
| Differentiated assessment | No — it is compliance with legal obligations |
| Extra time for students with adjustments | No — it is providing reasonable adjustments |
| Modifying content for students with disability | No — it is meeting your legal duties |
| Allowing rest breaks for students who need them | No — it is reasonable adjustment |

Giving students with adjustments the same quality of education as their peers is NOT giving them an advantage. It is meeting your legal obligations.

## INFORMED CONSENT

### What Requires Consent

- **Excursions** — written consent required, especially for high-risk activities
- **Medical treatment beyond first aid** — documented consent
- **Photos/publications** — individual student photos need consent (check school has blanket consent for general use)
- **Religious instruction** — must opt IN in WA (opt-out model was struck down)

### Consent Must Be

- **Informed** — parent knows exactly what they're consenting to (destination, activities, risks)
- **Voluntary** — not pressured or coerced
- **Specific** — cannot be blanket consent for everything. Consent for excursion does not equal consent for everything all year.

### First Aid

Implied consent exists for emergency first aid — you don't need a signed form to save a child's life. But for ongoing medical treatment or medication administration, documented consent is required.

## WA DEPARTMENT OF EDUCATION POLICIES

Key policies you must know (check WA DOE intranet for current versions):

1. **Student Behaviour Policy (2023)** — your authority for behaviour management, suspension, exclusion
2. **Restrictive Practices Guidelines** — physical intervention is a last resort only, must be documented, must be reported
3. **Student Health Policy** — medication administration, anaphylaxis, asthma management
4. **Child Protection Policy** — mandatory reporting procedures, guidance on responding to disclosure
5. **Student Attendance Policy** — obligations around monitoring and reporting attendance
6. **Use of Force Guidelines** — physical intervention limits, reporting requirements, must be last resort

**Practical rule:** If the policy says something different from what a colleague says, go with the policy and document your decision.

## PROFESSIONAL REGISTRATION

### TRBWA — Teacher Registration Board of WA

- **Must be registered** to teach in WA (non-negotiable)
- **Provisional registration** for graduate teachers (conditions apply)
- **Renewal** every 5 years — must demonstrate ongoing professional development
- **Fit and proper person test** — ongoing requirement

### What Can Disqualify Registration

- Serious criminal convictions
- Findings of professional misconduct
- Demonstrated serious negligence

### Professional Standards (AITSL)

Must maintain at minimum **Graduate** level for registration renewal.

| Standard | Relevance |
|----------|-----------|
| 4.1 — Establish supportive, orderly, safe learning environments | Duty of care, behaviour |
| 4.3 — Manage challenging behaviour | Suspension, duty of care |
| 4.4 — Foster positive relationships | Student welfare |
| 7.1 — Understand regulatory frameworks (legislation, codes) | THIS skill |
| 7.2 — Understand impact of teacher conduct on children | Professional conduct |
