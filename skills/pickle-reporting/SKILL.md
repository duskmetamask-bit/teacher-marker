# SKILL.md — pickle-reporting

## Purpose

Provides PickleNickAI with the knowledge to write high-quality A-E report comments for Australian F–6 teachers in Western Australia. Covers the WA assessment framework, achievement standard language, comment formulas, learning-area templates, EAL/D reporting, and positive language guidelines.

## Trigger

> "Write a report comment for [student] in [learning area] at [year level]."
> "Generate A-E descriptors for [learning area]."
> "Write a progress comment comparing [previous period] to now."
> "EAL/D student report comment for [student]."

Also activates when: generating batch reports, writing parent communication, or drafting IEP/PLP comments.

## 1. WA Assessment and Reporting Framework

### Standards-Referenced Assessment (AC9)

- Judge student performance against the **Achievement Standards** in the Australian Curriculum v9 (AC9), not against other students
- The A-E scale reflects how well the student is meeting the standard for their year level
- Evidence must come from: work samples, teacher observations, formal/informal assessments
- Moderation: compare judgments with colleagues to ensure consistency (AITSL Standard 5.3)

### Reporting Requirements (WA)

| Requirement | Detail |
|---|---|
| **A-E grades** | Most WA schools use A-E; some F–2 schools use alternative systems — always check school policy |
| **Written comments** | School-dependent format — adapt tone and length accordingly |
| **Progress comments** | Compare current period to previous reporting period |
| **Parent–teacher interviews** | Often scheduled mid- and end-of-year; comments should support conversation |

### AC9 Structure

Each learning area has **content descriptions** and matched **achievement standards**. Reports must reference the standard being assessed. For example:

- English: Language, Literature, Literacy across the curriculum
- Mathematics: Number, Algebra, Measurement, Space, Statistics, Probability
- Science: Science Understanding, Science as a Human Endeavour, Science Inquiry

## 2. A-E Grade Descriptors (General)

Apply these as a starting point — always adapt to the specific learning area and content being reported on.

### A — Excellent

- Demonstrates excellent understanding and application of knowledge and skills
- Work is of a high standard, consistently thorough and accurate
- Independently applies skills in new and complex contexts
- Shows initiative, curiosity, and deep engagement with the content

### B — Good

- Demonstrates good understanding and application of knowledge and skills
- Work is solid and shows strong competence
- Applies skills in familiar contexts confidently; sometimes in new contexts
- Shows reliable engagement with the content

### C — Satisfactory

- Demonstrates satisfactory understanding of core knowledge and skills
- Meets the essential requirements of the standard
- Applies skills in familiar contexts, usually with some support
- Working at the expected standard for the year level

### D — Developing

- Demonstrates developing understanding of core knowledge and skills
- Working towards the standard; not yet meeting core requirements
- Needs consistent support to complete familiar tasks
- Shows potential but requires targeted intervention

### E — Below Standard

- Requires significant ongoing support to work towards the standard
- Limited demonstration of knowledge and skills in this area
- May be working on individualised goals outside the year-level standard
- Evidence suggests need for significant targeted intervention and monitoring

## 3. Report Comment Formula

Use the **PERFECT formula** for every comment:

```
[Praise] — [Specific achievement] — [Area for development] — [Next step/strategy]
```

### Example (Writing — Year 5, Grade B)

"[Name] has demonstrated strong understanding of narrative writing structure, consistently creating texts with clear orientation, complication and resolution. He could further develop his use of descriptive language to enhance reader engagement. He is encouraged to focus on varying sentence openings in future writing tasks."

### Tips

- Always name the **specific skill** — not "good work"
- Use **curriculum language** from AC9 achievement standards
- Development areas should sound like **opportunities**, not weaknesses
- Next steps should be **actionable and specific**
- Keep comments to **2–4 sentences** for report cards
- Use [He/She] / [his/her] placeholders where needed — replace with actual pronouns

## 4. Learning Area Comment Templates

### English — Reading

| Grade | Template |
|---|---|
| **A** | Reads with fluency and expression, demonstrating strong comprehension of both literal and inferential meaning across a range of texts. |
| **B** | Reads with developing fluency and shows solid comprehension of texts, identifying main ideas and some inferential meaning. |
| **C** | Reads and demonstrates understanding of texts at [year] level, answering literal comprehension questions with accuracy. |
| **D** | Is developing reading strategies and shows emerging comprehension of simple texts with teacher support. |
| **E** | Requires support to engage with reading tasks and demonstrate comprehension of texts. |

### English — Writing

| Grade | Template |
|---|---|
| **A** | Creates sophisticated, well-structured texts for a range of purposes and audiences, demonstrating strong command of language features. |
| **B** | Creates well-structured texts with appropriate language features for the purpose and audience. |
| **C** | Creates texts that meet the purpose, using appropriate basic language features. |
| **D** | Is developing ability to create texts for different purposes with ongoing teacher support. |
| **E** | Requires individualised support to engage in writing tasks. |

### Mathematics

| Grade | Template |
|---|---|
| **A** | Demonstrates excellent mathematical reasoning and problem-solving skills, applying concepts accurately in complex contexts. |
| **B** | Demonstrates good understanding of mathematical concepts, applying skills accurately in familiar and some new contexts. |
| **C** | Demonstrates satisfactory understanding of core mathematical concepts and skills at this year level. |
| **D** | Is developing understanding of core mathematical concepts and requires targeted support. |
| **E** | Requires significant individualised support to engage with mathematical concepts. |

### Science

| Grade | Template |
|---|---|
| **A** | Demonstrates excellent scientific knowledge and inquiry skills, conducting thorough investigations with accuracy. |
| **B** | Demonstrates good scientific knowledge and conducts investigations with developing confidence. |
| **C** | Demonstrates satisfactory understanding of science concepts relevant to this year level. |
| **D** | Is developing scientific knowledge and requires guided support during investigations. |
| **E** | Requires individualised support to engage with scientific concepts and activities. |

### Humanities and Social Sciences (HASS) / Humanities

Adapt from English/Science templates. Focus on: historical knowledge, geographical concepts, civic and citizenship understanding, inquiry skills.

### Health and Physical Education (HPE)

Focus on: movement sequences, personal health choices, cooperation, safety. Adapt language to reflect physical and social-emotional development.

### The Arts (Visual Art, Music, Drama, Dance)

Focus on: creation process, technical skill, reflection, interpretation, engagement with art forms.

## 5. Year-Level Specific Comment Examples

### Foundation (Pre-Primary / Kindergarten)

"During [reporting period], [name] has settled well into the school routine. He engages positively with literacy and numeracy activities, showing particular strength in letter recognition and initial sound awareness. He is working towards confidently writing his name independently. To support his learning at home, we encourage identifying letters in everyday contexts such as signs, packaging, and books."

**Year 1–2:** Similar tone — focus on engagement, independence building, and home connection activities.

### Year 3–4

"[Name] has made good progress in [subject] this semester. He demonstrates solid understanding of [concept] and consistently applies [skill]. [Name] is encouraged to focus on [specific area] to further develop his skills. Regular practice of [skill] at home, such as [specific activity], would support his continued growth."

### Year 5–6

"[Name] demonstrates strong understanding of [subject area]. He approaches learning with enthusiasm and dedication, and his classwork reflects consistent quality and effort. To consolidate learning in [area], he is encouraged to [specific next step]. Engaging with [suggested resource or activity] would further strengthen his skills."

## 6. Progress Comments

### Showing Positive Progress

"[Name] has shown excellent growth in [area] since [previous reporting period]. He has improved in [specific skill] and is now confidently applying [concept] in complex contexts. This progress indicates [Name] is meeting expectations for [year level] and is on track to exceed the standard by end of year."

### Solid Progress

"[Name] has shown good growth in [area] since [previous reporting period]. He has strengthened his ability to [specific skill] and is now working solidly at the standard for [year level]. Continued focus on [area] will support him to confidently meet expectations."

### Not Making Expected Progress

"[Name]'s progress in [area] is not consistent with expectations for [year level]. We have implemented targeted support strategies including [specific strategies] and are monitoring his progress closely. A meeting to discuss [Name]'s learning is welcome — please contact the class teacher to arrange a time convenient for you."

## 7. EAL/D Student Comments

EAL/D students are assessed on their English language proficiency separately from their curriculum content knowledge. Use the EAL/D Proficiency Scale:

- **Beginning** — Early stages of English acquisition; needs significant support
- **Developing** — Building basic English; communicates with support
- **Consolidating** — Communicating in English with growing confidence; developing academic language
- **Extended** — Communicating fluently in English; academic language developing

### EAL/D Comment Formula

"[Name] is an EAL/D learner who has been studying English for [duration — e.g., 18 months]. His proficiency level is Consolidating. He demonstrates sound understanding of curriculum content when visual supports and concrete materials are provided. Home language use to discuss key concepts continues to support his English language development. He is encouraged to [specific strategy]."

### Key Considerations

- Assess **content knowledge** separately from **language proficiency**
- EAL/D students may have strong understanding but limited English to demonstrate it
- Use "when [support] is provided" language to reflect scaffolding
- Reference home language positively as a strength
- Do not lower academic expectations — scaffold the language, not the content

## 8. Positive Language Guidelines

### Always Do

- ✅ Focus on what the student **CAN do**
- ✅ Be **specific** — name the skill or concept
- ✅ Use **curriculum language** from AC9 achievement standards
- ✅ Frame development areas as **opportunities**
- ✅ Connect development to a **specific strategy or next step**
- ✅ Use **strength-based language** — growth-oriented

### Never Do

- ❌ Compare students to each other
- ❌ Use vague phrases: "tried hard", "nice work", "good effort"
- ❌ Use threatening language: "must improve immediately", "not acceptable"
- ❌ Make predictions: "will never understand", " incapable of..."
- ❌ Use absolutes that close the door: "cannot", "unable to"
- ❌ Write the same generic comment for every student
