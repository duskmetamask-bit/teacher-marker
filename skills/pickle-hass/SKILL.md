# SKILL.md — pickle-hass

## Purpose

**pickle-hass** is the definitive HASS (Humanities and Social Sciences) teaching knowledge base for PickleNickAI — built for Australian F–6 teachers. It provides comprehensive coverage of the AC9 HASS curriculum, inquiry frameworks, First Nations perspectives, cross-curriculum priorities, and practical classroom activities.

Triggered when a teacher needs:
- AC9 HASS code lookup or strand breakdown
- HASS lesson ideas, units, or inquiry projects
- Year-level scope and sequence for HASS
- First Nations content integration guidance
- HASS assessment strategies or rubrics
- Cross-curriculum priority alignment (Aboriginal & Torres Strait Islander, Sustainability, Asia)
- Civics & Citizenship, Economics & Business, Geography, or History content at any F–6 level

## 1. AC9 HASS Structure (F–6)

### 1.1 Two Strands

| Strand | Focus |
|--------|-------|
| **Knowledge and Understanding** | Content — what students learn about HASS topics |
| **Inquiry and Skills** | Process — how students investigate, question, and communicate |

### 1.2 AC9 Code Format

```
AC9H[year][K|I][number]
```

| Part | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| `AC9` | Australian Curriculum Version 9 |
| `H` | HASS subject area |
| `[year]` | Year level (F, 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6) |
| `[K|I]` | `K` = Knowledge and Understanding, `I` = Inquiry and Skills |
| `[number]` | Content descriptor number (varies by year) |

**Examples:**

| Code | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| `AC9HFKI01` | Foundation — Knowledge — descriptor 01 |
| `AC9H3I02` | Year 3 — Inquiry and Skills — descriptor 02 |
| `AC9H6K05` | Year 6 — Knowledge and Understanding — descriptor 05 |
| `AC9H4K03` | Year 4 — Knowledge and Understanding — descriptor 03 |

### 1.3 HASS Sub-Strands at a Glance

**Knowledge and Understanding — 4 sub-strands:**

## 3. Year-Level Progression (F–6)

| Year | History | Geography | Civics | Economics |
|------|---------|-----------|--------|-----------|
| **F** | Self, family, special places | Places near me | My school | Needs vs wants |
| **1** | Days and weeks, celebrations | Places near and far | School/community rules | People work |
| **2** | Past and present, transport | Weather, features | Groups I belong to | Goods and services |
| **3** | First contacts (local) | Places on maps | Community decision-making | Making choices |
| **4** | First contacts (colonial) | Landforms, water | Role of citizens | Earning/spending |
| **5** | Ancient Australia/world | Asia, sustainability | Democracy, government | Resources, trade |
| **6** | Federation, modern Australia | Global connections | Australian Constitution | Global economy |

### Progression Logic
- **F–2:** Personal → Family → Community — concrete and immediate
- **3–4:** Local → Regional — expanding sphere of influence
- **5–6:** National → Global — abstract, systemic thinking

## 4. First Nations Perspectives in HASS

### 4.1 Core Integration Principles

1. **Country and Place** — Always acknowledge the Traditional Owners of the land. Use correct names and language groups where known.
2. **Culture is living** — First Nations cultures are continuous, not just historical. Avoid framing only in past tense.
3. **Diverse perspectives** — There are many First Nations peoples; avoid generalising.
4. **Student identity** — All students should see themselves in the curriculum; non-Indigenous students benefit from respectful engagement with Country.
5. **Avoid deficit framing** — Focus on strengths, knowledge systems, and resilience. Never frame colonisation as "discovery" or invasion.

### 4.2 Indigenous Knowledge Systems

**Land management (Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander):**
- Fire-stick farming — using fire to manage land, encourage regrowth, control pests
- Water management — fish traps, irrigation systems, waterholes as shared resources
- Seasons calendars — reading Country through environmental indicators (flora, fauna, weather)
- Shared knowledge — Elders as knowledge keepers, oral traditions

**Astronomy:**
- Aboriginal star knowledge — navigation, seasons, Songlines connecting land and sky
- Torres Strait Islander celestial knowledge — navigation, tidal knowledge

**Seasonal calendars:**
- Many Aboriginal groups recognise 5–6 seasons rather than four
- Indicators: flowering plants, animal behaviour, wind patterns, food availability
- Teaching application: students create local seasonal calendars using observed indicators

### 4.3 Culturally Appropriate Protocols for Teaching First Nations Content

| Do ✅ | Don't ❌ |
|-------|---------|
| Invite local Elders or Knowledge Holders to share | Treat Indigenous culture as costume or performance |
| Use correct language group names | Use generic terms like "tribal" or "aboriginal" broadly |
| Check with local community before resources | Reproduce sacred or restricted material |
| Integrate First Nations perspectives year-round | Confine to Sorry Day or NAIDOC Week only |
| Centre Indigenous voice and agency | Frame Indigenous peoples only as victims |
| Teach truth about colonisation | Teach "peaceful settlement" narratives |
| Respect silence — some topics are not for sharing | Force students to share personal stories about identity |

## 5. Inquiry-Based HASS

### 5.1 The 5Es for HASS Inquiry

Developed from the science 5Es model, adapted for HASS:

| Phase | Description | Teacher Role | Student Activity |
|-------|-------------|--------------|-----------------|
| **Engage** | Spark curiosity, access prior knowledge | Pose a compelling question or stimulus (image, artefact, story, provocation) | Share what they know, raise questions |
| **Explore** | Gather information, investigate | Provide sources, arrange excursion/incursion, guide observation | Collect facts, interview, photograph, measure |
| **Explain** | Make sense of findings | Facilitate discussion, introduce key concepts/vocabulary | Share findings, build explanations |
| **Elaborate** | Apply understanding to new contexts | Pose extension challenge or comparative scenario | Extend learning, make connections |
| **Evaluate** | Reflect and assess | Review process and product, provide feedback | Self-assess, present, celebrate learning |

**HASS example — Year 3 "Our Community's Past and Present":**

- **Engage:** Show old photos of the local main street. "What has changed?"
- **Explore:** Walking excursion to local museum/heritage site, interview an Elder
- **Explain:** Class discussion: "What caused these changes? Who decided?"
- **Elaborate:** Compare our local area to a community in another country
- **Evaluate:** Students present a "Then and Now" poster with annotations

### 5.2 Primary Sources for Primary Students

**What counts as a primary source:**
- Old photographs (local, family)
- Objects and artefacts
- Letters, diaries, postcards
- Newspapers from the time
- Maps from the past
- Buildings and monuments
- Oral histories (interviews, song, story)
- Census data

**How to use them with F–6:**

| Year | Approach |
|------|----------|
| F–2 | Sorting activities (old/new, same/different), describe what they see in photos, act out historical moments |
| 3–4 | Compare two sources (e.g., old and new map of the same place), identify bias, annotate sources |
| 5–6 | Source analysis with structured prompts (Who made this? Why? Whose voice is missing? What can I trust?), larger inquiry projects |

### 5.3 Excursions and Incursions for HASS

**Planning an excursion:**
1. Define the inquiry question first — don't go without a question
2. Pre-visit: prepare students with what they will see, vocabulary, and what they need to find out
3. During: student-led observation tasks, photo/video documentation, sketch maps
4. Post-excursion: synthesis task (poster, presentation, narrative, model)

**Quality incursion options:**
- Local council or member of parliament visit
- Elder or Knowledge Holder talk
- Museum outreach programs
- Community police/fire station visit
- Local business owner (economics unit)

### 5.4 Digital Resources for HASS

| Tool | Use |

| **Trove** (trove.nla.gov.au) | Search historical newspapers, photographs, diaries — Australian content |

## 6. Cross-Curriculum Priorities

### 6.1 Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander Histories and Cultures

**Where it appears in HASS:**
- History units (all years, especially 3–6)
- Geography — Country/Place
- Civics — identity, belonging, citizenship
- Economics — Indigenous businesses, land rights, native title

**Key learning outcomes:**
- Understanding that Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander peoples have longest continuous cultures on Earth
- Knowledge of the impact of colonisation and its ongoing effects
- Respect for diverse cultures and identities
- Celebration of the richness and sophistication of First Nations knowledge systems

### 6.2 Sustainability

**Where it appears in HASS:**
- Geography — environmental change, sustainable environments
- Economics — resource management, future thinking
- History — environmental movements, changing land use

**Key learning outcomes:**
- Understanding environmental, social, and economic interconnections
- recognising that actions have consequences for future generations
- Developing values of stewardship and responsibility
- Evaluating solutions for sustainability challenges

### 6.3 Asia and Australia's Engagement with Asia

**Where it appears in HASS:**
- Geography Year 5 — Countries of Asia
- History Year 5 — Ancient world including Asian civilisations
- Economics — trade relationships
- Civics — Australia's international relationships

**Key learning outcomes:**
- Knowledge of the diverse characteristics of Asia
- Understanding shared histories and trade routes
- Respect for cultural diversity and intercultural understanding
- Recognition of Asia's growing influence in global affairs

## 7. Practical HASS Activities

### 7.1 Three Inquiry Projects Per Year Level

**Foundation:**

1. **"All About Me" — Who Am I and My Family?**
   - Strand: History (Personal and family history)
   - Inquiry question: "What makes my family special?"
   - Output: Family collage or "All About Me" book
   - Skills: Communicate, Questioning

2. **"Our Special Place" — Places in Our Community**
   - Strand: Geography (Places near me)
   - Inquiry question: "What makes this place special?"
   - Output: Photo walk → annotated photo display
   - Skills: Observing, Recording

3. **"Needs and Wants" — What Do We Really Need?**
   - Strand: Economics (Needs and wants)
   - Inquiry question: "Do we need this or just want it?"
   - Output: Sort cards into needs/wants, justify choices
   - Skills: Questioning, Analysing

**Year 1:**

1. **"Celebrations Around the World"**
   - Strand: History (Days and weeks, celebrations)
   - Inquiry question: "How do people celebrate different events?"
   - Output: Celebrations comparison chart + presentation
   - Skills: Researching, Communicating

2. **"Places Near and Far" — Comparing Places**
   - Strand: Geography (Places near and far)
   - Inquiry question: "How are places similar and different?"
   - Output: Place comparison poster (our suburb vs another)
   - Skills: Analysing, Comparing

3. **"People Who Help Us" — Community Workers**
   - Strand: Economics (People who work)
   - Inquiry question: "Who helps our community and how?"
   - Output: Visitor incursion → thank-you cards + role-play
   - Skills: Questioning, Communicating

**Year 2:**

1. **"Transport Through Time"**
   - Strand: History (Past and present)
   - Inquiry question: "How has transport changed over time?"
   - Output: Timeline display + "Which would you use and why?"
   - Skills: Chronological reasoning, Researching

2. **"Weather Watchers" — Our Climate**
   - Strand: Geography (Weather and features)
   - Inquiry question: "What's the weather doing this week and why?"
   - Output: 5-day weather diary + simple weather map
   - Skills: Observing, Recording, Analysing

3. **"Goods and Services" — What's Available Locally?**
   - Strand: Economics (Goods and services)
   - Inquiry question: "What goods and services does our suburb provide?"
   - Output: Class map of local businesses, categorised
   - Skills: Researching, Mapping, Communicating

**Year 3:**

1. **"First Contacts — Our Local Area"**
   - Strand: History (First contacts — local)
   - Inquiry question: "Who lived on this land before us, and what can we learn?"
   - Output: Local Indigenous history poster, acknowledge Traditional Owners
   - Skills: Primary source analysis, Respectful inquiry

2. **"Mapping Our World" — Places on Maps**
   - Strand: Geography (Places on maps)
   - Inquiry question: "What can maps tell us about a place?"
   - Output: Create a map of the school or local area with symbols and a legend
   - Skills: Mapping, Spatial thinking

3. **"Community Decision-Making" — How Does Our Community Make Choices?**
   - Strand: Civics (Community decision-making)
   - Inquiry question: "Who makes decisions in our community and how can we participate?"
   - Output: Class proposal for a community improvement + formal letter
   - Skills: Questioning, Evaluating, Communicating

**Year 4:**

1. **"First Contacts — Colonisation"**
   - Strand: History (First contacts — colonial)
   - Inquiry question: "What happened when Europeans colonised Australia?"
   - Output: Structured source analysis + respectful narrative account
   - Skills: Primary source analysis, Empathy, Historical reasoning

2. **"Landforms Around the World"**
   - Strand: Geography (Landforms and water)
   - Inquiry question: "How do landforms shape how people live?"
   - Output: Model or diorama of a landform + explanatory narration
   - Skills: Researching, Creating, Communicating

3. **"Being a Good Citizen" — Rights and Responsibilities**
   - Strand: Civics (Role of citizens)
   - Inquiry question: "What does it mean to be a good citizen?"
   - Output: Citizenship pledge + action plan for one improvement
   - Skills: Evaluating, Communicating, Taking action

**Year 5:**

1. **"Ancient Australia" — 60,000+ Years of History**
   - Strand: History (Ancient Australia/world)
   - Inquiry question: "How do we know about Australia's ancient past?"
   - Output: Timeline from first people to colonisation, oral history integration
   - Skills: Chronological reasoning, Source analysis

2. **"Countries of Asia" — Diversity and Connection**
   - Strand: Geography (Asia, sustainability)
   - Inquiry question: "What makes the countries of Asia unique?"
   - Output: Country profile for one Asian nation, compare with Australia
   - Skills: Researching, Comparing, Communicating

3. **"Democracy in Action" — Levels of Government**
   - Strand: Civics (Democracy, government)
   - Inquiry question: "How does democracy work in Australia?"
   - Output: Model parliament role-play, debate a local issue
   - Skills: Questioning, Evaluating, Communicating

**Year 6:**

1. **"Federation — How Did We Become One Nation?"**
   - Strand: History (Federation, modern Australia)
   - Inquiry question: "Why did the colonies federate and what was the impact?"
   - Output: Multimedia presentation on the path to federation
   - Skills: Chronological reasoning, Researching, Communicating

2. **"Global Connections" — Our Place in the World**
   - Strand: Geography (Global connections)
   - Inquiry question: "How is Australia connected to the rest of the world?"
   - Output: Infographic showing Australia's global trade, migration, and relationships
   - Skills: Analysing, Evaluating, Data interpretation

3. **"The Australian Constitution" — Our Governing Rules**
   - Strand: Civics (Australian Constitution)
   - Inquiry question: "What does the Constitution say and why does it matter?"
   - Output: Plain-language "Constitution explained" guide for a Year 4 audience
   - Skills: Researching, Analysing, Communicating (to younger audience)

### 7.2 Assessment Strategies

| Strategy | Best For | Examples |
|----------|----------|----------|
| **Oral presentation** | F–2 and up | "Tell us about your family" — speaking and listening assessment |
| **Poster / visual display** | All years | Annotated images, labelled diagrams, "Then and Now" posters |
| **Written explanation** | 3–6 | Paragraphs, reports, structured written responses to inquiry questions |
| **Model or diorama** | All years | Build a landform, historic event, or sustainable community |
| **Role-play / drama** | All years | Model parliament, historical re-enactment, business scenarios |
| **Multimedia presentation** | 3–6 | Canva, PowerPoint, video, digital story |
| **Field work / excursion report** | 3–6 | Structured observation log, reflection journal |
| **Source analysis task** | 3–6 | Structured analysis template (Who? Why? What's missing?) |
| **Inquiry journal / process journal** | All years | Documentation of thinking throughout inquiry cycle |
| **Exhibition / showcase** | All years | Students present products to an audience (peers, parents, community) |

### 7.3 AITSL Alignment

The Australian Institute for Teaching and School Leadership (AITSL) standards relevant to HASS teaching:

| AITSL Standard | What it looks like in HASS |
|----------------|--------------------------|
| **2.1** — Content selection | Select HASS content relevant to students' lives, community, and curriculum priorities |
| **2.2** — Sequence learning | Build HASS units with logical progression (concrete → abstract, local → global) |
| **2.3** — Use of curriculum resources | Use primary sources, AIATSIS, Trove, ABC Splash appropriately |
| **3.1** — Plan lesson sequences | Use the 5Es model to sequence HASS lessons |
| **3.2** — Establish challenge | Pose compelling inquiry questions that drive student investigation |
| **3.3** — Use resources | Select and use digital and physical HASS resources including First Nations sources |
| **3.4** — Student demonstration | Students demonstrate HASS understanding through diverse products |
| **5.1** — Assess | Use a range of HASS assessment strategies (not just written tests) |
| **5.2** — Provide feedback | Timely, specific feedback on inquiry products and process journals |
| **5.4** — Interpret data | Use assessment data to inform HASS teaching decisions |
| **6.2** — Engage with parents/carers | Share HASS learning through exhibitions and student-led parent sessions |
| **7.2** — Comply with legislation | Meet requirements for excursions, First Nations cultural protocols |
