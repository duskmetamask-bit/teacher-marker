## Purpose
Science teaching knowledge for Australian F-6 teachers using AC9. This skill covers the Australian Curriculum Version 9 Science, scientific inquiry skills, pedagogical approaches, and WA-specific context.

**Use when:** Generating science units, lesson plans, experiments, or answering questions about teaching science in Australian primary schools.

## AC9 SCIENCE STRUCTURE (F-6)

### Three Strands

**1. Science Understanding (U)**
Covers four sub-strands:
- **Biological sciences** — living things, life cycles, ecosystems
- **Physical sciences** — forces, motion, energy, materials
- **Chemical sciences** — properties of matter, changes
- **Earth and space sciences** — Earth's surface, weather, solar system

**2. Science as a Human Endeavour (H)**
How scientists work, the nature of science, contributions of scientists (including First Nations Australians)

**3. Science Inquiry (I)**
Skills: questioning, predicting, planning, conducting, processing, analysing, evaluating, communicating

### AC9 Code Format
```
AC9S[year][strand letter][number]
```

| Strand | Letter | Example |
|--------|--------|---------|
| Science Understanding | U | AC9SFU01 = Foundation, Science Understanding, descriptor 01 |
| Science as Human Endeavour | H | AC9S3H01 = Year 3, SHE, descriptor 01 |
| Science Inquiry | I | AC9S5I02 = Year 5, Inquiry, descriptor 02 |

### Foundation (F) — Science Understanding

**AC9SFU01** — Biological sciences: observe external features of plants and animals, describe grouping strategies
**AC9SFU02** — Physical sciences: describe how objects move (size, shape, material influence)
**AC9SFU03** — Chemical sciences: recognise objects can be composed of different materials

### Foundation (F) — Science as Human Endeavour

**AC9SFH01** — Explore ways people use observations and questions to learn about the natural world

### Foundation (F) — Science Inquiry

**AC9SFI01** — Pose questions and make predictions based on experiences
**AC9SFI02** — Engage in investigations safely, make observations using senses
**AC9SFI03** — Represent observations in templates, identify patterns with guidance
**AC9SFI04** — Compare observations with predictions
**AC9SFI05** — Share questions, predictions, observations and ideas with others

## SCIENTIFIC METHOD FOR PRIMARY

### The Problem with CUBES in Science
CUBES (Circle the key numbers, Underline the question, Box the operation, Evaluate, Solve) is for MATH WORD PROBLEMS — not for science. For science, use a different framework.

### Science Investigation Framework (Primary-Appropriate)

**OBSERVE (What do we see?)**
- Use all five senses (where safe)
- Describe exactly what is happening
- Record observations in text, drawings, photos

**HYPOTHESISE (What do we think will happen?)**
- Turn observations into a testable question
- "I think that... because..."
- Make a prediction based on prior knowledge

**EXPERIMENT (How will we test it?)**
- Design a fair test (only ONE variable changes)
- Keep everything else the same
- Collect data systematically

**CONCLUDE (What did we find out?)**
- Compare results to the hypothesis
- Use data to support the conclusion
- Identify limitations of the investigation

### Fair Testing (Year 3+)
Key concept: Only change ONE variable at a time.

**Example: Does the size of a ball affect how far it bounces?**
- Keep the same: surface (concrete), drop height, ball material
- Change: ball size (small, medium, large)
- Measure: distance bounced (cm)

**Year 4+ key vocabulary:** independent variable (what YOU change), dependent variable (what you MEASURE), controlled variables (what you KEEP THE SAME)

## YEAR LEVEL PROGRESSION (F-6)

### Foundation (F)

**Science Understanding:**
- Living vs non-living things (AC9SFU01)
- How objects move (AC9SFU02)
- Materials and their properties (AC9SFU03)

**Science Inquiry:**
- Ask questions, make predictions
- Observe using senses safely
- Record observations (drawings, photos, voice)
- Compare predictions to observations

**Hands-on ideas:**
- Sort living/non-living in the playground
- Race different objects down a ramp
- Explore materials: what bounces? what squishes?

### Year 1

**Science Understanding:**
- Needs of living things (water, food, air, shelter)
- Daily changes in weather and sky
- Using five senses

**Science Inquiry:**
- Simple guided observations
- Predict before investigating
- Record using provided templates

**Hands-on ideas:**
- Grow beans in different conditions (light/dark, water/dry)
- Make a weather chart over 2 weeks
- Mystery bag (describe objects by touch)

### Year 2

**Science Understanding:**
- Life cycles of animals and plants
- Habitats and why living things need them
- How objects can change

**Science Inquiry:**
- Make predictions before fair tests
- Use senses appropriately
- Begin to use simple data recording

**Hands-on ideas:**
- Butterfly lifecycle (painted lady caterpillars)
- Terrarium habitat investigation
- Explore how materials change (paper getting wet, ice melting)

### Year 3

**Science Understanding:**
- Living things: life cycles, growth, survival
- Weather: patterns, instruments
- Rocks and minerals (basic)

**Science Inquiry:**
- Conduct fair tests (one variable)
- Record observations in tables
- Compare predictions to results

**Hands-on ideas:**
- Plant growth experiment (different amounts of light)
- Build a weather station (rain gauge, wind vane)
- RockScratch test (streak, luster, texture)

### Year 4

**Science Understanding:**
- Living vs non-living (deepen)
- Body systems basics (skeleton, muscles, digestion)
- Electrical circuits (simple)

**Science Inquiry:**
- Controlled variables in fair tests
- Data recording (tables, simple graphs)
- Begin to evaluate their method

**Hands-on ideas:**
- Build circuits (buzzers, lights, switches)
- Food journey (modelling digestion)
- Ecosystem in a bottle

### Year 5

**Science Understanding:**
- Earth: water cycle, weather patterns, climate
- Body systems (circulatory, respiratory)
- Gravity and forces (contact vs non-contact)

**Science Inquiry:**
- Independent and dependent variables clearly identified
- Graphical representation of data
- Draw evidence-based conclusions

**Hands-on ideas:**
- Water cycle in a bag (observe condensation)
- Heart rate investigation (before/after exercise)
- Design a parachute (test variables affecting fall speed)

### Year 6

**Science Understanding:**
- Earth: landforms, rocks, geological time
- Solar system (sun, planets, moon phases)
- Electricity (circuits, conductors, insulators)

**Science Inquiry:**
- Full scientific investigation (all 8 skills)
- Design own fair tests
- Evaluate evidence and identify limitations

**Hands-on ideas:**
- Moon phase model (oreo cookies or flashlight in dark room)
- Test materials for conductivity
- Build a working burglar alarm

## CROSS-CURRICULUM PRIORITIES

### Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander Histories and Cultures

**Why it matters in Science:**
- First Nations Australians have sophisticated knowledge systems (astronomy, ecology, land management, materials science)
- This is NOT just about "Dreamtime stories" — it's about real science knowledge systems
- Must be integrated meaningfully, not tokenistically

**Examples to USE:**
- Noongar astronomical knowledge (seasonal calendars, star navigation)
- Aboriginal understanding of plant properties (medicinal, food, materials)
- Fire-stick farming and land management as ecological science
- Ocean knowledge and navigation of First Nations Australians
- Materials science in traditional tools and utensils

**Resources:**
- AIATSIS (aiatsis.gov.au)
- SCSA Aboriginal perspectives in Science units
- STEM Indigenous knowledge partnerships

### Sustainability

**Link to Science:**
- Environmental science: ecosystems, biodiversity, climate change
- Resource management: water, energy, materials
- Human impact on environments

**Classroom ideas:**
- School garden/worm farm investigation
- Energy audit (how can we use less electricity?)
- Water quality testing (local creek/pond)
- Biodiversity survey (what lives in our school grounds?)

### Asia and Australia's Engagement with Asia

- Science contributions from Asian scientists
- Weather and climate patterns across Asia
- Environmental issues across the region

## PEDAGOGICAL APPROACHES

### Hands-On / Inquiry-Based Learning

**Why it matters:** Science is not a spectator sport. Students learn by DOING, not by reading or listening.

**The 5E Model for Science:**
1. **Engage (5 min):** Hook curiosity — question, prop, video, demonstration
2. **Explore (15 min):** Students investigate in pairs/small groups — hands-on
3. **Explain (10 min):** Teacher consolidates, introduces vocabulary
4. **Elaborate (15 min):** Apply to new context or extend thinking
5. **Evaluate (5 min):** Formative check — verbal, exit ticket, observation

### Science Journals / Logbooks

**Why use them:** Document thinking, track learning, develop scientific vocabulary

**What to include:**
- Date and investigation title
- Question/hypothesis
- Prediction
- Observation records (drawings, labels, measurements)
- Data (tables, tally charts)
- Conclusion
- New questions

**Logbook prompts:**
- "Today I observed..."
- "I predicted... and found..."
- "This makes me wonder about..."
- "Next time I would..."

### Modelling and Representation

**Key concept:** Scientific phenomena often invisible — must be represented

**Examples:**
- Water cycle: baggies on windows, mini globe models
- Earth layers: playdough core samples
- Food chains: chain of cards/representations
- Body systems: organ models, tracing paper overlays

### Talk for Science (Scientific Vocabulary Development)

**Critical for EAL/D students:**
- Teach science words WITH concrete experiences
- Use visual supports (real objects, pictures, gestures)
- Build from everyday language to scientific language
- Example: "grows" → "develops" → "photosynthesis"

**Sentence starters for investigations:**
- "I predict that... because..."
- "My observation shows..."
- "The data tells us..."
- "I conclude that..."
- "One thing I noticed was..."

## SAFETY IN SCIENCE

### Non-Negotiables
- No tasting chemicals or unknown plants
- Eye protection for any liquid splashes
- Scalpels/scissors only with direct teacher supervision
- Heat sources (hot plates, burners) never left unattended
- All PPE used appropriately

### First Aid in Science
- Know where the eye wash station is
- First aid kit location and who is first aid trained
- Emergency procedure for burns, cuts, chemical exposure

### Risk Assessment
Before any investigation:
1. Identify hazards (heat, sharp tools, chemicals, living things)
2. Assess risk (high/medium/low)
3. Implement controls (minimise risk)
4. Teacher demonstrates before students handle
