## 1. AITSL PROFESSIONAL STANDARDS OVERVIEW

### The 4 Progression Levels

| Level | Who | Description |
|-------|-----|-------------|
| **Graduate** | Pre-service, new grads (0-2 yrs) | Entry to profession; demonstrates basic competencies |
| **Proficient** | Registered teachers | Competent, independent practice; most teachers sit here |
| **Highly Proficient** | Experienced, expert teachers | Deep knowledge; mentors others; highly effective |
| **Lead** | Leaders, head teachers, coordinators | Influential across school; drives practice improvement |

Graduate → Proficient is the first career milestone (usually within first 2-3 years).

### The 7 Standards at a Glance

| # | Focus |
|---|-------|
| 1 | Know students and how they learn |
| 2 | Know the content and how to teach it |
| 3 | Plan for and implement effective teaching |
| 4 | Create and maintain supportive learning environments |
| 5 | Assess, provide feedback, report |
| 6 | Engage in professional learning |
| 7 | Engage professionally with colleagues, parents, community |

## 2. AITSL STANDARDS BY STANDARD — F-6 FOCUS

### Standard 1 — Know students and how they learn

**Focus:** Understanding learner development, diversity, and individual differences.

- **1.1** Physical, social, intellectual development — understanding growth stages F-6, developmental milestones, how age influences learning capacity
- **1.2** How learning differs — learning styles, differentiation, Vygotsky's ZPD, prior knowledge activation, metacognition
- **1.3** Students with disability — adjustments under NCCD, capabilities-based planning, inclusive strategies, reasonable adjustments
- **1.4** Strategies for EAL/D and Aboriginal students — ESL scales, cultural safety, home language support, trauma-informed practice

**F-6 Context:** Primary teachers see huge developmental spread. A Year 1 student may be 5 or turning 7. Standard 1 reminds teachers to meet students where they are.

**Common evidence language:** "Used pre-assessment data to differentiate instruction", "Created Individual Education Plans for students with disability", "Applied Vygotsky's ZPD to scaffold tasks"

---

### Standard 2 — Know the content and how to teach it

**Focus:** Deep subject knowledge and pedagogical content knowledge.

- **2.1** Content and teaching strategies for learning area — knowing your subject AND how to teach it (pedagogical content knowledge)
- **2.2** Content selection and organisation — sequential, logical progression; scope and sequence; builds on prior knowledge
- **2.3** Use and impact of ICT in teaching — digital literacies, SAMR model, embedding technology meaningfully (not for its own sake)

**F-6 Context:** Primary teachers teach ALL learning areas. Specialist knowledge in areas like HASS, The Arts, Health can be shallower — requires intentional professional learning.

**Common evidence language:** "Planned units using backward design (WHERETO)", "Used worked examples and guided practice before independent tasks", "Integrated digital tools to enhance student understanding of fractions"

---

### Standard 3 — Plan for and implement effective teaching

**Focus:** Lesson planning, instructional strategies, and resource selection.

- **3.1** Establish challenging learning goals — SMART goals, growth mindset language, visible learning intentions, success criteria
- **3.2** Plan structured lessons — lesson arc (hook, explicit teaching, guided practice, independent, reflection), timing, transitions, resources
- **3.3** Use teaching strategies — questioning (Bloom's), explicit teaching, feedback loops, worked examples, feedback
- **3.4** Select and use resources — ICT, diverse texts, multimodal resources, Aboriginal perspectives, inclusive materials

**F-6 Context:** Clear learning intentions and success criteria are non-negotiable in WA F-6 classrooms. Observations and coachability are assessed under this standard.

**Common evidence language:** "I can statements displayed and referred to throughout lesson", "Used Think-Pair-Share to activate prior knowledge before explicit instruction", "Created success criteria with students to make learning visible"

---

### Standard 4 — Create and maintain supportive learning environments

**Focus:** Behaviour management, relationships, student wellbeing.

- **4.1** Support student participation — engagement strategies, inclusion, belonging, classroom culture
- **4.2** Manage classroom — routines, procedures, transitions, expectations, use of time
- **4.3** Manage challenging behaviour — de-escalation, preventive strategies, functional behaviour assessment, duty of care, restorative practices
- **4.4** Foster positive relationships — trust, respect, student voice, cultural safety

**F-6 Context:** Primary students need explicit routine teaching.Behaviour management is a top concern for graduate teachers. Strong teacher-student relationships are the single most effective intervention.

**Common evidence language:** "Established whole-class and small-group routines within first week", "Used proximity and non-verbal cues before verbal warnings", "Conducted restorative conversation after conflict on yard", "Created class agreement with students using respectful language"

---

### Standard 5 — Assess, provide feedback, report

**Focus:** Assessment for, of, and as learning. Data-driven feedback.

- **5.1** Assess student learning — formative (checking for understanding) and summative (end-of-unit); diagnostic assessment
- **5.2** Provide feedback — timely, specific, actionable, forward-focused; written and oral
- **5.3** Make consistent and comparable judgments — rubrics, marking guides, moderation with colleagues
- **5.4** Interpret student data — PAT results, observation data, work samples, running records, rubrics
- **5.5** Report on student achievement — A-E grades (WA mandate), narrative comments, progress comments

**F-6 Context:** WA schools MUST use A-E grades. Moderation ensures consistency. This standard is where pedagogy meets accountability.

**Common evidence language:** "Used exit tickets to check understanding and adjust instruction same lesson", "Provided feedback on drafts allowing student revision before final submission", "Participated in cross-marking moderation with stage team"

---

### Standard 6 — Engage in professional learning

**Focus:** Own growth, collegial improvement.

- **6.1** Identify own learning needs — reflection, student data analysis, peer feedback, self-assessment against standards
- **6.2** Engage in professional learning — courses, PLTs, mentoring, coaching, conferences, reading
- **6.3** Support colleagues — mentoring pre-service teachers, sharing practice, co-teaching, leading PLTs

**F-6 Context:** All teachers must maintain registration (TRBWA). 20+ hours of PD per year is standard. Graduate teachers need structured support plans.

**Common evidence language:** "Reviewed student PAT data to identify maths instruction gap; enrolled in professional course", "Mentored pre-service teacher through weekly planning meetings and observation debriefs", "Led whole-staff PLT on formative assessment strategies"

---

### Standard 7 — Engage professionally

**Focus:** Ethical practice, school community.

- **7.1** Meet professional obligations — AITSL Code of Conduct, school policies, duty of care, child safety
- **7.2** Comply with legislative and administrative requirements — WHS, privacy, mandatory reporting, NESA requirements
- **7.3** Engage with parents — communication (newsletters, Seesaw, interviews), parent-teacher nights, report discussions
- **7.4** Engage with community — local community context, Aboriginal community connections, excursions, cultural events

**F-6 Context:** Primary teachers have high parent contact. Building parent partnerships is critical. Mandatory reporting is a legal obligation.

**Common evidence language:** "Maintained accurate records in Sentral as required by department policy", "Reported suspected harm via mandatory reporting portal within 24 hours", "Used Seesaw to share weekly learning updates with families"

## 3. AC9 ACHIEVEMENT STANDARDS (F-6)

### What Achievement Standards Are

- **Describe the expected quality of work** at each year level
- **Used to determine A-E grades** — match student work to the standard
- **Part of the WA Assessment and Reporting framework** — schools must align reporting to AC9
- **Different from content descriptions** — content describes WHAT is taught; achievement describes WHAT quality looks like

### A-E Grade Descriptors (General)

| Grade | Descriptor |
|-------|------------|
| **A** | Excellent — demonstrates excellent understanding and application of knowledge, skills |
| **B** | Good — demonstrates good understanding and application |
| **C** | Satisfactory — demonstrates satisfactory understanding and application |
| **D** | Developing — demonstrates developing understanding and application |
| **E** | Below standard — requires additional support to demonstrate basic understanding |

**Important:** Grades must be determined by comparing student work to the achievement standard, NOT to other students. "Better than John's work" is not a valid grade rationale.

### English Achievement Standards (Summary)

| Year | Focus |
|------|-------|
| **Foundation** | Engage with stories, read simple texts with direction, write short texts using letters and known words |
| **Years 1-2** | Read and view familiar texts, create texts using language features (capitalisation, punctuation, simple sentences), recognise purpose and audience |
| **Years 3-4** | Analyse and evaluate texts, create sustained texts using paragraphs, complex sentences, descriptive language, show understanding of purpose |
| **Years 5-6** | Interpret and evaluate (infer, critique, analyse), create sophisticated texts with coherent structure, persuasive devices, genre control |

### Mathematics Achievement Standards (Summary)

| Year | Focus |
|------|-------|
| **Foundation** | Count, sort, describe objects and materials, create and continue simple patterns |
| **Years 1-2** | Number (place value, addition/subtraction), measurement (informal units), 2D shapes, simple data displays |
| **Years 3-4** | Multiplication/division strategies, fractions (including on a number line), area (using squares), angles, data displays with scales, time |
| **Years 5-6** | Decimals (to thousandths), percentages, volume (cubic cm), chance (numerical probabilities), algebra (relationships between variables), statistical investigations |

### Science Achievement Standards (Summary)

| Year | Focus |
|------|-------|
| **Foundation** | Observe and describe using senses, predict outcomes, use scientific words |
| **Years 1-2** | Living things and their basic needs, daily and seasonal changes, follow instructions to investigate |
| **Years 3-4** | Life cycles (animals and plants), body systems (basic), push/pull forces, observable changes in rocks, fair testing |
| **Years 5-6** | Earth systems (atmosphere, biosphere), solar system (Earth-sun-moon), body systems (skeletal, muscular, digestive), electricity (circuits, variables), investigations with controlled variables |

## 4. MODERATION PROCESS (WA)

### Why Moderation Matters

- Ensures **consistent A-E grades** across teachers, classes, and schools
- Uses **AC9 achievement standards** as reference points — not each teacher's internal benchmark
- **SCASA provides standardised samples** — annotated work samples showing A, B, C, D, E quality
- Required for **fair and comparable reporting** — legal/registration obligation

### Moderation Process (Step by Step)

1. **Select sample work** — typically 3-5 pieces per student (or a class set of samples)
2. **Teachers grade independently** — no discussion before; each teacher assigns a grade based on standard
3. **Compare to achievement standard** — hold work against the AC9 descriptor for that year level and learning area
4. **Discuss differences** — if teachers disagree, go back to the standard and the evidence; use SCASA samples as reference
5. **Reach consensus using evidence** — rationale must reference specific features of the standard; not personal benchmarks
6. **Apply to own cohort** — once consensus reached on samples, apply same lens to full cohort

### Moderation Traps

- **Comparison trap:** Comparing student to student instead of student to standard
- **Devil's advocate:** Accepting a lower grade to avoid conflict without evidence-based challenge
- **Leniency/severity drift:** Grading according to perceived effort or behaviour rather than achievement
- **Small sample bias:** Making big grading calls on 1-2 pieces instead of a range of evidence

## 5. REPORT WRITING (WA)

### Types of Reports

| Type | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **A-E Grades** | Mandatory in WA for most schools. Must align to AC9 achievement standards. |
| **Narrative Comments** | Describe what the student CAN do and areas for growth against the standard |
| **Progress Comments** | Compare current achievement to previous report — "now able to...", "continues to..." |

### A-E Grade Decision Process

1. Review multiple pieces of evidence (3+ work samples minimum)
2. Match evidence to achievement standard language
3. Determine the descriptor that BEST fits overall quality — don't average across pieces
4. Check with colleague/modern team if unsure
5. Apply grade

**Grade boundaries:** If a student is between two grades, lean toward the HIGHER grade if they show qualities of both; document reasoning in gradebook.

### Comment Frameworks

**Strength-first structure:**
1. What the student CAN do (specific, evidence-based)
2. Area for development (specific, not vague)
3. Strategy or suggestion (practical, actionable for parent and teacher)

**Progress comment structure:**
1. Where they were (last report)
2. Where they are now (specific evidence)
3. Where they're heading (next step)

### What Makes a Good Comment (F-6)

- **Specific, not vague:** "Uses letter-sound knowledge to decode unknown words" not just "Reading is improving"
- **Referenced to standard:** "Demonstrates understanding of fractions as equal parts of a whole (AC9)"
- **Avoids comparison:** "Better than most" or "needs to catch up" are not appropriate
- **Identifies next step:** "To improve, Sam should practice..." not just "Sam should work harder"
- **Parent-friendly:** No jargon; parents may not know terms like "phonemic awareness"

### Comment Length Guidelines

- **English/Mathematics:** 2-3 sentences per learning area minimum; more if complex needs
- **Other learning areas:** 1-2 sentences per area
- **Overall comment:** 3-5 sentences capturing general progress, behaviour, wellbeing

## 6. NAPLAN INTERPRETATION

### What NAPLAN Measures

| Domain | What it covers |
|--------|---------------|
| **Writing** | Persuasive text writing — ideas, structure, language, conventions |
| **Reading** | Comprehension, literal and inferential meaning, vocabulary |
| **Spelling** | Standard Australian English spelling |
| **Grammar/Punctuation** | Language conventions in context |
| **Numeracy** | Number, algebra, measurement, geometry, statistics, probability |

**NAPLAN is ONE snapshot — not a comprehensive assessment of student ability or teacher effectiveness.**

### Band Descriptions

| Bands | Years | Descriptor |
|-------|------|------------|
| **Band 1-2** | 3, 7 | Beginning — needs additional support |
| **Band 3-4** | 5, 9 | Developing — working toward standard |
| **Band 5-6** | 5, 7, 9 | At standard — expected for this year level |
| **Band 7-8** | 7, 9 | Strong — above expected standard |
| **Band 9-10** | 9 | Excellent — well above expected standard |

**Year 3 students are compared to the Year 3-4 band range. Year 5 students to Year 5-6. NAPLAN bands do NOT directly equate to A-E grades.**

### What NAPLAN Does NOT Measure

- Creativity, collaboration, critical thinking (unless embedded in literacy/numeracy)
- Practical performance tasks
- Social-emotional development
- Oral language (tested in some contexts but limited)
- Learning area achievement in Science, HASS, The Arts, etc.

### NAPLAN and Teaching

**What IS appropriate:**
- Using NAPLAN-like items to check understanding of specific areas (e.g., "this is the type of question students found difficult")
- Targeting specific literacy/numeracy gaps identified by NAPLAN data
- Teaching comprehension strategies that apply broadly (not test-specific)

**What is NOT appropriate:**
- Drilling past NAPLAN tests every year
- Using NAPLAN to predict student rankings
- Celebrating or punishing individual results publicly
- Teaching to the test format rather than underlying skills

### Using NAPLAN Data for Teaching

1. Get student individual reports (available to parents and schools)
2. Identify specific domain weaknesses — not just total score
3. Use school benchmark data to identify cohort-wide trends
4. Plan targeted instruction on specific gaps
5. Monitor with in-class assessment (NAPLAN is once a year; you need more frequent data)

## 7. AITSL PRACTICE EVIDENCE — WHAT IT LOOKS LIKE IN PRACTICE

### What Evidence Should Look Like

Good evidence is:
- **Specific:** Describes exactly what was done, said, observed
- **Observable:** Can be seen, heard, documented — not inferred
- **Linked to standard:** Names the focus area; explains why it matters
- **Student-centred:** Shows impact on learning, not just teacher activity

Bad evidence:
- Vague ("I differentiated for all learners")
- Activity-only ("I attended a PD")
- Inference-only ("The students really enjoyed it")

### Evidence Examples by Standard

**Standard 1.3 (Students with disability):**
> "Created Individual Education Plans for three students with disability in consultation with education assistants and parents. Plans included specific, measurable goals aligned to the Australian Curriculum (Year 4 English) with fortnightly review checkpoints. Two of three students achieved IEP goals by end of term."

**Standard 3.2 (Plan structured lessons):**
> "Used 5-part lesson structure (connect, hook, explicit teaching, guided practice, independent) for daily maths. Lesson timing displayed on board. Transitions between activities timed and practiced — reduced transition time from 8 minutes to 3 minutes over 4 weeks."

**Standard 4.3 (Manage challenging behaviour):**
> "Used a calm, respectful approach when redirecting a student who was off-task. Followed up privately with the student after class to discuss the learning intention and check in. Documented incident in Sentral. Student returned to task within 3 minutes with no further disruption."

**Standard 5.2 (Provide feedback):**
> "Provided written feedback on narrative drafts within 48 hours of submission. Each piece identified one specific strength and one actionable next step (e.g., 'Your main character shows great courage — consider showing this through dialogue next time so readers can hear her voice directly.'). 80% of students revised and improved drafts in subsequent submission."

**Standard 6.3 (Support colleagues):**
> "Mentored pre-service teacher (Miriam) through weekly 30-minute planning meetings. Observed her lessons twice per term and provided written feedback using AITSL language. Miriam rated confidence in lesson planning (pre: 3/10, post: 8/10) and was offered ongoing employment at conclusion of placement."

**Standard 7.3 (Engage with parents):**
> "Called parents of five at-risk students in first three weeks of term. Documented conversations in Sentral. Three of five parents attended parent-teacher interviews. Created individual communication plans for families with limited digital access — used phone calls and written notes in addition to Seesaw."

## 8. GRADE-TO-STANDARD MATCHING (Quick Reference)

When you need to match student work to a grade:

1. Read the **AC9 achievement standard** for that year level and learning area
2. Read the **A-E descriptor** for each grade
3. Ask: "Does this student demonstrate what the standard describes?"

| If the student... | Grade |
|------------------|-------|
| Demonstrates EXCELLENT understanding and application — exceeds typical for this level | **A** |
| Demonstrates GOOD understanding and application — clearly above standard | **B** |
| Demonstrates SATISFACTORY understanding and application — meets the standard adequately | **C** |
| Demonstrates DEVELOPING understanding — partially meets, needs support | **D** |
| Requires ADDITIONAL support to demonstrate basic understanding | **E** |

**Remember:** The standard is the benchmark — not the student's best work, not the class average, not your historical expectations. The standard.
