UNIT AT A GLANCE

**Year Level**Year 6
**Strand**Number

**Model**5E Instructional Model

**Driving Question***How do we add and subtract with integers, and how do we know which operation to perform first when solving complex problems?*

AUSTRALIAN CURRICULUM v9 — CONTENT DESCRIPTORS

General Capabilities

  • Numeracy — core
  • Literacy — mathematical explanation, algebraic notation
  • Critical & Creative Thinking — generalisation, proof, algebraic reasoning

    • BIG UNDERSTANDINGS

    Students will understand that:

  • Integers include positive and negative whole numbers and zero; they can be represented on a number line
  • Adding a negative number is equivalent to subtracting the absolute value
  • Subtracting a negative number is equivalent to adding the absolute value
  • Order of operations (BEDMAS/PEMDAS) ensures consistent results
  • Algebraic expressions can be simplified using the distributive law
  • Integer operations follow the same arithmetic properties as whole numbers

    • VOCABULARY

    Tier 2 (academic)

    integer, positive, negative, zero, number line, opposite, absolute value, expression, equation, term, coefficient, order of operations, BEDMAS

    Tier 3 (mathematical)

    distributive law, factorise, expand, variable, substitution, equivalent expression, balancing method

    LESSON 1.2 — What Are Integers?

    Duration: 50 minutes

    Type: Concept Introduction

    Learning Intentions

  • I am learning that integers include positive and negative whole numbers and zero
  • I am learning that negative numbers describe situations below a reference point

    • Success Criteria

  • I can give real-world examples of negative numbers
  • I can explain what negative means in context

    • I DO (15 min)

    "We've worked with whole numbers and decimals. Now we extend to INTEGERS — positive numbers, negative numbers, and zero."
    "Negative numbers appear everywhere: temperature below zero, altitude below sea level, bank account overdrawn, football losses."
    "The number line extends in both directions. Zero is the REFERENCE POINT."
    "+5 and -5 are OPPOSITES. They are the same distance from zero but on opposite sides."

    WE DO (20 min)

    Real-world integer examples:

  • Temperature: -3°C, +15°C. Which is colder?
  • Altitude: +8848m (Everest), -400m (Dead Sea). Which is lower?
  • Bank: +$500 (credit), -$200 (debt). Which is more?
  • Football: +3 (goal difference), -3 (goal difference). Which is better?

    • YOU DO (15 min)

  • "Give 3 real-world examples of negative numbers."
  • "What is the opposite of -7? What is the opposite of +12?"
  • EXTENSION: "If -10 is colder than -5, which is closer to zero?"

    • LESSON 1.3 — Integers on the Number Line

    Duration: 50 minutes

    Type: Guided Practice

    I DO (15 min)

    "The number line: negative numbers go LEFT from zero."
    "-4 is 4 units left of zero. +4 is 4 units right of zero."
    "To compare: -4 < -1 because -4 is further LEFT on the number line."
    "-3, 0, 2, -7 in order: -7 < -3 < 0 < 2."

    YOU DO (15 min)

  • Plot on number line: -5, 3, -1, 0, -8, 4
  • Compare: -4 ○ -3 (-4 < -3)
  • Order smallest to largest: 2, -5, 0, -1, 3, -6
  • EXTENSION: "Is -100 closer to zero than -50? How do you know?"

    LESSON 2.1 — Adding Integers: Same Sign

    Duration: 50 minutes

    Type: Explore

    Learning Intentions

  • I am learning to add two positive integers
  • I am learning to add two negative integers

    • Success Criteria

  • I can add (-3) + (-5) = -8
  • I can explain: same-sign integers add, keep the sign

    • I DO (15 min)

    "3 + 4 = 7. Positive + positive = positive."
    "(-3) + (-5). Start at -3 on the number line. Jump +5. -3 → -2 → -1 → 0 → 1 → 2. Answer: -8."
    "Same sign addition: ADD the absolute values. Keep the sign."
    "(-3) + (-5) = -(3+5) = -8."

    YOU DO (15 min)

  • 4 + 7 = ___ (-3) + (-5) = ___
  • (-8) + (-3) = ___ (-10) + (-5) = ___
  • "When you add two negatives, is the answer positive or negative?"
  • EXTENSION: "(-100) + (-200) = ?"

    • LESSON 2.2 — Adding Integers: Opposite Signs

    Duration: 50 minutes

    Type: Explore

    I DO (15 min)

    "3 + (-5). Start at 3. Jump -5. 3 → 2 → 1 → 0 → -1 → -2. Answer: -2."
    "The jumps went past zero! 3 + (-5) = -2."
    "(-3) + 5. Start at -3. Jump +5. -3 → -2 → -1 → 0 → 1 → 2. Answer: 2."
    "Opposite sign addition: SUBTRACT the absolute values. The answer takes the sign of the number with the LARGER absolute value."
    "3 + (-5): |3|=3, |-5|=5. 5-3=2. Answer takes sign of |-5|: -2."

    YOU DO (15 min)

  • 4 + (-7) = ___ (-3) + 8 = ___
  • (-10) + 6 = ___ 7 + (-4) = ___
  • "For (-10) + 6, which number has larger absolute value? What sign should the answer have?"
  • EXTENSION: "What is 0 + (-5)? What is 0 + 5?"

    • LESSON 2.3 — Integer Addition Strategies

    Duration: 50 minutes

    Type: Guided Practice

    I DO (12 min)

    Rule summary:

    Addition CaseRuleExample
    Positive + PositiveAdd, positive3 + 4 = 7
    Negative + NegativeAdd, negative(-3) + (-5) = -8
    Positive + NegativeSubtract absolute values; sign of larger3 + (-5) = -2
    Negative + PositiveSubtract absolute values; sign of larger(-3) + 5 = 2
    Any + 0Same number(-7) + 0 = -7

    YOU DO (15 min)

  • (-12) + (-8) = ___ 15 + (-7) = ___ (-9) + 4 = ___
  • "What is the sum of all integers from -5 to +5? Can you find a fast way?"
  • EXTENSION: "Prove that (-a) + a = 0 for any integer a."

    LESSON 3.1 — Subtracting Positive Integers

    Duration: 50 minutes

    Type: Explore

    I DO (15 min)

    "5 - 3 = 2. On the number line: start at 5, jump -3 → 2."
    "(-4) - 3. Start at -4. Jump -3. -4 → -5 → -6 → -7. Answer: -7."
    "When subtracting a positive from an integer, move LEFT on the number line."

    YOU DO (15 min)

  • 8 - 3 = ___ (-5) - 4 = ___
  • 3 - 8 = ___ (-2) - 7 = ___
  • "What is the pattern when you subtract a larger positive from a smaller positive?"
  • EXTENSION: "What is (-100) - 50?"

    • LESSON 3.2 — Subtracting Negative Integers

    Duration: 50 minutes

    Type: Explore

    Learning Intentions

  • I am learning that subtracting a negative is equivalent to adding the positive
  • I am learning that 5 - (-3) = 5 + 3 = 8

    • Success Criteria

  • I can explain: subtracting a negative means adding the opposite

    • I DO (15 min)

    "5 - (-3). Start at 5. Jump -(-3) = +3. 5 → 6 → 7 → 8. Answer: 8."
    "But wait: 5 + 3 = 8. So 5 - (-3) = 5 + 3 = 8!"
    "This is KEY: subtracting a negative = adding the positive."
    "Why? Because -(-3) means the opposite of -3, which is +3."
    "(-4) - (-6): -4 + 6 = 2."

    Write the rule:

    a - (-b) = a + b

    YOU DO (15 min)

  • 5 - (-3) = ___ (-4) - (-6) = ___
  • 7 - (-2) = ___ (-8) - (-3) = ___
  • "For 5 - (-3), why does the answer become 8 (bigger than 5)?"
  • EXTENSION: "If a - b = a + (-b), what is a - (-b)?"

    • LESSON 3.3 — Connecting Addition and Subtraction

    Duration: 50 minutes

    Type: Guided Practice

    I DO (12 min)

    "All subtraction can be rewritten as addition of the opposite:"
    "a - b = a + (-b)"
    "This means: if you can add integers, you can subtract integers!"

    YOU DO (15 min)

  • Rewrite as addition: 7 - 4 = ___ + ___ (-5) - 3 = ___ + ___
  • Solve using addition: 8 - (-2) = ___ (-7) - (-3) = ___
  • "Prove that 5 - (-3) = 5 + 3."
  • EXTENSION: "What is (-1) - (-1) - (-1) - (-1)?"

    LESSON 4.1 — BEDMAS

    Duration: 50 minutes

    Type: Explain

    Learning Intentions

  • I am learning the correct order of operations: BEDMAS
  • I am learning to solve expressions step by step

    • Success Criteria

  • I can apply BEDMAS to evaluate expressions
  • I can explain why order matters

    • I DO (15 min)

    BEDMAS = Brackets, Exponents, Division/Multiplication, Addition/Subtraction

    "3 + 4 × 2. B before DM before AS."
    "Step 1: Brackets? None."
    "Step 2: Exponents? None."
    "Step 3: Division/Multiplication? 4 × 2 = 8."
    "Step 4: Addition/Subtraction? 3 + 8 = 11."
    "Answer: 11. NOT 14 (which is wrong if you add first)."
    "Now: (3 + 4) × 2. Brackets FIRST: (3+4) = 7. Then 7 × 2 = 14. Different answer! That's why order matters."

    WE DO (20 min)

    BEDMAS practice:

  • 8 + 4 ÷ 2 = ___ (8 + 2 = 10)
  • (8 + 4) ÷ 2 = ___ (12 ÷ 2 = 6)
  • 10 - 3 × 2 = ___ (10 - 6 = 4)
  • 20 ÷ 5 + 3 × 2 = ___ (4 + 6 = 10)
  • (20 ÷ 5 + 3) × 2 = ___ (4+3)×2 = 14
  • 15 - (4 + 3) × 2 = ___ (15 - 7 × 2 = 15 - 14 = 1)

    • YOU DO (15 min)

  • 12 ÷ 3 + 2 × 3 = ___
  • (12 ÷ 3 + 2) × 3 = ___
  • "Why does (8 + 4) ÷ 2 give a different answer than 8 + 4 ÷ 2?"
  • EXTENSION: "Where would you add brackets to make 3 + 6 × 2 - 1 = 17?"

    • LESSON 4.2 — BEDMAS with Integers

    Duration: 50 minutes

    Type: Explain

    I DO (15 min)

    "(-3) + 4 × (-2)."
    "BEDMAS: multiply before add. 4 × (-2) = -8."
    "(-3) + (-8) = -11."
    "(-5) - (3 + 2) × (-1)."
    "Brackets: 3 + 2 = 5."
    "Multiplication: 5 × (-1) = -5."
    "Subtraction: (-5) - (-5) = (-5) + 5 = 0."

    YOU DO (15 min)

  • (-3) + 6 × 2 = ___ (BEDMAS: 6×2=12, -3+12=9)
  • 4 × (-3) + (-2) = ___
  • (-10) - (3 - 5) × 2 = ___
  • EXTENSION: "Prove that (-2) × (-2) × (-2) = -8."

    LESSON 5.1 — Expanding: Distributive Law

    Duration: 50 minutes

    Type: Explain

    Learning Intentions

  • I am learning to expand expressions using the distributive law
  • I am learning that 4(x + 3) = 4x + 12

    • Success Criteria

  • I can expand 4(x + 3) = 4x + 12

    • I DO (15 min)

    "4(x + 3) means 4 × (x + 3)."
    "Using the distributive law: 4 × x + 4 × 3 = 4x + 12."
    "This is the same as: (x + 3) × 4 = x × 4 + 3 × 4 = 4x + 12."
    "Proof with a number: x = 5. 4(5+3) = 4×8 = 32. 4×5 + 4×3 = 20+12 = 32. Same! ✓"

    WE DO (20 min)

    Expand:

  • 3(x + 4) = 3x + 12
  • 5(x - 2) = 5x - 10
  • 2(x + 7) = ___
  • 4(3x + 1) = ___

    • YOU DO (15 min)

  • 6(x + 2) = ___ 3(2x - 5) = ___
  • "Expand 2(x + 3) + 3(x + 1)."
  • EXTENSION: "If 4(x + 2) = 24, what is x?"

    • LESSON 5.2 — Expanding with Integers; Factorising

    Duration: 50 minutes

    Type: Explain

    I DO (15 min)

    With integers:

    "4(x - 3) = 4x - 12. (negative sign distributes)"
    "(-2)(x + 4) = -2x - 8."
    "(-3)(2x - 5) = -6x + 15."

    Factorising:

    "4x + 12 = 4(x + 3). Common factor: 4."
    "6x + 9 = 3(2x + 3). Common factor: 3."
    "5x - 10 = 5(x - 2)."

    YOU DO (15 min)

  • Expand: (-3)(x + 4) = ___ (-2)(3x - 5) = ___
  • Factorise: 6x + 8 = ___(___ + ___) 9x - 12 = ___(___ - ___)
  • "For (-2)(3x - 5) = -6x + 10, why does the last term become +10?"
  • EXTENSION: "Factorise: 6x + 9y."

    • LESSON 5.3 — Algebra in Context

    Duration: 50 minutes

    Type: Guided Practice

    WE DO (20 min)

    Solve two-step equations:

  • "Sam has x dollars. After receiving $5, he has $12. Find x." → x + 5 = 12, x = 7
  • "Sam has x dollars. After spending $5, he has $12. Find x." → x - 5 = 12, x = 17
  • "Sam has x dollars. After doubling his money and spending $3, he has $17. Find x." → 2x - 3 = 17, x = 10

    • YOU DO (15 min)

    Solve for x:

  • x + 5 = 12
  • 2x - 7 = 5
  • 3(x + 2) = 21
  • EXTENSION: "Sam has x dollars. After giving away half his money and $3, he has $10 left. Find x."

    LESSON 7.1 — Substitution

    Duration: 50 minutes

    Type: Elaborate

    I DO (15 min)

    "If x = -4, find 3x + 7."
    "3(-4) + 7 = -12 + 7 = -5."
    "If a = -3 and b = 5, find 2a + 3b."
    "2(-3) + 3(5) = -6 + 15 = 9."

    YOU DO (15 min)

  • If x = -3, find 4x + 5 = ___ 2x - 7 = ___

    • LESSON 7.2 — Two-Step Equations

    Duration: 50 minutes

    Type: Elaborate

    I DO (15 min)

    "Solve: 3x - 7 = 5."
    "Step 1: Add 7 to both sides. 3x = 12."
    "Step 2: Divide by 3. x = 4."
    "Solve: -2x + 5 = -3."
    "Step 1: Subtract 5 from both sides. -2x = -8."
    "Step 2: Divide by -2. x = 4."
    "Notice: dividing by a negative reverses the inequality sign direction."

    YOU DO (15 min)

  • 2x + 3 = 11 → x = ___
  • 4x - 5 = 7 → x = ___
  • -3x + 4 = -8 → x = ___
  • 2x - (-3) = 7 → x = ___
  • EXTENSION: "Solve: 5(x - 2) = -15."
