Learning Intention

We are learning to retell a familiar story in the correct sequence and answer questions about what we have read..

  • I can retell the beginning, middle and end of a story.
  • I can name the main character and where the story happens.
  • I can answer questions about a story using pictures and words.
  • I can talk about what happened in a story.
  • I can make a connection between the story and my own life.

Week 1 — GETTING TO KNOW THE STORY

Lesson 1: Cold Task (Assessment)

30 min Assessment
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- ---------------- ---------
Hook (5 min) Show the cover of *The Gruffalo*. Ask: What do you think this story might be about? Share predictions
Read (10 min) Read *The Gruffalo* aloud with expression. Show illustrations. Listen, look, follow
Task (15 min) Cold Task: Draw and write about the story of *The Gruffalo*. Show the beginning, middle and end. (Same task as Hot Task.) Independent drawing and writing
DifferentiationSentence starters available. Visual sequence cards for support.

Lesson 2: Reading The Gruffalo (I Do)

30 min Explicit Teaching
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- ---------------- ---------
Read (10 min) Reread *The Gruffalo* pp. 1–10. Stop at key moments: "What do you notice about the little mouse?" Point and share observations
Mini-Lesson (10 min) Introduce story vocabulary: Gruffalo, deep, dark, forest, terrified, screeches Repeat and mime new words
Share (10 min) "What was your favourite part so far?" Turn to a partner and share. Partner talk

Lesson 3: Who, What, Where? (We Do)

30 min Guided Practice
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- --------- ---------
I Do (5 min) Model: "In *The Gruffalo*, the WHO is the little mouse. The WHERE is the deep dark forest." Write on board. Observe
We Do (10 min) Together, identify WHO and WHERE using picture cards from the story. Add WHAT (mouse is scared, wants to walk). Contribute answers
You Do (15 min) Complete Who-What-Where worksheet with pictures and words Independent or supported work

Week 2 — STORY SEQUENCE

Lesson 1: Beginning, Middle, End (I Do)

30 min Explicit Teaching
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- ---------------- ---------
Read (10 min) Read *The Gruffalo* aloud again. Show a BME graphic organiser. Track story events
Mini-Lesson (10 min) BEGINNING: Mouse walks into the forest. MIDDLE: Mouse meets Gruffalo. END: Forest creatures are scared of the Gruffalo. Label on BME chart
You Do (10 min) Draw one picture for each part of the BME Sequential drawing

Lesson 2: Retelling with Pictures (We Do)

30 min Guided Practice
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- --------- ---------
I Do (5 min) Model using picture cards to retell *The Gruffalo* in order Watch and listen
We Do (15 min) Students sequence 8 picture cards in order with teacher support Order cards together
You Do (10 min) Retell the story to a partner using the picture cards Partner practice

Lesson 3: Our Story Map (You Do)

30 min Independent
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- --------- ---------
I Do (5 min) Show how to draw a story map: start, events, ending Observe
You Do (25 min) Draw your own story map for *The Gruffalo* using 6 boxes Creative independent work

Week 3 — CHARACTERS

Lesson 1: Who is in the Story? (I Do)

30 min Explicit Teaching
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- ---------------- ---------
Read (10 min) Read *The Gruffalo* again. List characters: Mouse, Gruffalo, all the forest creatures. Listen and watch
Mini-Lesson (10 min) Introduce character traits with picture cards: brave, clever, scared, friendly Match traits to characters
You Do (10 min) Colour and label the characters from *The Gruffalo* Character colouring activity

Lesson 2: Character Feelings (I Do → We Do)

30 min Explicit Teaching + Guided
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- --------- ---------
I Do (10 min) How does the mouse feel at the START? (scared) MIDDLE? (brave) END? (proud) Model with facial expression cards Observe and mimic
We Do (10 min) Together, make feeling faces for each character. How does the Gruffalo feel when he sees the mouse? Make faces and share
You Do (10 min) Draw the mouse's face at the beginning and end — how does it change? Compare emotions through drawing

Lesson 3: Draw Your Character (You Do)

30 min Independent
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- --------- ---------
I Do (5 min) Show how to draw the mouse step by step Follow drawing
You Do (25 min) Draw the mouse in detail. Add labels for character traits. Independent creative work

Week 4 — SETTING

Lesson 1: Where Does the Story Happen? (I Do)

30 min Explicit Teaching
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- ---------------- ---------
Read (10 min) Show forest pages from *The Gruffalo*. Identify: deep, dark, scary, tall trees, twisty paths. Describe settings
Mini-Lesson (10 min) Setting = WHERE and WHEN a story happens. In *The Gruffalo*, it is a deep dark forest. Record on chart
You Do (10 min) Find all the forest words in the story and highlight them Word hunt

Lesson 2: Forest Sounds and Sights (I Do → We Do)

30 min Explicit Teaching + Guided
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- --------- ---------
I Do (10 min) Close your eyes. I'm describing the forest... What can you HEAR? What can you SEE? Model using senses Close eyes, imagine
We Do (10 min) Build a class senses chart: Forest sounds (crunch, snap, hoot) / Forest sights (shadows, eyes, moonlight) Contribute ideas
You Do (10 min) Draw what you see and hear in the deep dark forest Creative drawing

Lesson 3: Create Your Setting (You Do)

30 min Independent
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- --------- ---------
You Do (30 min) Create a setting for your own story using the forest as inspiration. Use the 3 senses: See, Hear, Feel. Creative writing and drawing

Week 5 — STORY EVENTS

Lesson 1: What Happens First? (I Do)

30 min Explicit Teaching
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- ---------------- ---------
Read (10 min) Read the beginning of *The Gruffalo* (pp. 1–10). What happens first? Recall sequence
Mini-Lesson (10 min) Story events in order: Mouse is scared → makes a plan → meets creatures → meets Gruffalo → Gruffalo screeches Sequence on board
You Do (10 min) Cut and paste story events in order Sequencing activity

Lesson 2: The Problem (I Do → We Do)

30 min Explicit Teaching + Guided
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- --------- ---------
I Do (10 min) What is the problem in the story? The forest creatures want to eat the mouse! Identify problem
We Do (10 min) How does the mouse solve the problem? He says "Let's invite the Gruffalo!" Solution discussion
You Do (10 min) Draw and write the problem and the solution Written response

Lesson 3: How Does It End? (I Do)

30 min Explicit Teaching
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- ---------------- ---------
Read (10 min) Read the end of *The Gruffalo*. The mouse scares all the creatures away. He walks home safely. Listen
Mini-Lesson (10 min) Good story endings show what happened to each character. The mouse is happy. The creatures are gone. Discuss resolution
You Do (10 min) Draw the ending and write one sentence about what happened Sentence writing

Week 6 — MAKING CONNECTIONS

Lesson 1: My Connection to the Story (We Do)

30 min Guided Practice
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- --------- ---------
I Do (5 min) "Have you ever felt scared like the mouse? When?" Share a personal example. Listen
We Do (15 min) Turn and talk: What made you scared? What did you do? Share with your partner. Partner discussion
You Do (10 min) Draw and write about a time you felt scared Personal recount

Lesson 2: Similar Stories (I Do)

30 min Explicit Teaching
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- ---------------- ---------
Read (15 min) Read *Room on the Broom* by Julia Donaldson. Compare to *The Gruffalo*. Compare stories
Mini-Lesson (10 min) Both stories have: A brave character / A problem to solve / A happy ending Complete Venn diagram
You Do (5 min) Circle the things that are the same between the two stories Diagram activity

Lesson 3: Compare Two Stories (We Do)

30 min Guided Practice
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- --------- ---------
We Do (15 min) Complete a Venn diagram together: *The Gruffalo* vs *Room on the Broom* — same and different Group participation
You Do (15 min) Draw a picture of your favourite part from each story Creative drawing

Week 7 — DEEP READING

Lesson 1: Reread and Annotate (I Do → You Do)

30 min Guided → Independent
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- --------- ---------
I Do (5 min) Show how to use a "reading star" — mark favourite words, hard words, pictures we like Observe
You Do (25 min) Reread *The Gruffalo* with a reading star. Mark: ⭐ favourite part, ⚠️ hard word, 🖼️ best picture Independent reading and marking

Lesson 2: Reader's Theatre (We Do)

30 min Performance
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- --------- ---------
I Do (5 min) Assign roles: Narrator, Mouse, Gruffalo, creatures. Read lines aloud with expression. Role assignment
We Do (20 min) Perform *The Gruffalo* as a class. Use expression and voices. Group performance
You Do (5 min) Which role was your favourite? Why? Reflection

Lesson 3: Retell Practice (You Do)

30 min Independent
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- --------- ---------
You Do (30 min) Practice retelling *The Gruffalo* to a partner using picture cards. Record yourself retelling on the iPad. Peer retelling + recording

Criterion A (Excellent) B (Strong)
----------- --------------- ------------

**Character Identification** Names all characters correctly and describes their traits. Names main characters and one trait.
**Text Understanding** Answers comprehension questions with relevant evidence from text and illustrations. Answers most comprehension questions correctly.
**Making Connections** Makes clear, relevant personal connections and explains how they relate to the story. Makes a personal connection to the story.
**Recall and Detail** Recalls and includes specific and relevant details from the story. Recalls main events with some details.
--- --- ---
**Sequence** Draws/writes all major story events in correct order with detail. Draws/writes events in order with main events included.
**Characters** Names all characters with accurate trait descriptions. Names main characters.
**Connections** Makes multiple text-to-self connections with clear explanations. Makes one relevant connection.
