Unit Overview

Learning Intention:

We are learning to retell a familiar story in the correct sequence and answer questions about what we have read.

Success Criteria:

  • I can retell the beginning, middle and end of a story.
  • I can name the main character and where the story happens.
  • I can answer questions about a story using pictures and words.
  • I can talk about what happened in a story.
  • I can make a connection between the story and my own life.

    WEEK 1 — GETTING TO KNOW THE STORY

    Lesson 1: Cold Task (Assessment)

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Assessment

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Hook (5 min)Show the cover of *The Gruffalo*. Ask: What do you think this story might be about?Share predictions
    Read (10 min)Read *The Gruffalo* aloud with expression. Show illustrations.Listen, look, follow
    Task (15 min)Cold Task: Draw and write about the story of *The Gruffalo*. Show the beginning, middle and end. (Same task as Hot Task.)Independent drawing and writing

    Differentiation: Sentence starters available. Visual sequence cards for support.

    Lesson 2: Reading The Gruffalo (I Do)

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Explicit Teaching

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Read (10 min)Reread *The Gruffalo* pp. 1–10. Stop at key moments: "What do you notice about the little mouse?"Point and share observations
    Mini-Lesson (10 min)Introduce story vocabulary: Gruffalo, deep, dark, forest, terrified, screechesRepeat and mime new words
    Share (10 min)"What was your favourite part so far?" Turn to a partner and share.Partner talk

    Success Criteria Check: Can identify main character (little mouse).

    Lesson 3: Who, What, Where? (We Do)

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Guided Practice

    PhaseTeacherStudents
    I Do (5 min)Model: "In *The Gruffalo*, the WHO is the little mouse. The WHERE is the deep dark forest." Write on board.Observe
    We Do (10 min)Together, identify WHO and WHERE using picture cards from the story. Add WHAT (mouse is scared, wants to walk).Contribute answers
    You Do (15 min)Complete Who-What-Where worksheet with pictures and wordsIndependent or supported work

    WEEK 2 — STORY SEQUENCE

    Lesson 1: Beginning, Middle, End (I Do)

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Explicit Teaching

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Read (10 min)Read *The Gruffalo* aloud again. Show a BME graphic organiser.Track story events
    Mini-Lesson (10 min)BEGINNING: Mouse walks into the forest. MIDDLE: Mouse meets Gruffalo. END: Forest creatures are scared of the Gruffalo.Label on BME chart
    You Do (10 min)Draw one picture for each part of the BMESequential drawing

    Lesson 2: Retelling with Pictures (We Do)

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Guided Practice

    PhaseTeacherStudents
    I Do (5 min)Model using picture cards to retell *The Gruffalo* in orderWatch and listen
    We Do (15 min)Students sequence 8 picture cards in order with teacher supportOrder cards together
    You Do (10 min)Retell the story to a partner using the picture cardsPartner practice

    Lesson 3: Our Story Map (You Do)

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Independent

    PhaseTeacherStudents
    I Do (5 min)Show how to draw a story map: start, events, endingObserve
    You Do (25 min)Draw your own story map for *The Gruffalo* using 6 boxesCreative independent work

    WEEK 3 — CHARACTERS

    Lesson 1: Who is in the Story? (I Do)

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Explicit Teaching

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Read (10 min)Read *The Gruffalo* again. List characters: Mouse, Gruffalo, all the forest creatures.Listen and watch
    Mini-Lesson (10 min)Introduce character traits with picture cards: brave, clever, scared, friendlyMatch traits to characters
    You Do (10 min)Colour and label the characters from *The Gruffalo*Character colouring activity

    Lesson 2: Character Feelings (I Do → We Do)

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Explicit Teaching + Guided

    PhaseTeacherStudents
    I Do (10 min)How does the mouse feel at the START? (scared) MIDDLE? (brave) END? (proud) Model with facial expression cardsObserve and mimic
    We Do (10 min)Together, make feeling faces for each character. How does the Gruffalo feel when he sees the mouse?Make faces and share
    You Do (10 min)Draw the mouse's face at the beginning and end — how does it change?Compare emotions through drawing

    Lesson 3: Draw Your Character (You Do)

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Independent

    PhaseTeacherStudents
    I Do (5 min)Show how to draw the mouse step by stepFollow drawing
    You Do (25 min)Draw the mouse in detail. Add labels for character traits.Independent creative work

    WEEK 4 — SETTING

    Lesson 1: Where Does the Story Happen? (I Do)

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Explicit Teaching

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Read (10 min)Show forest pages from *The Gruffalo*. Identify: deep, dark, scary, tall trees, twisty paths.Describe settings
    Mini-Lesson (10 min)Setting = WHERE and WHEN a story happens. In *The Gruffalo*, it is a deep dark forest.Record on chart
    You Do (10 min)Find all the forest words in the story and highlight themWord hunt

    Lesson 2: Forest Sounds and Sights (I Do → We Do)

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Explicit Teaching + Guided

    PhaseTeacherStudents
    I Do (10 min)Close your eyes. I'm describing the forest... What can you HEAR? What can you SEE? Model using sensesClose eyes, imagine
    We Do (10 min)Build a class senses chart: Forest sounds (crunch, snap, hoot) / Forest sights (shadows, eyes, moonlight)Contribute ideas
    You Do (10 min)Draw what you see and hear in the deep dark forestCreative drawing

    Lesson 3: Create Your Setting (You Do)

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Independent

    PhaseTeacherStudents
    You Do (30 min)Create a setting for your own story using the forest as inspiration. Use the 3 senses: See, Hear, Feel.Creative writing and drawing

    WEEK 5 — STORY EVENTS

    Lesson 1: What Happens First? (I Do)

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Explicit Teaching

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Read (10 min)Read the beginning of *The Gruffalo* (pp. 1–10). What happens first?Recall sequence
    Mini-Lesson (10 min)Story events in order: Mouse is scared → makes a plan → meets creatures → meets Gruffalo → Gruffalo screechesSequence on board
    You Do (10 min)Cut and paste story events in orderSequencing activity

    Lesson 2: The Problem (I Do → We Do)

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Explicit Teaching + Guided

    PhaseTeacherStudents
    I Do (10 min)What is the problem in the story? The forest creatures want to eat the mouse!Identify problem
    We Do (10 min)How does the mouse solve the problem? He says "Let's invite the Gruffalo!"Solution discussion
    You Do (10 min)Draw and write the problem and the solutionWritten response

    Lesson 3: How Does It End? (I Do)

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Explicit Teaching

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Read (10 min)Read the end of *The Gruffalo*. The mouse scares all the creatures away. He walks home safely.Listen
    Mini-Lesson (10 min)Good story endings show what happened to each character. The mouse is happy. The creatures are gone.Discuss resolution
    You Do (10 min)Draw the ending and write one sentence about what happenedSentence writing

    WEEK 6 — MAKING CONNECTIONS

    Lesson 1: My Connection to the Story (We Do)

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Guided Practice

    PhaseTeacherStudents
    I Do (5 min)"Have you ever felt scared like the mouse? When?" Share a personal example.Listen
    We Do (15 min)Turn and talk: What made you scared? What did you do? Share with your partner.Partner discussion
    You Do (10 min)Draw and write about a time you felt scaredPersonal recount

    Lesson 2: Similar Stories (I Do)

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Explicit Teaching

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Read (15 min)Read *Room on the Broom* by Julia Donaldson. Compare to *The Gruffalo*.Compare stories
    Mini-Lesson (10 min)Both stories have: A brave character / A problem to solve / A happy endingComplete Venn diagram
    You Do (5 min)Circle the things that are the same between the two storiesDiagram activity

    Lesson 3: Compare Two Stories (We Do)

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Guided Practice

    PhaseTeacherStudents
    We Do (15 min)Complete a Venn diagram together: *The Gruffalo* vs *Room on the Broom* — same and differentGroup participation
    You Do (15 min)Draw a picture of your favourite part from each storyCreative drawing

    WEEK 7 — DEEP READING

    Lesson 1: Reread and Annotate (I Do → You Do)

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Guided → Independent

    PhaseTeacherStudents
    I Do (5 min)Show how to use a "reading star" — mark favourite words, hard words, pictures we likeObserve
    You Do (25 min)Reread *The Gruffalo* with a reading star. Mark: ⭐ favourite part, ⚠️ hard word, 🖼️ best pictureIndependent reading and marking

    Lesson 2: Reader's Theatre (We Do)

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Performance

    PhaseTeacherStudents
    I Do (5 min)Assign roles: Narrator, Mouse, Gruffalo, creatures. Read lines aloud with expression.Role assignment
    We Do (20 min)Perform *The Gruffalo* as a class. Use expression and voices.Group performance
    You Do (5 min)Which role was your favourite? Why?Reflection

    Lesson 3: Retell Practice (You Do)

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Independent

    PhaseTeacherStudents
    You Do (30 min)Practice retelling *The Gruffalo* to a partner using picture cards. Record yourself retelling on the iPad.Peer retelling + recording

    WEEK 8 — HOT TASK AND CELEBRATION

    Lesson 1: Hot Task (Assessment)

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Assessment

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Hook (5 min)Show the cover of *The Gruffalo*. "Today we will retell this story again. Show everything you have learned!"Engage
    Task (25 min)SAME TASK AS COLD TASK: Draw and write about the story of *The Gruffalo*. Show the beginning, middle and end. We are looking for growth from Week 1.Independent retell

    Compare Cold Task → Hot Task using rubric. Note growth in sequencing, detail, vocabulary.

    Lesson 2: Comparing Our Work (We Do)

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Analysis

    PhaseTeacherStudents
    We Do (15 min)Show a Cold Task sample and a Hot Task sample side by side. What is different? What improved?Observe and discuss
    Share (15 min)Students share one thing they did better in their Hot TaskCelebration of growth

    Lesson 3: Celebration (We Do)

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Celebration

    PhaseTeacherStudents
    Celebration (30 min)Reader's Theatre performance for families. Display Hot Task work. Award "Story Retelling Star" certificates.Performance and recognition

    ADAPTATIONS

    EAL Students

  • Use home language for initial story retell
  • Provide picture cards for all vocabulary items
  • Allow oral retelling as alternative to written
  • Visual word wall for story language (deep, dark, scary, clever, brave)
  • Allow L1 transliteration for Cold/Hot Task
  • Preview story with L1 version when available
  • Use picture sequence cards during retelling

    • Gifted Students

  • Retell from the Gruffalo's perspective
  • Write a sequel to The Gruffalo
  • Compare three Julia Donaldson stories
  • Create a new character who enters the forest
  • Write a "wanted" poster for the Gruffalo
  • Design a map of the deep dark forest

    • Students with NEPs

  • Reduce expectations to 2-part retell (beginning/end)
  • Provide a visual story map template to complete
  • Allow single-word or picture-based responses
  • Extended time for all tasks
  • One-on-one reading support
  • Use physical picture card sequences for retelling

    • ASSESSMENT NOTES

    Formative Assessment (Ongoing)

  • Story map drawings
  • Partner retell observations
  • Character feeling charts
  • Exit tickets: "Draw the problem and the solution"

    • Summative Assessment

  • Cold Task → Hot Task comparison (same rubric, measure growth)
  • Reader's Theatre performance checklist

    • Tracking Progress

  • Cold Task score → Hot Task score
  • Specific skills improved (sequence, character, connections)
  • Student self-assessment (thumbs up/half/down for each success criterion)
  • Next semester reading goals
