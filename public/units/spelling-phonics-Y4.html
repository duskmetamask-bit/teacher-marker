Unit Overview

Learning Intention:

We are learning to use Greek and Latin roots to decode and spell complex words, apply spelling generalisations, understand word origins, and use a dictionary independently.

Success Criteria:

  • I can identify and spell words using common Greek roots (tele, graph, therm, geo, bio, etc.).
  • I can identify and spell words using common Latin roots (port, dict, scrib/script, struct, etc.).
  • I can apply spelling generalisations (-able/-ible, -ance/-ence, -ology/-ology).
  • I can understand and use prefixes and suffixes derived from Greek and Latin.
  • I can use a dictionary and understand word origins.
  • I can spell complex academic and domain-specific vocabulary accurately.

    We Do (35 min)Complete: bio + logy = biology, geo + logy = geology, psych + ology = psychology, tele + scope = telescope, micro + scope = microscope, ther + mom + eter = thermometer. Sort: -ology words vs -scope words vs -meter words. Write sentences.Guided

    WEEK 4 — ADVANCED SUFFIXES

    Lesson 1: -able, -ible (I Do)

    时间: 50 minutes | Type: Explicit Teaching

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Mini-Lesson (20 min)-ABLE = can be done, worth doing (readable, comfortable, capable). -IBLE = same meaning, different spelling (possible, terrible, visible). Rule: If the root word ends in a consonant + e, usually -able (note + able = notable). If the root ends in -ib, usually -ible (vis + ible = visible). Both mean "capable of being." From Percy: "impossible" (not possible — im + possible).Learn
    Activity (20 min)Complete: comfort + able = comfortable, note + able = notable, love + able = lovable, reli + able = reliable. poss + ible = possible, terri + ble = terrible, vis + ible = visible, audi + ble = audible. Sort into -able vs -ible columns. Explain why each spelling.Practice
    You Do (10 min)Write 6 -able and 4 -ible words. Circle the suffix.Written practice

    Lesson 2: -ance, -ence (I Do → We Do)

    Time: 50 minutes | Type: Explicit Teaching + Guided

    PhaseTeacherStudents
    I Do (10 min)-ANCE and -ENCE = the state or quality of. Perform + ance = performance. Exist + ence = existence. Guide + ance = guidance. Deter + ence = deterrence. Note: -ence is used with words ending in -ent (presence from present), but -ance is more common with other roots. Both form nouns from verbs/adjectives.Learn
    We Do (35 min)Complete: perform + ance = performance, exist + ence = existence, guide + ance = guidance, attend + ance = attendance, independ + ence = independence,Violence + ence = violence. Sort: -ance vs -ence. Note patterns.Guided
    You Do (5 min)Write 5 -ance and 5 -ence words. Circle the suffix.Written practice

    Lesson 3: -ology, -ologist (You Do)

Write words with -ology, -ologist, -ological: geologist, biologist, mythological. 4. Write a paragraph using 4 -ology words.Full practice

    WEEK 5 — PREFIX/SUFFIX MEANINGS

    Lesson 1: Inter, trans, super (I Do)

    Time: 50 minutes | Type: Explicit Teaching

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Mini-Lesson (20 min)Semantic prefixes: INTER- = between (intervene, interact, interject, intercept, interview, interval). TRANS- = across/through (transport, transform, transplant, transmit, transparent, translate). SUPER- = above/beyond (supernatural, superpower, superstar, supervise, supermarket, superstition). These prefixes change meaning predictably.Learn
    Activity (20 min)Build: inter +vene = intervene, inter + act = interact, trans + form = transform, trans + plant = transplant, super + natural = supernatural, super + vis + e = supervise. Write each. Identify prefix.Practice
    You Do (10 min)Write 8 words with inter, trans, super. Circle the prefix. Define each using the prefix meaning.Written practice

    Lesson 2: Ob, ad, com- (I Do → We Do)

    Time: 50 minutes | Type: Explicit Teaching + Guided

    PhaseTeacherStudents
    I Do (10 min)Latin prefixes that change form: OB- = against (obstruct, obvious, obscure, obstacle). AD- = toward/to (adjust, admit, adopt, advise, adventure, announce). COM-/CON- = together/with (connect, communicate, compact, collect, combine, contact). Note: AD- changes to ac-, ad-, af-, ag-, al- depending on the following letter.Learn
    We Do (35 min)Complete: ob + struct = obstruct, ad + just = adjust, ad + mit = admit, con + nect = connect, col + lect = collect, com + bine = combine. Note the spelling changes when AD- meets different letters. Write each.Guided
    You Do (5 min)Write 6 words with ob-, ad-, com-/con-. Circle the prefix. Note any spelling changes.Written practice

    WEEK 6 — ETYMOLOGY AND WORD ORIGINS

    Lesson 1: Word History (I Do)

    Time: 50 minutes | Type: Explicit Teaching

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Mini-Lesson (20 min)Etymology = the history of a word. Words come from Greek, Latin, Old English, French. "Telephone" (Greek: tele=far, phone=sound). "Demigod" (Greek demi=half + god=English). "Mythology" (Greek mythos=story, logos=study). "Olympus" (Greek mountain where gods lived). Understanding etymology helps with spelling — if you know the root, you can spell the word.Learn
    Activity (20 min)Research etymology of 8 words from Percy: democracy, philosophy, geography, biography, telephone, telescope, demigod, catastrophe. Write origin language and meaning. Use dictionary.Research
    You Do (10 min)Write 2 words and their etymology from your research.Written practice

    Lesson 2: Latin/Greek Connections (I Do → We Do)

    Time: 50 minutes | Type: Explicit Teaching + Guided

    PhaseTeacherStudents
    I Do (10 min)Greek and Latin often combine: "photograph" (Greek photo + graph). "Television" (Greek tele + Latin vision). "Biology" (Greek bio + Greek logy). Notice how roots from different languages combine in English.Learn
    We Do (35 min)Identify origins: photograph (Greek), television (Greek + Latin), automobile (Greek auto + Latin mobile), photograph (Greek), geographer (Greek geo + Greek graph). Trace each word's roots. Create etymology charts.Guided
    You Do (5 min)Write 3 words with mixed Greek/Latin origins. Explain the etymology of each.Written practice

    Lesson 3: Etymology Research (You Do)

    Time: 50 minutes | Type: Independent

    PhaseTeacherStudents
    You Do (50 min)1. Research 10 words from Percy Jackson (god, hero, quest, monster, immortal, curse, prophecy, underworld, demigod, power). Write origin language and original meaning for each. 2. Create an etymology map: Show how 5 words trace back to Greek or Latin. 3. Write: "Understanding etymology helps me spell because..."Full research

    ADAPTATIONS

    EAL Students

  • Provide Greek/Latin root charts with L1 translations
  • Focus on 3 roots per week (not all at once)
  • Allow verbal etymology explanations before written
  • Provide word family lists with roots

    • Gifted Students

  • Research etymology of Percy Jackson character names
  • Explore more roots (aqua, audi, cent, dec, du, uni)
  • Create etymology presentations
  • Explore homophones from different origins
  • Write etymology research essays

    • Students with NEPs

  • Focus on 5 roots only (tele, graph, geo, bio, port)
  • Provide word lists with roots highlighted
  • Use visual root cards
  • Extended time
  • Simplify: Focus on 3 roots + basic -able/-ible only
