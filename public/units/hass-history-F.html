Unit Content

My Story, Our Story — History Unit Plan

Unit Duration: 7 weeks × 3 lessons per week = 21 lessons

Year Level: Foundation

Curriculum Link: Australian Curriculum HASS v9 — History (Foundation)

Unit Big Idea: Everyone has a story. Your family, your home, and your community all have histories. By learning about our own stories, we learn about ourselves and how we belong.

Cold Task: Week 1, Lesson 1 — Draw and talk about yourself as a baby and yourself today

Hot Task: Week 7, Lesson 1 — Share your family history story using drawings, photos, and words

Unit Overview

Learning Intention:

We are learning to explore our own histories, understand how we have changed over time, and share the stories of our families and communities.

Success Criteria:

  • I can talk about who I am and what my name means.
  • I can describe at least 3 ways I have changed from a baby to now.
  • I can sequence events in my life using words like "before," "after," and "next."
  • I can listen to and share stories from my family history.
  • I can describe what it means to belong to a family and a community.
  • I can compare my life today with life in the past.

    WEEK 1 — ENGAGE: All About Me

    Lesson 1: Cold Task — All About Me

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Assessment (Formative Pre-Assessment)

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Hook (5 min)Show a photo of the teacher as a baby. "This is me when I was a baby. What do you notice? How am I the same? How am I different?"Observe and compare
    Introduction (5 min)"This term we are learning about OUR stories — me, you, our families, and our community. We will learn where we come from, how we have grown, and how we belong."Listen
    Cold Task (20 min)"Draw a picture of yourself as a baby (or when you were very little) and a picture of yourself today. Label at least 2 things that are the same and 2 things that are different. Talk to a partner about your drawings."Independent drawing

    Differentiation: Photo from home provided if needed. Sentence starter: "When I was a baby, I..." / "Now I can..."

    Success Criteria Check: Student produces a drawing comparing themselves as a baby and today.

    Lesson 2: Who Am I?

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Engage (Hook)

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Who Am I? (10 min)"We are all special people. We each have a name, a face, a family, and a story. Who are you? What makes you YOU?"Discuss
    Identity Web (10 min)Teacher creates a class web with student names and identities: name, age, hair colour, eye colour, favourite colour, favourite food, family.Contribute
    I Am Special (10 min)"Everyone in this room is special. We all have things that are the same and things that are different. Both are important. Being different makes our class interesting!"Understand
    Share (5 min)"Turn to your partner and tell them 2 things about yourself that you are proud of."Share

    Success Criteria Check: Can share at least 2 things that make them unique.

    Lesson 3: My Name Story

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Engage (Concept Introduction)

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Names Matter (10 min)"Your name is a very important part of your story. Every name has a meaning or a special reason. Some names come from family, some from special people, some from a meaning like 'brave' or 'loved.'"Listen
    Name Investigation (10 min)Share where your name comes from, its meaning, or why you were named it. "I was named after my grandmother because..."Share story
    My Name Story (10 min)"Ask someone at home about your name. Why were you given this name? What does it mean? Who named you? Share what you find out tomorrow."Take question home

    Differentiation: For children whose name meaning is unknown, focus on who named them and why. EAL students may share names from their home language.

    Success Criteria Check: Asks someone at home about their name story.

    WEEK 2 — EXPLORE: How I Have Grown

    Lesson 1: Baby Me and Me Now

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Explore (Investigation)

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    How Have We Grown? (10 min)"Look around the room. Everyone here has grown from a tiny baby. What changes happen as we grow? We get taller, we learn to walk, we learn to talk, we learn to read and write!"Discuss
    Before and Now (10 min)Show images: baby crawling vs child running / baby being fed vs child eating with cutlery / baby sleeping in cot vs child in bed. "What changed? What is the same?"Compare
    Draw My Changes (10 min)"Draw yourself at 3 stages of life: as a baby, as a toddler, and today. Write or say 1 thing that changed at each stage."Draw

    Success Criteria Check: Can identify at least 2 changes that happen as people grow.

    Lesson 2: I Can Do So Many Things!

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Explore (Investigation)

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Growing Skills (10 min)"Think about all the things you can do now that you couldn't do as a baby. What new things have you learned? Walking, talking, reading, writing, drawing, playing sport, riding a bike, making friends?"Think
    Skills Timeline (10 min)"Can you put these skills in order? Baby → Toddler → Now. When did you learn each one? First words, first steps, first day of school, learning to ride a bike."Sequence
    Share a Skill Story (10 min)"Tell your partner about one skill you learned and how you learned it. Did you practice? Did someone help you?"Share

    Success Criteria Check: Can sequence at least 3 skills in the order they were learned.

    Lesson 3: Growing Up Timeline

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Explain (Explicit Teaching)

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    What Is a Timeline? (10 min)"A timeline shows things in order — what happened first, next, and last. We use timelines to tell our story."Learn
    My Life Timeline (15 min)Distribute a timeline template with 5 events. Students draw or write: I was born / I started walking / I started school / I learned to ride a bike / Today.Create
    Share Timelines (5 min)3–4 students share their timelines with the class.Share
    Exit Ticket (5 min)"Write or draw: What is one thing that happened BEFORE you started school?"Write

    Differentiation: EAL students draw events rather than writing. NEP students use a template with visual prompts.

    Success Criteria Check: Creates a simple timeline with at least 3 life events in sequence.

    WEEK 3 — EXPLORE + EXPLAIN: My Family

    Lesson 1: Who Is in My Family?

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Explore (Investigation)

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Families Come in All Shapes (10 min)"A family is a group of people who love and care for each other. Some families have a mum and a dad. Some have one parent. Some have two mums or two dads. Some have grandparents, aunts, uncles, or other important people. All families are real families and all families matter."Understand
    My Family Drawing (10 min)"Draw the people in your family. You don't have to draw everyone — just the people who live with you or who are part of your special family."Draw
    Family Sharing Circle (10 min)In a circle, each child shares: "In my family we have..." or "I live with..." (optional to share). Teacher affirms: "Thank you for sharing your family."Share

    Note: Handle sensitively — some children may have complex family situations. Teacher affirms all families.

    Success Criteria Check: Draws or describes members of their family.

    Lesson 2: Family Roles and Relationships

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Explain (Explicit Teaching)

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Families Help Each Other (10 min)"In families, everyone helps. What do the people in your family do to help? Mums, dads, grandparents, siblings — everyone has a job. Some people cook, some people clean, some people drive, some people read stories, some people give hugs."Discuss
    Family Helps Chart (10 min)"What does YOUR family need to function every day? Food, clothes, clean house, love, transport, sleep, play. Who does what?"Discuss
    All Families Work (10 min)"Every family works hard to take care of its members. There is no RIGHT or WRONG way to have a family. Every family's work is valuable."Understand
    Draw My Family Working (5 min)"Draw one thing your family does together to help each other."Draw

    Success Criteria Check: Can describe at least 1 way their family members help each other.

    Lesson 3: Families Are Different and the Same

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Explain (Explicit Teaching)

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Different but Same (10 min)"All families have things that are the same and things that are different. Same: everyone loves each other, everyone needs food and shelter, everyone plays and rests. Different: different houses, different cultures, different foods, different traditions."Learn
    Family Traditions (10 min)"What special things does your family do? Birthday celebrations, special foods for cultural or religious events, holidays, weekends? These are family traditions."Discuss
    Same and Different Sort (10 min)Image sort: birthday cake, prayer, cultural clothing, family dinner, holiday. "Do all families do this? Some families? Label: All families / Some families."Sort

    Differentiation: EAL students share traditions from their home culture. Gifted students: interview a family member about a tradition and report back.

    Success Criteria Check: Can identify at least 1 tradition their family has and 1 thing all families have in common.

    WEEK 4 — EXPLAIN: My Home

    Lesson 1: Where Do I Live?

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Explain (Explicit Teaching)

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    My Address (10 min)"Everyone has an address — a place where you live. Your address tells people where to find you. What is YOUR address?" Write on the board.Learn
    Different Types of Homes (10 min)Show images: house, apartment, townhouse, caravan, houseboat, retirement village. "People live in many different types of homes. What type of home do you live in?"Observe
    Draw My Home (10 min)"Draw the outside of your home. We will put all our homes on a class map."Draw

    Success Criteria Check: Knows their home address and can draw their home.

    Lesson 2: My Home Then and Now

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Explain (Explicit Teaching)

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Homes Have Changed (10 min)"Homes in the past looked different from homes today. Long ago in Australia, some people lived in gunyahs, mia mias, and bark shelters. Now most people live in houses and apartments with electricity and running water."Listen
    Then and Now Photos (15 min)Show historical photos: 1950s Australian home / 1920s cottage / modern home. "What is the same? What is different? Both have a roof, walls, somewhere to sleep, somewhere to cook. But materials, size, and features have changed."Compare
    Write (5 min)"Write 2 sentences: How is a home today different from a home long ago?"Write

    Success Criteria Check: Can identify at least 1 way homes today are different from homes in the past.

    Lesson 3: Places in My Neighbourhood

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Explain (Explicit Teaching)

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    What Is a Neighbourhood? (10 min)"A neighbourhood is the area around where you live — the streets, shops, parks, and places you go. Your neighbourhood is part of YOUR story."Learn
    Places in Our Neighbourhood (15 min)Create a class map of the local neighbourhood. Identify: school, shops, park, home, other places. Teacher uses a simple map or Google Maps.Map
    Walk and Look (if possible) (5 min)Brief walk to observe key features of local streets. Otherwise, use photos.Observe

    Differentiation: EAL students draw their neighbourhood. Gifted students: note directions (near/far, left/right) on the map.

    Success Criteria Check: Can name at least 3 places in their neighbourhood and their approximate location.

    WEEK 5 — ELABORATE: Then and Now

    Lesson 1: How Was Life Different?

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Elaborate (Application)

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Life in the Past (10 min)"Let's think about how life was different for children 50, 100, or even more years ago. Long ago: no mobile phones, no TV, no computers, no electric lights, fewer cars, different schools."Listen
    Then vs Now Sort (10 min)Image sort: radio vs iPhone / horse and cart vs car / candlelight vs electric light / handwritten letter vs email / outdoor toilet vs modern bathroom. "Which is then? Which is now?"Sort
    What Would Be Hardest? (10 min)"If you lived 100 years ago, what would be hardest for you? Why?"Discuss

    Success Criteria Check: Can sort at least 3 items into then and now categories.

    Lesson 2: Technology and Change

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Elaborate (Application)

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Technology Changes Lives (10 min)"Technology is tools and machines that make our lives easier. Technology has changed A LOT. Every generation uses different tools."Learn
    Grandparent Interview Highlights (10 min)Share highlights from any grandparent/family interviews if students asked: "What was the biggest change you remember from your childhood to now?"Share
    How My Life Is Easier (10 min)"Write or draw 2 things in your life that technology has made easier: TV, phones, cars, washing machines, lights."Write

    Success Criteria Check: Can name at least 2 ways technology has made their life easier than in the past.

    Lesson 3: Games and Play Then and Now

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Elaborate (Application)

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Games Children Play (10 min)"All children in all times have played games. But the games were different. Long ago: hide and seek, tag, skipping, jacks, marbles, cricket with a homemade bat. Now: video games, apps, organised sports, trampolines."Listen
    Play a Traditional Game (10 min)Teach and play a traditional game: 4-square / elastics / hopscotch / stuck in the mud. Discuss: "Children have played these games for a VERY long time. They are still fun today!"Play
    Which Do You Prefer? (10 min)"If you could choose one game from the past or one game from today, which would you choose? Why? Share with your partner."Discuss

    Differentiation: EAL students draw their favourite game. Gifted students: teach the class a traditional game from their home culture.

    Success Criteria Check: Can play at least 1 traditional game and explain why it was played in the past.

    WEEK 6 — ELABORATE + EVALUATE: Our Community Story

    Lesson 1: Community Helpers

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Evaluate (Critical Thinking)

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Who Helps Our Community? (10 min)"Our community has people who help us every day. Teachers help us learn. Doctors help us stay healthy. Police keep us safe. Firefighters protect us. Shopkeepers give us food. Bus drivers take us places."Discuss
    Community Helper Visits (15 min)If possible, invite a community helper (teacher, school cleaner, librarian, school crossing supervisor) to visit and share what they do. Alternatively, use images and teacher sharing about community helpers they know.Listen
    Draw a Community Helper (5 min)"Draw a community helper and write their name and what they do."Draw

    Success Criteria Check: Can name at least 2 community helpers and describe what they do.

    Lesson 2: Our Community's Past

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Evaluate (Critical Thinking)

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Our Community Has a Past (10 min)"Our community did not always look like it does today. Buildings have been built and changed. New streets have been added. Shops have changed names. People who lived here before us shaped our community."Listen
    Old Photos of Our Community (15 min)Show historical photos of the local area from the library, council archives, or Trove (trove.nla.gov.au). Is this the same place? What has changed? What is still the same?Observe
    Draw One Change (5 min)"Draw one thing in our community that might have looked different 50 years ago."Draw

    Success Criteria Check: Can describe at least 1 change that has happened in their community.

    Lesson 3: How We Belong

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Evaluate (Critical Thinking)

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    What Does It Mean to Belong? (10 min)"To belong means to be part of something — to feel at home, to feel accepted, to feel like you matter. You belong to your family, your class, your school, your neighbourhood. Belonging makes us happy and safe."Understand
    Where Do I Belong? (10 min)"Think: Where do you belong? Family ✓ / Class ✓ / Football team? / Dance class? / Scouts? / Cultural community?"Think
    Belonging Circle (10 min)"We all belong to this class. We are a CLASS COMMUNITY. What do we do together that makes us a community? We learn, we play, we share, we help each other."Discuss
    Draw a Belonging (5 min)"Draw a place or group where you belong."Draw

    Differentiation: EAL students may draw their family and home culture community. Gifted students: draw multiple places they belong.

    Success Criteria Check: Can name at least 2 places or groups where they belong and describe why belonging matters.

    WEEK 7 — EVALUATE: Hot Task and Celebration

    Lesson 1: Hot Task — My Family History Story

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Summative Assessment

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Set Up (5 min)"Today is your Hot Task. You will share YOUR family history story. You may bring photos from home, drawings you made, or objects that tell your story. Tell us: Who is in your family? What is special about your family? How is your family part of our community?"Listen
    Planning (5 min)Students think about what they will share. Can use their drawings from previous weeks.Think
    Hot Task (20 min)Students create a "My Family History" display — drawings, photos, objects. They may talk to the class about their display or draw a picture of their family doing something special together.Create and share

    Assessment Note: Use rubric to assess. Compare to Cold Task for growth analysis.

    Lesson 2: Story Sharing

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Evaluate (Self-Assessment)

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Gallery Walk (10 min)Family history displays set up around the room. Students walk around looking at each other's work. Sticky notes: "I learned something new about..."Gallery walk
    Sharing Circle (15 min)"Who would like to share one thing from their family story? We listen respectfully — every family's story is special."Share
    Compare Cold and Hot Tasks (5 min)"Look at your Cold Task drawing from Week 1. What is different between that drawing and what you know now?"Compare

    Success Criteria Check: Shares at least 1 element of their family history story.

    Lesson 3: Celebration — Our Stories

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Celebration

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Certificate (10 min)Award "Family History Detective" certificates.Receive
    Class Book (15 min)"Our Stories" class book: each child contributes one page with a drawing of their family. Bound and placed in the class library.Contribute
    What We Learned (5 min)"What is one thing you learned about yourself or your family in this unit?"Share

    DIFFERENTIATION NOTES

    EAL Learners

  • Home Language: Students may share family stories in their home language with translation support.
  • Visual Presentations: Family history shared through drawings and photos rather than writing.
  • Partner Support: EAL students paired with supportive partners for sharing activities.
  • Cultural Sensitivity: Respect all family structures and cultural practices.

    • Gifted Learners

  • Research Extension: Interview 2 family members about changes they have seen in their lifetime.
  • Creative Extension: Create a family history book with multiple generations.
  • Museum Exploration: Visit a local museum to find objects from the past.

    • Students with NEPs

  • Reduced Tasks: Focus on 2–3 key events rather than full timeline.
  • Oral Option: All tasks may be presented verbally.
  • Extra Time: Additional time for all activities.
  • Word Banks: Provided for all written tasks.

    • ASSESSMENT NOTES

    Formative Assessment (Ongoing)

  • Cold Task review (Week 1)
  • Family drawing check (Week 3)
  • Timeline check (Week 2)
  • Neighbourhood map (Week 4)
  • Exit tickets (Weeks 2.3, 4.3)

    • Summative Assessment

  • Cold Task (Week 1, Lesson 1): Pre-assessment drawing. Establishes baseline.
  • Hot Task (Week 7, Lesson 1): Post-assessment. Family history display and sharing. Assessed against rubric.
  • Class Book (Week 7): Cumulative class publication.
