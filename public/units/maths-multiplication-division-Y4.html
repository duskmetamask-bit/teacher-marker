    A–E RUBRIC — Multiplication and Division (Year 4)

    Aligned to: AC9M4N03, AC9M4N04, AC9M5N04

    Criterion 1: Multiplication Facts (Fluency)

    GradeDescriptor
    **A**Recalls all facts to 10×10 instantly; any order; uses known facts to derive unknown facts efficiently
    **B**Recalls most facts reliably; uses strategies to derive unknown facts
    **C**Recalls facts with some prompting; uses strategies for ×6, ×7, ×8, ×9
    **D**Uses skip-counting or strategies for ×6-×9; not yet fluent
    **E**Relies on skip-counting or objects

    Criterion 2: Mental Strategies — 2-Digit × 1-Digit

    GradeDescriptor
    **A**Uses jump, split, and compensation strategies fluently for 2-digit × 1-digit; explains strategy choice; mental computation efficient
    **B**Uses jump and split reliably for 2-digit × 1-digit; beginning compensation; strategy choice explained
    **C**Uses jump or split with scaffolding; beginning to select strategies
    **D**Uses repeated addition or skip-counting for 2-digit × 1-digit
    **E**Unable to multiply 2-digit by 1-digit mentally

    Criterion 3: Standard Algorithm — 2-Digit × 1-Digit

    GradeDescriptor
    **A**Uses standard algorithm accurately for 2-digit × 1-digit with regrouping; records steps clearly; estimates to check
    **B**Uses standard algorithm reliably; regrouping attempted with minor errors
    **C**Uses standard algorithm without regrouping; beginning to apply with regrouping
    **D**Uses informal recording; algorithm attempted with significant support
    **E**Unable to use formal algorithm

    Criterion 4: 3-Digit × 1-Digit

    GradeDescriptor
    **A**Uses mental strategies and standard algorithm for 3-digit × 1-digit reliably; estimates; checks answers
    **B**Uses algorithm for 3-digit × 1-digit with minor errors; beginning to estimate
    **C**Uses algorithm for 3-digit × 1-digit with scaffolding; estimation developing
    **D**Uses 2-digit × 1-digit algorithm with support; 3-digit attempted with scaffolding
    **E**Unable to multiply 3-digit by 1-digit

    Criterion 5: Division — 2-Digit ÷ 1-Digit

    GradeDescriptor
    **A**Divides 2-digit by 1-digit with remainders; uses chunking or standard algorithm; checks using multiplication
    **B**Divides 2-digit by 1-digit reliably; handles remainders; checks most answers
    **C**Divides 2-digit by 1-digit with scaffolding; skip-counting used as backup
    **D**Divides using repeated subtraction with support
    **E**Unable to divide 2-digit by 1-digit

    Criterion 6: Factors, Multiples, and Squares

    GradeDescriptor
    **A**Finds all factors of numbers to 50; identifies multiples reliably; square numbers recognised; explains relationships
    **B**Finds factors and multiples reliably; beginning to identify square numbers
    **C**Finds factors with scaffolding; multiples identified using skip-counting
    **D**Identifies obvious factors with support
    **E**Unable to identify factors or multiples

    SUCCESS CRITERIA — STUDENT-FACING

    I can:

  • [ ] Recall all multiplication facts to 10×10
  • [ ] Multiply 2-digit numbers by 1-digit using mental strategies
  • [ ] Use the standard algorithm for 2-digit × 1-digit multiplication
  • [ ] Multiply 3-digit numbers by 1-digit
  • [ ] Divide 2-digit numbers by 1-digit (with remainders)
  • [ ] Find factors and multiples of numbers
  • [ ] Solve multiplication and division word problems

    • WEEK 1 — ENGAGE

    Focus: Cold Task + Mental multiplication strategies

    5E Phase: Engage

    LESSON 1.2 — Jump Strategy: 2-Digit × 1-Digit

    Duration: 50 minutes

    Type: Concept Introduction

    Learning Intentions

  • I am learning to multiply 23 × 3 using the jump strategy
  • I am learning to jump by tens and ones

    • Success Criteria

  • I can solve 23 × 3 = 69 using jump strategy

    • I DO (15 min)

    "23 × 3. Jump strategy: 23 × 3 = (20 + 3) × 3 = 20×3 + 3×3 = 60 + 9 = 69."
    "Jump: 23, 43, 63. Jump by 20 three times. Then 3, 3, 3. Three jumps of 3. Total jumps = 69."

    YOU DO (15 min)

  • 34 × 2 = (30×2) + (4×2) = 60 + 8 = ___
  • 17 × 5 = (10×5) + (7×5) = 50 + 35 = ___
  • 26 × 3 = ___
  • EXTENSION: "23 × 4 = ?"

    • LESSON 1.3 — Split and Compensation

    Duration: 50 minutes

    Type: Guided Practice

    I DO (12 min)

    Split: 34 × 3 = (30+4)×3 = 90+12 = 102

    Compensation: 19 × 5 = 20×5 - 5 = 100 - 5 = 95

    YOU DO (15 min)

  • 24 × 4 = (20×4)+(4×4) = 80+16 = ___
  • 18 × 5 = 20×5 - 2×5 = 100 - 10 = ___
  • 27 × 3 = (30×3) - (3×3) = 90 - 9 = ___
  • EXTENSION: "36 × 4 = ?"

    • WEEK 2 — EXPLORE

    Focus: Standard algorithm 2-digit × 1-digit

    5E Phase: Explore

    LESSON 2.1 — Standard Algorithm

    Duration: 50 minutes

    Type: Explore

    I DO (15 min)

    ```

    23

    × 7

    21 (3×7=21, write 1, carry 2)

    14_ (2×7=14, plus carried 2 = 16, write 16)

    161

    ```

    "Step 1: Ones. 3×7=21. Write 1 in ones. Carry 2 tens."
    "Step 2: Tens. 2×7=14 tens. Plus carried 2 = 16. Write 16."

    YOU DO (15 min)

  • 34 × 7 = ___ (4×7=28, carry 2; 3×7=21+2=23 → 238)
  • 26 × 8 = ___
  • 45 × 6 = ___
  • EXTENSION: "Use the algorithm for 67 × 7."

    • WEEK 5 — EXPLORE

    Focus: Division 2-digit ÷ 1-digit

    5E Phase: Explain

    LESSON 5.1 — Chunking (Repeated Subtraction)

    Duration: 50 minutes

    Type: Explain

    I DO (15 min)

    "72 ÷ 5. Chunking: subtract chunks of 5."
    "72 - 5×10=50 (10 chunks). Remaining: 22. 22 - 5×4=20 (4 more chunks). Remaining: 2."
    "10 + 4 = 14 remainder 2. Check: 5×14 + 2 = 70+2 = 72. ✓"

    YOU DO (15 min)

  • 85 ÷ 4: 4×20=80, remainder 5, 5÷4=1 r1 → 20+1=21 r1 (Check: 4×21+1=84+1=85 ✓)
  • 67 ÷ 5 = ___ r ___
  • 91 ÷ 6 = ___ r ___
  • EXTENSION: "Use chunking for 156 ÷ 8."
