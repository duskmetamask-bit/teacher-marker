Hot Task: Week 8, Lesson 1 — Same task, measure growth

Unit Overview

Learning Intention:

We are learning to construct simple sentences using capital letters, full stops, and common words, and to understand that words can be grouped to make sentences.

Success Criteria:

  • I can say a sentence and write it with a capital letter at the start and a full stop at the end.
  • I can identify the subject (who) and predicate (what happens) in a simple sentence.
  • I can use common nouns (naming words) and verbs (doing words) in sentences.
  • I can hear and write the initial sounds in simple words.
  • I can use question marks and exclamation marks with support.

    WEEK 1 — WHAT IS A SENTENCE?

    Lesson 1: Cold Task (Assessment)

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Assessment

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Hook (5 min)Show *The Very Hungry Caterpillar*. "I am going to say some things. If they are sentences, stand up. If they are not sentences, sit down." Model: "The caterpillar." (not) "The caterpillar ate a leaf." (yes)Physical response
    Read (10 min)Read *The Very Hungry Caterpillar* aloud. Notice how each sentence is complete.Listen
    Task (15 min)Cold Task (same as Hot Task): Look at 5 pictures from the story. Write or draw a sentence about each picture. Remember: A sentence has a capital letter at the start and a full stop at the end.Independent sentence writing

    Differentiation: Sentence starter cards. Picture support. Allow verbal responses.

    Lesson 2: Sentence or Not? (I Do)

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Explicit Teaching

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Mini-Lesson (15 min)A sentence is a group of words that tells a complete thought. Examples: "The sun is shining." (complete) vs "is shining" (not a sentence — incomplete thought). Show with actions: complete thought = full body, incomplete = hunch over.Learn distinction
    Activity (10 min)I say groups of words. Students vote: Is it a sentence or not? "The cat" (no) vs "The cat slept." (yes) Use 8 examples.Practice
    Share (5 min)"How do you know if something is a sentence?" — It has a WHO or WHAT and tells something about it.Discussion

    Success Criteria Check: Can identify complete vs incomplete sentences.

    Lesson 3: Saying Sentences Aloud (We Do)

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Guided Practice

    PhaseTeacherStudents
    I Do (5 min)"We say sentences before we write them." Say each sentence clearly with a voice. Show how to use your voice to show a sentence is finished (slow down at the end).Observe
    We Do (15 min)Practice saying sentences about *The Very Hungry Caterpillar* aloud. Students say a sentence about each page. Clap the sentence rhythm.Oral practice
    You Do (10 min)Draw a picture of one sentence from the story. Write the sentence underneath. Check: Does it have a who or what? Does it tell something?Sentence drawing

    WEEK 2 — CAPITAL LETTERS AND FULL STOPS

    Lesson 1: Big Letter, Stop Sign (I Do)

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Explicit Teaching

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Mini-Lesson (15 min)Every sentence starts with a BIG LETTER (capital). Every sentence ends with a STOP SIGN (full stop). Show: "i ate a leaf" → "I ate a leaf." The "I" is always capital. Show on chart.Learn rule
    Activity (10 min)Practice: "the caterpillar was hungry." → Add capital and full stop → "The caterpillar was hungry." Do 5 more together.Guided practice
    You Do (5 min)Write 3 sentences about the story using capital letters and full stops.Independent writing

    Lesson 2: Writing Our First Sentences (We Do)

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Guided Practice

    PhaseTeacherStudents
    I Do (5 min)Model writing a sentence about the story: "The caterpillar ate an apple." Sound out each word. Write with capitals and full stop.Observe
    We Do (15 min)Together, write 3 sentences about *The Very Hungry Caterpillar*: 1. The caterpillar... 2. He ate... 3. The butterfly... Each student writes in their book.Group writing
    You Do (10 min)Write 2 more sentences about the story on your own. Use capital letters and full stops.Independent practice

    Lesson 3: Sentence Hunt (You Do)

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Independent

    PhaseTeacherStudents
    You Do (25 min)Hunt through *The Very Hungry Caterpillar* for sentences. Find 10 sentences and copy them. Circle the capital letter. Underline the full stop.Sentence hunt
    Share (5 min)Share 3 of your favourite sentences.Discussion

    WEEK 3 — WHO AND WHAT (NOUNS)

    Lesson 1: Naming Words (I Do)

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Explicit Teaching

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Mini-Lesson (10 min)Nouns = naming words. WHO words (people, animals): caterpillar, mother, baby. WHAT words (things): apple, leaf, sun. Show with word cards.Learn concept
    Activity (15 min)Hunt around the room. "Name 5 things you can see." "Name 5 people in the room." Put words on a class noun chart: PeopleAnimalsThings.Hunt activity
    You Do (5 min)Write 3 nouns in each column of the noun chart.Written practice

    Lesson 2: Types of Nouns (I Do → We Do)

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Explicit Teaching + Guided

    PhaseTeacherStudents
    I Do (10 min)Three types of nouns: People (teacher, child) / Animals (caterpillar, bird) / Things (apple, sun). Sort picture cards into the 3 groups.Sort
    We Do (15 min)Find nouns in *The Very Hungry Caterpillar*. Sort them: PeopleAnimalsThings. Record in reading journals.Guided sorting
    You Do (5 min)Draw 3 pictures of nouns — one from each group. Label each.Creative activity

    Lesson 3: Finding Nouns (You Do)

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Independent

    PhaseTeacherStudents
    You Do (30 min)Read a short passage (5 sentences) from the story. Highlight or circle all the nouns. Write them in a list. Sort them: PeopleAnimalsThings.Independent noun work

    WEEK 4 — DOING WORDS (VERBS)

    Lesson 1: Action Words (I Do)

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Explicit Teaching

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Mini-Lesson (10 min)Verbs = doing words or action words. When we do something, we use a verb. "The caterpillar crawled." (crawled is a verb) "The sun shone." (shone is a verb). Show with actions: walk, run, jump, eat, sleep, fly.Learn concept
    Activity (15 min)Play Verb Charades: I say a noun, you act out what it does. Then I write the verb. Students add gestures to verbs.Active learning
    You Do (5 min)Draw yourself doing something. Write the verb underneath (ran, jumped, ate, sat).Verb drawing

    Lesson 2: Verbs in Sentences (I Do → We Do)

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Explicit Teaching + Guided

    PhaseTeacherStudents
    I Do (10 min)"The noun does the verb." The caterpillar ATE. The sun SHONE. The bee FLEW. Show how noun + verb makes a sentence.Model
    We Do (15 min)Match nouns and verbs to make sentences: CaterpillarateThe applesatThe sunshoneThe childslept. Make full sentences.Matching activity
    You Do (5 min)Write 3 sentences using a noun and a verb. Underline the verb.Independent writing

    Lesson 3: Verb Hunt (You Do)

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Independent

    PhaseTeacherStudents
    You Do (30 min)Read a passage from *The Very Hungry Caterpillar*. Find all the verbs. Write them in a list. Then use 3 of them in sentences.Independent verb hunt

    WEEK 5 — SIMPLE SENTENCES

    Lesson 1: Subject and Predicate (I Do)

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Explicit Teaching

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Mini-Lesson (10 min)Every sentence has two parts: SUBJECT (who or what the sentence is about) and PREDICATE (what the subject does or is). "The caterpillar crawled." WHO/WHAT = The caterpillar (subject). DID WHAT = crawled (predicate).Learn
    Activity (15 min)Split sentences: The sunshone. The applefell. The caterpillarate. Students say the two parts. Draw a dividing line.Practice
    You Do (5 min)Write 3 sentences. Draw a line between the subject and predicate.Independent practice

    Lesson 2: Building Sentences (We Do)

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Guided Practice

    PhaseTeacherStudents
    We Do (20 min)Build sentences with word cards: Subject cards (The caterpillar, The sun, The apple) + Predicate cards (ate a leaf, was bright, fell down). Mix and match to make sentences. Write them with capitals and full stops.Group construction
    You Do (10 min)Build 4 sentences of your own using your own subjects and predicates. Write them.Independent building

    Lesson 3: Expanding Sentences (I Do → You Do)

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Explicit Teaching + Independent

    PhaseTeacherStudents
    I Do (5 min)Short sentence: "The caterpillar ate." Expanded sentence: "The very hungry caterpillar ate a green leaf." We can make sentences longer by adding more words!Learn expansion
    You Do (25 min)Start with a short sentence: "The caterpillar slept." Add more words: "The caterpillar slept in a warm cocoon." Add again: "The fat, happy caterpillar finally slept in a safe, warm green cocoon." Write your expansions in your book.Independent expansion

    WEEK 6 — QUESTION MARKS AND EXCLAMATION MARKS

    Lesson 1: Asking Questions (I Do)

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Explicit Teaching

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Mini-Lesson (10 min)Sentences that ask a question end with a question mark (?). "What did the caterpillar eat?" "Where did the butterfly go?" Show the difference between a statement (full stop) and a question (question mark) using voice: statement = flat, question = rising.Learn punctuation
    Activity (15 min)Change statements to questions: "The caterpillar ate an apple." → "Did the caterpillar eat an apple?" "The sun is shining." → "Is the sun shining?" Do 5 together.Transformation
    You Do (5 min)Write 3 question sentences about *The Very Hungry Caterpillar*. Remember: capital letter start, question mark end.Independent writing

    Lesson 2: Exciting Sentences (I Do → We Do)

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Explicit Teaching + Guided

    PhaseTeacherStudents
    I Do (10 min)Exclamation mark (!) = shows strong feeling. "The caterpillar is out!" "What a beautiful butterfly!" "I am so hungry!" Use a loud voice for exclamation sentences.Learn
    We Do (15 min)Change sentences to exclamation sentences: "The butterfly is beautiful." → "What a beautiful butterfly!" "I am happy." → "I am so happy!" Practice with voice.Guided transformation
    You Do (5 min)Write 3 exclamation sentences about the story. Read them with loud, excited voices!Written practice

    Lesson 3: Punctuation Review (You Do)

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Independent

    PhaseTeacherStudents
    You Do (30 min)Review all punctuation learned: . ? ! Write 5 sentences about the story — at least one of each type (statement, question, exclamation).Punctuation review

    WEEK 7 — WORD ORDER

    Lesson 1: Words in Order (I Do)

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Explicit Teaching

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Mini-Lesson (10 min)Words must be in the RIGHT ORDER to make sense. "Caterpillar the ate apple" (wrong) vs "The caterpillar ate an apple" (correct). In English, we say WHO first, then WHAT they do.Learn order
    Activity (15 min)Scrambled words: I write words on cards in wrong order. Students rearrange to make sense: "big very was This caterpillar" → "This caterpillar was very big." Do 5 scrambles.Scramble activity
    You Do (5 min)Fix 5 scrambled sentences on a worksheet. Write them correctly.Independent correction

    Lesson 2: Scrambled Sentences (We Do)

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Guided Practice

    PhaseTeacherStudents
    We Do (20 min)Work in pairs to unscramble 8 sentences. Write each correct sentence with capitals and full stops. Check with the class.Pair work
    You Do (10 min)Create 3 scrambled sentences of your own for a partner to fix. Trade and solve.Creation

    Lesson 3: Story Sentences (You Do)

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Independent

    PhaseTeacherStudents
    You Do (30 min)Write a short story (4–5 sentences) about the caterpillar's journey. Use your best sentences: capital letters, full stops, nouns, verbs. Draw pictures to go with your sentences.Story writing

    WEEK 8 — HOT TASK AND CELEBRATION

    Lesson 1: Hot Task (Assessment)

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Assessment

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Hook (5 min)"Today we will show everything we know about sentences. You will write sentences about pictures, use capital letters and full stops, and show nouns and verbs."Engage
    Task (25 min)SAME TASK AS COLD TASK: Look at 5 pictures from *The Very Hungry Caterpillar*. Write or draw a sentence about each picture. Remember: A sentence has a capital letter at the start and a full stop at the end. Show you know about nouns and verbs.Independent sentence writing

    Compare Cold Task → Hot Task using rubric. Note growth in sentence construction, punctuation, noun/verb use.

    Lesson 2: Comparing Our Work (We Do)

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Analysis

    PhaseTeacherStudents
    We Do (15 min)Compare a Cold Task sample → Hot Task sample. What improved? Circle examples of capital letters, full stops, nouns, verbs.Analysis
    Share (15 min)Students share one thing they improved atCelebrations

    Lesson 3: Celebration (We Do)

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Celebration

    PhaseTeacherStudents
    Celebration (30 min)"Sentence Stars" display of student work. Award certificates for punctuation, noun knowledge, verb knowledge.Display and recognition

    ADAPTATIONS

    EAL Students

  • Use home language to explain sentence concepts
  • Provide sentence starters: "The ___ is ___" / "The ___ can __"
  • Allow verbal sentence construction before writing
  • Use picture cards for noun/verb identification
  • Allow L1 transliteration for assessment
  • Provide bilingual word lists for key vocabulary
  • Physical/gestural activities for verb learning

    • Gifted Students

  • Introduce proper nouns (names, places) — capital letter rule extension
  • Explore compound sentences (using "and")
  • Create simple poems using nouns and verbs
  • Write a sequel story with complex sentences
  • Explore contractions (can't, won't)
  • Introduce adjectives for sentence expansion

    • Students with NEPs

  • Reduce to constructing 2–3 sentences
  • Provide sentence templates with blanks to fill
  • Use large print and visual supports
  • Extended time for all tasks
  • One-on-one support for sentence writing
  • Allow verbal responses as alternative to written
  • Focus on one skill (capital letters OR full stops) per session
