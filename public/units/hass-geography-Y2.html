HASS/Geography Year 2 7 Weeks 21 Lessons

People live in different places around the world. The features of a place affect how people live — what they eat, what they wear, how they travel, and what they do for work and fun. Understanding places helps us understand each other.

Learning Intention

We are learning to describe how people's lives are shaped by the features of places and how people in different places meet their needs..

  • I can describe how people in at least 2 places meet their basic needs (food, water, shelter, clothing).
  • I can explain how the features of a place affect what people eat, wear, and do.
  • I can compare how people live in places that are similar and places that are different.
  • I can use sources (photos, stories, maps) to find out about places.
  • I can use directional language (near/far, north/south/east/west, inside/outside) to describe where places are.
  • I can ask and answer questions about places and the people who live there.

Week 1 — ENGAGE: How Do People Live?

Lesson 1: Cold Task — How Do People Live in Different Places?

40 min Assessment (Formative Pre-Assessment)
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- ---------------- ---------
Hook (5 min) Show two contrasting photos: a city in Asia and a rural village in Africa. "What do you see? How are the places different? How might people's lives be different too?" Observe and compare
Introduction (5 min) "Today we start a new unit about people and places. We will travel around the world without leaving our classroom. We will learn how the place you live in shapes how you live." Listen
Cold Task (30 min) "Draw two places where people live. Label: What do the houses look like? What is the weather like? How do people get food? How do people travel? Write 2 sentences: How are these two places different?" Year 2 expectation: drawing + 4+ labels + 2 sentences. Independent
DifferentiationImage word bank. Sentence starters: "People in this place live differently because..." / "Both places have..." / "One difference is..."

Lesson 2: Needs in Every Place

40 min Engage (Hook)
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- ---------------- ---------
What Do We All Need? (10 min) "Every person in the world needs the same things: food, water, shelter, clothing, safety, and love. No matter where you live — in Australia, Japan, Brazil, or Africa — everyone has these needs." Listen
Needs Sort (10 min) Show image cards: bread, rain jacket, house, tap, hat, fence. "All people need food, water, shelter, and clothing. But different places have different ways of meeting these needs." Sort needs
Needs Around the World (15 min) Show three examples: Australia (supermarket, tap water, brick house) / Japan (convenience store, tap water, wooden house) / Rural Kenya (market, river, mud hut). "Same needs, different ways of meeting them." Observe
Connect (5 min) "How do YOU meet your needs? How is it similar to or different from how people in Japan or Kenya meet their needs?" Think

Lesson 3: What Do People Need? — Inquiry Launch

40 min Engage (Concept Introduction)
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- ---------------- ---------
Geographer's Questions (10 min) "Geographers ask questions about places and people: Where is it? What is it like there? How do people live? How is it similar to or different from our place? We will use these questions all unit." Learn inquiry questions
Inquiry Journal (10 min) Distribute inquiry journals (simple booklet). "This is your inquiry journal. Every lesson, you will write or draw something new. You will have questions you want to answer about places." Receive journals
My Question (10 min) "Write your BIG question for this unit. Something like: 'How do children in [place] live differently from me?' 'Why do houses look different in different places?' 'What do people eat in [place]?'" Write inquiry question
Share (10 min) 3–4 students share their inquiry questions. Teacher writes them on chart paper for ongoing reference. Share questions
DifferentiationEAL students draw their inquiry question. Gifted students: write 2 inquiry questions.

Week 2 — EXPLORE: Places Around the World

Lesson 1: Visiting 3 Places

40 min Explore (Investigation)
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- ---------------- ---------
Virtual Visit Introduction (5 min) "We are going on a virtual field trip today. We will visit 3 places: an island in the Philippines, a city in Canada, and a town in outback Australia. Pack your geo-eyes!" Prepare
Place 1: Island Philippines (10 min) Show images: tropical island, stilt houses over water, fishing boats, palm trees, market. "This island is hot and near the ocean. People fish for food. Houses are built on stilts over water." Observe
Place 2: City Canada (10 min) Show images: Toronto skyline, snowy winter, subway, multicultural food markets, brick houses. "Toronto is a cold city with snow in winter. People work in offices. Food comes from farms and stores." Observe
Place 3: Outback Australia (10 min) Show images: red earth, sparse trees, cattle station, windmill, small community. "The outback is very dry. People live far apart. Some work on cattle stations. Water comes from bores." Observe
Compare (5 min) "Which of these 3 places would you most like to visit? Why?" Share

Lesson 2: Hot, Cold, and In Between — Climate Zones

40 min Explore (Investigation)
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- ---------------- ---------
Hot, Cold, and In Between (10 min) "The world has different climate zones — hot near the equator, cold near the poles, and temperate in between. Our location on the globe determines our climate." Listen
Climate Zone Map (10 min) Show a world climate map. Equator marked in red. Identify: tropical (hot and wet), arid (hot and dry), temperate (mild), cold (polar), Mediterranean. Point to Australia (varied), Canada (cold/temperate), Brazil (tropical), Japan (temperate). Observe
What Grows Where? (10 min) "Climate determines what grows. In hot wet places: mangoes, bananas, rice. In cold places: wheat, potatoes, apples. In between: a mix. Climate shapes food." Connect climate to food
Climate Zone Sort (10 min) Image sort: palm tree (tropical), wheat field (temperate), cactus (arid), iceberg (polar). Which climate zone does each belong to? Sort

Lesson 3: What Grows Where and Why?

40 min Explain (Explicit Teaching)
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- ---------------- ---------
Plants Suit Their Climate (10 min) "Plants are matched to their climate. Cacti store water for hot dry climates. Rainforest trees have broad leaves for wet tropical climates. Apple trees need cold winters to grow well. Nature matches plants to places." Listen
Food and Climate Connection (15 min) Show: bananas (tropical countries), wheat (temperate/prairie countries), rice (tropical Asia), potatoes (cold climates). "Where do these foods grow best? What climate do they need?" Connect food to climate
Our Australian Food Map (10 min) "In Australia, what grows near us? WA produces wheat, barley, wool, lamb, seafood. Tropical north produces mangoes, bananas, sugarcane. Climate shapes what we eat." Learn local food geography
Write (5 min) "Write 2 sentences: Why does rice grow in hot wet places? Why does wheat grow in temperate places?" Write
DifferentiationEAL students draw the food-climate connection. Gifted students: map a week's worth of meals to where the food came from.

Week 3 — EXPLORE + EXPLAIN: Food and Shelter

Lesson 1: Food in Different Places

40 min Explore (Investigation)
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- ---------------- ---------
Where Does Food Come From? (10 min) "All food comes from places. Milk comes from cows that eat grass. Bread comes from wheat that grows in fields. Fish comes from the ocean or rivers. Food has a geography." Listen
Markets Around the World (15 min) Show market images: Thai floating market (boats, tropical produce), European farmers market (cheese, bread, vegetables), African village market (grains, local produce, spices), Australian supermarket (global food, variety). Describe each. Observe
Food Geography Investigation (10 min) "Choose one food you ate yesterday. Where did it come from? Use the Food Origin Map (classroom display) or your knowledge." Trace food origins. Investigate
Write (5 min) "Write 2 sentences: Where did one food you ate yesterday come from? How did it travel to you?" Write

Lesson 2: Homes Around the World

40 min Explain (Explicit Teaching)
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- ---------------- ---------
Homes Are Built for Their Places (10 min) "Every home is built to suit its place and climate. The materials used, the shape of the roof, the size of windows — all respond to the local environment." Listen
Homes Gallery (15 min) Show 6 types: Igloo (Arctic — snow insulates, dome shape resists wind) / Moroccan riad (thick stone walls, central courtyard, small windows) / Australian outback homestead (corrugated iron, verandas, thick walls) / Balinese home (open-sided, raised floor, tropical ventilation) / Norwegian stave house (steep roof for snow, wood) / Brazilian favela (informal, recycled materials, densely packed). Describe each. Observe
Why Are They Different? (10 min) "Homes look different because of CLIMATE, AVAILABLE MATERIALS, CULTURE, and NUMBER OF PEOPLE. In snowy Norway, you need a steep roof. In hot Morocco, you need thick walls. In tropical Bali, you need open airflow." Understand reasons
Draw Your Ideal Home (5 min) "Design a home suited to our local Perth/Australian climate. Label 2 features that suit our weather." Design

Lesson 3: What We Wear and Why

40 min Explain (Explicit Teaching)
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- ---------------- ---------
Clothing Has Geography (10 min) "What you wear depends on where you live. In hot places, people wear light cotton. In cold places, people wear wool and fur. In rainy places, people wear waterproof coats. Clothing is geographic." Listen
Clothing Around the World (15 min) Show images: Inuit parkas (fur-lined, hooded) / Arab thobe (long, loose, light-coloured for desert heat) / Indian saree (light cotton, colourful) / Scottish kilt (wool, for cold wet climate) / Brazilian carnival costume (light, bright, for tropical heat) / Japanese kimono (silk, layered). Describe each. Observe
Climate and Clothing (10 min) "Look at your own clothes right now. What is the weather like outside? What would you wear if you lived in the Arctic? What would you wear if you lived in the Sahara Desert?" Connect to self
Draw Outfit for a Climate (5 min) "Draw an outfit for a specific climate (Arctic, Desert, or Tropical). Label 1 feature that makes it suitable." Draw
DifferentiationEAL students draw and label with teacher support. Gifted students: research traditional clothing of a specific country.

Week 4 — EXPLAIN: How People Travel

Lesson 1: Getting from Place to Place

40 min Explain (Explicit Teaching)
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- ---------------- ---------
How Do You Get to School? (10 min) "How do you get to school? Walk, car, bus, bike? Your transport choice depends on distance, safety, cost, and what is available. In other places, children get to school very differently." Discuss
Transport Around the World (15 min) Show images: children in boats to school (Bali, Southeast Asia), school bus in USA, walking to school in rural Kenya, rickshaw in India, subway in Japan, camel in Sahara. "Same destination (school), very different journeys." Observe
Transport and Place (10 min) "Transport is shaped by PLACE. Rivers and oceans → boats. Deserts → camels or 4WD. Cities → trains and buses. Islands → ferries and planes. The terrain and geography of a place determines how people move." Understand connection
Draw a Journey (5 min) "Draw how you get to school or how someone in another place might get to school." Draw

Lesson 2: Transport and Place — Terrain and Infrastructure

40 min Explain (Explicit Teaching)
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- ---------------- ---------
Terrain Shapes Transport (10 min) "Mountains have winding roads and cable cars. Deserts have tracks through sand. Islands have boats and planes. Rivers need bridges or ferries. The land determines what transport is possible." Listen
Transport Solutions Around the World (15 min) Show: mountain railway in Switzerland / highway through Australian outback / ferry to Indonesian islands / dogsled in Arctic Canada / cable car in Venezuelan Andes. Describe terrain-transport match. Observe
Infrastructure (10 min) "Infrastructure is the built stuff that helps us travel: roads, bridges, railways, airports, ports. Countries with more infrastructure make travel easier. Some places have very little infrastructure." Understand infrastructure
Transport Map of Our Area (5 min) "What transport infrastructure do we have near us? Draw or list: roads, bus stops, train lines, paths." Draw

Lesson 3: Communication Across Places

40 min Explain (Explicit Teaching)
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- ---------------- ---------
How Do We Send Messages? (10 min) "People in all places need to communicate. How do you send a message to a friend? Phone, internet, letters? How did people communicate 100 years ago? Now let's see how places affect communication." Discuss
Communication Through Place (15 min) Show: satellite dishes in remote outback Australia / internet cafes in Africa / no internet in very remote places / letters carried by camels in Sahara historically. "Not everyone has the same communication options. Geography affects access." Observe
The Digital Divide (10 min) "Some places have fast internet. Some places have no internet at all. This is called the digital divide — the gap between people who have internet access and those who don't. It is affected by geography and money." Understand divide
Write (5 min) "Write 2 sentences: How does where you live affect how you communicate? What would communication be like if you lived somewhere without internet?" Write
DifferentiationEAL students draw communication methods from 2 places. Gifted students: research how one remote community communicates.

Lesson 1: Jobs in Different Places

40 min Explain (Explicit Teaching)
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- ---------------- ---------
Jobs Suit Places (10 min) "The jobs people do are shaped by WHERE they live. Near the ocean → fisherpeople. On farms → farmers and animal workers. In cities → office workers, teachers, doctors, builders. In forests → rangers, timber workers." Listen
Jobs Gallery (15 min) Show images: fisherman casting net (Southeast Asia) / cattle rancher on horse (Australia outback) / office worker (any major city) / market seller (Africa, Asia) / park ranger (any national park). For each: What place supports this job? Observe

Lesson 2: Children Around the World

40 min Explain (Explicit Teaching)
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- ---------------- ---------
Children's Lives Differ (10 min) "Children in Australia go to school, play sports, watch TV, eat dinner with family. Children in other places do similar things but in different ways. Let's compare." Listen
A Day in Children's Lives (15 min) Show 3 examples: Australian Year 2 child (school, swim training, dinner) / Child in rural Tanzania (walks 5km to school, helps with farming, plays outside) / Child in Japan (school, music class, bath). Compare: school, play, family time, chores. Observe and compare
What Is the Same? (10 min) "Despite differences, ALL children need food, water, shelter, education, play, and love. All children play games. All children go to school (where school is available). We share more than we differ." Discuss

Lesson 3: Games and Celebrations

40 min Explain (Explicit Teaching)
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- ---------------- ---------
Games Have Geography (10 min) "Every place has its own games and sports. In Australia: swimming, AFL, cricket. In Japan: origami, kendama, anime. In Brazil: football (soccer), capoeira. In Canada: ice hockey. In Scotland: shinty. Games reflect place and culture." Listen
Traditional Games (15 min) Show:sepak takraw (Southeast Asia — ball made of rattan) / kabaddi (India — tag game) / haka (New Zealand — Maori war dance) / Inuit blanket toss (Arctic) / Chinese dragon dance. Describe each. Observe
Celebrations Around the World (10 min) "Every culture celebrates. Chinese New Year (dragon dances, lanterns), Mardi Gras (Brazil/USA — carnivals), NAIDOC Week (Australia — Indigenous culture celebration), Diwali (India — festival of lights). Celebrations reflect culture and place." Observe
My Favourite Game (5 min) "What is your favourite game or sport? Is it popular in other places too?" Discuss
DifferentiationEAL students draw a game from their home culture. Gifted students: teach the class a traditional game from a culture studied.

Lesson 2: Urban and Rural

40 min Elaborate (Application)
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- ---------------- ---------
Urban vs Rural (10 min) "Australia has URBAN places (cities and towns) and RURAL places (countryside, farms, remote areas). Most Australians live in cities — about 86%! Only a small number live in rural and remote areas." Listen
Urban Life (10 min) Cities: lots of people, tall buildings, many shops, schools, hospitals, trains and buses, parks, entertainment. "Perth, Sydney, Melbourne — urban places." Note urban features
Rural Life (10 min) Countryside: fewer people, farms, wide-open spaces, long distances between towns, mining, cattle/sheep stations. "In the Wheat Belt or the Kimberley — rural places." Note rural features
Where Do They Live? (10 min) "Why do more people live in cities? Jobs, services, schools, hospitals, entertainment. Rural areas have fewer services but more space and important industries (farming, mining)." Discuss reasons
Compare (5 min) "Would you rather live in a city or the countryside? Why?" Write 2 sentences. Write

Lesson 3: First Australians and Country

40 min Elaborate (Application)
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- ---------------- ---------
Country Is Not Just a Place (10 min) "For Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander peoples, Country is much more than a location. Country is the land, water, plants, animals, and spiritual connections that belong to a particular group of people. Looking after Country is a cultural responsibility." Listen
Connection to Place (15 min) "Aboriginal peoples have lived in Australia for over 65,000 years — the oldest continuous culture on Earth. They knew every waterhole, every seasonal change, every plant and animal. Their knowledge of place is profound." Understand depth
Caring for Country (10 min) "Aboriginal fire management, marine management, and land management practices are now used in modern conservation. Indigenous knowledge and Western science together protect our environment better than either alone." Understand integration
Respectful Reflection (5 min) "Write 1 sentence: What does it mean to care for Country?" Write
