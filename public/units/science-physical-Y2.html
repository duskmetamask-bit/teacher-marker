Learning Intention

We are learning to identify different types of energy (light, sound, heat, movement), describe how energy makes things happen, and investigate how energy can be transferred from one place to another or changed from one type to another..

  • I can identify light, sound, heat, and movement as different types of energy.
  • I can describe how light helps us see, sound helps us hear, and heat helps us feel warm.
  • I can investigate how energy can be transferred (light from sun to Earth, sound from source to ear).
  • I can describe how energy changes form (movement to electricity to light).
  • I can predict what will happen when energy is transferred or changed.
  • I can use science words to explain energy and movement.

Week 1 — ENGAGE: What is Energy?

Lesson 1: Cold Task — Draw How Energy Makes Things Happen

40 min Assessment (Formative Pre-Assessment)
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- ---------------- ---------
Hook (5 min) Show a torch turned off and then turned on. "What made the light come on? ENERGY! What is energy? Where does it come from?" Observe
Task (30 min) Cold Task: "Draw something that uses energy. It could be: a light bulb, a speaker playing music, a heater, a car moving, the sun, a person running. Draw WHAT is using energy. Draw WHERE the energy comes from. Write 2–3 sentences explaining how the energy makes this thing work." Draw, label, write
Share (5 min) 3–4 students briefly share their drawings Share
DifferentiationEAL students draw and point to energy source. NEP students: draw one example with teacher scribing.

Lesson 2: Energy Is Everywhere

40 min Engage (Concept Introduction)
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- ---------------- ---------
Energy Walk (10 min) Walk around the school (or classroom). Identify things that USE energy: lights, fans, computers, voices, the sun, plants growing, a bouncing ball. "Is energy being used? Where does it come from?" Walk and identify
Energy Sources (10 min) "Where does ALL energy come from? THE SUN! The sun gives us light and heat energy. Plants use light to grow (food energy). Animals eat plants (food energy). Fossil fuels store ancient sun energy." Learn about energy sources
Four Types Introduction (15 min) "There are four main types of energy we will learn about: LIGHT (from the sun, bulbs), SOUND (from voices, speakers), HEAT (from the sun, heaters), MOVEMENT (from our bodies, engines)." Introduce as four categories. Learn four types
Sort Activity (5 min) Students receive cards: torch (light), voice (sound), fire (heat), running (movement). Sort into four types. Sort energy cards

Lesson 3: Types of Energy

40 min Engage (Vocabulary Building)
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- ---------------- ---------
Deep Dive into Types (15 min) "LIGHT: from the sun, light bulbs, torches. LIGHT helps us SEE. SOUND: from voices, instruments, speakers. SOUND helps us HEAR. HEAT: from the sun, heaters, fires. HEAT helps us feel WARM. MOVEMENT: from our muscles, engines. MOVEMENT helps us GO." Listen and categorise
Energy Detective (15 min) Students become "Energy Detectives." Walk to 5 stations in the room. At each station: identify what type of energy is being used. Record: STATION — TYPE OF ENERGY — WHERE THE ENERGY COMES FROM. Conduct energy audit
Share Findings (10 min) Class discussion: "What was the most interesting energy use you found? Where did that energy come from originally?" Share
DifferentiationEAL students draw energy types. Gifted students: research one energy source in depth — where does it come from and what type of energy is it?

Week 2 — EXPLORE: Light Energy

Lesson 1: Light Sources

40 min Explore (Investigation)
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- ---------------- ---------
What Makes Light? (10 min) "A LIGHT SOURCE is something that MAKES its own light." List: sun, light bulbs, torches, stars, candles, fireflies. "Most things are NOT light sources — they REFLECT light. Like the moon — it doesn't make its own light, it reflects sunlight." Learn about light sources
Natural vs Artificial Light Sources (15 min) Students sort cards: NATURAL (sun, fire, candle, stars, fireflies) vs ARTIFICIAL (light bulb, torch, phone screen, street light, TV). Sort light source cards
Light Source Hunt (10 min) "Find 5 light sources in the school. Are they natural or artificial?" Draw and label in science books. Hunt for light sources
Exit Ticket (5 min) "Is a mirror a light source? Why or why not?" Written response

Lesson 2: How Light Travels

40 min Explore (Investigation)
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- ---------------- ---------
Light Travels in Straight Lines (10 min) "Light travels in STRAIGHT LINES. Watch." Shine a torch through three cardboard sheets with holes. If holes aren't aligned, you can't see the light. Align them and light passes through. "Light must travel in straight lines!" Observe straight-line travel
Investigation (15 min) Students test: can light bend around corners? Shine torch at a wall — light only reaches directly exposed areas. Can light go through a tunnel? Test with bent vs straight tubes. Test light travel
Shadow Investigation (10 min) "If light travels in straight lines, what happens when something blocks it? SHADOW!" Test: place an object in front of torch. Move object closer — shadow gets bigger. Move object farther — shadow gets smaller. Investigate shadow formation
Record (5 min) Draw: light source → object → shadow. Label: LIGHT TRAVELS IN STRAIGHT LINES. Draw and label

Lesson 3: Light and Materials

40 min Explain (Explicit Teaching)
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- ---------------- ---------
Three Material Types (10 min) "Materials behave differently with light: TRANSPARENT = light passes through (clear glass, water). TRANSLUCENT = some light passes through (frosted glass, sunglasses). OPAQUE = no light passes through (wood, metal)." Demonstrate with flashlights and materials. Learn three types
Sort Materials (15 min) Students test materials: clear plastic, coloured plastic, tissue paper, cardboard, aluminium foil, mirror, water, oil. Test: does light pass through? Does it pass through clearly or partly? Record in table. Test and sort materials
Apply (10 min) "Why do we use frosted glass in bathrooms? (Translucent — you can see some light/shape but not detail.) Why do we use opaque curtains at night? (Block all light so we can sleep.)" Apply understanding
Exit Ticket (5 min) "Name one transparent, one translucent, and one opaque material in this room." Written response
DifferentiationEAL students physically sort materials. Gifted students: investigate why mirrors reflect (rather than transmit) light — what's special about reflective surfaces?

Week 3 — EXPLORE: Sound Energy

Lesson 1: Making Sounds

40 min Explore (Investigation)
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- ---------------- ---------
Vibrations Make Sound (10 min) "Sound is made by VIBRATIONS." Stretch a rubber band between two pencils. Pluck it — you can see it vibrating. Touch the jar with rice while a tuning fork vibrates near it — rice jumps. "When things vibrate, they make sound." Observe vibrations
Make Your Own Sounds (15 min) Students investigate: drum (tap and feel vibration), guitar (rubber band stretched over box, pluck), tambourine (shake), chime bar (hit and feel). After each: can you FEEL the vibration? Can you SEE the vibration? Explore sound-making
Vibration Observation Sheet (10 min) Record: WHAT I HIT/SHAKE — DID I FEEL A VIBRATION? — DID I SEE A VIBRATION? — LOUD OR QUIET SOUND? Complete observation sheet
Exit Ticket (5 min) "What makes sound?" Written response

Lesson 2: How Sound Travels

40 min Explore (Investigation)
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- ---------------- ---------
Sound Needs a Medium (10 min) "Sound travels through AIR. But can it travel through OTHER things?" Demonstration: ringing bell inside a jar — you can hear it. Remove air from jar (vacuum pump) — bell rings but you can't hear it. "Sound needs AIR to travel!" Observe sound without air
Sound Through Solids and Liquids (15 min) Test: tap a pencil on the table — can you hear it through the desk? (Yes — sound travels through SOLID.) Put head in water — can you hear someone tapping the pool? (Yes — sound travels through LIQUID.) "Sound can travel through solids, liquids, and gases." Test sound travel
Sound Wave Drawing (10 min) Draw: SOUND SOURCE (vibrating object) → waves traveling through AIR → EAR. Label: VIBRATIONS, SOUND WAVES, AIR. Draw sound wave diagram
Discussion (5 min) "Why can astronauts not hear each other speak in space? (No air = no medium for sound to travel through.) But they can talk using RADIOS — radio waves are light energy, not sound." Discuss

Lesson 3: Sound and Distance

40 min Elaborate (Application)
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- ---------------- ---------
Louder vs Quieter (10 min) Teacher claps loudly — all students hear. Whisper — students at back may not hear. "Sound gets QUIETER as it travels farther. The energy spreads out." Observe volume change
Distance Test (15 min) One student stands at front and whispers. Another student stands 1m away and reports what they hear. Move to 3m, 5m, 10m. At what distance can you no longer hear the whisper? Record results. Test hearing distance
Sound Barriers (10 min) "What can block sound? Soft things like curtains, foam, and carpets ABSORB sound. Hard things like walls REFLECT sound. Can you think of examples?" (Headphones block sound, earplugs absorb sound.) Explore sound barriers
Draw Your Learning (5 min) Draw: SOUND SOURCE → getting quieter as it travels farther → eventually cannot be heard. Label: SOUND TRAVELS IN ALL DIRECTIONS AND GETS QUIETER OVER DISTANCE. Draw and label
DifferentiationEAL students draw the distance test. Gifted students: research why echoes happen and what the speed of sound is.

Week 4 — EXPLAIN: Heat Energy

Lesson 1: Heat vs Temperature

40 min Explain (Explicit Teaching)
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- ---------------- ---------
What is Heat? (10 min) "HEAT is a type of energy that moves from HOT things to COLD things. HEAT is NOT the same as TEMPERATURE. Temperature tells us HOW HOT or COLD something is. Heat tells us how much ENERGY is moving." Learn distinction
Hot vs Cold Test (10 min) Place one hand in warm water, one hand in cold water. Then place both hands in lukewarm water. "Strange! The SAME water feels hot to one hand and cold to the other! This is why we need THERMOMETERS — they give us an accurate reading, not our feelings." Experience temperature
Thermometer Reading (15 min) Show different thermometers: digital, mercury, infrared. Read classroom temperature, temperature of warm water, cold water, ice. Record. Read thermometers
Record (5 min) "HEAT moves from hot things to cold things. TEMPERATURE measures how hot something is." Write in science books. Write definition

Lesson 2: Heat Transfer

40 min Explain (Explicit Teaching)
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- ---------------- ---------
Conduction Introduction (10 min) "Heat can MOVE. When you put a metal spoon in hot soup, the spoon gets hot. The HEAT moved from the soup to the spoon. This is CONDUCTION — heat moving through a SOLID." Learn conduction
Conduction Test (15 min) Materials on a metal rod: paper clip, pin with wax, butter. Heat one end of rod. Heat conducts to other end — paper clip gets hot, butter on pin melts, pin falls off. Test: copper vs steel vs glass rods. Test conduction
Insulation Introduction (10 min) "Some materials CONDUCT heat well (metals = CONDUCTORS). Some materials TRAP heat and don't let it through (wool, foam, air = INSULATORS)." Learn insulation
Insulation Test (5 min) Wrap ice cubes in: wool, plastic wrap, paper, cotton wool. Predict which will melt slowest. Observe after 10 minutes. Test insulation

Lesson 3: Insulators and Conductors in Real Life

40 min Elaborate (Application)
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- ---------------- ---------
Real-World Conductors (10 min) "We use CONDUCTORS in cooking — metal pots and pans conduct heat from the stove to the food. We use INSULATORS to keep heat in or out — woollen jumpers keep us warm, foam cups keep drinks hot." Learn real-world uses
Design Challenge (15 min) "Design a HOT DRINK CARRIER that keeps a hot drink warm. You can use: cardboard, bubble wrap, wool, newspaper, plastic. Which materials will you use? Why?" Draw design and label: CONDUCTOR or INSULATOR for each layer. Design solution
Share Designs (10 min) 3–4 students share their designs. Class votes on best insulator design. Share
Exit Ticket (5 min) "Why do we use plastic handles on metal pots?" Written response
DifferentiationEAL students draw and label their designs. Gifted students: research vacuum flasks (Thermos) — how do they keep drinks hot/cold for hours?

Week 5 — ELABORATE: Movement Energy

Lesson 1: Pushes and Pulls Review

40 min Elaborate (Review)
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- ---------------- ---------
Force Recap (10 min) "A FORCE is a PUSH or a PULL. Forces make things START moving, STOP moving, go FASTER, SLOW DOWN, or CHANGE DIRECTION." Review with examples. Review forces
Force Investigation (15 min) Students test: How much force is needed to move different objects? Use a Newton meter (or spring scale). Measure: book, pencil case, chair, heavy box. Record: OBJECT — FORCE NEEDED (in numbers). Measure forces
Fair Test (10 min) "To make this a FAIR TEST, what must we keep the same? (Same person pulling, same spring scale, same direction.) What changed? (The object.)" Discuss fair testing
Record (5 min) "The heavier the object, the MORE force is needed to move it." Write conclusion. Write conclusion

Lesson 2: Speed and Distance

40 min Elaborate (Investigation)
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- ---------------- ---------
What is Speed? (10 min) "SPEED is how FAST something moves. If something moves FAR in SHORT time = FAST SPEED. If something moves a SHORT distance in a LONG time = SLOW SPEED." Learn speed concept
Speed Test (15 min) Roll different balls (tennis ball, basketball, marble) down a ramp. Measure: distance rolled in 2 seconds. Which rolled farthest? Which was fastest? Test ball speeds
Variables (5 min) "To make this fair: same ramp height, same ball size? No — we ARE changing ball SIZE to see how it affects speed." Discuss variables
Record (10 min) Complete table: BALL TYPE — DISTANCE IN 2 SECONDS — FAST/MEDIUM/SLOW. Draw bar graph. Complete table and graph

Lesson 3: Friction

40 min Explain (Explicit Teaching)
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- ---------------- ---------
What is Friction? (10 min) "FRICTION is a force that SLOWS THINGS DOWN when they rub against each other." Demonstrate: slide book across desk vs slide book across carpet. Which slowed down faster? (Carpet — more friction.) Learn friction
Friction Investigation (15 min) Test how far different objects slide on different surfaces: wooden desk, carpet, tile, sandpaper, plastic mat. Same force applied each time. Record: SURFACE — DISTANCE SLID — MORE/LESS FRICTION. Test friction
Real-World Friction (10 min) "When is FRICTION USEFUL? (Shoes on ground, car tyres on road, writing with a pencil.) When is FRICTION A PROBLEM? (Skateboard wheels, waterslides, skiing — you WANT less friction.)" Discuss
Exit Ticket (5 min) "Why do we put oil in machines?" Written response
DifferentiationEAL students draw friction examples. Gifted students: research why ice is slippery and how ice skating works at the physics level.

Week 6 — ELABORATE + EXPLAIN: Energy Transfers

Lesson 1: Energy Chain

40 min Explain (Explicit Teaching)
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- ---------------- ---------
Energy Transfers (10 min) "Energy TRANSFERS when it moves from ONE thing to ANOTHER. The sun's light energy → transfers to a solar panel → becomes electrical energy → transfers to a light bulb → becomes light energy again." Draw energy chain on board. Learn energy transfer
Build an Energy Chain (15 min) Students receive scenario cards and build chains: (1) SUN → PLANT → ANIMAL → HUMAN → RUNNING → KICKING BALL (light → chemical → chemical → movement → movement). (2) BATTERY → TORCH → LIGHT + HEAT (electrical → light + heat). (3) SUN → FOOD → HUMAN → THINKING (light → chemical → electrical in brain → thought). Build energy chains
Draw Your Own Chain (10 min) "Design your own energy chain. Start with one type of energy. Show how it transfers to another type. Label each type of energy." Draw energy chain
Share (5 min) 3–4 students share energy chains Share

Lesson 2: Energy in Our Lives

40 min Elaborate (Real-World Application)
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- ---------------- ---------
Morning Energy Audit (10 min) "Think about your morning. You woke up (chemical energy from food). You turned on a light (electrical energy → light). You ate breakfast (chemical energy). You walked to school (movement energy). All day, energy is transferring around us!" Think about daily energy
Energy Transfer Hunt (15 min) Students walk the school and identify energy transfers: Solar panel → lights (light → electrical → light), Computer → screen (electrical → light + sound), Fan → air movement (electrical → movement). Record in table. Hunt for energy transfers
Write About It (10 min) "Describe one energy transfer you found in the school. What was the STARTING energy? What was the ENDING energy? Did any energy change type?" Write description
Exit Ticket (5 min) "Name one energy transfer that starts with light energy from the sun." Written response

Lesson 3: Energy Transformations

40 min Explain (Explicit Teaching)
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- ---------------- ---------
What is Transformation? (10 min) "Energy TRANSFORMS when it changes from one TYPE to another. Light → Electrical (solar panel). Electrical → Light (bulb). Chemical → Movement (food to muscles). Movement → Electrical (dynamo on bike). Movement → Heat (rubbing hands together)." Learn transformation
Transformational Machines (15 min) Identify what transforms energy: Hair dryer: electrical → heat + movement / Solar panel: light → electrical / Bicycle dynamo: movement → electrical / Speaker: electrical → sound / Fan: electrical → movement. Identify transformations
Energy Transformation Diagram (10 min) Draw: ENERGY INPUT → TRANSFORMER → ENERGY OUTPUT. Example: Electrical energy → Light bulb → Light + Heat energy. Draw transformation diagram
Apply to Week's Learning (5 min) "This week we learned: light travels in straight lines, sound needs a medium, heat conducts through solids, movement needs force. All of these are about ENERGY moving or changing!" Connect concepts
DifferentiationEAL students draw energy transformations. Gifted students: research the law of conservation of energy — energy cannot be created or destroyed, only transformed.
