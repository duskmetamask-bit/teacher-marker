UNIT AT A GLANCE

**Year Level**Year 4
**Strand**Number
**Duration**8 weeks × 3 lessons/week = 24 lessons
**Model**5E Instructional Model
**Pedagogical Frame**Gradual Release: I Do → We Do → You Do
**Big Ideas**Numbers to 100,000 can be added and subtracted using efficient mental and written strategies; rounding and estimation are essential tools; the standard algorithm extends with understanding of place value
**Driving Question***How do we add and subtract efficiently with very large numbers, and how do we know our answers are right?*

AUSTRALIAN CURRICULUM v9 — CONTENT DESCRIPTORS

General Capabilities (embedded throughout)

  • Numeracy — core
  • Literacy — problem-solving language, justification
  • Critical & Creative Thinking — efficiency, generalisation

    • BIG UNDERSTANDINGS

    Students will understand that:

  • Place value extends to 100,000 — each digit's value is 10× the place to its right
  • Mental strategies (compensation, jump, split) remain efficient even for large numbers
  • The standard algorithm is a compact recording of the mental strategies
  • Estimation to the nearest 10,000 or 1,000 is a reliable checking tool
  • Addition and subtraction are inverse operations — always verify
  • Real-world contexts require choosing the right operation and strategy

    • VOCABULARY

    Tier 2 (academic)

    place value, partition, regroup, compensation, efficient, estimate, round, reasonable, algorithm, digit, operation

    Tier 3 (mathematical)

    standard algorithm, mental computation, inverse operation, reasonableness, compatible numbers, place holder, partition notation

    LESSON 5.1 — Standard Algorithm: 5-Digit Subtraction

    Duration: 50 minutes

    Type: Explain

    Learning Intentions

  • I am learning to subtract 5-digit numbers using the standard algorithm
  • I am learning to handle multiple tradings

    • Success Criteria

  • I can solve 80,004 - 34,567 using the standard algorithm

    • I DO (15 min)

    ```

    80,004

  • 34,567

    • ```

    "Ones: 4 - 7. Can't! Trade tens → but tens is 0! Trade hundreds → hundreds is 0! Trade thousands → thousands is 0! Trade ten-thousands → 8 → 7."
    "7 ten-thousands → 6 ten-thousands + 10 thousands."
    "10 thousands → 9 thousands + 10 hundreds."
    "10 hundreds → 9 hundreds + 10 tens."
    "10 tens → 9 tens + 10 ones."
    "Ones: 14 - 7 = 7."
    "Tens: 9 - 6 = 3."
    "Hundreds: 9 - 5 = 4."
    "Thousands: 9 - 4 = 5."
    "Ten-thousands: 6 - 3 = 3."
    "Answer: 45,437."

    • LESSON 5.2 — Subtraction: Tricky Zero Cases

    Duration: 50 minutes

    Type: Explain

    I DO (15 min)

    "70,030 - 23,456."

    Walk through the full algorithm.

    LESSON 7.1 — Multi-Operation Problems

    Duration: 50 minutes

    Type: Elaborate

    I DO (12 min)

    "A school has two campuses. Campus A has 45,678 books. Campus B has 34,567 books. They receive a donation of 12,345 new books split evenly between campuses. How many books does Campus A have then?"
    "Step 1: 45,678 + (12,345 ÷ 2)? Wait — split evenly means each gets the same. But 12,345 is odd. Let's say: they receive 12,000 books split evenly. Each gets 6,000."
    "Campus A: 45,678 + 6,000 = 51,678."
    "Step 2: Combined: 45,678 + 34,567 = 80,245 total before donation. After: 80,245 + 12,000 = 92,245."
