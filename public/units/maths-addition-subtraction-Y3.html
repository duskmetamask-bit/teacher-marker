General Capabilities (embedded throughout)

  • Numeracy — core
  • Literacy — problem-solving language, mathematical explanation
  • Critical & Creative Thinking — strategy selection, justification, generalisation

    • BIG UNDERSTANDINGS

    Students will understand that:

  • Place value extends to thousands and enables flexible partitioning of large numbers
  • Mental strategies (compensation, jump, split) work for numbers of any size
  • The formal column algorithm is efficient but requires understanding of place value
  • Rounding and estimation help check answers before and after calculating
  • Addition and subtraction are inverse operations — always verify with the opposite
  • Real-world problems require selecting the right operation and strategy

    • ESSENTIAL QUESTIONS

  • How do we add and subtract efficiently with numbers to 10,000?
  • What is the difference between mental and written strategies?
  • How do we round numbers to make estimation useful?
  • Why does the column algorithm work?
  • How do we know when to add and when to subtract in word problems?

    • VOCABULARY — TIERED

    Tier 1 (everyday)

    add, subtract, plus, minus, total, difference, estimate, round, close to, about, check

    Tier 2 (academic)

    place value, thousands, hundreds, tens, ones, regroup, rename, compensation, jump strategy, split strategy, efficient, digit, round to nearest

    Tier 3 (mathematical)

    mental computation, written algorithm, column addition, column subtraction, trading, compatible numbers, inverse operation, reasonableness, partition, regrouping

    LESSON 1.2 — Place Value to 10,000

    Duration: 50 minutes

    Type: Concept Introduction (I Do → We Do)

    Resources: MAB blocks, place value charts

    Learning Intentions

  • I am learning to read, write, and understand numbers to 10,000
  • I am learning that thousands, hundreds, tens, and ones are the building blocks

    • Success Criteria

  • I can explain the value of each digit in a 4-digit number
  • I can build 4-digit numbers in different ways

    • I DO (15 min)

    Write 4,582 on the board.

    "4 thousands + 5 hundreds + 8 tens + 2 ones. 4,000 + 500 + 80 + 2 = 4,582."

    Build with MAB: 4 thousand-flats, 5 hundred-flats, 8 rods, 2 cubes.

    "The 4 is not worth 4 — it's worth 4,000. The 5 is worth 500. The 8 is worth 80. The 2 is worth 2."
    "How many thousands in 10,000? 10 thousands = 10,000."
    "What about 3,049? 3 thousands + 0 hundreds + 4 tens + 9 ones. Notice the 0 — we must write it!"

    WE DO (20 min)

    Place value challenge:

    Write the number:

  • 5 thousands + 3 hundreds + 7 tens + 6 ones = ___
  • 8 thousands + 0 hundreds + 4 tens + 1 one = ___
  • 6 thousands + 2 hundreds + 0 tens + 9 ones = ___

    • What is the number?

  • 7 thousands + 5 hundreds + 3 tens + 8 ones → 7,538
  • 9 thousands + 0 hundreds + 6 tens + 0 ones → 9,060

    • YOU DO (15 min)

  • Write the value of each digit in 6,294: ___ thousands = ___ ___ hundreds = ___ ___ tens = ___ ___ ones = ___

  • "True or false: 3,049 = 3 thousands + 4 tens + 9 ones. Explain."
  • EXTENSION: "What is the largest 4-digit number? The smallest? What is the largest 4-digit with a 0 in the tens place?"

    Type: Explore

    = 690

    LESSON 3.1 — Think-Addition Strategy

    Duration: 50 minutes

    Type: Explore

    Resources: MAB blocks

    Learning Intentions

  • I am learning to use think-addition to subtract 3-digit numbers
  • I am learning to find the difference by counting up from the smaller number

    • Success Criteria

  • I can solve 523 - 247 by finding what + 247 = 523
  • I can explain think-addition as counting up to the difference

    • I DO (15 min)

    "523 - 247. Think-addition: what + 247 = 523?"
    "247 + ? = 523"
    "247 + 200 = 447. Need 76 more. 447 + 70 = 517. Need 6 more. 517 + 6 = 523."
    "So: 200 + 70 + 6 = 276. 523 - 247 = 276."
    "Check: 276 + 247 = 276 + 200 = 476, +47 = 523. ✓"

    Learning Intentions

  • I am learning to subtract 4-digit numbers in column format
  • I am learning to trade when the digit being subtracted is larger

    • Success Criteria

  • I can solve 6,234 - 2,567 in column format
  • I can explain each trade

    • I DO (15 min)

    ```

    6,234

  • 2,567

    • ```

    "Ones: 4 - 7. Can't! Trade 1 ten → 10 ones: 14 - 7 = 7."
    "Tens: 2 tens - 1 (after trade) = 1 ten remaining, minus 6. Can't! Trade 1 hundred → 10 tens: 12 - 6 = 6."
    "Hundreds: 1 hundred - 1 (after trade) = 0 hundreds remaining, minus 5. Can't! Trade 1 thousand → 10 hundreds: 10 - 5 = 5."
    "Thousands: 5 (after trade) - 2 = 3."

    ```

    5 12 1 12 ← after all trades: 6000-1000=5000, 200-100=100, 30-10=20, 4+10=14

    6,234

  • 2,567

    • 3,667

    ```

    "6,234 - 2,567 = 3,667. Check: 3,667 + 2,567 = 6,234. ✓"

    • LESSON 5.2 — Subtraction: Zero as a Placeholder

    Duration: 50 minutes

    Type: Explain / Guided Practice

    Resources: MAB blocks

    Learning Intentions

  • I am learning to subtract when zeros appear in the minuend
  • I am learning that 3,004 means 3 thousands + 0 hundreds + 0 tens + 4 ones

    • Success Criteria

  • I can solve 4,002 - 1,567 in column format
  • I can explain why trading through zeros requires multiple trades

    • I DO (15 min)

    "4,002 - 1,567."
    "4,002 = 4 thousands + 0 hundreds + 0 tens + 2 ones."
    "To subtract in the ones: 2 - 7. Can't! Need to trade from tens — but tens is 0! Must trade from hundreds — also 0! Must trade from thousands."

    ```

    3 9 9 12

    4,002

  • 1,567

    • ```

    "Trade 1 thousand → 10 hundreds. Trade 1 hundred → 10 tens. Trade 1 ten → 10 ones."
    "Now: 4 thousands = 3 thousands + 10 hundreds. 0 hundreds = 9 hundreds (after trading 1 to tens). 0 tens = 9 tens (after trading 1 to ones)."
    "Ones: 12 - 7 = 5."
    "Tens: 9 - 6 = 3."
    "Hundreds: 9 - 5 = 4."
    "Thousands: 3 - 1 = 2."
    "Answer: 2,435."

    WE DO (20 min)

  • 5,004 - 2,567 = ___
  • 6,020 - 3,456 = ___
  • 7,000 - 2,345 = ___

    • YOU DO (15 min)

  • 4,003 - 1,567 = ___
  • "For 4,003 - 1,567, how many times did you need to trade? Explain."
  • EXTENSION: "What is 10,000 - 1?"

    • LESSON 5.3 — Problem-Solving with Subtraction

    Duration: 50 minutes

    Type: Guided Practice

    I DO (12 min)

    Problem type review:

  • Take away: Start with X, remove Y. How many left? → Subtract
  • Difference: Compare two groups. How many more/fewer? → Subtract (bigger minus smaller)
  • Part-part-whole: Whole is known, one part known. Find the other part. → Subtract

    • WE DO (20 min)

  • "A stadium has 8,456 seats. 3,789 are filled. How many are empty?"
  • "Sam has $4,500. A bike costs $2,345. How much more does Sam need?"
  • "The population of Town A is 6,234. Town B is 4,567. How many more people in Town A?"

    • YOU DO (15 min)

    Choose 2:

    Problem A: "Town A has 8,234 people. Town B has 3,567 fewer. How many in Town B?"

    Problem B: "A shop starts with 7,000 items. At the end of the week, 2,345 remain. How many were sold?"

    Problem C: "Max had $6,000. After buying a laptop for $3,456 and a chair for $789, how much does he have left?"

    LESSON 6.1 — Rounding to Nearest 100 and 1000

    Duration: 50 minutes

    Type: Elaborate / Concept Development

    Resources: Number lines, rounding posters

    Learning Intentions

  • I am learning to round 4-digit numbers to the nearest 100
  • I am learning to round to the nearest 1000

    • Success Criteria

  • I can round 3,456 to the nearest 100
  • I can round 4,567 to the nearest 1000

    • I DO (15 min)

    "3,456 to the nearest 100."
    "Is 3,456 closer to 3,400 or 3,500?"
    "Look at the tens digit: 5. 5 or more → round UP to 3,500. 4 or less → round DOWN to 3,400."
    "3,456: the tens digit is 5. 5 or more → round UP. So 3,456 ≈ 3,500."

    Number line:

    |---|---3,450---|---3,460---|

    3,400 ← midpoint 3,450 → 3,500

    "3,450 is exactly halfway. Our rule: 5 or more rounds UP."
    "3,567 to the nearest 1000: look at hundreds digit = 5 → round UP to 4,000."

    WE DO (20 min)

    Round to nearest 100:

  • 2,345 → nearest 100 = ___
  • 3,672 → nearest 100 = ___
  • 4,850 → nearest 100 = ___
  • 1,249 → nearest 100 = ___

    • Round to nearest 1000:

  • 3,456 → nearest 1000 = ___
  • 7,823 → nearest 1000 = ___
  • 5,500 → nearest 1000 = ___ (5,500 is exactly halfway — round UP to 6,000)
  • 2,499 → nearest 1000 = ___

    • YOU DO (15 min)

  • Round 4,567 to nearest 100 = ___. Nearest 1000 = ___.
  • "For 3,450, why might someone round to 3,000 instead of 3,400 or 3,500?"
  • "Circle the better estimate for 3,456 + 2,789: 5,000 / 6,000 / 7,000"
  • EXTENSION: "Round 49,999 to nearest 100, 1000, and 10,000. What do you notice?"

    • LESSON 6.2 — Estimation Before Calculating

    Duration: 50 minutes

    Type: Elaborate / Guided Practice

    Learning Intentions

  • I am learning to estimate BEFORE I calculate to help catch errors
  • I am learning that estimation gives a "ballpark" answer

    • Success Criteria

  • I can round numbers to estimate a sum before adding
  • I can round numbers to estimate a difference before subtracting

    • I DO (15 min)

    "Before I add 3,456 + 2,789, I estimate."
    "Round to nearest 100: 3,456 ≈ 3,500. 2,789 ≈ 2,800. 3,500 + 2,800 = 6,300."
    "My estimate is around 6,300. If my actual answer is 5,200 or 7,100 — I know something went wrong!"
    "Now I calculate: 3,456 + 2,789 = 6,245. Close to 6,300 ✓."

    WE DO (20 min)

    Estimate first, then calculate:

  • 4,567 + 2,345: Estimate ≈ ___, Calculate = ___
  • 6,789 + 3,456: Estimate ≈ ___, Calculate = ___
  • 8,234 - 3,567: Estimate ≈ ___, Calculate = ___

    • YOU DO (15 min)

  • Estimate, then calculate: 5,678 + 3,456 = ___
  • Estimate, then calculate: 9,000 - 4,567 = ___
  • "For problem 1, is your calculated answer close to your estimate? If not, what might be wrong?"
  • EXTENSION: "Estimate: 4,567 + 3,891 + 2,345 ≈ ___. How did you estimate?"

    • LESSON 6.3 — Using Estimation to Check Answers

    Duration: 50 minutes

    Type: Elaborate / Guided Practice

    I DO (12 min)

    "We estimate BEFORE. But we can also use estimation to CHECK AFTER."
    "I calculated 4,567 + 2,789 = 7,356."
    "Estimate: 4,600 + 2,800 = 7,400. My answer is 7,356 — close to 7,400 ✓."
    "But I calculated 4,567 + 2,789 = 7,256. That's 100 off from 7,356. My estimate would catch this error!"

    WE DO (20 min)

    Error detection:

    Each answer has an error. Use estimation to find it:

  • 5,678 + 3,456 = 9,134 (actual: 9,134 — is this correct?)
  • 7,234 - 3,567 = 3,677 (actual: 3,667 — find the error)
  • 4,567 + 2,789 = 7,356 (estimate: 4,600+2,800=7,400 — is 7,356 reasonable?)

    • YOU DO (15 min)

  • Calculate 6,789 + 4,567 = ___. Use estimation to check.
  • Calculate 10,000 - 4,567 = ___. Use estimation to check.
  • "Which is more reliable — estimation or exact calculation? Why do we need both?"
  • EXTENSION: "A student calculated 5,000 + 3,000 = 8,000 exactly. Is this always right? When might it be wrong?"

    LESSON 7.1 — Multi-Step Word Problems

    Duration: 50 minutes

    Type: Elaborate / Guided Practice

    Learning Intentions

  • I am learning to solve problems that require more than one step
  • I am learning to write intermediate steps clearly

    • Success Criteria

  • I can solve two-step and three-step word problems
  • I can identify what each step requires

    • I DO (15 min)

    "A school buys 3,456 maths books and 2,789 reading books. Each book costs $12. How much do they spend?"
    "Step 1: How many books in total? 3,456 + 2,789 = 6,245 books."
    "Step 2: Cost. 6,245 × $12 = ? Wait — this is multiplication. Let's simplify: each book costs $10. 6,245 × $10 = $62,450. Close enough!"
    "Alternative: 3,456 books + 2,789 books = 6,245. School spends $6,245 on maths books and $2,789 on reading books. Total: $6,245 + $2,789 = $9,034."
    "Two steps. Write each step. Don't combine them."

    WE DO (20 min)

    Multi-step problems:

  • "A stadium has 8,500 seats. 3,456 are filled for the morning session. In the afternoon, 2,789 more people enter. How many people in the stadium?"

    • Step 1: 8,500 - 3,456 = 4,044 empty. OR: 3,456 + 2,789 = 6,245 in stadium? Clarify interpretation.

  • "A warehouse has 7,000 boxes. 2,345 are moved to Store A. Then 1,678 more are moved to Store B. How many boxes are left?"
  • "Town A has 8,234 people. Town B has 3,567 fewer. Town C has 2,890 more than Town B. How many in Town C?"

    • YOU DO (15 min)

    Choose 1:

    Problem A: "Max earned $5,000. He spent $2,345 on a laptop and $789 on software. How much does he have left?"

    Problem B: "A library has 6,000 books. 2,345 are borrowed in Term 1. 1,890 are returned. 3,456 are borrowed in Term 2. How many books does the library have?"

    Problem C: "Town X has 8,234 people. Town Y has 3,567 fewer than Town X. Town Z has 2,890 more than Town Y. What is the total population of all three towns?"

    LESSON 7.2 — Operation Selection

    Duration: 50 minutes

    Type: Elaborate / Guided Practice

    I DO (12 min)

    Operation decision guide:

    SituationOperationSignal words
    Combine groupsADDaltogether, total, combined, more (coming)
    Remove/take awaySUBTRACTleft, remaining, take away, spent, sold
    Compare groupsSUBTRACTmore than, fewer than, difference, how much taller
    Find unknown partSUBTRACTwhat is left, how many did NOT...

    WE DO (20 min)

    For each problem, identify the operation AND solve:

  • "There are 4,567 adults and 3,890 children. How many people altogether?"
  • "A store had 9,000 items. 3,456 were sold. How many are left?"
  • "Sam has $8,234. Tom has $3,567. How much more does Sam have?"
  • "Town A has 8,234 residents. Town B has 3,567 fewer. How many does Town B have?"
  • "A school has 7,000 books. 2,345 are from the library. The rest are classroom books. How many classroom books?"

    • YOU DO (15 min)

  • For each: identify operation and solve.
  • Write your own problem for each of the 4 situations above.
  • EXTENSION: "Write a problem that requires BOTH addition and subtraction in the same problem. Hint: start with a total, remove some, add some back."
