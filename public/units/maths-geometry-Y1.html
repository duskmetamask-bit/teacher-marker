    • WEEK 1 — ENGAGE

    Focus: Cold Task + Shape properties

    5E Phase: Engage

    LESSON 1.2 — Describing 2D Shapes

    Duration: 50 minutes

    Type: Concept Introduction

    Learning Intentions

  • I am learning to describe 2D shapes by their properties
  • I am learning that shapes have different numbers of sides and corners

    • Success Criteria

  • I can describe a rectangle by its sides and corners

    • I DO (15 min)

    "SQUARE: 4 straight sides, 4 equal sides, 4 corners."
    "RECTANGLE: 4 straight sides, 2 long sides, 2 short sides, 4 corners."
    "TRIANGLE: 3 straight sides, 3 corners."
    "CIRCLE: 1 curved side, no corners."

    YOU DO (15 min)

  • Complete: Rectangle: ___ sides, ___ corners
  • "What is the same about a square and a rectangle? What is different?"
  • Draw a shape with 5 sides and 5 corners. What is it called?
  • EXTENSION: "Can a shape have no straight sides?"

    • LESSON 1.3 — Sorting by Properties

    Duration: 50 minutes

    Type: Guided Practice

    YOU DO (30 min)

  • Sort shapes by: NUMBER OF SIDES. How many groups?
  • Sort shapes by: CURVED OR STRAIGHT SIDES.
  • Sort by YOUR OWN rule. Tell the class.
  • EXTENSION: "Can you sort shapes by both rules at the same time?"

    • WEEK 2 — EXPLORE

    Focus: Symmetry

    5E Phase: Explore

    LESSON 2.1 — Line Symmetry

    Duration: 50 minutes

    Type: Explore

    Learning Intentions

  • I am learning that symmetry means one half matches the other half
  • I am learning to draw lines of symmetry

    • Success Criteria

  • I can draw the line of symmetry on a butterfly

    • I DO (15 min)

    "Fold a paper. Draw half a butterfly on one side. Cut it out. Open it!"
    "Both sides match! This is SYMMETRY."
    "The FOLD LINE is the LINE OF SYMMETRY. The shape is the same on both sides."
    "Some shapes have more than one line of symmetry."

    YOU DO (15 min)

  • Does a square have a line of symmetry? Draw it.
  • Draw 3 shapes that have line symmetry.
  • "Can a circle have more than one line of symmetry? YES / NO. Explain."
  • EXTENSION: "How many lines of symmetry does a rectangle have?"

    • LESSON 2.2 — Creating Symmetrical Designs

    Duration: 50 minutes

    Type: Guided Practice

    YOU DO (30 min)

  • Create a symmetrical design using coloured squares.
  • Fold paper. Draw half a heart. Cut it out. Open.
  • "Is your name symmetrical? Check using a mirror."
  • EXTENSION: "Create a design with TWO lines of symmetry."

    • WEEK 3 — EXPLORE

    Focus: Flips, slides, turns

    5E Phase: Explain

    LESSON 3.1 — Flips, Slides, Turns

    Duration: 50 minutes

    Type: Explain

    Learning Intentions

  • I am learning to identify and perform flips, slides, and turns
  • I am learning that a slide keeps the shape looking the same

    • Success Criteria

  • I can show a flip, a slide, and a turn

    • I DO (15 min)

    "SLIDE (translation): move the shape without turning. Points in the same direction."
    "FLIP (reflection): turn the shape over. Like a mirror image. Left becomes right."
    "TURN (rotation): rotate the shape. Point a different direction."

    YOU DO (15 min)

  • Draw a triangle. SLIDE it to the right.
  • Draw a triangle. FLIP it.
  • Draw a triangle. TURN it 90 degrees.
  • EXTENSION: "After 4 quarter turns, what does the shape look like? Is it the same?"

    • LESSON 3.2 — Half Turns and Quarter Turns

    Duration: 50 minutes

    Type: Explain

    I DO (12 min)

    "QUARTER TURN: turn to the next corner. Like 12 o'clock to 3 o'clock on a clock."
    "HALF TURN: turn to the opposite side. 12 o'clock to 6 o'clock."
    "FULL TURN: all the way around. Back to the start."

    YOU DO (15 min)

  • Turn your book 90 degrees (quarter turn). Which way is it facing?
  • Turn 180 degrees (half turn). Which way?
  • "After 4 quarter turns, have you made a full turn? YES / NO"
  • EXTENSION: "If you flip a square, does it look different?"

    • WEEK 4 — ELABORATE

    Focus: Maps and directions

    5E Phase: Elaborate

    LESSON 4.1 — Following Directions on a Map

    Duration: 50 minutes

    Type: Elaborate

    I DO (12 min)

    "A MAP shows where things are from above."
    "On this map: the tree is NORTH. The house is SOUTH. East is right, West is left."
    "From the house to the tree: go NORTH."

    YOU DO (15 min)

  • Draw a simple map: school, park, shop. Label N, S, E, W.
  • "Start at the school. Go 2 squares north, 1 square east. Where are you?"
  • Give directions from the shop to the park.
  • EXTENSION: "Can you give directions using only left and right, not north and south?"
