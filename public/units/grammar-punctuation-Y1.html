Unit Content

Grammar and Punctuation Unit — Year 1

Unit Duration: 8 weeks × 3 lessons per week = 24 lessons

Year Level: Year 1

Curriculum Link: AC9E1LY01, AC9E1LY02, AC9E1LY03, AC9E1SP01, AC9E1SP02

Focus: Sentence structure, common and proper nouns, verbs and tenses, punctuation (full stop, comma, question mark, exclamation mark)

Mentor Text: Where the Wild Things Are by Maurice Sendak (1963)

Cold Task: Week 1, Lesson 1 — Construct and punctuate sentences independently

Hot Task: Week 8, Lesson 1 — Same task, measure growth

Unit Overview

Learning Intention:

We are learning to construct sentences using subject-predicate structure, identify and use common nouns, verbs and adjectives, and apply punctuation conventions accurately.

Success Criteria:

  • I can write a complete sentence with subject and predicate.
  • I can identify and use common nouns (person, place, thing).
  • I can identify and use verbs in past and present tense.
  • I can use adjectives to add detail to sentences.
  • I can use full stops, question marks, exclamation marks and commas correctly.
  • I can use connective words (and, but, because) to join sentences.

    WEEK 1 — SENTENCE STRUCTURE

    Lesson 1: Cold Task (Assessment)

    Time: 35 minutes | Type: Assessment

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Hook (5 min)Show cover of *Where the Wild Things Are*. "Max causes mischief and sails to the land of the Wild Things. Today you will write sentences about this story."Engage
    Read (10 min)Read *Where the Wild Things Are* aloud. Stop to notice how sentences sound.Listen
    Task (20 min)Cold Task (same as Hot Task): Write 6 sentences about Max and the Wild Things. Use capital letters, full stops, question marks, or exclamation marks. Include nouns and verbs. Circle your nouns. Underline your verbs. Year 1: minimum 6 sentences.Independent sentence writing

    Differentiation: Sentence starter cards. Noun/verb word banks. Picture support.

    Lesson 2: Subject and Predicate (I Do)

    Time: 35 minutes | Type: Explicit Teaching

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Mini-Lesson (15 min)A sentence has two parts: SUBJECT (who or what the sentence is about) and PREDICATE (what the subject does or is). From *Where the Wild Things Are*: "Max sailed across the sea." SUBJECT = Max. PREDICATE = sailed across the sea. Model with 5 more examples.Learn
    Activity (15 min)Split sentences on cards: "The wild things made Mischief." → "The wild things" (subject)"made mischief" (predicate). Students identify both parts for 8 sentences.Practice
    Share (5 min)"How do you know which part is the subject and which is the predicate?"Discussion

    Success Criteria Check: Can identify subject and predicate in a sentence.

    Lesson 3: Writing Complete Sentences (We Do)

    Time: 35 minutes | Type: Guided Practice

    PhaseTeacherStudents
    I Do (10 min)Model writing a complete sentence: Max is king. Check: Is there a subject? (Max) Is there a predicate? (is king) Does it have a capital letter and full stop?Observe
    We Do (15 min)Write 5 sentences about the story. For each: Say it aloud. Identify subject and predicate. Write it. Check capitals and full stops.Group writing
    You Do (10 min)Write 3 sentences of your own about the story. Check each one: Subject? Predicate? Capital? Full stop?Independent writing

    WEEK 2 — COMMON NOUNS

    Lesson 1: Naming Words (I Do)

    Time: 35 minutes | Type: Explicit Teaching

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Mini-Lesson (10 min)Nouns = naming words. Common nouns = everyday words for people, places, and things. boy, dog, house, tree, city. Examples from *Where the Wild Things Are*: boy (Max), forest, ocean, crown, bed. vs Proper nouns (specific names): Max, Sendak — these are special names.Learn
    Activity (15 min)Sort nouns: dog, Perth, teacher, Mary, tree, hospital, cat, Africa. Common nouns vs proper nouns. Discuss: Common nouns can be any of that thing. Proper nouns are specific names and always have capital letters.Sorting
    You Do (10 min)Write 5 common nouns and 5 proper nouns. Label each type.Written practice

    Lesson 2: Types of Nouns (I Do → We Do)

    Time: 35 minutes | Type: Explicit Teaching + Guided

    PhaseTeacherStudents
    I Do (10 min)Three types of common nouns: PERSON (boy, mother, king) / PLACE (forest, ocean, room) / THING (crown, boat, bed). Show with picture cards from the story.Learn
    We Do (15 min)Find all the nouns in a page from *Where the Wild Things Are*. Sort into: PersonPlaceThing. Write in a three-column chart.Guided sorting
    You Do (10 min)Draw pictures of one noun from each column. Write the noun and its type underneath.Creative activity

    Lesson 3: Nouns in Sentences (You Do)

    Time: 35 minutes | Type: Independent

    PhaseTeacherStudents
    You Do (35 min)Write 6 sentences about *Where the Wild Things Are*. Each sentence must have at least one noun. Label the noun in each sentence: "The [noun]..." Highlight or underline all nouns.Integrated writing

    WEEK 3 — VERBS AND TENSE

    Lesson 1: Action Words (I Do)

    Time: 35 minutes | Type: Explicit Teaching

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Mini-Lesson (10 min)Verbs = action words — what someone or something DOES. From *Where the Wild Things Are*: roared, sailed, ate, slept, made (mischief), wore, ran. Model with actions: Say the verb and act it out.Learn
    Activity (15 min)Verb charades: I write a verb on a card. Students act it out. Class guesses. Then write the verb. Actions from the story: ROARED, SAILED, SLEPT, ATE, DANCED.Action game
    You Do (10 min)Write 5 action verbs from the story. Use each in a sentence.Written practice

    Lesson 2: Past and Present (I Do → We Do)

    Time: 35 minutes | Type: Explicit Teaching + Guided

    PhaseTeacherStudents
    I Do (10 min)Tense = when the action happens. PRESENT = happening now (roar, sail, eat). PAST = already happened (roared, sailed, ate). Add -ED for regular past verbs. Examples: "Max sails." (present) "Max sailed." (past)Learn
    We Do (15 min)Change present verbs to past: roast → roasted, dance → danced, jump → jumped, sail → sailed. Notice the -ED pattern. Make sentences with past verbs.Guided transformation
    You Do (10 min)Write 3 sentences in PAST TENSE about the story. Write 3 sentences in PRESENT TENSE. Label each.Written practice

    Lesson 3: Verb Hunt (You Do)

    Time: 35 minutes | Type: Independent

    PhaseTeacherStudents
    You Do (35 min)Read pages from *Where the Wild Things Are*. Find 10 verbs. Write them in two columns: Present tensePast tense. Use 5 of them in sentences.Independent verb hunt

    WEEK 4 — ADJECTIVES

    Lesson 1: Describing Words (I Do)

    Time: 35 minutes | Type: Explicit Teaching

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Mini-Lesson (10 min)Adjectives = describing words. They tell us MORE about a noun. "the wild things" → "the HUGE wild things" — HUGE is the adjective. From the story: enormous, fierce, sharp-toothed, terrible, dark.Learn
    Activity (15 min)Add adjectives to nouns: the dog → the fluffy dog → the small fluffy dog. the monster → the terrifying monster → the enormous terrifying monster. Do 8 examples together.Guided practice
    You Do (10 min)Write 5 adjective + noun combinations from the story. Circle the adjective. Underline the noun.Written practice

    Lesson 2: Adding Detail (I Do → We Do)

    Time: 35 minutes | Type: Explicit Teaching + Guided

    PhaseTeacherStudents
    I Do (5 min)"Adjectives make our writing more interesting." Compare: "Max had a meal." vs "Max had an enormous delicious meal."Observe
    We Do (20 min)Enhance sentences about the story. Start with: "Max wore a crown." → "Max wore a small golden crown." "The wild things roared." → "The enormous fierce wild things roared loudly." Build sentences together.Sentence enhancement
    You Do (10 min)Write 4 enhanced sentences about the story. Underline the adjectives. Circle the nouns.Independent enhancement

    Lesson 3: Adjective Hunt (You Do)

    Time: 35 minutes | Type: Independent

    PhaseTeacherStudents
    You Do (35 min)Read the story. Find all the adjectives. Write them in a list. Sort them: Size (huge, enormous, tiny) / Colour (dark, bright) / Feeling (terrible, fierce, wild) / Other. Use 5 in your own sentences about the story.Independent hunt

    WEEK 5 — CONNECTIVES

    Lesson 1: Joining Words (I Do)

    Time: 35 minutes | Type: Explicit Teaching

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Mini-Lesson (10 min)Connectives = words that JOIN ideas. Three important connectives: AND (adds more), BUT (shows contrast), BECAUSE (shows reason). "Max was small AND fierce." "Max was wild BUT not dangerous." "Max became king BECAUSE he was brave."Learn
    Activity (15 min)Complete sentences with AND, BUT, BECAUSE: "The wild things roared ___ Max made them stop." (but/because) "The ocean was dark ___ wild." (and/but) "Max went home ___ he was hungry." (because/but). Do 8 together.Practice
    You Do (10 min)Write 3 sentences — one using AND, one using BUT, one using BECAUSE.Written practice

    Lesson 2: Joining Sentences (I Do → We Do)

    Time: 35 minutes | Type: Explicit Teaching + Guided

    PhaseTeacherStudents
    I Do (5 min)Connectives can join two ideas in one sentence: "Max sailed away AND he did not come back." "The food was delicious BUT I was not hungry." "The monster was scary BECAUSE it had sharp teeth."Learn
    We Do (20 min)Join pairs of short sentences using AND, BUT, BECAUSE. 8 pairs about the story. Check: Does the joined sentence make sense?Guided joining
    You Do (10 min)Write 4 joined sentences about the story. Use at least one of each connective.Independent joining

    Lesson 3: Using and, but, because (You Do)

    Time: 35 minutes | Type: Independent

    PhaseTeacherStudents
    You Do (35 min)Write a short story (8+ sentences) about a time you misbehaved (like Max!). Use: AND, BUT, BECAUSE. Circle your connectives. Check your punctuation.Integrated story writing

    WEEK 6 — PUNCTUATION DEEPENING

    Lesson 1: Full Stops and Question Marks (I Do)

    Time: 35 minutes | Type: Explicit Teaching

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Mini-Lesson (10 min)Full stop (.) = statement, information, finished thought. Question mark (?) = asking a question, voice goes up. "Max sailed across the sea." vs "Who sailed across the sea?" Model the difference with voice.Learn
    Activity (15 min)Change statements to questions: "The wild things roared." → "Did the wild things roar?" "Max was home." → "Was Max home?" "The boat was small." → "Was the boat small?" Do 8 together.Transformation
    You Do (10 min)Write 5 sentences about the story — at least 2 statements and 2 questions. Label each with the correct ending mark.Written practice

    Lesson 2: Exclamation Marks (I Do → We Do)

    Time: 35 minutes | Type: Explicit Teaching + Guided

    PhaseTeacherStudents
    I Do (10 min)Exclamation mark (!) = strong feeling, surprise, excitement, or emphasis. "The wild things are coming!" "What a storm!" "I am the king!" Uses a loud, excited voice.Learn
    We Do (15 min)Change statements to exclamation: "The food was huge." → "What huge food!" "Max ran away." → "Max ran away!" "I am back." → "I am home!" Practice with expression.Guided transformation
    You Do (10 min)Write 5 exclamation sentences about the story. Read each with the correct expression!Written practice

    Lesson 3: Commas in Lists (You Do)

    Time: 35 minutes | Type: Independent

    PhaseTeacherStudents
    Mini-Lesson (5 min)Commas separate items in a list: "Max wore a crown, a red cape, and enormous boots." The comma goes between items. The word "and" before the last item.Learn
    You Do (30 min)Write 4 sentences with lists about the story. Use commas correctly: "The wild things had enormous eyes, sharp teeth, and wild hair." "Max ate soup, bread, and cake." Check each sentence.Written practice

    WEEK 7 — APPLYING GRAMMAR IN WRITING

    Lesson 1: Wild Thing Descriptions (We Do)

    Time: 35 minutes | Type: Guided Practice

    PhaseTeacherStudents
    We Do (35 min)Write a description of one of the Wild Things. Include: Name (proper noun), what it looks like (adjectives), what it does (verbs in past tense), where it lives (noun). Use AND, BUT, BECAUSE. Check punctuation. Share with a partner and edit.Integrated writing

    Lesson 2: Story Sentences (You Do)

    Time: 35 minutes | Type: Independent

    PhaseTeacherStudents
    You Do (35 min)Write a prequel or sequel to *Where the Wild Things Are*. At least 10 sentences. You must include: Subject + predicate structure, 5+ nouns, 5+ verbs (past tense), 5+ adjectives, 3+ sentences with connectives (AND, BUT, BECAUSE), correct punctuation.Full story writing

    Lesson 3: Editing Our Work (We Do)

    Time: 35 minutes | Type: Guided Practice

    PhaseTeacherStudents
    We Do (15 min)Editing checklist: Capital letters at start of sentences? Full stops, question marks, or exclamation marks at the end? Nouns and verbs used? Adjectives for detail? Connectives for flow? Swap papers with a partner and check.Peer editing
    You Do (20 min)Edit your own story using the checklist. Rewrite one improved sentence.Self-editing

    WEEK 8 — HOT TASK AND CELEBRATION

    Lesson 1: Hot Task (Assessment)

    Time: 35 minutes | Type: Assessment

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Hook (5 min)"Today you will show everything you know about sentences, nouns, verbs, adjectives, connectives, and punctuation. Be proud of your growth!"Engage
    Task (30 min)SAME TASK AS COLD TASK: Write 6 sentences about Max and the Wild Things. Use capital letters, full stops, question marks, or exclamation marks. Include nouns and verbs. Circle your nouns. Underline your verbs. Year 1: minimum 6 sentences.Independent sentence writing

    Compare Cold Task → Hot Task using rubric. Note growth in all grammar and punctuation skills.

    Lesson 2: Comparing Our Work (We Do)

    Time: 35 minutes | Type: Analysis

    PhaseTeacherStudents
    We Do (20 min)Compare Cold Task → Hot Task. Count improvements: More nouns? More verbs? Better punctuation? Connectives used? Adjectives added? Use a rubric checklist.Analysis
    Share (15 min)Students share specific grammar improvements. "I used to forget full stops. Now I always use them."Celebrations

    Lesson 3: Celebration (We Do)

    Time: 35 minutes | Type: Celebration

    PhaseTeacherStudents
    Celebration (35 min)"Wild Grammar Stars" display. Read selected sentences aloud. Award "Grammar Champion" certificates.Display and recognition

    ADAPTATIONS

    EAL Students

  • Provide noun/verb/adjective word banks with pictures
  • Sentence frames: "The [noun] [verb] [prep] the [noun]"
  • Allow L1 sentence construction first, then translate
  • Visual grammar posters for reference
  • Pair with supportive partner for peer checking
  • Allow verbal sentence construction before writing
  • Pre-teach tense patterns with physical actions

    • Gifted Students

  • Introduce superlative and comparative adjectives (-er, -est)
  • Explore action verb variety (crept vs moved)
  • Write complex sentences with multiple connectives
  • Create a grammar puzzle for the class
  • Explore adverbs (how, when, where words)
  • Introduce possessive nouns (Max's crown)

    • Students with NEPs

  • Reduce to 3–4 sentences per task
  • Provide sentence starters and word banks
  • Allow verbal responses for initial practice
  • Extended time for all tasks
  • One-on-one support for editing
  • Simplify to noun/verb identification only (no connective requirement)
  • Use large print and visual supports
