Unit Overview

Learning Intention:

We are learning to write facts about animals and the world — telling what something looks like, where it lives, and what it does.

Success Criteria:

  • I can write facts about an animal or thing.
  • I can write a sentence that tells the main idea (topic sentence).
  • I can write 3 facts about my topic.
  • I can draw a picture that matches my facts.
  • I can use a full stop and capital letter.

    WEEK 1 — WHAT IS AN INFORMATIVE TEXT?

    Lesson 1: Cold Task (Assessment)

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Assessment

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Hook (5 min)Show a National Geographic Kids magazine or page. "Look at this! It tells us REAL facts about animals. Today YOU will write facts about an animal."Look and think
    Task (25 min)Cold Task: Choose an animal you know about. Draw a picture of it. Write 3 facts about it. Year 1: 3–5 sentences, pictures.Independent writing

    Differentiation: Animal word bank available. Sentence starters.

    Lesson 2: Looking at National Geographic Kids (I Do)

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Explicit Teaching

    PhaseTeacherStudents
    Read (15 min)Show National Geographic Kids pages. Look at: The photographs / The captions / The short sentences / The clear layout. What makes these facts clear and interesting?Analyze features
    Discuss (10 min)"Informative texts tell FACTS — true things. Not opinions (what someone thinks). Facts can be proven. 'A dolphin is a mammal' is a fact. 'Dolphins are cute' is an opinion."Distinguish
    Share (5 min)"What animals do you know facts about?"Share

    Success Criteria Check: Can distinguish a fact from an opinion.

    Lesson 3: Fact or Opinion? (We Do)

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Guided

    PhaseTeacherStudents
    We Do (20 min)Show 8 statements — 4 facts, 4 opinions. Sort as a class: FACT or OPINION? "A cat has four legs." [Fact] "Cats are the best pets." [Opinion].Sort statements
    You Do (10 min)Write 1 fact and 1 opinion about any animal. Share with partner — partner guesses which is which.Write fact/opinion

    WEEK 2 — CHOOSING YOUR TOPIC

    Lesson 1: I Am an Expert (I Do)

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Explicit Teaching

    PhaseTeacherStudents
    I Do (10 min)"You are an EXPERT on something. You know facts that other people might not know. What do YOU know a lot about?" Show expertise examples: A vet knows about animals. A chef knows about food. YOU know about animals, places, sports, games.Think
    You Do (20 min)Brainstorm: What am I an expert on? Make a list of 5 animals or things I know about. Circle the one I know the MOST about.Select topic

    Lesson 2: Gathering Facts (We Do)

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Guided

    PhaseTeacherStudents
    I Do (10 min)"To write an informative text, you need FACTS. Where can we find facts?" Books, websites, magazines, videos, asking experts. Show fact-gathering tools: Books, Nat Geo Kids, reliable websites.Learn sources
    We Do (15 min)Class research: Choose an animal (e.g., penguins). Find 5 facts: What does it look like? Where does it live? What does it eat? What does it do? How does it move?Research together
    You Do (5 min)Start gathering facts for YOUR animal. Write 3 facts you already know or can find.Gather facts

    Lesson 3: Fact Cards (You Do)

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Art + Writing

    PhaseTeacherStudents
    You Do (25 min)Create a FACT CARD for your animal: Write 5 facts. Include a small picture or photo. Include WHERE you found the fact (source).Create fact card
    Share (5 min)Share your fact card with a partner. Did they learn something new?Share

    WEEK 3 — WRITING FACTS

    Lesson 1: Topic Sentences (I Do)

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Explicit Teaching

    PhaseTeacherStudents
    I Do (10 min)"A TOPIC SENTENCE tells the reader what the whole text is about. It goes at the start." Model: "Penguins are birds that cannot fly." This sentence tells you the whole text will be about penguins and their flying.Learn topic sentence
    Mini-Lesson (10 min)A good topic sentence: Tells the topic / Is one sentence / Makes the reader want to know more. Examples: "Elephants are the biggest land animals." / "Dogs are loyal friends to humans." / "Butterflies start as caterpillars."Note qualities
    You Do (10 min)Write the topic sentence for YOUR animal. Circle the topic. Underline what you will tell about it.Write topic sentence

    Lesson 2: Writing the Facts (We Do)

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Writing

    PhaseTeacherStudents
    I Do (10 min)"After the topic sentence, we write FACTS. Each fact should tell something different." Model: Topic sentence: "Kangaroos are Australian animals." Fact 1: "They have strong back legs for jumping." Fact 2: "They carry their babies in a pouch." Fact 3: "They eat grass and leaves."Observe
    We Do (10 min)Write facts for the class animal together. Topic sentence + 3 facts.Write together
    You Do (10 min)Write your topic sentence and 3 facts for your animal. Each fact should be about a different part of your animal.Write facts

    Lesson 3: Checking Our Facts (You Do)

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Editing

    PhaseTeacherStudents
    You Do (25 min)Check your facts: Are they TRUE? Can you prove them? Use a book or website to check. Fix any facts that are wrong.Check accuracy
    Share (5 min)Share your best fact with the classShare

    WEEK 4 — STRUCTURE

    Lesson 1: Beginning, Middle, End (I Do)

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Explicit Teaching

    PhaseTeacherStudents
    I Do (10 min)"An informative text has a beginning, middle and end, just like a story. BEGINNING: The topic sentence. MIDDLE: The facts. END: A finishing sentence — like 'Now I know lots about [topic]!' or 'Isn't [topic] amazing?'"Learn structure
    You Do (20 min)Add an ending sentence to your facts. Make it a good finishing sentence that sums up what you told.Add ending

    Lesson 2: Putting Facts in Order (We Do)

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Guided

    PhaseTeacherStudents
    We Do (20 min)Sort facts in order: Start with the most important fact. Then the next. Then the next. Last fact should be interesting — something surprising. Read aloud — does it flow?Order facts
    You Do (10 min)Reorder your facts if needed. Number them 1, 2, 3 in the best order.Reorder

    Lesson 3: Drafting (You Do)

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Writing

    PhaseTeacherStudents
    You Do (30 min)Write a full draft of your informative text: Topic sentence → Fact 1 → Fact 2 → Fact 3 → Ending sentence. Read it back — does it make sense?Write draft

    WEEK 5 — CAPTIONS AND LABELS

    Lesson 1: Adding Captions (I Do)

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Explicit Teaching

    PhaseTeacherStudents
    I Do (10 min)"A CAPTION is a sentence under a picture that tells what is happening." Show National Geographic captions: "A penguin waddles across the ice." / "A baby kangaroo peek out of their mother's pouch." Captions add extra information.Learn captions
    You Do (20 min)Draw a picture of your animal. Add a caption underneath — one sentence that tells what is in the picture.Draw and caption

    Lesson 2: Labels (We Do)

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Art + Writing

    PhaseTeacherStudents
    I Do (10 min)"A LABEL is a word that points to a part of something." Show labelled diagram: Elephant — label on trunk, ears, legs. Labels help readers understand the picture better.Learn labels
    We Do (10 min)Draw an animal with labels: Label 3 parts with arrows pointing to them. Write the part name next to each arrow.Draw with labels
    You Do (10 min)Add labels to your picture: Point to 3 parts of your animal. Write the name of each part.Add labels

    Lesson 3: Improving Our Work (You Do)

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Editing

    PhaseTeacherStudents
    You Do (25 min)Add to your informative text: 3 facts + picture + caption + labels. Make it look like a page from a Nat Geo Kids magazine!Enhance
    Share (5 min)Share your page with a partner — does it teach them something new?Share

    WEEK 6 — EDITING

    Lesson 1: Capital Letters and Full Stops (I Do)

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Editing

    PhaseTeacherStudents
    I Do (10 min)"Check your writing: Every sentence starts with a capital letter. Every sentence ends with a full stop." Model checking: "the elephant has big ears. it lives in africa." Fix: "The elephant has big ears. It lives in Africa."Check
    You Do (20 min)Read your whole text. Put a capital letter at the start of every sentence. Put a full stop at the end of every sentence.Edit

    Lesson 2: Finger Spaces (You Do)

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Editing

    PhaseTeacherStudents
    You Do (25 min)Check: Are there finger spaces between your words? Read it to a partner — can they read it easily? Fix any words that are squished together.Check spaces
    Share (5 min)Read your text to a partner — they check for spaces and capitals and full stopsPeer check

    Lesson 3: Peer Checking (We Do)

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Peer Feedback

    PhaseTeacherStudents
    We Do (25 min)Swap texts with a partner. Check: Capital letters at start? Full stops at end? Finger spaces? Facts are true? Topic sentence at start? Ending sentence present? Write 2 stars and 1 wish.Peer check
    You Do (5 min)Fix anything your partner noticedFix

    WEEK 7 — PUBLISHING

    Lesson 1: Good Copy (You Do)

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Publishing

    PhaseTeacherStudents
    You Do (30 min)Write or type a clean final copy of your informative text. Make it neat and clear. Leave room for the picture.Create final copy

    Lesson 2: Pictures (You Do)

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Art

    PhaseTeacherStudents
    You Do (30 min)Add a big, clear picture of your animal. Use colours neatly. Add your labels to the picture. Add a caption underneath.Illustrate

    Lesson 3: Display (We Do)

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Sharing

    PhaseTeacherStudents
    We Do (30 min)Display all informative texts in the class. Make a class "Nat Geo Kids" wall or book. Walk around and read each other's work.Display and read

    WEEK 8 — HOT TASK AND CELEBRATION

    Lesson 1: Hot Task (Assessment)

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Assessment

    PhaseTeacherStudents
    Hook (5 min)"Think about the animal you wrote about in Week 1. Now you know how to write facts better! Write a page that teaches us something."Recall
    Task (25 min)SAME TASK: Write facts about an animal. Include: Topic sentence, 3 facts, picture with labels, caption. Year 1: 4–6 sentences.Independent assessment

    Compare Cold Task → Hot Task using rubric. Note growth.

    Lesson 2: Comparing Our Work (We Do)

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Analysis

    PhaseTeacherStudents
    We Do (15 min)Compare Week 1 and Week 8 work. "What is different? What is better?" Look for: More facts / Better sentences / Better picture / Labels and captionCompare
    Share (15 min)Students share one thing they improvedCelebrate

    Lesson 3: Celebration (We Do)

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Celebration

    PhaseTeacherStudents
    Celebration (30 min)Make a class book of all informative texts. Read it together. Award certificates: Best Facts, Best Picture, Best Labels, Most Improved.Celebrate

    RUBRIC — Informative Writing (Year 1)

    AC9E1LY02 — Create short informative texts using facts and words from own knowledge

    CriterionA (Excellent)B (Strong)C (Satisfactory)D (Developing)E (Beginning)
    **Facts**3+ accurate, interesting facts about the topic. Facts are well-chosen — teach the reader something new.3 accurate facts. Mostly interesting.2–3 facts present. Some may be basic or slightly inaccurate.1–2 facts. May be vague or inaccurate.No facts or all inaccurate.
    **Topic Sentence**Clear topic sentence at the start. Tells reader exactly what the text is about.Topic sentence present and clear.Topic sentence present but may be vague.Attempt at topic sentence.No topic sentence.
    **Structure**Clear beginning, middle, end. Facts in logical order. Ending sentence present.Has structure. Facts mostly in order. Ending present.Structure present but uneven.Attempt at structure. Unclear.No structure.
    **Picture/Labels/Caption**High quality picture with clear, accurate labels and a descriptive caption. Adds to the text significantly.Good picture. Labels and caption present and mostly clear.Picture present. Some labels or caption.Picture but labels/caption missing or unclear.No picture or labels.
    **Punctuation & Grammar**Consistent capitals and full stops. Finger spaces clear. Sentences make sense.Minor errors. Mostly clear.Some errors. Sentences mostly clear.Noticeable errors.Significant errors.

    ADAPTATIONS

    EAL Students

  • Allow home language for initial fact gathering
  • Provide picture-word association for animal vocabulary
  • Sentence starters for topic sentence and ending
  • Allow oral presentation with teacher scribe
  • Provide animal fact sheets in home language if available

    • Gifted Students

  • Choose a less common animal with more complex facts
  • Add a second picture with more labels
  • Write a compare-and-contrast: "How is X different from Y?"
  • Research and add habitat facts

    • Students with Additional Needs

  • Provide pre-selected animal with fact sheet
  • Reduce to 2 facts
  • Provide picture to colour and label
  • Allow scribe for writing
  • Extra time
  • One-on-one support

    • ASSESSMENT NOTES

    Formative Assessment (Ongoing)

  • Fact cards
  • Draft writing
  • Peer checking
  • Teacher conferences (Weeks 3, 5, 6)

    • Summative Assessment

  • Cold Task → Hot Task comparison (same rubric, measure growth)
  • Published text assessed with rubric

    • Tracking Progress

    Document: Cold → Hot scores, fact quality, structure clarity, presentation.
