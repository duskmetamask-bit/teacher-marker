HASS/Geography Year 1 7 Weeks 21 Lessons

The world is made up of many different places. Each place has its own features, climate, and the people and animals that live there. We can explore places near and far to understand our amazing world.

Learning Intention

We are learning to describe the features of different places around the world and explain how and why places are similar or different..

  • I can describe the features of at least 3 different places on Earth.
  • I can explain how a place near us is similar to or different from a place far away.
  • I can locate Australia and at least 3 other countries on a map or globe.
  • I can describe how the weather or climate affects the features of a place.
  • I can use sources (photos, maps, stories) to find out about places.
  • I can ask questions about places I want to learn more about.

Week 1 — ENGAGE: Our Big World

Lesson 1: Cold Task — Places on Earth

40 min Assessment (Formative Pre-Assessment)
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- ---------------- ---------
Hook (5 min) Show a globe or world map. "Look at this. What do you see? What is the big blue part? What are the brown and green parts?" Observe and wonder
Introduction (5 min) "Our Earth is a giant ball with places all over it. There are places with lots of water, places with tall mountains, places with hot sand, and places with cold snow. Our job this unit is to explore our amazing world." Listen
Cold Task (30 min) "Draw two places on Earth. One can be in Australia and one somewhere else in the world. Label what makes each one different — the features, the weather, or what lives there. Write 2 sentences: Why is each place special?" Independent drawing and writing
DifferentiationImage word bank of world places. Sentence starters: "One place is..." / "Both places have..." / "Each place is different because..."

Lesson 2: What is a Globe?

40 min Engage (Hook)
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- ---------------- ---------
Meet the Globe (10 min) "A globe is a model of the Earth. It shows all the continents and oceans in their correct places. Just like a soccer ball is a model of a ball, a globe is a model of Earth." Listen
Find the Land (10 min) Pass around a globe or show on projector. "The brown and green parts are LAND. The blue parts are WATER. Can you find Australia? Can you find the big ocean?" Find Australia and oceans
Find the Continents (10 min) "There are 6 continents: Africa, Antarctica, Asia, Australia, Europe, North America, and South America. Let's find them together." Chant continents
Play It Back (10 min) "Close your eyes. I say a continent, you point to where you think it is. Open your eyes to check." Point and check

Lesson 3: Australia Is Home

40 min Engage (Concept Introduction)
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- ---------------- ---------
Australia on the Globe (10 min) "We live in Australia. It is a country and a continent — a very big island! Australia is in the Southern Hemisphere. That means we are below the equator." Locate Australia
Australia's Features (15 min) Show images: beaches, deserts, rainforests, mountains, coral reefs, cities. "Australia has many different features. What features do you know about in Australia?" Observe and share
Our Local Area (10 min) "We live in a specific part of Australia. What town or suburb do we live in? What features do we see every day?" Locate local area
Exit Ticket (5 min) "Name one feature of Australia that you would like to learn more about." Write one feature
DifferentiationEAL students draw a feature of Australia. Gifted students: find Australia on a real map and name 3 neighbouring countries.

Week 2 — EXPLORE: Features of Places

Lesson 1: Natural Features of Places

40 min Explore (Investigation)
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- ---------------- ---------
What Are Natural Features? (10 min) "Natural features are things in a place that nature made — not people. Mountains, rivers, beaches, forests, caves, and volcanoes are all natural features. They were here before people!" Listen and note
Natural Features Gallery (15 min) Show 8 images: Mount Everest, Amazon River, Sahara Desert, Great Barrier Reef, Amazon Rainforest, Niagara Falls, Grand Canyon, Antarctic ice sheet. "What do you notice? What is making each one?" Observe and discuss
Natural Features Sort (10 min) Image sort: mountains, houses, rivers, roads, beaches, schools, forests, cars. "Which are natural features? Which are built by people?" Sort into groups
Draw a Natural Feature (5 min) "Draw one natural feature from today's lesson." Draw

Lesson 2: Built Features of Places

40 min Explore (Investigation)
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- ---------------- ---------
What Are Built Features? (10 min) "Built features are things in a place that people made. Houses, schools, roads, bridges, shops, airports, and stadiums are all built features." Listen
Built Features Around the World (15 min) Show images: Eiffel Tower (France), Great Wall of China, Sydney Opera House, Taj Mahal (India), Empire State Building (USA), Pyramids (Egypt). "These are famous built features. People travel from all over the world to see them!" Observe
Built vs Natural (10 min) "Can you find a built feature near our school? Can you find a natural feature? Let's go on a built features hunt!" Plan a hunt
Built Features Hunt (5 min) Students draw or list 3 built features they can find near school or from memory. Hunt and record

Lesson 3: Describing Places Near and Far

40 min Explain (Explicit Teaching)
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- ---------------- ---------
Describing Like a Geographer (10 min) "Geographers describe places using their features. They ask: What is the land like? Is there water? What grows there? What is built there? What is the weather like?" Learn geographic questions
Model Description (10 min) Show a photo of the Amazon rainforest. "This is the Amazon rainforest in Brazil. It has tall trees, hot and wet weather, lots of rivers, many colourful birds and animals. Few roads or buildings. It is a natural place." Listen to model
Describe Australia (10 min) Show a photo of Australia. "Now let's describe Australia. Hot and dry in the middle, wet in the tropics, coastal cities, many natural features." Model using feature vocabulary. Apply to Australia
Describe a Place (10 min) Students choose one place from the Cold Task or Lesson 2 and write 3–5 sentences describing it using geographic vocabulary. Write description
DifferentiationEAL students draw their description first. NEP students use a sentence starter bank.

Week 3 — EXPLORE + EXPLAIN: Weather and Climate

Lesson 1: What is Weather?

40 min Explain (Explicit Teaching)
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- ---------------- ---------
What is Weather? (10 min) "Weather is what it is like outside right now — sunny, rainy, windy, hot, cold, cloudy, snowy. Weather changes every day. Today might be sunny, tomorrow might be rainy. That's weather!" Listen
Weather Walk (10 min) Go outside. "What is today's weather? Is it hot or cold? Is there wind? Is the sky blue or grey? What can we see in the sky?" Observe weather
Weather Chart (10 min) Begin a class weather chart. Track: date, temperature, sky condition, wind, precipitation. Add today's entry. "We will update this each day." Contribute to chart
Weather Report (10 min) "Write 2–3 sentences: What is today's weather like? How does it make you feel? What should you wear?" Write weather report

Lesson 2: Climate Around the World

40 min Explain (Explicit Teaching)
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- ---------------- ---------
Weather vs Climate (10 min) "Climate is the usual weather of a place over a long time. Our Australian climate is mostly warm and sunny. The Arctic climate is very cold and icy. Climate tells you what to EXPECT in a place." Listen and differentiate
Climate Regions (15 min) Show images by climate: Hot desert (Sahara) / Tropical (Bali) / Temperate (London) / Cold/Arctic (Alaska) / Mediterranean (Athens). Describe each. Observe
Climate Comparison (10 min) "In a hot place, you wear light clothes and use fans. In a cold place, you wear warm coats and use heaters. Climate affects how we live." Discuss
Draw the Climate (5 min) "Draw what the climate is like in one place around the world. Write 1 sentence about what to wear there." Draw

Lesson 3: Weather Affects Places

40 min Explain (Explicit Teaching)
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- ---------------- ---------
Weather Shapes Places (10 min) "Weather changes the land over time. Rain makes rivers swell. Wind shapes sand dunes. Heat cracks the earth. Cold freezes water into ice. The weather of a place helps shape its features." Listen
Weather Evidence (10 min) Show images: wind-shaped rocks, river-carved canyons, beach formed by waves, rainforest created by rainfall. "Rainfall created the rainforest. Waves created this beach. Weather made these features." Observe
Climate Affects Animals (10 min) "Animals are suited to their climate. Polar bears have thick fur for cold Arctic climates. Camels have humps to survive hot desert climates. Frogs need wet places. What climate are YOU suited to?" Discuss
Apply (10 min) "Write 2 sentences: How does the weather or climate affect the features of a place? Example: Lots of rain makes thick forests grow." Write application
DifferentiationEAL students draw climate-place connections. Gifted students: research one animal and its climate.

Week 4 — EXPLAIN: Countries Near and Far

Lesson 1: Countries on a Map

40 min Explain (Explicit Teaching)
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- ---------------- ---------
What is a Country? (10 min) "A country is a piece of land with its own government. Australia is our country. There are about 200 countries in the world. We are going to learn about countries near and far from Australia." Listen
Countries on the Globe (15 min) Show countries on the globe: Australia (home), Japan (nearby Asia), Brazil (South America), United Kingdom (Europe), United States of America (North America). Locate each relative to Australia. Find countries
Mapping Practice (10 min) Distribute printed world maps (simple). Students locate and colour: Australia (yellow), Japan (red), Brazil (blue), UK (green), USA (orange). Label each country name. Colour and label
Exit Ticket (5 min) "Name one country far from Australia and one country near Australia." Write answer

Lesson 2: Let's Visit Japan

40 min Explain (Explicit Teaching)
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- ---------------- ---------
Japan Introduction (10 min) Show flag, map location relative to Australia, population. "Japan is an island country in Asia. It is made up of many islands. Japan's climate is varied — cold snowy winters in the north, hot humid summers in the south." Locate and learn
Japan's Features (15 min) Show images: Mount Fuji (natural), Tokyo city (built), cherry blossoms (natural/seasonal), bullet train (built), sushi (cultural), ancient temples (built/cultural). Describe each. Observe
Japan vs Australia (10 min) Compare: Both island countries / Japan has mountains, Australia has deserts / Both have coastal cities / Different languages and cultures. Compare
Draw One Feature (5 min) "Draw one feature of Japan from today." Draw

Lesson 3: Let's Visit Brazil

40 min Explain (Explicit Teaching)
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- ---------------- ---------
Brazil Introduction (10 min) Show flag, map location relative to Australia, Amazon rainforest, carnival culture. "Brazil is the largest country in South America. Most of it is tropical. It has the Amazon rainforest — the lungs of the Earth." Locate and learn
Brazil's Features (15 min) Show images: Christ the Redeemer statue (Rio), Amazon River, Iguazu Falls, Carnival, favelas (built/slums), diverse wildlife. Describe each. Observe
Brazil vs Australia vs Japan (10 min) "What is the same about Japan, Brazil, and Australia? All are island or mostly-island countries. They all have tropical or varied climates. They all have famous natural and built features." Compare
Exit Ticket (5 min) "Write 1 sentence: What is one feature of Brazil?" Write

Week 5 — EXPLAIN: Similarities and Differences

Lesson 1: Same but Different

40 min Explain (Explicit Teaching)
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- ---------------- ---------
Same but Different (10 min) "Places can be the same in some ways and different in others. Australia and Brazil are both countries with beaches. But Australia has more desert and Brazil has more rainforest. Same and different!" Understand concept
Find the Same (15 min) Show image sets: Australian beach vs Brazilian beach / Japanese city vs Australian city / Arctic vs desert. "What is the same? What is different?" Compare
Our Class Venn Diagram (10 min) Draw a large Venn diagram on the board. Students contribute: "Australia" features on one side, "Japan" features on the other, shared features in the middle. Contribute to diagram
Write a Similarity and Difference (5 min) "Write 1 sentence about a similarity and 1 sentence about a difference between two places we have learned about." Write

Lesson 2: Comparing Our Place to Another

40 min Explain (Explicit Teaching)
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- ---------------- ---------
Our Local Place (10 min) "Let's describe our local area as geographers. What features do we have? What is our weather usually like? What do people do here?" Describe local area
Compare to Japan (15 min) Create a comparison chart: Local area Japan. Features: land type, buildings, weather, animals, food, transport.
Compare to Brazil (10 min) "Now compare our local area to Brazil. What is the same? What is very different?" Compare
Write Comparison (5 min) "Write 2 sentences: How is our local area similar to or different from Brazil?" Write

Lesson 3: Venn Diagram Practice

40 min Explain (Explicit Teaching)
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- ---------------- ---------
Venn Diagram Review (10 min) "A Venn diagram shows two things and how they are the same and different. The two circles overlap. In the overlap goes what is the SAME. In the outer parts goes what is DIFFERENT." Review
Guided Venn (15 min) Model: Australia vs Japan using a Venn diagram. Country Australia
Independent Venn (15 min) Students choose two places (Australia/Brazil, or Australia/Japan, or local area/Brazil) and complete a Venn diagram. Complete Venn
DifferentiationEAL students use picture cards to complete Venn. Gifted students complete 3-place comparison.

Week 6 — ELABORATE: People in Places

Lesson 1: How Do People Use Places?

40 min Elaborate (Application)
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- ---------------- ---------
People Need Places (10 min) "People use places to live, work, learn, play, and rest. A city has offices for work, schools for learning, parks for playing, and homes for resting. How do we use our local place?" Discuss
Land Use in Our Area (15 min) Show a simple land use map or aerial photo of the local area. Identify: residential (homes), commercial (shops), recreational (parks), institutional (schools, churches). Identify land use
Land Use Far Away (10 min) Show a land use image of Tokyo or São Paulo. "What do you see? Same types of places but different arrangements." Compare
Draw Your Land Use (5 min) "Draw our local area and label 3 types of land use you can find." Draw

Lesson 2: Homes Around the World

40 min Elaborate (Application)
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- ---------------- ---------
Homes Suit Their Places (10 min) "Homes are built to suit the place. In cold places, homes are built thick to keep warmth in. In hot places, homes are built with thick walls and small windows to keep heat out. In rainy places, roofs are steep so rain slides off." Listen
Homes Around the World (15 min) Show images: Inuit snow house (igloo) / Moroccan riad (thick walls) / Australian outback homestead / Dutch house (steep roofs) / Japanese traditional house (paper screens, raised floors). Describe each. Observe
Why Are They Different? (10 min) "The climate and available materials affect the home. Inuit used snow because it was available and insulated well. Moroccan homes used stone for coolness. Australian homesteads used corrugated iron. Climate shapes homes." Understand reason
Design Your Home (5 min) "Design a home suited to our local climate. Draw it and label 1 feature that suits our weather." Design

Lesson 3: Animals Around the World

40 min Elaborate (Application)
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- ---------------- ---------
Animals Suit Their Places (10 min) "Animals are found in places suited to them. Polar bears live in the Arctic because they have thick fur. Camels live in deserts because they can store water. Koalas live in Australia because they eat eucalyptus leaves." Listen
Biome Map (15 min) Show a world biome map: tropical rainforest (Amazon, Congo, Indonesia), desert (Sahara, Arabian), temperate forest, Arctic/tundra, savanna (Africa). Where do different animals live? Identify biomes
Australian Animals (10 min) "Australia has animals found NOWHERE else. Kangaroos, koalas, platypus, wombats, emus, cockatoos. Our wildlife is unique because Australia is an island continent with varied climates." Note Australian uniqueness
Draw an Animal in Its Place (5 min) "Draw one animal in the place where it lives. Label 1 feature of the place that helps the animal survive." Draw
DifferentiationEAL students use animal picture cards. Gifted students: create a food chain from an ecosystem.

Week 7 — ELABORATE + EVALUATE: Caring for Places

Lesson 1: Who Looks After Our World?

40 min Evaluate (Critical Thinking)
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- ---------------- ---------
Global Carers (10 min) "Our world is looked after by many people: rangers protect national parks, scientists study climate change, governments make environmental laws, and communities take care of local parks and beaches." Listen
Indigenous Land Management (10 min) "Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander peoples have looked after Country for over 65,000 years. They are the oldest continuous culture on Earth. Their knowledge of land and water management is now used in modern conservation." Understand knowledge
Local Carers (10 min) "Who looks after OUR local place? Council workers, volunteers, teachers, students. YOU can be a carer of our world." Identify local carers
Thank You Cards (10 min) "Write a note of thanks to someone who looks after a place you know." Write

Lesson 2: How Can We Care for Our World?

40 min Evaluate (Critical Thinking)
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- ---------------- ---------
The Challenge (10 min) "Our world faces challenges: climate change, pollution, deforestation, species extinction. These affect places everywhere. But there are things we can do." Introduce challenges
What Can We Do? (15 min) Action list: Reduce waste (no litter, recycling, compost) / Save water / Plant trees / Protect wildlife / Use less energy / Learn about places and teach others. Learn actions
Global Actions (10 min) "Young people around the world are taking action. SchoolStrike4Climate. Beach clean-ups. Reforestation projects. You are never too young to care." Understand youth agency
My Action Plan (5 min) "Write or draw 3 things you will do to care for our world." Write action plan
DifferentiationEAL students draw their actions. Gifted students: research one global conservation project.

Lesson 3: Our Planet Promise

40 min Evaluate (Critical Thinking)
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- ---------------- ---------
Read the Promise (10 min) "A promise is a pledge — a commitment to do something. Today each of you will make a Planet Promise — a promise to do something concrete to care for our world." Understand
Write the Promise (15 min) "Your promise must be SPECIFIC and REALISTIC. Not 'I will save the world' but 'I will pick up 3 pieces of rubbish each lunch time' or 'I will turn off the tap while brushing my teeth.'" Write specific promise
Share the Promise (10 min) Volunteers share their promise. Class creates a collective "We Promise" poster displayed in the classroom. Share and display
Sign It (5 min) Students sign the class poster. "This is our commitment. We mean it." Sign

Week 8 — EVALUATE: Hot Task and Reflection

Lesson 1: Hot Task — Show What You Know

40 min Summative Assessment
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- ---------------- ---------
Set Up (5 min) "Today is your Hot Task. Choose a place far from Australia (Japan or Brazil). Describe its features — natural and built. Explain 1 similarity and 1 difference between this place and our local area." Listen
Planning (10 min) Students plan: Which place will I describe? What are its features? How is it similar to/different from home? Use a planner. Plan
Hot Task (25 min) "Write a description of a far place: Where is it? What does it look like? What is the weather/climate like? What makes it special? How is it similar to/different from our local area?" Year 1: drawing + 5–8 sentences. Independent completion
DifferentiationEAL students use bilingual dictionaries and word banks. NEP students may dictate or present orally.

Lesson 2: Cold Task vs Hot Task — Growth Reflection

40 min Evaluate (Self-Assessment)
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- ---------------- ---------
Compare (15 min) Place Cold Task next to Hot Task. "Use a green texta to circle 3 things you did BETTER in your Hot Task. Use a red texta to circle 1 thing you could improve." Compare work samples
Growth Statement (10 min) "I have grown as a geographer because..." Write 3–5 sentences about growth. Write growth statement
Share (10 min) Volunteers share one thing they know now that they didn't know at the start. Share
What Surprised You? (5 min) "What surprised you most about places around the world?" Share
