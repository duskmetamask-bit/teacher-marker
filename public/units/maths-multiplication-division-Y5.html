    • WEEK 1 — ENGAGE

    Focus: Decimal multiplication

    5E Phase: Engage

    LESSON 1.2 — Decimal × Whole Number

    Duration: 50 minutes

    Type: Concept Introduction

    Learning Intentions

  • I am learning to multiply decimals by whole numbers
  • I am learning that 3.4 × 5 = 17.0

    • Success Criteria

  • I can solve 3.4 × 5 = 17.0

    • I DO (15 min)

    "3.4 × 5. Think: 3.4 = 3 + 0.4."
    "3×5 = 15. 0.4×5 = 2.0. 15 + 2 = 17."
    "Or: 34 × 5 = 170. Then put decimal back: 17.0."
    "Decimal moves one place because 3.4 has one decimal place."

    YOU DO (15 min)

  • 2.5 × 4 = ___ (2×4=8, 0.5×4=2, total=10)
  • 3.2 × 5 = ___
  • 4.1 × 3 = ___
  • 1.5 × 6 = ___
  • EXTENSION: "What is 2.5 × 8?"

    • LESSON 1.3 — Decimal Multiplication Patterns

    Duration: 50 minutes

    Type: Guided Practice

    YOU DO (30 min)

  • 4.2 × 5 = ___ 6.3 × 4 = ___
  • 5 × 3.7 = ___ (same — commutative)
  • "If 7 × 3.5 = 24.5, what is 3.5 × 7?"
  • EXTENSION: "What is 8 × 2.5?"

    • WEEK 2 — EXPLORE

    Focus: 2-digit × 2-digit multiplication

    5E Phase: Explore

    LESSON 2.1 — Area Model for 2-Digit × 2-Digit

    Duration: 50 minutes

    Type: Explore

    I DO (15 min)

    "23 × 47. Area model: 20×40=800, 20×7=140, 3×40=120, 3×7=21. Total: 800+140+120+21=1081."

    ```

    47

    × 23

    141 (23×7)

    460 (23×40, shift one place)

    1081

    ```

    YOU DO (15 min)

  • 23×47: 20×40=___, 20×7=___, 3×40=___, 3×7=___ → Total=___
  • Use the algorithm: 34 × 23 = ___
  • EXTENSION: "What is 45 × 67?"

    • WEEK 3 — EXPLORE

    Focus: Factors, multiples, prime factorisation

    5E Phase: Explain

    LESSON 3.1 — Factorisation

    Duration: 50 minutes

    Type: Explain

    I DO (15 min)

    "Prime factorisation: write a number as a product of prime factors."
    "60 = 2×2×3×5 = 2²×3×5"
    "Factor tree: 60 → 6×10 → (2×3)×(2×5) = 2×2×3×5"

    YOU DO (15 min)

  • 48 = 2×2×2×2×3 = 2⁴×3
  • 72 = ___×___×___×___ = ___
  • 90 = ___
  • EXTENSION: "LCM of 8 and 12." (24)

    • LESSON 3.2 — LCM and GCF

    Duration: 50 minutes

    Type: Explain

    I DO (12 min)

    "LCM: smallest common multiple. Multiples of 8: 8,16,24,32,40,48... Multiples of 12: 12,24,36,48... LCM=24."
    "GCF: greatest common factor. Factors of 24: 1,2,3,4,6,8,12,24. Factors of 36: 1,2,3,4,6,9,12,18,36. GCF=12."

    YOU DO (15 min)

  • LCM of 6 and 8: ___
  • GCF of 24 and 36: ___
  • LCM of 4 and 10: ___
  • EXTENSION: "LCM of 12 and 18."

    • WEEK 4 — EXPLORE

    LESSON 4.1 — Decimal Division

    Duration: 50 minutes

    Type: Explain

    I DO (15 min)

    "12.6 ÷ 3. 12÷3=4, 0.6÷3=0.2. Answer: 4.2."
    "Or: 126÷3=42. Put decimal back: 4.2."

    YOU DO (15 min)

  • 15.6 ÷ 3 = ___ (15÷3=5, 0.6÷3=0.2 → 5.2)
  • 14.4 ÷ 4 = ___
  • 27.5 ÷ 5 = ___
  • EXTENSION: "24.8 ÷ 8 = ?"
