Unit Overview

Learning Intention:

We are learning to demonstrate spelling mastery across complex vocabulary, use independent spelling strategies, understand etymology at an advanced level, and prepare for high school spelling demands.

Success Criteria:

  • I can spell complex academic and literary vocabulary accurately.
  • I can use independent spelling strategies when encountering unknown words.
  • I can understand and apply advanced etymology to decode and encode words.
  • I can demonstrate spelling accuracy across formal and informal contexts.
  • I can self-assess and self-correct spelling errors effectively.
  • I can apply all spelling rules and generalisations consistently.

    WEEK 1 — SPELLING CONSOLIDATION

    Lesson 1: Cold Task (Assessment)

    Time: 55 minutes | Type: Assessment

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Hook (5 min)"The Giver explores how language shapes perception — and how precise language matters. Today we will test your spelling mastery as you prepare for high school."Engage
    Read (15 min)Read selected passages from *The Giver*. Notice Lowry's precise word choice: "precision," "reciprocal," "intergenerational."Listen
    Task (35 min)Cold Task (same as Hot Task): 1. Spell 15 complex words: extraordinary, coincidence, preliminary, ridiculous, temperature, accommodate, occurrence, embarrass, millennium, harass, maintenance, neighbour, occasionally, recommended, independent. 2. Etymology: trace the origin of 3 words using dictionary. 3. Write a formal paragraph (150+ words) showing spelling accuracy across all word types. Year 6: 95%+ accuracy expected.Independent assessment

    Lesson 2: Review of Advanced Patterns (I Do)

    Time: 55 minutes | Type: Explicit Teaching

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Mini-Lesson (20 min)Advanced patterns review: -able/-ible, -ance/-ence, -tion/-sion, prefixes (inter-, trans-, super-, anti-, auto-), Greek/Latin roots, ie/ei rule, silent letters. Identify personal patterns that need work. Use diagnostic to identify individual spelling gaps.Learn
    Activity (25 min)Diagnostic spelling assessment: 30 words spanning all patterns. Mark each as correct or incorrect. Tally errors by category. Identify: Which patterns do I know? Which need practice? Create a personal spelling goals list.Assessment
    You Do (10 min)Write 3 personal spelling goals for this term.Written goals

    Success Criteria Check: Can identify personal spelling strengths and gaps.

    Lesson 3: Self-Assessment (We Do)

    Time: 55 minutes | Type: Guided Practice

    PhaseTeacherStudents
    We Do (40 min)Self-assessment: Review the diagnostic. Sort errors: 1. Rule not known. 2. Rule known but not applied. 3. Careless error. 4. New/unknown word. For each error category, identify the strategy to fix it. Create a personal spelling improvement plan.Guided analysis
    You Do (15 min)Write a reflection: "My spelling strengths are... My spelling areas to improve are... My strategy for improvement is..."Written reflection

    WEEK 2 — LITERARY VOCABULARY

    Lesson 1: Narrative Language (I Do)

    Time: 55 minutes | Type: Explicit Teaching

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Mini-Lesson (20 min)Literary vocabulary: protagonist, antagonist, narrative, exposition, climax, resolution, metaphor, symbolism, irony, theme, motif, flashback, foreshadowing, dialogue, protagonist, characterisation. From *The Giver*: Jonas is the protagonist — his transformation is the narrative arc.Learn
    Activity (25 min)Build literary words: nar + a + tive = narrative, char + act + er = character, lit + er + a + ture = literature, sym + bol + ism = symbolism, for + tell + ing = foreshadowing. Sort into CATEGORIES: CHARACTERPLOTTECHNIQUE.Building
    You Do (10 min)Write 8 literary vocabulary words. Use each in a sentence about *The Giver*.Written practice

    Lesson 2: Descriptive Words (I Do → We Do)

    Time: 55 minutes | Type: Explicit Teaching + Guided

    PhaseTeacherStudents
    I Do (10 min)Descriptive words from literature: extraordinary, meticulous, perpetual, ambiguous, profound, conspicuous, eloquent, resilient, vindicate, arbitrary. These words appear in formal literary analysis.Learn
    We Do (35 min)Build: extra + ordinary = extraordinary, metic + ulous = meticulous (no — metic+u+lous), per + pet + ual = perpetual, am + big + uous = ambiguous, pro + found = profound. Write sentences. Note: Some are from Latin roots.Guided
    You Do (10 min)Write 6 descriptive words. Circle the Latin/Greek root. Define the word using the root meaning.Written practice

    Lesson 3: Literary Analysis Terms (You Do)

    Time: 55 minutes | Type: Independent

Sort 20 words: protagonist, narrative, metaphor, ambiguous, perpetual, characterisation, climax, irony, eloquent, resolution, foreshadowing, meticulous, profound, symbolism, dialogue, flashback, extraordinary, theme, resilient, vindicate — into LITERARY DEVICECHARACTERLANGUAGE/STYLE columns. 3. Write a paragraph analysing *The Giver* using 6 literary terms.Full practice

    WEEK 3 — ADVANCED ETYMOLOGY

    Lesson 1: Latin and Greek Deep Dive (I Do)

    Time: 55 minutes | Type: Explicit Teaching

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Mini-Lesson (20 min)Deep etymology: Every word has a story. From *The Giver*: "Sameness" — sameness is a concept in the community. "The community" — from Latin comm + unit + as. "Precision" — pre + cis + ion (pre = before, cise = cut). "Reciprocal" — re + cip + rocal (back + take + relating to).Learn
    Activity (25 min)Trace etymology: precision (pre + cis + ion), reciprocal (re + cip + rocal), community (com + mun + ity), generation (gen + er + ation), transfer (trans + fer), convey (con + vey), intervene (inter + ven + e), ambiguous (am + big + uous). Write origin and meaning chain.Research
    You Do (10 min)Choose 4 words. Trace etymology. Write the full word history.Written research

    Lesson 2: Word Family Research (I Do → We Do)

    Time: 55 minutes | Type: Explicit Teaching + Guided

    PhaseTeacherStudents
    I Do (10 min)Word family research: PORT (carry): transport, portable, export, import, support, report, reportedly, reporting, transportation, exporter, importer, supporter. Each word traces to Latin portare. One root generates many words — all share the same spelling pattern.Learn
    We Do (40 min)Research 3 word families: DICT (say): dictate, predict, contradict, diction, dictionary, indict, prediction, contradictory, dictatorial, benediction. STRUCT (build): construct, destruction, instruct, structure, infrastructure, constructive, destructional. Scribe/script: describe, script, manuscript, prescription, inscription, scribble, scripted, description. Build as many words as possible. Note patterns.Research
    You Do (5 min)Choose one word family. Write all words with their meanings.Written practice

    Lesson 3: Etymology Project (You Do)

    Time: 55 minutes | Type: Independent

    PhaseTeacherStudents
    You Do (55 min)Etymology research project: 1. Choose 10 words from *The Giver*. 2. Use dictionary to trace each word's etymology: WordOriginal languageOriginal meaningHow it came to mean what it means now. 3. Write a 200-word reflection: "Understanding etymology helps my spelling because..." 4. Present 3 words to the class with full etymology stories.Full project

    WEEK 4 — SPELLING STRATEGIES

    Lesson 1: Multi-Strategy Approach (I Do)

    Time: 55 minutes | Type: Explicit Teaching

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Mini-Lesson (20 min)Multi-strategy spelling: 1. SOUND IT OUT — segment phonemes. 2. PATTERN SPOTTING — find known patterns. 3. ROOT ANALYSIS — break into morphemes. 4. Etymology RESEARCH — trace word history. 5. DICTIONARY CHECK — verify. 6. CONTEXT CLUES — does it make sense? 7. SPELLING RULE — apply the rule. From *The Giver*: "Whenever you encounter an unknown word, use ALL strategies."Learn
    Activity (25 min)Apply all strategies to challenging words: extraordinary, millennium, accommodate, occurrence, embarrass, maintenance, recommended, occasionally, particularly, immediately. For each: Sound it out. Find patterns. Break into roots. Check etymology. Look up. Use in context. Write which strategy was most useful for each word.Practice
    You Do (10 min)Choose 5 unknown words. Apply ALL 7 strategies. Write the result for each strategy.Written practice

    Lesson 2: Dictionary Mastery (I Do → We Do)

    Time: 55 minutes | Type: Explicit Teaching + Guided

    PhaseTeacherStudents
    I Do (10 min)Dictionary mastery: Guide words. Entry word. Pronunciation (IPA). Part of speech. Etymology (origin). Multiple meanings (numbered definitions). Usage notes. Build each skill systematically.Learn
    We Do (35 min)Use dictionary to find: 1. The guide words on the page. 2. Pronunciation of 5 words using IPA. 3. Etymology of 5 words. 4. Which definition matches the context in a sentence. 5. Any usage notes. Write full entries for 5 words.Guided
    You Do (10 min)Use dictionary to verify spelling of 10 tricky words. Write each word, page number, guide words, and pronunciation.Written practice

    Lesson 3: Peer Teaching (You Do)

    Time: 55 minutes | Type: Independent

    PhaseTeacherStudents
    You Do (55 min)1. Choose a spelling pattern or rule you know well. 2. Prepare a 5-minute mini-lesson to teach it to a partner. 3. Teach your partner. 4. Your partner teaches you their pattern. 5. Write: "Teaching spelling to someone else helped me understand it better because..."Teaching practice

    WEEK 5 — FORMAL AND INFORMAL

    Lesson 1: Register-Aware Spelling (I Do)

    Time: 55 minutes | Type: Explicit Teaching

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Mini-Lesson (20 min)Register-aware spelling: In formal writing, precise spelling matters MORE. In informal writing (texts, notes), minor errors are more acceptable. Formal contexts: essays, reports, applications. Informal contexts: texts, casual notes. From *The Giver*: Jonas writes in a precise, formal way — Lowry's word choice is deliberate and precise.Learn
    Activity (25 min)Write the same idea in formal and informal registers: Formal: "I am writing to inform you of my decision." Informal: "Just wanted to let u know my decision." Note: Informal spelling is NOT wrong — it's appropriate to context. But formal contexts demand precision. Write both registers.Practice
    You Do (10 min)Write 3 formal sentences and 3 informal messages about the same topic. Note the spelling differences between registers.Written practice

    Lesson 2: Formal Word Choice (I Do → We Do)

    Time: 55 minutes | Type: Explicit Teaching + Guided

    PhaseTeacherStudents
    I Do (10 min)Formal word choice requires precise spelling. Common confusions: affect/effect, than/then, there/their/they're, to/too/two, your/you're, its/it's, practice/practise, advise/advice, fewer/less. These MUST be correct in formal writing.Learn
    We Do (35 min)Complete formal sentences with correct word choice: "It was a significant ___ on the environment." (affect/effect) "I would prefer to go ___ than stay here." (then/than) "___ going to be late." (their/they're/there) Write 10 formal sentences using these confusions correctly.Guided
    You Do (10 min)Write a formal paragraph (100 words) using all 10 commonly confused words correctly.Written practice

    Lesson 3: Context Spelling (You Do)

    Time: 55 minutes | Type: Independent

    PhaseTeacherStudents
    You Do (55 min)1. Write a formal analytical paragraph about *The Giver* (150+ words). Use 10+ complex vocabulary words correctly. Check all spelling. 2. Write an informal text conversation between two characters from *The Giver* (100+ words). Note: Informal spelling is acceptable here — this is deliberate register difference. 3. Write: "Formal and informal spelling differ because..."Full practice

    WEEK 6 — PROOFREADING MASTERY

    Lesson 1: Advanced Proofreading (I Do)

    Time: 55 minutes | Type: Explicit Teaching

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Mini-Lesson (20 min)Advanced proofreading: Systematic approach. 1. Read once for meaning. 2. Read again for spelling — word by word. 3. Circle uncertain words. 4. Check punctuation. 5. Verify homophones in context. 6. Look up uncertain words. 7. Read aloud. 8. Final check. From *The Giver*: Lowry proofs carefully — each word is deliberate.Learn
    Activity (25 min)Proofread a complex text with 15 deliberate errors. Find all errors. Write corrections. Explain why each was wrong. Apply the full 8-step process.Practice
    You Do (10 min)Proofread your own previous writing. Find and fix 5 errors using the systematic approach.Written practice

    Lesson 2: Error Analysis (I Do → We Do)

    Time: 55 minutes | Type: Explicit Teaching + Guided

    PhaseTeacherStudents
    I Do (10 min)Error analysis = understanding WHY you make spelling errors. Categories: Rule not known. Rule applied incorrectly. Confusion with similar words. Careless error. New/unknown word. Pattern: "I always mix up X and Y." Fix: Specific strategy for that error type.Learn
    We Do (35 min)Analyse errors from previous work: 1. Sort errors into categories. 2. For each error, identify the type. 3. Write the specific strategy to prevent this error in future. 4. Create a personal error-prevention checklist.Guided
    You Do (10 min)Write: "My most common spelling errors are... The strategy I will use to fix them is..."Written reflection

    Lesson 3: Accuracy Writing (You Do)

    Time: 55 minutes | Type: Independent

    PhaseTeacherStudents
    You Do (55 min)1. Write a 300-word piece about a topic of your choice. 2. Apply full proofreading process. 3. Count errors. 4. Analyse each error: type, cause, fix. 5. Rewrite perfectly. 6. Write: "To achieve spelling accuracy, I need to..."Full process

    WEEK 7 — HIGH SCHOOL PREPARATION

    Lesson 1: Complex Vocabulary (I Do)

    Time: 55 minutes | Type: Explicit Teaching

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Mini-Lesson (20 min)High school vocabulary: analytical, synthesise, evaluate, interpret, justify, demonstrate, assess, comprehend, investigate, significant, theoretical, perspective. These words appear across all high school subjects. Their spelling must be mastered.Learn
    Activity (25 min)Build: ana + lyt + ical = analytical, syn + thes + ise = synthesise, eval + u + ate = evaluate, inter + pret = interpret, jus + ti + fy = justify, demon + strate = demonstrate. Write each. Note: -ise/-ize spelling variations exist — be consistent with your choice.Building
    You Do (10 min)Write 10 high school vocabulary words. Use each in a sentence showing you understand the meaning.Written practice

    Lesson 2: Subject-Area Spelling (I Do → We Do)

    Time: 55 minutes | Type: Explicit Teaching + Guided

    PhaseTeacherStudents
    I Do (10 min)Subject-area spelling: Science (hypothesis, photosynthesis, atmosphere), Maths (equation, geometry, algebra), History (civilisation, archaeological, chronological), English (narrative, characterisation, thematic), Geography (topography, hemisphere, vegetation). Each subject has specific vocabulary.Learn
    We Do (35 min)Subject-area spelling challenge: Write 5 words from each of 5 subjects (25 words total). Check spelling against reference. Sort into correct subject columns. Identify any cross-subject words (words that appear in multiple subjects).Guided
    You Do (10 min)Write 3 subject-specific words you will need for high school next year. Explain why each will be important.Written preparation

    Lesson 3: Independent Writing (You Do)

    Time: 55 minutes | Type: Independent

Use at least 15 high school-level vocabulary words correctly. 3. Apply full proofreading process. 4. Aim for 100% spelling accuracy. 5. Count errors and analyse. 6. Write: "My spelling is ready for high school because..."Full preparation
