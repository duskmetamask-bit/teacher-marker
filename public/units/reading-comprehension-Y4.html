Learning Intention

We are learning to analyse how characters develop across a narrative, examine how themes and symbols are constructed, and evaluate author craft with evidence-based interpretation..

  • I can track and explain how a character changes across a narrative.
  • I can analyse how the author uses language, structure and symbolism to create meaning.
  • I can make complex inferences about character motivation with text evidence.
  • I can synthesise information from multiple parts of the text to form interpretations.
  • I can evaluate an author's craft choices and explain their effect on the reader.

Week 1 — INTRODUCTION TO THE TEXT

50 min Assessment
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- ---------------- ---------

Task (20 min) Cold Task (same as Hot Task): In *The Wild Robot*, the robot named Roz must survive alone on an island. Analyse: How does Roz interact with the island's animals? 50 min Explicit Teaching
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- ---------------- ---------
Read (15 min) Reread Chapter 1 and 2. How is Roz described? What does she do? How does she think? Identify traits

Share (20 min) "What do you notice about how Roz communicates with the animals?" Discuss her persistence. Record observations in reading journals. Discussion

Lesson 3: The Island World (We Do)

50 min Guided Practice
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- --------- ---------
I Do (10 min) Model building a setting map: The island has rocky shores, dense forest, freshwater streams. What does each area represent for the story? Observe

You Do (15 min) Draw the island map from Roz's perspective. Label the key areas and what they mean for her survival. Creative analysis

Week 2 — CHARACTER DEVELOPMENT

Lesson 1: How Does Roz Change? (I Do)

50 min Explicit Teaching
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- ---------------- ---------
Read (15 min) Read Chapters 5–8 (Roz learns from the animals, discovers her purpose). Follow text
Mini-Lesson (10 min) Character development = how a character changes over time. Roz starts as a machine with a task, changes because... Model: "At the start, Roz only knows how to follow orders. In Chapter 6, she learns to... This changes her because..." Learn concept
You Do (25 min) Track Roz's development across chapters 1–8. Fill in a character development chart: Start of book What changes her

Lesson 2: Key Turning Points (I Do → We Do)

50 min Explicit Teaching + Guided
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- --------- ---------
I Do (10 min) Turning points = moments when a character makes a decision that changes everything. In *The Wild Robot*, the death of the heron family is a turning point. Why? Identify
We Do (25 min) Find the 3 turning points in chapters 1–10 together. For each: What happened? Why was it a turning point? How did it change Roz? Group analysis
You Do (15 min) Write about one turning point in detail: What happened, why it mattered, how Roz changed because of it. Use text evidence. Written analysis

Lesson 3: Character Arc Maps (You Do)

50 min Independent
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- --------- ---------
You Do (50 min) Create a character arc map for Roz: Start (task-oriented robot) → Rising action (learning from animals) → Climax (biggest challenge) → Resolution (what Roz becomes). Show how she changes physically and emotionally. Use quotes and page references. Visual character analysis

Week 3 — THE ROBOT-NATURE CONFLICT

Lesson 1: Machine vs Living World (I Do)

50 min Explicit Teaching
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- ---------------- ---------
Read (15 min) Read Chapters 9–12 (Roz builds shelter, learns about the seasons). Follow
Mini-Lesson (15 min) Central conflict = Machine vs Nature. Why is this tension important? How does it drive the story? Model: "The animals fear Roz because she is a machine. She must prove she can be trusted. This creates the central tension of the book." Learn concept
You Do (20 min) Create a T-chart: Machine traits Living/nature traits. Then: Where does Roz fit? Where does she not fit?

Lesson 2: Adaptation (I Do → We Do)

50 min Explicit Teaching + Guided
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- --------- ---------
I Do (10 min) Adaptation = changing to survive. How does Roz adapt? She learns animal behaviours, she modifies her body, she builds tools. Real-world example: humans adapt to environments. Connect
We Do (25 min) Track how Roz adapts through the first half of the book. Create an adaptation chain: She couldn't climb → She learned from the otters → She built a climbing device → She can now... Trace
You Do (15 min) Write: What does Peter's Brown message about adaptation seem to be? Find text evidence. Written reflection

Lesson 3: What Makes Something Alive? (We Do)

50 min Discussion
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- --------- ---------
We Do (30 min) Philosophical discussion: Is Roz alive? What makes something alive? Movement? Learning? Feeling? Connection? Use text evidence: Does Roz feel? Does she make choices? Does she form relationships? Deep discussion
You Do (20 min) Write a defence: "I believe Roz is/is not truly alive because... (text evidence)". Be ready to defend your position. Written position

Week 4 — SYMBOLISM

Lesson 1: What Does the Nest Symbolise? (I Do)

50 min Explicit Teaching
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- ---------------- ---------
Read (15 min) Read Chapters 13–15 (Roz adopts the young geese, teaches them migration). Follow
Mini-Lesson (15 min) Symbolism: A symbol is something that represents more than itself. The NEST represents HOME, BELONGING, FAMILY. Model finding and proving symbol meanings. Learn concept
You Do (20 min) Track the nest through the story: Where does it appear? What does it mean in each place? Write: "The nest is a symbol of ___ in this story because..." Written analysis

Lesson 2: Water, Fire, Tools (I Do → We Do)

50 min Explicit Teaching + Guided
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- --------- ---------
I Do (10 min) Key symbols in *The Wild Robot*: WATER (life, change, danger), FIRE (destruction, transformation, warmth), TOOLS (human vs nature, ingenuity). Find examples from the text. Identify symbols
We Do (25 min) Hunt for all water/fire/tool references in Chapters 1–18. Categorise each: What does it symbolise in context? Create a symbol tracker. Group search
You Do (15 min) Choose the most important symbol and write an analysis: Symbol What it literally means

Lesson 3: Symbol Tracking (You Do)

50 min Independent
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- --------- ---------
You Do (50 min) Create a comprehensive symbol tracking chart for the entire novel: Symbol First appearance

Week 5 — AUTHOR'S CRAFT

Lesson 1: How Does Brown Tell the Story? (I Do)

50 min Explicit Teaching
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- ---------------- ---------
Read (15 min) Read Chapters 16–19. Notice the chapter length, sentence length, use of illustration, tone. Notice craft
Mini-Lesson (15 min) Author's craft = the deliberate choices an author makes to tell a story effectively. Brown uses: Short chapters (build momentum), Illustrations (show vs tell), Simple language (deep meaning), Chapter titles (hints). Identify techniques
You Do (20 min) Find 5 examples of Brown's craft choices. For each: What did the author do? Why did they do it? What effect does it have on you as a reader? Evidence collection

Lesson 2: Voice and Perspective (I Do → We Do)

50 min Explicit Teaching + Guided
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- --------- ---------
I Do (10 min) The narrator of *The Wild Robot* is third-person but close to Roz's perspective. This means we see the world through her eyes. How does this affect our connection to Roz? Analyze perspective
We Do (25 min) Compare two passages: How does Brown describe an event when Roz is present? How does he describe it when she is not? What does this tell us about the narrator's closeness to Roz? Guided analysis
You Do (15 min) Write: "If the story was told from the perspective of the fox Brightbill, it would be different because..." Perspective writing

Lesson 3: Craft Analysis (We Do → You Do)

50 min Guided → Independent
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- --------- ---------
We Do (25 min) Together, write a craft analysis paragraph: "Peter Brown uses [TECHNIQUE] in [CHAPTER] to [EFFECT]. For example, he writes '...' which shows/creates/proves..." Group writing
You Do (25 min) Write your own craft analysis paragraph about one technique Brown uses. Include a direct quote with page number. Independent writing

Week 6 — THEMES AND INTERPRETATION

Lesson 1: Major Themes (I Do)

50 min Explicit Teaching
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- ---------------- ---------
Read (15 min) Read Chapters 20–23 (the migration, the storm, Roz's sacrifice). Follow
Mini-Lesson (15 min) Major themes in *The Wild Robot*: Nature vs Technology / What Makes Us Human / Belonging / Parent and Child / Adaptation and Survival. Model finding theme evidence. Learn
You Do (20 min) Identify the 2–3 strongest themes in the story. Find 3+ pieces of evidence for each. Write in your reading journal. Theme identification

Lesson 2: Multiple Interpretations (I Do → We Do)

50 min Explicit Teaching + Guided
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- --------- ---------
I Do (10 min) Different readers can have different valid interpretations of the same text. "I think the theme is about belonging" → VALID. "I think the theme is about nature vs technology" → ALSO VALID if supported by evidence. Learn
We Do (30 min) Class debate: What is the MOST important theme? Split into groups. Each group defends their theme with evidence. Vote on which has the strongest support. Debate
You Do (10 min) Write: My interpretation of the most important theme is... because... Written position

Lesson 3: Defending Your View (You Do)

50 min Independent
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- --------- ---------
You Do (50 min) Write a defended interpretation: 1. State your main interpretation of the story's theme. 2. Provide 3 pieces of text evidence from different chapters. 3. Explain how each piece supports your interpretation. 4. Address one counter-argument (what someone who disagrees might say). 5. Explain why your view still stands. Full analytical essay

Week 7 — SYNTHESIS AND EVALUATION

Lesson 1: Whole-Text Analysis (We Do)

50 min Guided Practice
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- --------- ---------
We Do (50 min) Whole-novel synthesis: 1. How does Roz change from Chapter 1 to the final chapter? 2. What symbols recur and how do they deepen meaning? 3. What is Brown's message about technology and nature? 4. How does the author's craft affect your emotional response? Discuss and note. Group synthesis

Lesson 2: Book Comparison (I Do)

50 min Explicit Teaching
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- ---------------- ---------
Read (15 min) Compare to a film or other book about robots and nature (*Wall-E*, *Big Hero 6*, *The Iron Giant*). What are the similarities in theme? The differences in craft? Cross-text
Mini-Lesson (15 min) Compare craft: *Wall-E* is visual, minimal dialogue. *The Wild Robot* uses illustration with richer text. Compare character development. Create a comparison chart. Chart
You Do (20 min) Write: "The way *The Wild Robot* handles the nature vs technology theme is more/less effective than *Wall-E* because..." Comparative writing

Lesson 3: Critical Review (You Do)

50 min Independent
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- --------- ---------
You Do (50 min) Write a critical review: 1. What is the book about? (summary without spoilers) 2. What does the author do well? (craft analysis with examples) 3. What did you personally take away from this story? 4. Who would enjoy this book and why? 5. Star rating (1–5) with justification. Full review
