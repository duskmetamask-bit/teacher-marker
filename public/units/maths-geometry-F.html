UNIT AT A GLANCE

**Year Level**Foundation (Pre-Primary / Kindergarten)
**Strand**Space

**Big Ideas**2D shapes: circles, squares, triangles, rectangles; positional language (above, below, beside, in front, behind); flipping and sliding

    A–E RUBRIC — Geometry (Foundation)

    Aligned to: AC9MFSP01, AC9MFSP02

    Criterion 1: Recognising and Naming 2D Shapes

    GradeDescriptor
    **A**Names circles, triangles, squares, and rectangles reliably; identifies shapes in different orientations; recognises shapes in real objects
    **B**Names most shapes reliably; beginning to recognise in different orientations
    **C**Names shapes with scaffolding; orientations that differ from typical confuse
    **D**Names 1-2 shapes with significant support
    **E**Unable to name 2D shapes

    Criterion 2: Sorting and Describing Shapes

    GradeDescriptor
    **A**Sorts shapes using own criteria and describes why; explains shape properties (straight/curved sides, corners)
    **B**Sorts using criteria; description developing
    **C**Sorts with scaffolding; description limited
    **D**Sorts with significant support
    **E**Unable to sort shapes

    Criterion 3: Positional Language

    GradeDescriptor
    **A**Uses positional language (above, below, beside, in front, behind, between) correctly to describe and position objects; follows multi-step positional directions
    **B**Uses positional language reliably; most terms used correctly
    **C**Uses 2-3 positional terms with scaffolding
    **D**Uses 1-2 positional terms with significant support
    **E**Unable to use positional language

    Criterion 4: Geometric Reasoning

    GradeDescriptor
    **A**Explains why a shape is what it is; recognises shapes in the environment; connects shapes to real objects
    **B**Explains shape naming with prompting; recognises shapes in environment
    **C**Beginning to connect shapes to real objects
    **D**Matches shapes with significant support
    **E**Unable to connect shapes to real objects

    SUCCESS CRITERIA — STUDENT-FACING

    I can:

  • [ ] Name and draw circles, triangles, squares, and rectangles
  • [ ] Sort shapes by their properties
  • [ ] Tell if a shape has straight or curved sides
  • [ ] Tell if a shape has corners
  • [ ] Use words like above, below, beside, in front, behind
  • [ ] Draw things in the right position

    • WEEK 1 — ENGAGE

    Focus: Cold Task + Naming 2D shapes

    5E Phase: Engage

    LESSON 1.2 — Circles, Squares, Triangles, Rectangles

    Duration: 40 minutes

    Type: Concept Introduction

    Learning Intentions

  • I am learning to name and recognise circles, squares, triangles, and rectangles
  • I am learning the difference between shapes

    • Success Criteria

  • I can name and draw all four basic shapes

    • I DO (15 min)

    " CIRCLE: round, like a ball. No corners. (Show examples)"
    " TRIANGLE: three sides, three corners. (Show examples)"
    " SQUARE: four equal sides, four corners. All sides the same. (Show examples)"
    " RECTANGLE: four sides, four corners. Like a square but not all sides equal. (Show examples)"

    WE DO (15 min)

    Shape hunt: Find shapes in the room.

    Shape sort: Cards showing shapes. Name each one.

    YOU DO (10 min)

  • Colour circles red, triangles blue, squares green, rectangles yellow.
  • Draw: a circle, a triangle, a square, a rectangle.
  • "Name two things in this room that are circles."

    • LESSON 1.3 — Shapes in Different Orientations

    Duration: 40 minutes

    Type: Guided Practice

    I DO (12 min)

    "A triangle can point up, down, sideways — it's still a triangle!"
    "A square rotated is still a square."
    "The SHAPE doesn't change even when we turn it."

    YOU DO (15 min)

  • Draw a triangle pointing DOWN.
  • Draw a square turned sideways.
  • "Can you find a rectangle that is standing on its short end? Draw it."
  • EXTENSION: "What happens to a rectangle if you rotate it 90 degrees?"

    • WEEK 2 — EXPLORE

    Focus: Sorting shapes

    5E Phase: Explore

    LESSON 2.1 — Sorting by Properties

    Duration: 40 minutes

    Type: Explore

    Learning Intentions

  • I am learning to sort shapes into groups
  • I am learning to explain HOW I sorted them

    • Success Criteria

  • I can sort shapes and explain my sorting rule

    • I DO (15 min)

    "I sorted shapes into two groups: STRAIGHT SIDES and CURVED SIDES."
    "Circle has curved sides only. Square has straight sides only. Triangle has straight sides only. Rectangle has straight sides."
    "Some shapes have CORNERS. Corners are where two sides meet."

    YOU DO (15 min)

  • Sort by: CURVED SIDES / STRAIGHT SIDES
  • Sort by: HAS CORNERS / NO CORNERS
  • "Sort the shapes two different ways. Tell your partner the rule."
  • EXTENSION: "Can a shape have both curved and straight sides?"

    • WEEK 3 — EXPLORE

    Focus: Positional language

    5E Phase: Explore

    LESSON 3.1 — Above, Below, Beside

    Duration: 40 minutes

    Type: Explore

    Learning Intentions

  • I am learning to use positional words: above, below, beside
  • I am learning to follow positional directions

    • Success Criteria

  • I can draw a shape above, below, and beside other shapes

    • I DO (15 min)

    "ABOVE means: on top of, higher than."
    "BELOW means: under, lower than."
    "BESIDE means: next to, on the side."

    Draw on board: ☐ above ○ → ○ below ☐

    YOU DO (15 min)

  • Draw a star ABOVE a moon.
  • Draw a fish BELOW a boat.
  • Draw a cat BESIDE a tree.
  • "Point to something above your head. Point to something below your desk."

    • LESSON 3.2 — In Front, Behind, Between

    Duration: 40 minutes

    Type: Guided Practice

    I DO (12 min)

    "IN FRONT: before, at the front."
    "BEHIND: after, at the back."
    "BETWEEN: in the middle of two things."

    YOU DO (15 min)

  • "In front of you is ___. Behind you is ___."
  • Draw: A dog BETWEEN two cats.
  • "Sit BETWEEN two people in your group."
  • EXTENSION: "Can you stand BETWEEN the door and the window?"

    • WEEK 4 — EXPLORE

    Focus: Describing shapes

    5E Phase: Explain

    LESSON 4.1 — What Makes a Shape?

    Duration: 40 minutes

    Type: Explain

    YOU DO (30 min)

  • Circle the shapes that have exactly 3 corners.
  • "A shape has 4 straight sides. What could it be?"
  • "A shape has no straight sides. What is it?"
  • "Draw a shape that has 4 corners. Name it."

    • WEEK 5 — EXPLORE

    Focus: Flips and slides

    5E Phase: Elaborate

    LESSON 5.1 — Flips and Slides

    Duration: 40 minutes

    Type: Elaborate

    Learning Intentions

  • I am learning to identify when a shape has flipped or slid
  • I am learning the words: flip, slide, turn

    • Success Criteria

  • I can tell if a shape has been flipped or slid

    • I DO (15 min)

    "SLIDE: move it without turning. The shape looks the same."
    "FLIP: turn it over. It looks like a mirror image."
    "TURN: rotate it. Point a different direction."

    YOU DO (15 min)

  • (Show shape in different positions) Is this a slide or a flip?
  • Draw a slide and a flip of a triangle.
  • "Can you flip a circle? Does it look different?"
  • EXTENSION: "If you flip a square twice, does it look the same?"

    Focus: Hot Task and celebration
