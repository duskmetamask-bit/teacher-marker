Unit Content

Sky and Landscape — Earth and Space Sciences Unit Plan

Unit Duration: 8 weeks × 3 lessons per week = 24 lessons

Year Level: Year 1

Curriculum Link: Australian Curriculum Science v9 — Earth and Space Sciences

Unit Big Idea: Observable changes occur in the sky and on the land every day and across seasons. The sun, moon, stars, and clouds move in predictable patterns.

Cold Task: Week 1, Lesson 1 — Draw and describe what the sky looks like at different times

Hot Task: Week 8, Lesson 1 — Observe and record sky changes over a day and explain patterns you notice

Unit Overview

Learning Intention:

We are learning to observe and describe changes in the sky and landscape over time, explain the patterns of day and night and the sun's movement, describe the moon's appearance over time, and explain how seasons affect living things and landscapes.

Success Criteria:

  • I can describe what the sky looks like at different times of day.
  • I can explain why we have day and night (Earth rotates).
  • I can observe and describe the moon's changing appearance.
  • I can name the four seasons and describe what happens in each.
  • I can use science words: rotate, orbit, sunrise, sunset, season, pattern, observe.

    WEEK 1 — ENGAGE: Sky Watching

    Lesson 1: Cold Task — Draw the Sky

    Time: 40 minutes | Type: Assessment

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Hook (5 min)Go outside. Look up. "What do you notice about the sky right now? Is it different from other times?"Observe
    Task (30 min)Cold Task: "Draw the sky at THREE different times: morning, midday, and afternoon/evening. For each drawing: What does the sky look like? Where is the sun? What colours can you see? Year 1: draw 3 skies with labels. Year 2: add 2 sentences about each time."Draw
    Share (5 min)3–4 students shareShare

    Success Criteria Check: Student draws sky at different times with some observation of sun position and sky colour.

    Lesson 2: Day and Night

    Time: 40 minutes | Type: Engage

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Why Do We Have Day and Night? (15 min)"The EARTH is always spinning (ROTATING). One side of Earth faces the SUN = DAY. The other side faces AWAY from the sun = NIGHT. Earth takes 24 hours to rotate once. When Australia is facing the sun, it is day here. When Australia rotates to face away, it becomes night."Learn
    Model It (10 min)Use a globe and a torch. Fix torch as sun. Rotate globe. When Perth faces the sun = day. When Perth faces away = night.Model
    Night Sky (10 min)"What do we see at NIGHT? MOON, STARS, sometimes planets. In the DAY: SUN, blue sky, clouds. The stars and moon don't disappear — the bright sun makes them hard to see."Learn
    Write (5 min)"Day and night happen because the Earth ROTATES (spins)."Write

    Success Criteria Check: Explains day and night as caused by Earth's rotation with a model.

    Lesson 3: The Moving Sun

    Time: 40 minutes | Type: Engage

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Sun Path (15 min)"The sun appears to move across the sky during the day. SUNRISE: sun comes up in the EAST. The sun is LOW in the sky in the morning. SUNSET: sun goes down in the WEST. The sun is highest at MIDDAY. The sun doesn't really move — the Earth rotates!"Learn
    Observe Shadows (10 min)Go outside. Place a stick in the ground. Mark where the shadow is NOW. "We will come back later and see if the shadow has moved."Mark shadow
    Predict and Check (10 min)"Predict: where will the shadow be in the afternoon? Why?" Check after recess/lunch. Has it moved?Check
    Write (5 min)"The sun appears to move across the sky from east to west. The Earth's rotation causes day and night."Write

    Success Criteria Check: Observes shadow movement and explains the sun's apparent daily motion.

    WEEK 2 — EXPLORE: Patterns in the Sky

    Lesson 1: Sun Movement Investigation

    Time: 40 minutes | Type: Explore

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Record Sun Position (15 min)Over a week, students record sun position at the same time each day (e.g., 10am). Use compass directions (East/North/West) or clock face (where in the sky?). Record in table.Record
    Analyse Pattern (15 min)"What do you notice? Is the sun in the same position every day? (In Perth: in summer, the sun is higher; in winter, lower.)"Analyse
    Seasonal Variation (10 min)"The sun's path changes with seasons! In SUMMER: sun rises more to the east, higher in the sky, longer days. In WINTER: sun rises more to the north, lower in the sky, shorter days. Perth is in the southern hemisphere!"Learn
    Draw (5 min)Draw summer sun path vs winter sun path.Draw

    Success Criteria Check: Records sun position and identifies seasonal differences in sun height.

    Lesson 2: Shadows Throughout the Day

    Time: 40 minutes | Type: Explore

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Shadow Investigation (15 min)Students place an object in the same spot. Mark shadow position and length at 9am, 12pm, 3pm. Measure shadow length with a ruler. Record.Investigate
    Pattern Discovery (15 min)"What pattern did you find? Morning shadows: long, point east. Midday shadows: shortest, point south. Afternoon shadows: long, point west. The shadow moves as the sun appears to move."Discover pattern
    Why Shadows Change (10 min)"Shadow changes because the sun appears in different positions. When sun is low, shadow is long. When sun is high, shadow is short. The shadow always points AWAY from the sun."Learn
    Draw (5 min)Draw shadow positions at morning, midday, afternoon. Label: LONG SHADOW / SHORT SHADOW.Draw

    Success Criteria Check: Investigates shadow changes and identifies the relationship between sun position and shadow length/direction.

    Lesson 3: Stars and Moon

    Time: 40 minutes | Type: Explore

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Stars (10 min)"STARS are giant balls of hot gas, very far away. The sun is our closest star. At night, we can see thousands of stars. Stars make patterns called CONSTELLATIONS. In Australia: the Southern Cross (Crux) is on our flag. Indigenous Australians saw constellations as stories."Learn
    Moon (10 min)"The MOON is our closest neighbour in space. It does NOT make its own light — it reflects sunlight. The moon goes around (ORBITS) the Earth once per month (~29.5 days). As it orbits, we see different amounts of its sunlit side = MOON PHASES."Learn
    Moon Observation (15 min)Students draw what the moon looks like RIGHT NOW (or make a prediction if not visible). "Over the next month, we will observe the moon and track how its shape changes."Observe
    Write (5 min)"The moon reflects light from the sun. The moon orbits the Earth."Write

    Success Criteria Check: Explains that the moon reflects sunlight and orbits the Earth.

    WEEK 3 — EXPLAIN: The Moon

    Lesson 1: Moon Observation Over Time

    Time: 40 minutes | Type: Explain

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Moon Phases (15 min)"The moon changes shape in a regular pattern over about 4 weeks: NEW MOON — not visible (dark). CRESCENT — thin sliver. FIRST QUARTER — half moon (right side lit). GIBBOUS — almost full. FULL MOON — fully lit circle. Then it goes BACKWARDS: Gibbous, Quarter, Crescent, New."Learn
    Record Moon for a Month (15 min)Students keep a moon diary: draw the moon each night (or check app/picture if cloudy). After 2 weeks, look for pattern.Keep diary
    Why Does It Change? (10 min)"The moon doesn't actually change shape. The sun lights up HALF the moon at all times. As the moon orbits, we see different amounts of the lit half. We never see the dark side of the moon from Earth."Learn
    Exit Ticket (5 min)"Draw the phases in order: Full → ? → ? → New."Draw

    Success Criteria Check: Observes moon over time and correctly orders moon phases.

    Lesson 2: Moon Phases in Detail

    Time: 40 minutes | Type: Explain

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Detailed Phases (20 min)NEW MOON: moon between Earth and sun, dark side faces us. WAXING CRESCENT: growing from new toward first quarter. FIRST QUARTER: right half lit (in northern hemisphere — in southern hemisphere, left half lit — but we see it from below!). WAXING GIBBOUS: almost full, growing. FULL MOON: full face lit. WANING GIBBOUS: shrinking. LAST QUARTER: opposite half lit. WANING CRESCENT: thin sliver shrinking back to new."Learn
    Oreo Cookie Activity (10 min)Use Oreo cookies (or paper circles). Separate cream. Scrape to show different moon phase shapes. Match to moon phase names.Activity
    Draw All Phases (10 min)Draw all 8 phases in order. Label each.Draw

    Success Criteria Check: Correctly identifies and sequences all 8 major moon phases.

    Lesson 3: Moon and Us

    Time: 40 minutes | Type: Elaborate

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Moon's Effects (10 min)"The moon affects Earth: GRAVITY — moon's gravity causes OCEAN TIDES (high and low tides). Without the moon, tides would be much weaker. ANIMAL BEHAVIOUR — some animals use moon for navigation (migrating birds, sea turtles). HUMANS — full moon is linked to folklore and may affect sleep (disputed evidence)."Learn
    Moon Stories (15 min)Indigenous Australian cultures have rich astronomical knowledge. The Yolngu people: moon is a man carrying a torch (explains full moon brightness). Many cultures: full moon linked to special events. Students share if they know moon stories from their culture.Share
    Design Your Own Moon Story (10 min)"Design a story to explain why the moon changes shape. Your story must include: characters, a reason for the changing shape, and a pattern."Design
    Write (5 min)"The moon has phases that repeat in a regular cycle."Write

    Success Criteria Check: Explains at least 1 effect of the moon on Earth (tides) and creates a moon story.

    WEEK 4 — ELABORATE: Seasons in Australia

    Lesson 1: Four Seasons

    Time: 40 minutes | Type: Explain

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Seasons in Australia (15 min)"AUSTRALIA is in the SOUTHERN HEMISPHERE. Our seasons are OPPOSITE to the northern hemisphere. SUMMER (Dec–Feb): hottest, longest days, sun high, beach weather. AUTUMN (Mar–May): cooling down, days getting shorter, leaves fall from deciduous trees. WINTER (Jun–Aug): coldest, shortest days, sun lowest in sky, rain in Perth. SPRING (Sep–Nov): warming up, flowers blooming, wildflowers in Perth."Learn
    Compare to Northern Hemisphere (10 min)"When it is SUMMER in Australia, it is WINTER in Europe and North America. This is because Earth is tilted on its axis (23.5°). When Earth's southern hemisphere is tilted toward the sun = southern summer. When tilted away = southern winter."Learn
    Perth Seasons (10 min)Perth has a Mediterranean climate: hot dry summers, mild wet winters. This is different from eastern Australia which has more humidity in summer. Perth's seasons: very sunny summer, rainy winter, lovely spring and autumn.Learn
    Draw Seasons Wheel (5 min)Draw a 4-part wheel. Label: SUMMER (Dec-Feb), AUTUMN (Mar-May), WINTER (Jun-Aug), SPRING (Sep-Nov). Add one key feature for each.Draw

    Success Criteria Check: Correctly names and orders all 4 seasons with correct Australian months.

    Lesson 2: How Seasons Affect Us

    Time: 40 minutes | Type: Elaborate

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Seasonal Activities (15 min)SUMMER: swimming, beach, BBQs, cricket, school holidays at Christmas. AUTUMN: back to school, cooler weather, AFL footy, harvest season. WINTER: rugging up, hot chocolate, indoor activities, school holidays in July. SPRING: wildflower season, outdoor sports, school concerts.Learn
    Seasonal Clothing (10 min)SUMMER: shorts, t-shirts, thongs, sunscreen, hat. WINTER: jeans, jumpers, jackets, boots, scarf. Which seasons use which?Sort
    Design Your Wardrobe (10 min)"Design an outfit for summer and one for winter. What would you wear? Why is each suitable for the season?"Design
    Write (5 min)"In summer I wear [clothes] because it is [hot]. In winter I wear [clothes] because it is [cold]."Write

    Success Criteria Check: Connects seasonal weather to appropriate clothing and activities.

    Lesson 3: Animals and Seasons

    Time: 40 minutes | Type: Elaborate

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Seasonal Animal Behaviour (15 min)"Animals respond to seasons: BIRDS: some migratory birds (cuckoos, swallows) arrive in spring to breed, leave in autumn. KANGAROOS: less active in very hot summer days (shade-seeking). KOALAS: breed in spring/summer, less active in heat. SNAKES: more active in warmer months, hibernate in winter."Learn
    Hibernation and Migration (15 min)"Some animals escape harsh conditions: MIGRATION: birds fly to warmer places. HIBERNATION: echidnas and some bats enter deep sleep through winter (reduced metabolism). AESTIVATION: some frogs sleep through hot dry summer."Learn
    Investigate One Animal (10 min)Research: How does ONE Australian animal respond to seasons? Draw and write findings.Research

    Success Criteria Check: Investigates seasonal behaviour of at least 2 Australian animals.

    WEEK 5 — ELABORATE: Weather Patterns

    Lesson 1: Weather Types

    Time: 40 minutes | Type: Explain

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Weather Elements (10 min)"WEATHER is made up of: TEMPERATURE (hot/cold), PRECIPITATION (rain/snow/hail), WIND (calm/windy), CLOUD COVER (sunny/cloudy/overcast), HUMIDITY (dry/humid). Weather reports talk about all these elements."Learn
    Weather Types (15 min)SUNNY: clear sky, warm. CLOUDY: clouds cover sky. OVERCAST: completely covered. RAINY: precipitation falling. WINDY: strong air movement. STORMY: thunder, lightning, heavy rain. FOGGY: droplets suspended in air reducing visibility.Learn
    Weather Sort (10 min)Sort weather cards into: HOT WEATHER / COLD WEATHER / WET WEATHER / WINDY WEATHER.Sort
    Write (5 min)"Weather includes: temperature, clouds, rain, and wind."Write

    Success Criteria Check: Describes weather using at least 4 weather elements.

    Lesson 2: Weather Records

    Time: 40 minutes | Type: Explore

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Weather Diary (15 min)Students keep a 5-day weather diary: record each day: DATE, TEMPERATURE (estimate or check thermometer), CLOUD COVER (sunny/partly cloudy/cloudy/rainy), WIND (calm/breezy/windy), ANY PRECIPITATION (yes/no).Record
    Analyse Weather Patterns (15 min)After 5 days: What patterns did you notice? Were some days similar? Was there a pattern in temperature vs cloud cover?Analyse
    Compare to Climate (10 min)"WEATHER = day-to-day atmospheric conditions. CLIMATE = average weather over a long period. Perth's climate: Mediterranean — hot dry summers, mild wet winters."Learn
    Draw Graph (5 min)Draw a simple bar graph of temperatures over 5 days.Draw

    Success Criteria Check: Keeps a weather diary for 5 days and identifies at least 1 weather pattern.

    Lesson 3: Predicting Weather

    Time: 40 minutes | Type: Elaborate

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Weather Signs (15 min)"People have predicted weather for thousands of years using signs: CLOUDS: dark clouds → rain coming. WIND DIRECTION: northerly winds in Perth bring cold fronts from the south (winter rain). BAROMETER: falling pressure → storm approaching. RED SKY AT NIGHT: proverb says clear weather coming. ANIMAL BEHAVIOUR: birds flying low → rain."Learn
    Modern Forecasting (10 min)"Modern weather forecasting: SATELLITES photograph clouds from space. WEATHER STATIONS record temperature, pressure, wind, humidity. SUPERCOMPUTERS run models to predict future weather. BOM (Bureau of Meteorology) provides forecasts."Learn
    Make a Prediction (10 min)Based on current observations (clouds, wind, pressure), predict tomorrow's weather for Perth. Explain your prediction using evidence.Predict
    Write (5 min)"Weather can be predicted using observations and technology."Write

    Success Criteria Check: Makes a weather prediction using observable evidence.

    WEEK 6 — EXPLAIN: Looking at Land

    Lesson 1: Types of Land

    Time: 40 minutes | Type: Explain

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Landforms (15 min)"LANDFORMS = natural shapes on Earth's surface. MOUNTAINS: high, steep, pointed or rounded peaks. HILLS: lower than mountains, rounded tops. PLAINS/VALLEYS: flat or gently undulating areas. COAST: where land meets ocean. DESERTS: very dry areas with little rain. RIVERS: flowing water that carves channels. PLATEAUS: flat raised areas."Learn
    Perth Landforms (10 min)"Perth and WA landforms: SWAN COASTAL PLAIN: where Perth is built. DARLING ESCARPMENT: ridge of ancient rocks east of Perth. INLAND: wheatbelt, goldfields, deserts. COAST: beaches, limestone cliffs, Rottnest Island."Learn
    Landform Sort (10 min)Sort landform cards: mountain, hill, plain, coast, river, desert. Match images to names.Sort
    Draw (5 min)Draw one landform and label it.Draw

    Success Criteria Check: Identifies at least 5 different landform types.

    Lesson 2: How Land Changes

    Time: 40 minutes | Type: Explain

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Natural Land Change (15 min)"Land changes naturally over time: WEATHERING: rain, heat, cold break rocks into smaller pieces. EROSION: wind, water, ice move broken rock pieces away. DEPOSITION: eroded material settles in new places (river deltas, beaches). All these happen slowly — over millions of years!"Learn
    Fast Land Change (10 min)"FAST changes: EARTHQUAKES (shake and crack land), VOLCANIC ERUPTIONS (lava covers land), FLOODS (move huge amounts of sediment), LANDSLIDES (mountain material slides down)."Learn
    Human Land Change (10 min)"Humans change land: MINING (digging into earth), DEFORESTATION (clearing trees), BUILDING (construction changes land), FARMING (changes soil and vegetation). These changes can be faster than natural processes."Learn
    Write (5 min)"Land changes through natural processes and human activities."Write

    Success Criteria Check: Identifies at least 3 types of natural land change and at least 1 human cause.

    Lesson 3: Land Use

    Time: 40 minutes | Type: Elaborate

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    How Do We Use Land? (15 min)"HUMAN LAND USE: RESIDENTIAL (housing), AGRICULTURAL (farming crops and livestock), INDUSTRIAL (factories, mining), COMMERCIAL (shops, offices), RECREATIONAL (parks, beaches, sports fields), CONSERVATION (national parks, nature reserves)."Learn
    Perth Land Use (10 min)"Perth: most people live on the Swan Coastal Plain. Rural areas: wheat and sheep farming. Industrial areas: Kwinana, Welshpool. National parks: Kings Park, Yanchep, John Forrest."Learn
    Land Use Conflict (10 min)"Sometimes land uses conflict: HOUSING vs CONSERVATION (building houses vs protecting bushland). FARMING vs WATER QUALITY (farming near rivers affects water). MINING vs TOURISM (mining impacts scenery)."Learn
    Design (5 min)"Design a piece of land. How would you use it? Why?"Design

    Success Criteria Check: Identifies at least 4 types of land use and evaluates trade-offs.
