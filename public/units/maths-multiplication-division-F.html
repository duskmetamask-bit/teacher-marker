UNIT AT A GLANCE

**Year Level**Foundation (Pre-Primary / Kindergarten)
**Strand**Number
**Duration**8 weeks × 3 lessons/week = 24 lessons
**Model**5E Instructional Model
**Pedagogical Frame**Gradual Release: I Do → We Do → You Do
**Mentor Text***Pete the Cat and His Four Groovy Buttons* — Eric Litwin
**Big Ideas**Multiplication is making equal groups; division is sharing equally or making equal groups; doubling is a powerful beginning to multiplication
**Driving Question***What happens when we make equal groups, and what happens when we share equally?*

AUSTRALIAN CURRICULUM v9 — CONTENT DESCRIPTORS

Foundation

CodeDescriptor
**AC9MFN01**Name and order numbers to at least 20; use counting strategies to solve problems involving unequal sharing or grouping, including skip counting, counting on, and counting backwards
**AC9MFN03**Partition collections of objects into two groups and describe the parts; acknowledge the parts relate to the whole in the context of practical situations
**AC9MFN04**Add and subtract numbers within 10, using concrete and pictorial strategies including taking from, separating, and comparing collections

General Capabilities

  • Numeracy — core
  • Personal & Social Capability — sharing, turn-taking

    • BIG UNDERSTANDINGS

    Students will understand that:

  • Equal groups mean each group has the SAME number of objects
  • Multiplication means making equal groups and counting the total
  • Division means sharing equally or finding how many in each group
  • Doubling means making two groups the same size
  • Skip-counting is faster than counting one by one
  • Arrays arrange objects in equal rows and columns

    • ESSENTIAL QUESTIONS

  • What makes groups equal?
  • How do we make equal groups?
  • How do we share fairly?
  • What is doubling?
  • How can skip-counting help us?

    • VOCABULARY — TIERED

    Tier 1 (everyday)

    group, equal, same, share, each, altogether, double, pairs, rows, how many in each

    Tier 2 (academic)

    equal groups, unequal groups, fair share, double, count by 2s, count by 5s, array, rows, columns

    Tier 3 (mathematical)

    multiplication, division, skip-counting, factor, product, quotient, dividend, divisor

    A–E RUBRIC — Multiplication and Division (Foundation)

    Aligned to: AC9MFN01, AC9MFN03, AC9MFN04

    Criterion 1: Counting and Skip-Counting

    GradeDescriptor
    **A**Counts by 2s, 5s, and 10s from 0 reliably; uses skip-counting to solve problems; connects skip-counting to equal groups
    **B**Counts by 2s and 5s from 0 reliably; beginning to use skip-counting as a strategy
    **C**Counts by 2s and 5s from 0 with some support; beginning to connect to equal groups
    **D**Counts by 2s with support; skip-counting requires physical objects
    **E**Unable to skip-count; counts by ones only

    Criterion 2: Recognising and Creating Equal Groups

    GradeDescriptor
    **A**Independently sorts objects into equal groups; creates equal groups with given constraints; explains why groups are equal
    **B**Creates equal groups reliably; beginning to sort and compare groups; explains equal vs. unequal
    **C**With support, creates equal groups within 10; beginning to recognise equal vs. unequal
    **D**Creates equal groups of small numbers (≤5) with significant scaffolding
    **E**Unable to create equal groups; requires significant support

    Criterion 3: Doubling

    GradeDescriptor
    **A**Doubles numbers to 10 automatically; explains doubling as "twice as much"; connects doubling to equal groups
    **B**Doubles numbers to 10 reliably; beginning to connect to equal groups
    **C**Doubles with concrete materials; begins to recall doubles to 5+5
    **D**Doubles 1-5 with support; physical doubling required
    **E**Unable to double; does not demonstrate understanding

    Criterion 4: Sharing (Division as Sharing)

    GradeDescriptor
    **A**Shares collections equally between 2 and 3 groups independently; explains that equal sharing means each person gets the same; relates sharing to subtraction
    **B**Shares between 2 groups reliably; attempts sharing between 3 groups with minor errors
    **C**Shares between 2 groups with scaffolding; physical sharing required; beginning to connect to equal groups
    **D**Shares within 6 between 2 groups with significant support
    **E**Unable to share equally; does not demonstrate understanding

    Criterion 5: Arrays

    GradeDescriptor
    **A**Creates arrays with given rows and columns; counts array total using skip-counting; explains rows and columns
    **B**Creates arrays for 2×3, 2×4, 3×3; beginning to use skip-counting to find total
    **C**With support, creates simple arrays (2×2, 2×3); counts all to find total
    **D**Creates arrays with significant scaffolding; counts all
    **E**Unable to create arrays; does not demonstrate understanding

    Criterion 6: Mathematical Reasoning

    GradeDescriptor
    **A**Explains thinking in words and drawings; connects multiplication to real situations; justifies reasoning
    **B**Explains thinking with prompting; connects to real situations with support
    **C**Uses drawings to represent problems; limited verbal explanation
    **D**Relies on adult interpretation; drawings may not reflect problem
    **E**Unable to communicate mathematical thinking

    EAL/D Students

  • Accept oral explanation
  • Allow processing time

  • Investigate: what happens when you double twice?
  • Challenge: what is double double 2? (2×2×2 = 8)
  • Problem-posing with equal groups

    LESSON 1.2 — Equal Groups: What Is Multiplication?

    Duration: 40 minutes

    Type: Concept Introduction

    Learning Intentions

  • I am learning that multiplication means making equal groups
  • I am learning that equal groups have the SAME number in each group

    • Success Criteria

  • I can tell if groups are equal or unequal
  • I can make equal groups

    • I DO (12 min)

    "Pete has 4 buttons on each row of his shirt. That's 2 ROWS. 4 and 4. That's GROUPS OF 4."
    "2 groups of 4. Each group has the SAME number. That's called MULTIPLICATION."
    "When we make equal groups, we put the SAME number in EACH group."

    WE DO (18 min)

    Equal or unequal sort:

    Cards showing groups. Sort into: EQUAL / UNEQUAL.

    Make equal groups:

  • Put 4 in each group × 2 groups = 8
  • Put 3 in each group × 3 groups = 9

    • YOU DO (10 min)

  • Circle the equal groups: 3 groups of 4 / 3 groups of 4 and 1 group of 5
  • Make 3 groups of 2 using counters.
  • "Are these groups equal? 4 and 4 and 4? YES / NO"

    • LESSON 1.3 — Making Equal Groups

    Duration: 40 minutes

    Type: Guided Practice

    Learning Intentions

  • I am learning to make equal groups from a collection
  • I am learning to count equal groups

    • Success Criteria

  • I can make 3 groups of 2 from 6 counters
  • I can count equal groups

    • I DO (12 min)

    "I have 6 counters. I want to make 3 equal groups. How many in each?"
    "6 shared across 3 groups. 2 in each group."
    "2 + 2 + 2 = 6. That's the same as 3 groups of 2."
    "We can say: 3 × 2 = 6. Three groups of two equals six."
    "The × sign means GROUPS OF."

    WE DO (18 min)

    Make equal groups:

  • Make 4 groups of 2: ___ altogether
  • Make 3 groups of 3: ___ altogether
  • Make 2 groups of 5: ___ altogether
  • Make 5 groups of 2: ___ altogether

    • YOU DO (10 min)

  • Make 4 groups of 3. How many altogether? Draw and write: 4 × 3 = ___
  • Make 2 groups of 6. How many altogether? ___
  • EXTENSION: "Can you make equal groups of 4 from 10 counters? Why or why not?"

    LESSON 2.1 — Sharing Equally Between 2 People

    Duration: 40 minutes

    Type: Explore

    Learning Intentions

  • I am learning to share objects equally between 2 people
  • I am learning that fair sharing means each person gets the same

    • Success Criteria

  • I can share 8 objects equally between 2 people
  • I can explain that fair sharing means equal amounts

    • I DO (12 min)

    "Pete has 8 buttons. He wants to share them equally between himself and his friend. How many does each get?"

    Model with counters: give 1 button to Pete, 1 to friend. Repeat until all given.

    Pete: 4. Friend: 4. Each gets the SAME.

    "8 shared equally between 2 = 4 each."
    "This is DIVISION. We write: 8 ÷ 2 = 4."
    "The ÷ sign means SHARE EQUALLY."

    WE DO (18 min)

    Sharing practice:

  • Share 6 counters between 2 people: ___ each
  • Share 10 counters between 2 people: ___ each
  • Share 8 counters between 2 people: ___ each
  • Share 4 counters between 2 people: ___ each

    • YOU DO (10 min)

  • Share 12 between 2: ___ ÷ 2 = ___
  • "Sam shares 10 stickers between 2 friends. How many each?"
  • EXTENSION: "Sam shares 9 between 2 friends. Is this fair? What happens?"

    • LESSON 2.2 — Sharing Equally Between 3 People

    Duration: 40 minutes

    Type: Explore

    I DO (12 min)

    "12 cookies shared between 3 friends. Each friend gets the same. How many?"

    Model: give 1 to each friend. Repeat. Each gets 4.

    "12 ÷ 3 = 4."

    YOU DO (10 min)

  • Share 9 between 3: 9 ÷ 3 = ___
  • Share 6 between 3: 6 ÷ 3 = ___
  • Share 15 between 3: ___ ÷ 3 = ___
  • EXTENSION: "Share 10 between 3. Can you share equally? What's left over?"

    • LESSON 2.3 — Equal Sharing and Taking Away

    Duration: 40 minutes

    Type: Guided Practice

    I DO (12 min)

    "Division and subtraction are connected. 12 ÷ 3 = 4 means: how many 3s are in 12?"
    "Subtraction version: take away 3 at a time. 12 - 3 = 9, 9 - 3 = 6, 6 - 3 = 3, 3 - 3 = 0. How many 3s did we take away? 4!"

    YOU DO (10 min)

  • How many 2s in 8? 8 ÷ 2 = ___ (using subtraction)
  • How many 3s in 9? 9 ÷ 3 = ___
  • EXTENSION: "How many 4s in 12?"

    LESSON 3.1 — Doubling

    Duration: 40 minutes

    Type: Explore

    Learning Intentions

  • I am learning to double numbers to 10
  • I am learning that doubling means making two groups the same size

    • Success Criteria

  • I can double 4 to get 8
  • I can explain: double means twice as much

    • I DO (12 min)

    "Double means TWO GROUPS the same size. Double 4: I have 4, I make another group of 4. Two groups of 4. Total = 8."
    "4 + 4 = 8. That's the same as 2 × 4 = 8."
    "Doubling is the beginning of multiplying by 2!"

    WE DO (18 min)

    Double practice:

  • Double 3 = ___ (3 + 3 = 6)
  • Double 5 = ___
  • Double 2 = ___
  • Double 6 = ___
  • Double 4 = ___

    • YOU DO (10 min)

  • Double 7 = ___ Double 8 = ___
  • "Draw double 5. Write the number sentence."
  • EXTENSION: "What is double 10? Can you double 12?"

    • LESSON 3.2 — Skip-Counting by 2s and 5s

    Duration: 40 minutes

    Type: Explore

    I DO (12 min)

    "Skip-counting is faster than counting one by one. Count by 2s: 2, 4, 6, 8, 10..."
    "Count by 5s: 5, 10, 15, 20..."
    "Skip-counting by 2s: 2, 4, 6 — that's the 2× table: 1×2, 2×2, 3×2..."

    WE DO (18 min)

    Skip-counting practice:

    Count by 2s: 2, 4, ___, ___, ___, ___, ___, ___, ___, ___

    Count by 5s: 5, 10, ___, ___, ___, ___, ___, ___, ___, ___

    Fill in the gaps:

  • 2, 4, 6, ___, 10, ___, 14, ___, ___
  • 5, 10, ___, ___, 25, ___, ___, ___, ___

    • YOU DO (10 min)

  • Count by 2s to 20.
  • Count by 5s to 50.
  • "How many groups of 2 make 12?"
  • EXTENSION: "Count by 3s: 3, 6, ___, 12, ___"

    • LESSON 3.3 — Skip-Counting to Find Totals

    Duration: 40 minutes

    Type: Guided Practice

    I DO (12 min)

    "5 groups of 2. Skip-count by 2: 2, 4, 6, 8, 10. Total: 10. That's 5 × 2 = 10."
    "4 groups of 5. Skip-count by 5: 5, 10, 15, 20. Total: 20. That's 4 × 5 = 20."

    YOU DO (10 min)

  • 3 groups of 5 = ___ (skip-count)
  • 5 groups of 2 = ___
  • 4 groups of 3 = ___ (if accessible)
  • EXTENSION: "How many groups of 2 make 20?"

    LESSON 4.1 — Groups Of: The Multiplication Sentence

    Duration: 40 minutes

    Type: Explain

    Learning Intentions

  • I am learning to write multiplication as "groups of"
  • I am learning to write 3 × 4 to mean 3 groups of 4

    • Success Criteria

  • I can write "3 groups of 4" as 3 × 4 = 12

    • I DO (12 min)

    "3 groups of 4 means: 3 sets, each with 4."
    "4 + 4 + 4 = 12."
    "We write: 3 × 4 = 12. Three times four equals twelve."
    "The × sign means GROUPS OF. 3 groups of 4."

    WE DO (18 min)

    Write as multiplication:

  • 4 groups of 2 = ___ × ___ = ___
  • 2 groups of 5 = ___ × ___ = ___
  • 3 groups of 4 = ___ × ___ = ___
  • 5 groups of 2 = ___ × ___ = ___

    • YOU DO (10 min)

  • 4 groups of 3 = 4 × 3 = ___
  • 6 groups of 2 = 6 × 2 = ___
  • Draw 2 groups of 6. Write the multiplication.
  • EXTENSION: "2 × 6 and 6 × 2 — are they the same?"

    • LESSON 4.2 — Arrays

    Duration: 40 minutes

    Type: Explain

    Learning Intentions

  • I am learning that an array arranges objects in equal rows
  • I am learning to count an array using skip-counting

    • Success Criteria

  • I can create a 3×4 array and count it

    • I DO (12 min)

    "An ARRAY is objects arranged in EQUAL ROWS."
    "2 rows of 4: ●●●● / ●●●●"
    "That's 2 × 4 = 8."
    "Count by rows: 4, 8. Count by columns: 2, 4, 6, 8."

    WE DO (18 min)

    Create arrays:

  • 3 rows of 4 = ___ (skip-count 4, 8, 12)
  • 4 rows of 3 = ___ (skip-count 3, 6, 9, 12)
  • 2 rows of 5 = ___
  • 5 rows of 2 = ___

    • Notice: 3×4 and 4×3 BOTH = 12. Commutative property in Foundation!

    YOU DO (10 min)

  • Draw a 3×2 array. How many?
  • Draw a 2×6 array. How many?
  • "Are 2×6 and 6×2 the same? YES / NO. How can you prove it?"
  • EXTENSION: "Can you make an array with 12 dots in two different ways?"

    LESSON 5.1 — Multiplication Story Problems

    Duration: 40 minutes

    Type: Explain

    I DO (12 min)

    "There are 4 plates. Each plate has 3 apples. How many apples altogether?"
    "4 groups of 3. 4 × 3 = 12."
    "Draw: ●●● / ●●● / ●●● / ●●●. Count by 3s: 3, 6, 9, 12."

    YOU DO (15 min)

  • "There are 5 bags. Each bag has 2 books. How many books?"
  • "There are 3 tables. Each table has 4 children. How many children?"
  • "Pete puts 4 buttons on each of 2 shirts. How many buttons?"

    • LESSON 5.2 — Division Story Problems

    Duration: 40 minutes

    Type: Explain

    I DO (12 min)

    "12 cookies shared between 4 friends. How many each?"
    "12 ÷ 4 = 3. Each friend gets 3."
    "Or: 12 cookies. Groups of 3. How many groups? 12 ÷ 3 = 4."

    YOU DO (15 min)

  • "10 crayons shared between 2 people. How many each?"
  • "15 stars shared between 3 people. How many each?"
  • "24 apples. Groups of 6. How many groups?"

    • LESSON 5.3 — Choosing Multiplication or Division

    Duration: 40 minutes

    Type: Guided Practice

    I DO (12 min)

    "If the question says: GROUPS OF or EACH HAS or ROWS — think MULTIPLY."
    "If the question says: SHARE EQUALLY or DIVIDE or SPLIT — think DIVIDE."

    YOU DO (15 min)

  • "5 plates, 3 on each — how many? × or ÷?"
  • "12 shared between 3 — how many each? × or ÷?"
  • "4 rows of 6 — how many? × or ÷?"
