Learning Intention

We are learning to describe objects by their properties and explore how objects can be changed by pushing, pulling, twisting, and folding..

  • I can describe objects using size, shape, colour, texture, and hardness.
  • I can sort objects by one property at a time.
  • I can predict what will happen when I push, pull, twist, or fold an object.
  • I can observe and describe what changed after an object was pushed, pulled, twisted, or folded.
  • I can use科学 words (science words) to describe objects and changes.

Week 1 — ENGAGE: What Are Objects Made Of?

Lesson 1: Cold Task — Sort and Explain

30 min Assessment (Formative Pre-Assessment)
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- ---------------- ---------
Introduction (5 min) Show a collection of objects: a ball, a block, a pencil, a leaf, a sock, a cup. "Look at these objects. What do you see?" Look and observe
Task (20 min) Cold Task: "Sort these objects into groups. Tell us HOW you sorted them and WHY you put things together." Foundation: sort and verbally explain. Sort objects, explain sorting rule
Share (5 min) "Who sorted by colour? Who sorted by shape? Who sorted by what the object is made of?" Share sorting approaches
DifferentiationEAL students may sort physically and point to show their sorting rule. NEP students: reduced number of objects (4 items).

Lesson 2: Describe It!

30 min Engage (Vocabulary Building)
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- ---------------- ---------
Model Describing (10 min) Hold up a ball. "I can describe this ball. It is ROUND. It is SMOOTH. It is RED. It is HARD. It is SMALL." Use hand gestures for each word. Repeat with a pencil: LONG, CYLINDRICAL, YELLOW, SMOOTH, HARD. Listen and watch
Introduce Word Wall (10 min) Show word wall cards: big/small, long/short, rough/smooth, hard/soft, round/square, red/blue/green/yellow. Say each word, show picture. Point to words, repeat
Describe a Partner Game (10 min) "Find something in the room that is ROUND. Find something that is HARD. Find something that is SMOOTH." Students move to the object. Find and show objects

Lesson 3: How Are Objects the Same and Different?

30 min Engage (Comparison)
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- ---------------- ---------
Same and Different (10 min) Hold up two objects: a big ball and a small ball. "How are these the SAME? (Both round, both bounce.) How are they DIFFERENT? (Different sizes.)" Model thinking aloud. Observe and contribute
Partner Sort Challenge (15 min) In pairs, receive a bag of objects. "First, find TWO things that are the SAME. Tell your partner how they are the same. Then find TWO things that are DIFFERENT. Tell your partner how they are different." Partner exploration
Share (5 min) Each pair shares one "same" and one "different" with the class. Share findings
DifferentiationEAL students use hand gestures to show same/different. NEP students: same-activity only with teacher guidance.

Week 2 — EXPLORE: Big, Medium, Small and Everything In Between

Lesson 1: Size Sorting

30 min Explore (Investigation)
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- ---------------- ---------
Size Introduction (10 min) Show three objects: a big book, a medium cup, a small marble. "BIG, MEDIUM, and SMALL are sizes. Can you show me something BIG in this room? Something SMALL?" Show objects in room
Size Ordering (10 min) Put 5 objects on the table in random order: large pillow, medium shoe, small button, large towel, medium crayon. "Can you put these from SMALLEST to BIGGEST?" Model thinking: "This one looks smallest... let me check by putting them next to each other." Order objects by size
Record (10 min) Students draw the 5 objects in order from smallest to biggest in their science books. Draw size ordering

Lesson 2: Does Size Change How We Use It?

30 min Explore (Investigation)
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- ---------------- ---------
Think About It (10 min) "If I gave you a HUGE toothbrush, could you brush your teeth? If I gave you a TINY chair, could you sit on it?" Show images. "Why not? How does SIZE change how we USE things?" Discuss
Investigation (15 min) Students receive 3 objects of different sizes that do the same job: 3 spoons (tiny, medium, huge), 3 cups, 3 papers. "Can you use the HUGE spoon to eat? Can you write with the TINY crayon? Test it out!" Test different sized objects
Discuss Findings (5 min) "What happened when you tried to use the wrong-sized tool? Why do we need the RIGHT size?" Share observations

Lesson 3: Size and Strength

30 min Explore (Investigation)
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- ---------------- ---------
Question Time (10 min) "Does BIGGER mean STRONGER? Let's find out." Show a big piece of paper and a small piece of paper. "Which one do you think will hold more blocks? WHY do you think that?" Record predictions. Predict and justify
Test It (15 min) Students place blocks on paper circles of different sizes until the paper collapses. Count how many blocks fit. Record results. Conduct strength test
Draw Findings (5 min) Draw a picture of what happened in science books. "The BIG paper held [number] blocks. The SMALL paper held [number] blocks." Draw and label
DifferentiationEAL students draw results rather than write numbers. NEP students: count-aloud support.

Week 3 — EXPLORE: Texture and Hardness

Lesson 1: Rough, Smooth, Soft, Hard

30 min Explore (Sensory Investigation)
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- ---------------- ---------
Texture Touch Bags (10 min) Place objects in cloth bags: bag 1 — smooth (marble, glass, silk), bag 2 — rough (sandpaper, rock, bark). Students reach in WITHOUT LOOKING and describe what they feel. "It feels SMOOTH." / "It feels ROUGH." Feel and describe
Hard vs Soft (10 min) "Let's learn two more words: HARD and SOFT." Demonstrate: tap a wooden block (hard), squeeze a soft toy (soft). "Can you find something HARD? Something SOFT?" Find and categorize
Texture Scavenger Hunt (10 min) "Find 3 things in the room that are ROUGH. Find 3 things that are SMOOTH. Find 3 things that are HARD. Find 3 things that are SOFT." Draw or write what you found. Scavenger hunt

Lesson 2: Texture Walk

30 min Explore (Movement and Senses)
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- ---------------- ---------
Prepare the Walk (5 min) Set up stations around the room with different textures: sandpaper station, cotton wool station, cardboard station, tile station, carpet station. "We are going on a TEXTURE WALK. Walk quietly to each station. Use your FEET to feel the texture. Use your HANDS to touch the material." Prepare to move
Texture Walk (15 min) Students rotate through 5 stations. At each station: draw what the texture feels like and write or say one word to describe it. Walk and record
Class Texture Chart (10 min) Compile results: "What did sandpaper feel like?" (Rough, scratchy.) "What did cotton wool feel like?" (Soft, fluffy.) Create class chart. Contribute to chart

Lesson 3: Hard vs Soft — What Can We Build?

30 min Explore (Design Activity)
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- ---------------- ---------
Challenge Introduction (5 min) "Today you are BUILDERS. Your job: build the tallest tower you can using HARD things. Then build another tower using SOFT things. Which will be taller? Which will be stronger?" Listen to challenge
Build Time (20 min) Provide materials: HARD basket — wooden blocks, plastic bricks, metal罐子, hard books / SOFT basket — pillows, cushions, soft toys, cotton wool balls, fabric. Students build both towers. Build towers
Compare (5 min) "What happened? Which tower stood up better? Which fell over? WHY do you think that happened?" Discuss
DifferentiationEAL students draw their towers. Gifted students: add a rule — towers must have 3 floors. NEP students: build one tower with teacher support.

Week 4 — EXPLAIN + EXPLORE: Pushes and Pulls

Lesson 1: Pushing Things

30 min Explore (Forces Introduction)
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- ---------------- ---------
Push Demonstration (10 min) "A PUSH is when you move something AWAY from you." Demonstrate: push a toy car, push a door closed, push a ball. "Watch what my hands do. My hands move AWAY from the object." Watch demonstrations
Push Practice (15 min) "Find 5 things you can PUSH in the room. Push each one. What happened? Did it move? Did it go fast or slow?" Students record: "I pushed a _____. It _____." Find and push objects
Class Discussion (5 min) "What things did you push? What happened when you pushed them?" Share

Lesson 2: Pulling Things

30 min Explore (Forces)
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- ---------------- ---------
Pull Demonstration (10 min) "A PULL is when you move something TOWARDS you." Demonstrate: pull a door open, pull a toy with string, pull a chair towards you. "Watch my hands. My hands move TOWARDS my body." Watch demonstrations
Push vs Pull Sort (10 min) Show picture cards: opening a drawer (pull), closing a drawer (push), pulling a wagon, pushing a shopping trolley, pulling a sled, pushing a wheelbarrow. "PUSH or PULL?" Students call out answer. Sort push/pull cards
Pull Practice (10 min) "Find 5 things you can PULL in the room. Pull each one carefully. What happened?" Record in science books. Find and pull objects

Lesson 3: Push or Pull — Which Way?

30 min Elaborate (Application)
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- ---------------- ---------
Challenge Setup (10 min) "Imagine you need to open a jar. Do you push or pull? What about closing a window? What about opening a door?" Students predict and act out each scenario. Predict and act
Push-Pull Investigation (15 min) Students receive scenario cards: opening a book, closing a lid, pulling a trolley, pushing a trolley up a ramp, pulling a kite string, pushing a shopping basket. For each: draw the action and write PUSH or PULL. Complete scenario cards
Share (5 min) "Which was harder to decide — push or pull? WHY?" Discuss
DifferentiationEAL students draw and label with teacher support. NEP students: 4 scenarios instead of 6.

Week 5 — ELABORATE: Twisting, Bending, Folding

Lesson 1: Twisting Things

30 min Elaborate (Investigation)
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- ---------------- ---------
What is Twisting? (10 min) "TWIST means to turn something around and around in opposite directions." Demonstrate with a rubber band: twist it, it scrunches up. Demonstrate with a毛巾: twist it, water comes out. "Watch what happens when I twist." Watch and observe
Twisting Investigation (15 min) Students receive materials: rubber band, paper, cloth, spring. Twist each one. Draw what happens BEFORE and AFTER twisting. Twist and record
What Changed? (5 min) "When I twisted the rubber band, it got SMALLER/SHORTER. When I twisted the cloth, it got TIGHTER." Discuss. Share observations

Lesson 2: Bending and Folding

30 min Elaborate (Investigation)
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- ---------------- ---------
Bending Demo (10 min) "BEND means to change the shape of something so it's not straight anymore." Demonstrate: bend a pipe cleaner into a hook, bend a ruler, try to bend a metal spoon (it doesn't bend easily). "Some things BEND easily. Some things DON'T bend." Observe bending
Will It Bend? Test (15 min) Students test materials: paper, cardboard, metal spoon, wooden stick, rubber band, plastic straw. Record: CAN BEND / CANNOT BEND. Test and record
Folding Introduction (5 min) "FOLD means to bend one part over another part." Demonstrate: fold paper in half, fold a towel. "When you fold, you make something SMALLER." Watch folding demo

Lesson 3: What Changes, What Doesn't?

30 min Elaborate (Summary)
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- ---------------- ---------
Changes We Can See (10 min) "We have learned that objects can CHANGE. We can PUSH them, PULL them, TWIST them, BEND them, and FOLD them." Create a class poster with images of each type of change. Contribute to poster
Reversible or Not? (15 min) "Some changes can be UNDONE. If I fold a piece of paper, I can unfold it — it's back to normal! If I tear paper, can I un-tear it?" Test: fold/unfold paper ✓, twist/un-twist rubber band ✓, break/crayon ✗ (can't un-break). Test reversibility
Draw Your Learning (5 min) Draw one change you investigated this week. Label it PUSH, PULL, TWIST, BEND, or FOLD. Draw and label
DifferentiationEAL students draw and verbally explain changes. Gifted students: predict which changes will be reversible BEFORE testing.

Week 6 — HOT TASK

Lesson 1: Hot Task — Describe, Predict, Change

30 min Summative Assessment
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- ---------------- ---------
Introduce Hot Task (5 min) "Today you will show me everything you know about objects and changes. You will: 1) DESCRIBE an object using size, shape, texture, and hardness. 2) PREDICT what will happen if you twist, bend, or fold it. 3) CHANGE the object and describe what actually happened." Listen carefully
Plan (10 min) Students choose their object and plan: What will I describe? What change will I make? What do I think will happen? Plan their task
Complete Hot Task (15 min) Students complete the task: draw their object, write descriptions, make predictions, perform the change, describe results. Independent completion
DifferentiationEAL students may verbally explain their object and change. NEP students: reduced writing — draw and label with teacher scribing.

Lesson 2: Compare Cold Task to Hot Task — Growth Analysis

30 min Evaluate (Self-Assessment)
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- ---------------- ---------
Retrieve Cold Task (5 min) Return Cold Task work from Week 1. "Look at how you sorted objects. Look at what you wrote or said." Review Cold Task
Look at Hot Task (5 min) Compare the two pieces of work. "What can you do NOW that you couldn't do at the start?" Compare work
Write a Growth Statement (15 min) "I have grown as a scientist because..." Draw or write 2–3 sentences about what they learned. Write growth statement
Share (5 min) Volunteers share one thing they are proud of Share

Lesson 3: Celebration

30 min Celebration
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- ---------------- ---------
Gallery Walk (10 min) All Hot Tasks displayed. Students walk and look at each other's work. Sticky notes: "Great describing!" Walk gallery
Certificate Award (10 min) Award "Scientist Explorer" certificates. "You can describe objects using size, shape, texture, and hardness. You can push, pull, twist, bend, and fold objects. You are amazing scientists!" Receive certificates
What Will We Learn Next? (10 min) "What other things about objects and materials do you want to learn about?" Record responses for future units. Share interests
