COLD TASK

Name: _________________________ Date: _____________ Year: _______

Part A — Length

  • Measure this line in centimetres: ___ cm
  • Which is longer: 5cm or 50mm? How do you know?
  • Order from shortest to longest: 1m, 45cm, 100cm, 60cm

    • Part B — Formal Units

  • How many centimetres in 1 metre? ___
  • How many grams in 1 kilogram? ___

    • Part C — Mass

  • Which is heavier: 500g or 1kg? Circle.
  • Estimate: A book weighs about ___ kg.

    • Part D — Capacity

  • Which holds more: 500mL or 1L? Circle.
  • Estimate: A cup holds about ___ mL.

    • Part E — Perimeter

  • A rectangle is 6cm long and 4cm wide. What is its perimeter? Show working.

  • [ ] Measure in centimetres and metres
  • [ ] Convert between cm and m (100cm = 1m)
  • [ ] Find the perimeter of a rectangle
  • [ ] Measure in grams and kilograms
  • [ ] Measure in millilitres and litres
  • [ ] Estimate before I measure
  • [ ] Choose the right unit for the job

    • WEEK 1 — ENGAGE

    Focus: Cold Task + cm and m

    5E Phase: Engage

    LESSON 1.2 — Centimetres and Metres

    Duration: 50 minutes

    Type: Concept Introduction

    Learning Intentions

  • I am learning to measure using centimetres and metres
  • I am learning that 100cm = 1m

    • Success Criteria

  • I can measure a line in cm; I know 100cm = 1m

    • I DO (15 min)

    "Centimetres and metres are FORMAL units — everyone uses them the same way."
    "A centimetre (cm) is about the width of your finger. A metre (m) is about the length of your arms spread wide."
    "100cm = 1m. Centi means 100."

    YOU DO (15 min)

  • Measure these lines in cm:
  • 100cm = ___ m
  • 2m = ___ cm 300cm = ___ m
  • EXTENSION: "How many cm is 1.5m?"

    • LESSON 1.3 — Measuring Accurately

    Duration: 50 minutes

    Type: Guided Practice

    I DO (12 min)

    "Start at 0 on the ruler. Not at the edge — at the 0 line!"
    "Line up the 0 with one end of the object. Read the number at the other end."

    YOU DO (15 min)

  • Measure the length of your pencil: ___ cm
  • Measure the width of your desk: ___ cm
  • Measure the length of the room in metres: ___ m
  • EXTENSION: "The room is 8m long and 6m wide. What is its perimeter?"

    • WEEK 2 — EXPLORE

    Focus: Perimeter

    5E Phase: Explore

    LESSON 2.1 — Finding Perimeter

    Duration: 50 minutes

    Type: Explore

    Learning Intentions

  • I am learning that perimeter is the distance around the outside of a shape
  • I am learning to find perimeter by adding all sides

    • Success Criteria

  • I can find the perimeter of a rectangle 6cm × 4cm

    • I DO (15 min)

    "Perimeter: walk around the outside. Total distance."
    "Rectangle: 6cm + 4cm + 6cm + 4cm = 20cm."
    "Or: (6+4)×2 = 20cm. L+L+W+W or 2×(L+W)."

    YOU DO (15 min)

  • Find perimeter: rectangle 8cm × 5cm = ___
  • Find perimeter: rectangle 10cm × 3cm = ___
  • "A square has sides of 7cm. What is its perimeter?"
  • EXTENSION: "A rectangle has perimeter 24cm. Length is 8cm. What is the width?"

    • WEEK 3 — EXPLORE

    Focus: Grams and kilograms

    5E Phase: Explain

    LESSON 3.1 — Grams and Kilograms

    Duration: 50 minutes

    Type: Explain

    I DO (15 min)

    "Grams (g) and kilograms (kg) measure mass."
    "1kg = 1000g. Kilo means 1000."
    "A book: about 500g. A bag of flour: 1kg."

    YOU DO (15 min)

  • 1kg = ___ g 2kg = ___ g 500g = ___ kg
  • "Which is heavier: 750g or 1kg?"
  • Estimate: A banana weighs about ___ g.
  • EXTENSION: "If you have 2.5kg and 1500g, what is the total in kg?"

    • WEEK 4 — EXPLORE

    Focus: Millilitres and litres

    5E Phase: Explain

    LESSON 4.1 — Millilitres and Litres

    Duration: 50 minutes

    Type: Explain

    I DO (15 min)

    "Millilitres (mL) and litres (L) measure capacity."
    "1L = 1000mL."
    "A cup: about 250mL. A milk carton: 1L."

    YOU DO (15 min)

  • 1L = ___ mL 2.5L = ___ mL 500mL = ___ L
  • "Which holds more: 750mL or 1L?"
  • Estimate: This container holds about ___ mL.
  • EXTENSION: "If I have 3L and I use 1.5L, how much is left?"

    • WEEK 5 — ELABORATE

    Focus: Problem-solving

    5E Phase: Elaborate

    LESSON 5.1 — Multi-Step Problems

    Duration: 50 minutes

    Type: Elaborate

    YOU DO (35 min)

  • "A rectangle is 12cm by 8cm. What is its perimeter?"
  • "Sam buys 1.5L of juice. He pours 3 cups of 250mL each. How much juice is left?"
  • "A bag of flour is 1kg. Max uses 250g. How much is left?"
  • EXTENSION: "A square has perimeter 36cm. What is the length of one side?"
