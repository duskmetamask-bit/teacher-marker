Unit Content

Our Amazing World — Earth and Space Sciences Unit Plan

Unit Duration: 6 weeks × 3 lessons per week = 18 lessons

Year Level: Foundation

Curriculum Link: Australian Curriculum Science v9 — Earth and Space Sciences

Unit Big Idea: The world around us is always changing. We can observe daily and seasonal changes in the sky, weather, and living things. These changes affect what we do and how we feel.

Cold Task: Week 1, Lesson 1 — Draw the sky today and how it changes

Hot Task: Week 6, Lesson 1 — Draw and describe what changes you observe over a day and over seasons

Unit Overview

Learning Intention:

We are learning to observe and describe daily and seasonal changes in the sky, weather, and living things, explain how changes affect our daily activities, and recognise patterns in changes.

Success Criteria:

  • I can describe what the sky looks like at different times of day (day and night).
  • I can describe weather changes (sunny, cloudy, rainy, windy, hot, cold).
  • I can name the four seasons and describe what each season is like.
  • I can explain how changes in weather affect what we do.
  • I can use words like: sunny, rainy, cloudy, hot, cold, day, night, season.

    WEEK 1 — ENGAGE: The Sky Above Us

    Lesson 1: Cold Task — Draw the Sky

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Assessment

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Hook (5 min)Step outside. Look up. "What do you see in the SKY? What do you see TODAY? Is it the same as yesterday?"Look up
    Task (20 min)Cold Task: "Draw the SKY today. What do you see? Is it sunny? Cloudy? What colour is the sky? Is it day or night? Draw the sky and label what you see. Year F: draw. Year 1: add 1–2 labels."Draw the sky
    Share (5 min)3–4 students shareShare

    Success Criteria Check: Student draws the sky showing some observational detail (sun/clouds/colours).

    Lesson 2: Day and Night

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Engage

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Day and Night (10 min)"In the DAYTIME: the SUN is in the sky. It is bright and warm. We can see colours. At NIGHT: the SUN is on the other side of the Earth. It is dark. We see the MOON and STARS."Learn
    Sunrise and Sunset (10 min)"The SUN seems to move across the sky. In the morning, the sun rises (comes up). In the evening, the sun sets (goes down). The sun doesn't really move — the Earth moves!"Learn
    Sky Observation (10 min)Look out the window or go outside. "What does the sky look like right now? What colour is it? Is the sun visible?" Draw the sky at this moment.Draw
    Draw and Label (5 min)Draw SUN or MOON and STARS depending on time of day. Label: DAY or NIGHT.Draw

    Success Criteria Check: Distinguishes between day sky (sun, blue) and night sky (moon, stars) with observation.

    Lesson 3: What's in the Sky?

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Engage

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Things in the Sky (10 min)"The SKY contains: SUN (day), MOON (night), STARS (night), CLOUDS (day and night), BIRDS (flying), PLANES (flying), KITES."Learn
    Sky Walk (10 min)Go outside. Look up! Find 5 things in the sky. Draw what you see.Walk and draw
    Share (5 min)Share what you found in the sky.Share
    Write (5 min)"I saw in the sky: [list]."Write

    Success Criteria Check: Identifies at least 5 things that can be seen in the sky.

    WEEK 2 — EXPLORE: Weather Changes

    Lesson 1: Sunny or Cloudy?

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Explore

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    What Is Weather? (10 min)"WEATHER = what the sky and air are like RIGHT NOW. It can be SUNNY, CLOUDY, RAINY, WINDY, SNOWY, FOGGY. Weather changes from day to day."Learn
    Weather Observation (10 min)Look outside or check the window. "What is the weather like TODAY? Is it sunny or cloudy? Is it hot or cold?" Draw the weather.Observe and draw
    Weather Sort (5 min)Sort weather picture cards: SUNNY, CLOUDY, RAINY, WINDY.Sort
    Write (5 min)"Today the weather is [sunny/cloudy]. The sky is [blue/grey]."Write

    Success Criteria Check: Observes and describes today's weather using at least 1 weather word.

    Lesson 2: Hot and Cold

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Explore

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Hot and Cold (10 min)"Weather can be HOT (warm, like summer), MILD (not too hot, not too cold), or COLD (chilly, like winter). When it is hot, we feel uncomfortable and want water. When it is cold, we shiver and want warm clothes."Learn
    Temperature Walk (10 min)Walk to 3 places: in the sun, in the shade, near a window. "Which feels warmest? Which feels coldest?" Feel with hands (teacher supervised).Walk and feel
    Record (5 min)Draw the warmest and coldest spots. Label: HOT / COLD.Draw
    Discuss (5 min)"Why is it warmer in the sun? Why is the shade cooler?" (Simple: sun warms things up.)Discuss

    Success Criteria Check: Investigates hot and cold areas and can explain the sun warms things.

    Lesson 3: Rain and Wind

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Explore

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Rain (10 min)"When it RAINS, water falls from clouds. The water comes from the sky! It fills rivers, dams, and soil. Plants need rain to grow. Animals need water to drink."Learn
    Wind (10 min)"The WIND is moving air. When wind blows, leaves rustle, clouds move, and kites fly. Wind is caused by the sun heating the air unevenly — warm air rises, cool air rushes in to replace it."Learn
    Weather Pictures (5 min)Draw: RAIN (cloud with water drops), WIND (lines showing direction).Draw
    Song or Rhyme (5 min)Sing weather song or do weather actions: "Rain, rain, go away, come again another day."Sing

    Success Criteria Check: Identifies rain and wind as weather phenomena.

    WEEK 3 — EXPLORE: The Four Seasons

    Lesson 1: What Is a Season?

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Explore

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    What Is a Season? (10 min)"A SEASON is a time of the year with particular weather. In AUSTRALIA we have 4 seasons: AUTUMN (March–May), WINTER (June–August), SPRING (September–November), SUMMER (December–February). Each season has different weather and changes."Learn
    Season Sort (10 min)Sort picture cards by season: sunny beach, bare trees, flowers blooming, snow. Match to season.Sort
    What Season Is It Now? (5 min)"What season is it RIGHT NOW in Perth? What does it look and feel like?"Discuss
    Draw (5 min)Draw the current season. Label: AUTUMN / WINTER / SPRING / SUMMER.Draw

    Success Criteria Check: Names all 4 seasons and correctly identifies the current season.

    Lesson 2: Autumn and Winter

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Explore

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Autumn (10 min)"AUTUMN (March–May): days get shorter, weather gets cooler. LEAVES change colour and fall off some trees (deciduous trees). Some animals start to prepare for winter (migrate or hibernate). In Perth: weather changes from warm to cool, some leaves fall."Learn
    Winter (10 min)"WINTER (June–August): shortest days, longest nights, coldest weather. Some places get snow (not Perth!). In Perth: cold, rainy, some mornings foggy. Plants grow slowly. Some animals sleep through winter (hibernation). People wear warm coats, scarves, beanies."Learn
    Draw Autumn and Winter (10 min)Draw one thing that happens in autumn and one thing in winter. Label each.Draw

    Success Criteria Check: Describes at least 2 changes in autumn and 2 changes in winter.

    Lesson 3: Spring and Summer

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Explore

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Spring (10 min)"SPRING (September–November): days get longer, weather gets warmer. FLOWERS bloom. Baby animals are born (lambs, chicks). Trees grow new leaves. In Perth: wildflowers bloom, weather gets warmer, days get longer."Learn
    Summer (10 min)"SUMMER (December–February): longest days, hottest weather. In Perth: very hot, sunny, some days above 40°C. Bushfire season. People go to the beach, swim, wear shorts and thongs. Lightning and storms can happen."Learn
    Compare Summer and Winter (5 min)"What is different between summer and winter? Days: longer in summer. Weather: hotter in summer. What we wear: shorts in summer, warm clothes in winter."Compare
    Draw (5 min)Draw one thing that happens in spring and one thing in summer.Draw

    Success Criteria Check: Describes at least 2 changes in spring and 2 changes in summer.

    WEEK 4 — EXPLAIN: Changes in Our World

    Lesson 1: How Animals Change

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Explain

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Animals and Seasons (10 min)"Animals CHANGE with the seasons. BIRDS: some birds fly north for winter (migrate). BIRDS THAT STAY: may grow thicker feathers. KOALA: less active in winter (cold), more active in summer. SNAKE: less active in cold months (hibernate). BIRDS in spring: build nests, lay eggs."Learn
    What Do Animals Do? (10 min)Sort animal cards by season behaviour: migrate (fly north), hibernate (sleep through winter), stay active (kangaroos, birds).Sort
    Draw (5 min)Draw one animal that changes with seasons. Label the season.Draw
    Write (5 min)"In [season], [animal] [behaviour]."Write

    Success Criteria Check: Identifies at least 2 animals that change their behaviour with seasons.

    Lesson 2: How Plants Change

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Explain

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Plants Through Seasons (10 min)"Plants CHANGE with the seasons. AUTUMN: some trees lose leaves (deciduous — JAKE trees in Perth). WINTER: many plants stop growing (dormant), some lose leaves. SPRING: new leaves grow, flowers bloom. SUMMER: plants grow quickly, some need lots of water."Learn
    Observe Plants (10 min)Look at plants around the school. Have they changed? Are there flowers? New leaves? Dead leaves?Observe
    Draw (5 min)Draw a plant changing through seasons. Show spring (flowers) and autumn (falling leaves).Draw
    Write (5 min)"Plants grow new [leaves/flowers] in spring."Write

    Success Criteria Check: Observes and describes plant changes with seasons.

    Lesson 3: How We Change

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Explain

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    We Change Too! (10 min)"We CHANGE with the seasons too! SUMMER: wear shorts, thongs, t-shirts, sunscreen, go to beach, swim. WINTER: wear jumpers, jeans, jackets, scarves, boots, drink hot drinks. SPRING/AUTUMN: light jacket, jeans."Learn
    Dress for the Weather (10 min)Show clothes: summer outfit vs winter outfit. "What do we wear in summer? What do we wear in winter?"Sort
    Draw (5 min)Draw yourself dressed for hot weather and dressed for cold weather.Draw
    Write (5 min)"In summer I wear [clothes]. In winter I wear [clothes]."Write

    Success Criteria Check: Identifies appropriate clothing for hot and cold weather.

    WEEK 5 — ELABORATE: Changes We Observe

    Lesson 1: Weather and What We Wear

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Elaborate

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Weather and Activities (10 min)"We do DIFFERENT things in different weather. SUNNY/HOT: go to beach, swim, play outside. COLD/RAINY: stay inside, play board games, read books. WINDY: fly kites. What do YOU do in different weather?"Discuss
    Weather Sort Activity (10 min)Picture cards: beach, indoor reading, kite flying, swimming, walking in rain. Sort into WEATHER TYPE that matches.Sort
    Draw (5 min)Draw yourself doing something fun in the current weather.Draw
    Write (5 min)"When it is [weather], I like to [activity]."Write

    Success Criteria Check: Connects weather to appropriate activities.

    Lesson 2: Sky Changes

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Elaborate

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    The Sun Moves (10 min)"In the morning: sun is low in the east. In the middle of the day: sun is high. In the evening: sun is low in the west. The sun appears to MOVE across the sky. Actually the Earth is rotating (spinning)!"Learn
    Observe Shadows (10 min)Go outside. Place an object on the ground. Look at its SHADOW. Where is it? Draw the object and its shadow. "The shadow shows where the sun is."Observe
    Draw Morning and Afternoon Shadows (5 min)"If we came back in the afternoon, where would the shadow be? Draw your prediction."Draw
    Draw (5 min)Draw the sun in different positions (morning, midday, afternoon).Draw

    Success Criteria Check: Observes shadows and notes that the sun's position changes through the day.

    Lesson 3: Changes Over Time

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Elaborate

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Changes Over a Day (10 min)"Think about a typical day. MORNING: sky gets light, sun rises, we wake up. MIDDAY: sun is highest, it is the hottest part of the day. AFTERNOON: sun gets lower, starts to set. EVENING/NIGHT: sky gets dark, stars and moon appear, we go to sleep."Learn
    Changes Over a Year (10 min)"Think about a whole year. WINTER: short days, cold. SPRING: flowers bloom. SUMMER: long days, hot. AUTUMN: leaves fall. Then it repeats! Changes happen again and again."Learn
    Draw Changes (5 min)Draw the sky at morning and at night.Draw
    Write (5 min)"Changes happen every day and every season."Write

    Differentiation: EAL students draw changes. Gifted students: make a simple weather diary to record daily observations over the week.

    Success Criteria Check: Identifies changes that happen daily (day/night, sun position) and seasonally (temperature, activities).

    WEEK 6 — HOT TASK

    Lesson 1: Hot Task

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Summative

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Introduce (5 min)"Today: draw and describe changes you observe over a day and over seasons. Draw: morning sky vs night sky. What do you wear in hot weather vs cold weather? What do animals do in different seasons?"Instructions
    Complete (20 min)Students complete.Complete
    Share (5 min)3–4 students share.Share

    Lesson 2: Compare and Celebrate

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Evaluate

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Compare (10 min)Cold Task vs Hot Task. What do you know now?Compare
    Write (10 min)"I learned that..."Write
    Celebrate (10 min)"You are wonderful scientists! You can observe the sky, describe weather, and understand seasons!"Celebrate

    Lesson 3: Reflection

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Celebration

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Gallery Walk (10 min)Display all work.Walk
    What Did We Learn? (10 min)Share favourite learning.Share
    Certificate (5 min)Award "Weather and Seasons Explorer" certificates.Awards
    Future Questions (5 min)What do you want to learn about next?Discuss
