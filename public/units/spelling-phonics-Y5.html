Hot Task: Week 8, Lesson 1 — Same task, measure growth

Unit Overview

Learning Intention:

We are learning to spell academic and domain-specific vocabulary accurately, use morphological analysis for complex words, apply spelling rules consistently, and self-correct spelling errors.

Success Criteria:

  • I can spell academic vocabulary from multiple subject domains accurately.
  • I can use morphological analysis to decode and encode complex words.
  • I can apply spelling rules and generalisations consistently.
  • I can identify and correct spelling errors in my own and others' writing.
  • I can use spelling strategies independently when encountering unknown words.
  • I can maintain spelling accuracy across extended writing tasks.

    WEEK 1 — ACADEMIC VOCABULARY

    Lesson 1: Cold Task (Assessment)

    Time: 55 minutes | Type: Assessment

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Hook (5 min)"In *Holes*, Stanley Yelnats learns that every word in a story matters — and so does every letter in a word. Today we will test our ability to spell academic vocabulary accurately."Engage
    Read (15 min)Read a selection from *Holes* (Chapters 1–5). Notice Sachar's precise word choice and spelling.Listen
    Task (35 min)Cold Task (same as Hot Task): 1. Spell 12 academic words: hypothesis, analysis, conclusion, evidence, experiment, variable, data, interpret, evaluate, significant, methodology, phenomena. 2. Spell 8 domain words from science, history, maths: atmosphere, civilization, equation, democracy, ecosystem, metabolism, architecture, chronology. 3. Write a paragraph showing spelling accuracy across multiple word types. Year 5: 90%+ accuracy expected.Independent assessment

    Lesson 2: Subject-Specific Words (I Do)

    Time: 55 minutes | Type: Explicit Teaching

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Mini-Lesson (20 min)Subject-specific words appear in particular areas of study and require specific spellings. From *Holes*: "kilometer" (measurement), "corruption" (social studies), "vegetable" (science). Each subject has key vocabulary that must be spelled correctly.Learn
    Activity (25 min)Word sort by subject domain: Science (hypothesis, experiment, ecosystem), History (civilization, archaeology, chronology), Maths (equation, variable, data), English (analyse, interpret, evaluate). Sort 24 words. Identify the subject and explain why each belongs there.Sorting
    You Do (10 min)Write 3 words from each of 4 subject domains. Check spelling against reference.Written practice

    Success Criteria Check: Can spell academic vocabulary from multiple domains.

    Lesson 3: Vocabulary Building (We Do)

    Time: 55 minutes | Type: Guided Practice

    PhaseTeacherStudents
    We Do (40 min)1. Dictation: "The hypothesis was supported by the experimental data. The archaeologist analysed the ancient civilization. The equation required careful interpretation." 2. Word family expansion: analyse → analysis, analyst, analytical; experiment → experimental, experimentation; interpret → interpretation, interpretive, interpreter. 3. Write sentences using word families.Guided practice
    You Do (15 min)Write 5 complex sentences using words from the dictation. Underline the academic vocabulary. Check spelling.Written practice

    WEEK 2 — SCIENCE AND MATHS WORDS

    Lesson 1: Scientific Vocabulary (I Do)

    Time: 55 minutes | Type: Explicit Teaching

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Mini-Lesson (20 min)Scientific vocabulary: atmosphere, ecosystem, metabolism, organism,Photosynthesis, biodiversity, hypothesis, variable, gravity, friction, thermal, momentum. From *Holes*: "The camp was in a dry, hot area" — discuss arid climate vocabulary. Science spelling: -sis → -sis (analysis, photosynthesis, osmosis), -tion for noun forms.Learn
    Activity (25 min)Build: atmo + sphere = atmosphere, eco + system = ecosystem, photo + synthesis = photosynthesis, hypo + thesis = hypothesis, vari + able = variable. Write each word. Identify roots and suffixes. Note the -sis and -tion patterns.Building
    You Do (10 min)Write 8 scientific words. Circle the suffix. Write the root meaning.Written practice

    Lesson 2: Mathematical Terms (I Do → We Do)

    Time: 55 minutes | Type: Explicit Teaching + Guided

    PhaseTeacherStudents
    I Do (10 min)Mathematical vocabulary: equation, equation, formula, algebra, geometry, perimeter, diameter, circumference, numerator, denominator, parallel, perpendicular. Many math words come from Greek roots: geo = earth, metron = measure.Learn
    We Do (35 min)Build and spell: equa + tion = equation, geo + metry = geometry, peri + meter = perimeter, dia + meter = diameter, circum + ference = circumference, par + allel = parallel, al + gebra = algebra. Sort into Greek root categories.Guided
    You Do (10 min)Write 6 mathematical words. Identify the Greek root in each.Written practice

    Lesson 3: Spelling Science (You Do)

    Time: 55 minutes | Type: Independent

    PhaseTeacherStudents
    You Do (55 min)1. Dictation: "The atmosphere created an ecosystem where organisms could conduct photosynthesis. The equation involved variables that affected the perimeter." 2. Sort 20 words: photosynthesis, atmosphere, hypothesis, algebra, variable, geometry, biodiversity, circumference, experiment, denominator, metabolism, parallel, friction, equation, ecosystem, formula, gravity, chronological, organism, perpendicular. Into: SCIENCEMATHS. 3. Write 3 sentences using one science and one maths word.Full practice

    WEEK 3 — HISTORY AND GEOGRAPHY

    Lesson 1: Historical Terms (I Do)

    Time: 55 minutes | Type: Explicit Teaching

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Mini-Lesson (20 min)Historical vocabulary: civilization, archaeology, chronology, ancient, medieval, monarchy, democracy, revolution, empire, colony, artefact, primary source, settlement, migration. From *Holes*: "The town of Green Lake was established in the 1800s." Historical spelling: -tion, -ian, -al suffixes.Learn
    Activity (25 min)Build: civil + ization = civilization, archae + logy = archaeology, chrono + logy = chronology, mon + archy = monarchy, demo + cracy = democracy, rev + olution = revolution. Note: cracy = rule, archy = rule/ruler. Write each.Building
    You Do (10 min)Write 8 historical terms. Identify suffix pattern (-tion, -ian, -al, -cy).Written practice

    Lesson 2: Geographic Vocabulary (I Do → We Do)

    Time: 55 minutes | Type: Explicit Teaching + Guided

    PhaseTeacherStudents
    I Do (10 min)Geographic vocabulary: topography, latitude, longitude, hemisphere, equator, continent, peninsula, archipelago, vegetation, climate, population, infrastructure. From *Holes*: "Camp Green Lake was in Texas." Texas vocabulary: desert, arid, plains, reservoir.Learn
    We Do (35 min)Build: topo + graphy = topography, lati + tude = latitude, longi + tude = longitude, hemi + sphere = hemisphere, conti + nent = continent, archi + pelago = archipelago. Sort into LANDWATERMEASUREMENT columns.Guided
    You Do (10 min)Write 6 geographic words. Circle the root. Explain what the word means using the root meanings.Written practice

    Lesson 3: Historical Spelling (You Do)

    Time: 55 minutes | Type: Independent

    PhaseTeacherStudents
    You Do (55 min)1. Dictation: "The ancient civilization was located on a peninsula. The archaeology dig revealed artefacts that changed the chronology of the settlement." 2. Sort 16 words: civilization, latitude, peninsula, archaeology, longitude, artefact, equator, monarchy, continent, archipelago, democracy, primary source, climate, infrastructure, revolution, vegetation — into HISTORYGEOGRAPHY columns. 3. Write a paragraph using 4 historical and 4 geographic terms.Full practice

    WEEK 4 — MORPHOLOGICAL CONSOLIDATION

    Lesson 1: Affix Review (I Do)

    Time: 55 minutes | Type: Explicit Teaching

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Mini-Lesson (20 min)Affix review: PREFIXES (un-, re-, pre-, dis-, mis-, super-, inter-, trans-, anti-, auto-, multi-) and SUFFIXES (-tion, -sion, -ment, -ness, -ful, -less, -ly, -er, -est, -able, -ible, -ance, -ence, -ology). Each adds meaning. Combined with roots, they build complex words. From *Holes*: "The holes were dug by the boys." (passive).Learn
    Activity (25 min)Build complex words: super + conduct + iv + ity = superconductivity, inter + nation + al = international, pre + dict + ion = prediction, dis + respect + ful = disrespectful, auto + bio + graphy = autobiography. Write each. Identify all parts.Building
    You Do (10 min)Build 6 complex words using prefixes, roots, and suffixes. Break each into parts.Written practice

    Lesson 2: Root Connection (I Do → We Do)

    Time: 55 minutes | Type: Explicit Teaching + Guided

    PhaseTeacherStudents
    I Do (10 min)Root connection: Many words share roots. PORT (carry): transport, portable, export, import. DICT (speak): dictate, predict, contradict. STRUCT (build): construct, destruction, instructor. Scribe/script (write): describe, manuscript, prescription. When you know ONE root word, you can spell OTHERS.Learn
    We Do (35 min)Create word families: PORT: transport, portable, export, import, support, reporting, reportedly, transposition. DICT: dictate, predict, contradict, diction, dictionary, indict. Build as many as possible.Guided
    You Do (10 min)Choose 3 root families. Build as many words as possible for each. Write all words.Written practice

    Lesson 3: Complex Word Building (You Do)

    Time: 55 minutes | Type: Independent

    PhaseTeacherStudents
    You Do (55 min)1. Build from a root: STRUCT → construction, destructive, destructible, structurally, infrastructure. 2. Dictation: "The international construction project required transportation of materials. The prediction proved contradictory to the instructions." 3. Write 10 complex words. Circle the root. Underline the suffix. 4. Write a paragraph using 5 complex words.Full practice

    WEEK 5 — SPELLING RULES

    Lesson 1: ie and ei (I Do)

    Time: 55 minutes | Type: Explicit Teaching

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Mini-Lesson (20 min)Spelling rule: "I before E, except after C, or when sounding like /ay/ as in neighbour and weigh." Examples: believe (i before e), receive (after c), their (ei = /air/), eight (ei = /ay/). From *Holes*: "The guard's name was Mr. Sir." (Sir — i before e doesn't apply here).Learn
    Activity (25 min)Apply the rule: Write the correct form: "bel__ve" → believe, "rec__ve" → receive, "th__r" → their, "n__ghbour" → neighbour, "w__gh" → weigh, "s__ze" → size, "c__ling" → ceiling, "for__gn" → foreign. Sort: i before eei after cei as /ay/.Practice
    You Do (10 min)Write 8 words following the ie/ei rule. Mark which category each falls into.Written practice

    Lesson 2: c and g (I Do → We Do)

    Time: 55 minutes | Type: Explicit Teaching + Guided

    PhaseTeacherStudents
    I Do (10 min)Soft vs hard C and G: C followed by E, I, Y is SOFT: circle, centre, electricity, cycle. C followed by A, O, U is HARD: cat, cot, cut. G followed by E, I, Y is SOFT: giant, gentle, gym. G followed by A, O, U is HARD: garden, god, gun.Learn
    We Do (35 min)Sort: circle, centre, carpet, certain, electricity, celebrate, magic, gentle, gyroscope, genuine, geography, program, catastrophe, energy, fantastic. Identify: SOFT c (ce, ci, cy) vs HARD c (ca, co, cu). SOFT g (ge, gi, gy) vs HARD g (ga, go, gu). Write in correct columns.Guided
    You Do (10 min)Write 4 soft c words and 4 soft g words. Circle the soft spelling pattern.Written practice

    Lesson 3: Silent Letters (You Do)

    Time: 55 minutes | Type: Independent

    PhaseTeacherStudents
    You Do (55 min)1. Sort words with silent letters: knight, wrote, island, psychology, gnome, Wednesday, February, honest, Wednesday, column, crumb, subtle, rhythm, Wednesday. Into: KN, WR, GN, MB, PS, SC, RH columns. 2. Dictation: "The knight wrote honestly on Wednesday. The gnome climbed the column." 3. Write 8 words with silent letters. Circle the silent part. Explain the silent letter.Full practice

    WEEK 6 — SPELLING ACCURACY

    Lesson 1: Proofreading (I Do)

    Time: 55 minutes | Type: Explicit Teaching

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Mini-Lesson (20 min)Proofreading = reading carefully to find and fix errors. Strategy: Read slowly. Check each word. Circle words you're unsure about. Use a dictionary. Read aloud. Check homophones in context. Check endings (-ed, -ing, -ly). From *Holes*: "Checking spelling matters — a mistake can change the whole story."Learn
    Activity (25 min)Proofread a passage with 10 deliberate spelling errors. Find each error. Write the correction. Explain what the error was. Check: Would the dictionary confirm your correction?Practice
    You Do (10 min)Write a short passage (100 words). Add 5 deliberate errors. Exchange with a partner. Proofread and correct each other's work.Written practice

    Lesson 2: Self-Correction (I Do → We Do)

    Time: 55 minutes | Type: Explicit Teaching + Guided

    PhaseTeacherStudents
    I Do (10 min)Self-correction process: 1. Write draft. 2. Wait. 3. Read aloud. 4. Circle uncertain words. 5. Look up in dictionary. 6. Rewrite with corrections. 7. Final check. This process improves spelling accuracy over time.Learn
    We Do (35 min)Write a draft paragraph about *Holes*. Apply self-correction process: read aloud, circle uncertain words, look up, rewrite, final check. Compare first draft to final draft — note all corrections.Guided
    You Do (10 min)Write a final paragraph. Apply full self-correction process. Count: How many corrections did you make? Which patterns did you fix?Written practice

    Lesson 3: Peer Editing (You Do)

    Time: 55 minutes | Type: Independent

    PhaseTeacherStudents
    You Do (55 min)1. Write a 200-word piece about a topic of your choice. 2. Exchange with a partner. 3. Partner proofread: Circle words that might be misspelled. Check homophones. Mark uncertain words. 4. Discuss findings. 5. Rewrite with corrections. 6. Write: "The 3 spelling corrections I made were..." 7. Discuss: Why didn't I catch these myself?Full process

    WEEK 7 — EXTENDED WRITING

    Lesson 1: Spelling in Context (We Do)

    Time: 55 minutes | Type: Guided Practice

    PhaseTeacherStudents
    We Do (55 min)Write an analytical paragraph about *Holes* — why Sachar's precise language matters. Use at least 8 academic vocabulary words (hypothesis, analysis, evidence, interpret, etc.). Underline all academic vocabulary. Check each word's spelling. Peer check. Rewrite.Full writing

    Lesson 2: Domain Writing (You Do)

    Time: 55 minutes | Type: Independent

    PhaseTeacherStudents
    You Do (55 min)Write a "What I Learned" piece about the history/geography/science of *Holes*. Include: Scientific terms, historical terms, geographic terms, complex morphological words. At least 300 words. Underline 10+ domain-specific words. Check spelling of all underlined words.Extended writing

    Lesson 3: Final Piece (We Do)

    Time: 55 minutes | Type: Guided Practice

    PhaseTeacherStudents
    We Do (30 min)Final piece: Write about any topic. At least 400 words. Must include: 5 words with prefixes, 5 words with suffixes, 3 Greek roots, 3 Latin roots, 3 homophones used correctly. Full self-correction process.Writing
    You Do (25 min)Final check of your piece. Mark: Prefix words, suffix words, root words, homophone usage. Count: How many correctly spelled domain-specific words did you include?Self-assessment
