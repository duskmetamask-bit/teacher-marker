COLD TASK

Name: _________________________ Date: _____________ Year: _______

Part A — 3D Objects

  • Name this 3D object: (draw a cube)
  • How many faces does it have? ___ How many edges? ___ How many vertices? ___
  • Draw a CYLINDER. Label the faces.

    • Part B — 2D Shapes on 3D Objects

  • What 2D shape is on each face of a cube? ___
  • What 2D shape is on the face of a cylinder? ___

    • Part C — Transformations

  • On this grid, draw the original shape, then draw a SLIDE to the right:

    • (grid with a triangle)

  • Draw a shape. Flip it. Describe what changed.

    • Part D — Symmetry

  • Draw all lines of symmetry on a square.
  • Does this shape have symmetry? YES / NO (draw an asymmetrical shape)

  • On this grid (3×3), the school is at (1,1) and the park is at (3,3). What direction is the park from the school?

    A–E RUBRIC — Geometry (Year 2)

    Aligned to: AC9M2SP01, AC9M2SP02, AC9M3SP01

    Criterion 1: 3D Objects — Faces, Edges, Vertices

    GradeDescriptor
    **A**Describes and compares 3D objects using faces, edges, vertices; classifies objects; creates and matches nets
    **B**Describes 3D objects reliably; classifies with minor errors; nets attempted
    **C**Describes with scaffolding; beginning to classify; nets require support
    **D**Names obvious 3D objects with significant support
    **E**Unable to describe 3D objects

    Criterion 2: 2D Shapes on 3D Objects

    GradeDescriptor
    **A**Identifies 2D shapes on faces of 3D objects reliably; explains why some shapes appear on some objects
    **B**Identifies shapes on common objects; explanations developing
    **C**Identifies with scaffolding; begins to explain
    **D**Identifies obvious shapes with support
    **E**Unable to identify 2D shapes on 3D objects

    Criterion 3: Transformations

    GradeDescriptor
    **A**Identifies and performs flips, slides, and turns accurately; describes transformations; predicts results
    **B**Identifies and performs transformations reliably; predictions mostly correct
    **C**Identifies transformations with scaffolding; performance requires support
    **D**Identifies slides only with significant support
    **E**Unable to identify transformations

    Criterion 4: Symmetry

    GradeDescriptor
    **A**Identifies and draws all lines of symmetry; determines symmetry in irregular shapes
    **B**Draws lines of symmetry reliably; identifies most shapes
    **C**Draws lines of symmetry with scaffolding
    **D**Identifies obvious symmetry with support
    **E**Unable to identify symmetry

    Criterion 5: Maps and Coordinates

    GradeDescriptor
    **A**Uses coordinates (A1, B3) and directional language reliably; plots and describes paths; creates accurate maps
    **B**Uses coordinates and directions mostly correctly; maps with minor errors
    **C**Uses simple coordinates with scaffolding; directions developing
    **D**Uses simple directional language with significant support
    **E**Unable to use coordinates or directional language

    • WEEK 1 — ENGAGE

    Focus: Cold Task + 3D objects

    5E Phase: Engage

    LESSON 1.2 — What Is a 3D Object?

    Duration: 50 minutes

    Type: Concept Introduction

    Learning Intentions

  • I am learning to name and describe 3D objects
  • I am learning that 3D objects have faces, edges, and vertices

    • Success Criteria

  • I can name a cube, cylinder, sphere, cone, pyramid, and prism

    • I DO (15 min)

    "3D OBJECTS: real things you can hold. They have LENGTH, WIDTH, and HEIGHT."
    "CUBE: 6 square faces, 12 edges, 8 vertices. (Show cube)"
    "SPHERE: round ball. No faces, edges, or vertices. (Show ball)"
    "CYLINDER: 2 circular faces, 1 curved surface. (Show can)"
    "CONE: 1 circular face, 1 point. (Show ice cream cone)"
    "PYRAMID: triangular faces meet at a point. (Show pyramid)"
    "PRISM: same cross-section all along. (Show rectangular prism)"

    WE DO (15 min)

    Sort 3D objects: Cards or real objects. Sort by name.

    Build with blocks: Build a tower using cylinders and cubes.

    YOU DO (10 min)

  • Name: cube, sphere, cylinder, cone, pyramid, prism
  • "What 3D object is a dice? A tennis ball? A tent?"
  • EXTENSION: "What is the difference between a cylinder and a cone?"

    • LESSON 1.3 — Faces, Edges, Vertices

    Duration: 50 minutes

    Type: Guided Practice

    I DO (12 min)

    "FACE: flat or curved surface."
    "EDGE: where two faces meet — a line."
    "VERTEX/VERTICES: corners where edges meet."
    "This cube: 6 faces, 12 edges, 8 vertices."

    YOU DO (15 min)

  • A cylinder: ___ faces, ___ edges, ___ vertices
  • A triangular pyramid: ___ faces, ___ edges, ___ vertices
  • "Can you find a 3D object with no vertices?"
  • EXTENSION: "How many faces does a rectangular prism have? What shapes are the faces?"

    • WEEK 2 — EXPLORE

    Focus: 2D shapes on 3D objects; nets

    5E Phase: Explore

    LESSON 2.1 — What 2D Shapes Are on 3D Objects?

    Duration: 50 minutes

    Type: Explore

    I DO (15 min)

    "Faces of a cube: squares."
    "Faces of a cylinder: circles and rectangles."
    "Faces of a triangular prism: triangles and rectangles."

    YOU DO (15 min)

  • What 2D shapes are on a pyramid with a square base?
  • "A dice has square faces. What 3D object is it?"
  • Build a 3D object from playdough. Press it onto paper. What 2D shapes do you see?
  • EXTENSION: "Can you find a 3D object that has ONLY triangles on its faces?"

    • LESSON 2.2 — Nets

    Duration: 50 minutes

    Type: Explore

    I DO (12 min)

    "A NET is a 2D shape that FOLDS to make a 3D object."
    "This is a net of a cube. Fold it — it becomes a cube."

    YOU DO (15 min)

  • Match nets to 3D objects.
  • Draw a net of a cube.
  • "Can a cube have more than one net? YES / NO. Draw another."
  • EXTENSION: "Draw a net of a triangular prism."

    • WEEK 3 — EXPLORE

    Focus: Maps and coordinates

    5E Phase: Explain

    LESSON 3.1 — Using Coordinates and Grid References

    Duration: 50 minutes

    Type: Explain

    Learning Intentions

  • I am learning to use coordinates (A1, B3) to describe positions on a grid
  • I am learning to plot positions on a map

    • Success Criteria

  • I can find (2,3) on a grid; I can describe where (B,3) is

    • I DO (15 min)

    "A map grid: rows (1,2,3) going up. Columns (A,B,C) going across."
    "The school is at B2. The park is at D4."
    "To describe the path: Start at B2. Go up 2 squares to D2. Go right 2 squares to D4."

    YOU DO (15 min)

  • Plot: A1, B3, C2 on a grid.
  • "Start at A1. Go east 2, north 1. Where are you?"
  • Draw a simple map of your bedroom. Label furniture using coordinates.
  • EXTENSION: "Start at (3,3). Go south 2, west 2, north 1. Where are you?"

    • LESSON 3.2 — Giving Directions

    Duration: 50 minutes

    Type: Explain

    YOU DO (30 min)

  • "Give directions from the shop to the park on your map."
  • "My house is at A3. My school is at C1. What direction is the school from my house?"
  • "Draw a treasure map. Give coordinates to find the treasure."
  • EXTENSION: "Can you describe a path using only north/south/east/west?"

    • WEEK 4 — EXPLORE

    Focus: Transformations on grids

    5E Phase: Explain

    LESSON 4.1 — Flips, Slides, Turns on Grids

    Duration: 50 minutes

    Type: Explain

    I DO (15 min)

    "On a grid, we can track transformations."
    "SLIDE: shift right/left/up/down. The shape points the same way."
    "FLIP: reflect across a line. Left becomes right."
    "TURN: rotate around a point. The shape turns."

    YOU DO (15 min)

  • On the grid, slide the shape 3 squares right.
  • On the grid, flip the shape across the line.
  • On the grid, turn the shape 90 degrees clockwise.
  • EXTENSION: "If you slide right 2 and up 3, where does (2,3) end up?"

    • WEEK 5 — ELABORATE

    Focus: Symmetry and angles

    5E Phase: Elaborate

    LESSON 5.1 — Lines of Symmetry

    Duration: 50 minutes

    Type: Elaborate

    I DO (12 min)

    "LINE OF SYMMETRY: a line where you can fold the shape and both halves match exactly."
    "Square: 4 lines of symmetry (2 diagonals, 2 through midpoints)."
    "Rectangle: 2 lines of symmetry."
    "Isosceles triangle: 1 line of symmetry."

    YOU DO (15 min)

  • How many lines of symmetry does an equilateral triangle have?
  • Draw a shape with 2 lines of symmetry.
  • "Does a regular pentagon have more or fewer lines of symmetry than a square?"
  • EXTENSION: "How many lines of symmetry does a circle have?"

    • LESSON 5.2 — Right Angles

    Duration: 50 minutes

    Type: Elaborate

    I DO (12 min)

    "A RIGHT ANGLE is exactly 90 degrees. Like the corner of a piece of paper."
    "A square has 4 right angles. A rectangle has 4 right angles."
    "If an angle is LESS than 90° it is ACUTE. If it is MORE than 90° it is OBTUSE."

    YOU DO (15 min)

  • Which shapes in our shapes set have right angles?
  • "Is an acute angle bigger or smaller than a right angle?"
  • "Draw a shape with 4 right angles."
  • EXTENSION: "How many right angles does a rectangular prism have?"
