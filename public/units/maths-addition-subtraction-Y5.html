Mathematics Unit Plan — Addition and Subtraction

Year 5 | 8 Weeks | Australian Curriculum v9

Mentor Text: How Much Is a Million? by David M. Schwartz
A picture book exploring enormous numbers and the wonder of large quantities — perfect for estimation and place value at the millions scale.

UNIT AT A GLANCE

**Year Level**Year 5
**Strand**Number
**Duration**8 weeks × 3 lessons/week = 24 lessons
**Model**5E Instructional Model
**Pedagogical Frame**Gradual Release: I Do → We Do → You Do
**Big Ideas**Addition and subtraction extend to 6-digit whole numbers and decimal fractions; efficient strategies apply to all numbers; estimation and justification are essential
**Driving Question***How do we add and subtract with very large numbers and decimals, and how do we explain and justify our strategies?*

AUSTRALIAN CURRICULUM v9 — CONTENT DESCRIPTORS

Year 5

CodeDescriptor
**AC9M5N01**Interpret, compare and order numbers of any size with positive and negative numbers and decimals to at least 1000, using place value understanding to locate numbers on a number line and round to given place values
**AC9M5N02**Solve problems involving addition and subtraction with decimals to at least 3 decimal places, using efficient calculation strategies including the formal algorithm
**AC9M5N03**Solve problems that require adding and subtracting fractions with the same or related denominators, using flexible calculation strategies

General Capabilities

  • Numeracy — core
  • Literacy — mathematical explanation, justification
  • Critical & Creative Thinking — generalisation, proof

    • BIG UNDERSTANDINGS

    Students will understand that:

  • Place value extends to millions and to decimal fractions (tenths, hundredths, thousandths)
  • Addition and subtraction of decimals require careful alignment of decimal points
  • Adding and subtracting fractions requires common denominators
  • Efficient strategies and the formal algorithm both require place value understanding
  • Estimation and inverse operations are essential checking tools
  • Generalisations about operations apply universally

    • VOCABULARY

    Tier 2 (academic)

    place value, decimal, decimal point, thousandths, common denominator, equivalent fraction, algorithm, estimate, round, regroup, compensate

    Tier 3 (mathematical)

    formal algorithm, flexible strategy, inverse operation, reasonableness, partition notation, mixed numeral, terminating decimal

    Criterion 1: Place Value — Decimals to Thousandths

    GradeDescriptor
    **A**Reads, writes, and orders decimals to thousandths; explains the value of each digit; rounds to nearest tenth or hundredth; uses place value to justify comparisons
    **B**Reads and writes decimals to thousandths reliably; rounds to nearest tenth with minor errors
    **C**Reads and writes decimals to hundredths; rounds to nearest tenth with scaffolding; beginning thousandths
    **D**Reads and writes decimals to hundredths with support; rounding requires significant scaffolding

    Criterion 2: Decimal Addition

    GradeDescriptor
    **A**Adds decimals to 3 decimal places efficiently; aligns decimal points correctly; uses estimation to check; applies to real-world contexts
    **B**Adds decimals to 3 decimal places reliably; decimal point alignment mostly correct; checks using estimation
    **C**Adds decimals to 2 decimal places with scaffolding; beginning to extend to 3 places; needs support with decimal alignment
    **D**Adds decimals to 1 decimal place with significant scaffolding; decimal alignment problematic
    **E**Unable to add decimals; significant support required

    Criterion 3: Decimal Subtraction

    GradeDescriptor
    **A**Subtracts decimals to 3 decimal places efficiently; handles zero as placeholder correctly; checks using addition; applies to real-world contexts
    **B**Subtracts decimals to 3 decimal places reliably; zero-as-placeholder attempted with minor errors; checks using addition
    **C**Subtracts decimals to 2 decimal places with scaffolding; beginning to extend to 3 places
    **D**Subtracts decimals to 1 decimal place with significant scaffolding
    **E**Unable to subtract decimals; significant support required

    Criterion 4: Fraction Addition and Subtraction

    GradeDescriptor
    **A**Adds and subtracts fractions with same and related denominators fluently; converts between mixed numerals and improper fractions; applies to problem-solving contexts
    **B**Adds and subtracts fractions with same denominators reliably; attempts related denominators with minor errors
    **C**Adds fractions with same denominator using visual models; subtraction attempted with scaffolding
    **D**Adds fractions with same denominator with significant scaffolding; visual models required
    **E**Unable to add fractions; significant support required

    Criterion 5: Estimation and Mathematical Reasoning

    GradeDescriptor
    **A**Rounds decimals to nearest whole, tenth, hundredth confidently; uses estimation effectively before and after calculating; justifies strategy choice; explains reasoning
    **B**Rounds decimals to nearest tenth reliably; uses estimation to check most answers; justifies strategy choice with prompting
    **C**Rounds decimals to nearest whole number with scaffolding; uses estimation to check with prompting
    **D**Rounds to nearest whole with significant scaffolding; estimation used only with teacher direction
    **E**Does not demonstrate estimation skills

    Criterion 6: Problem-Solving

    GradeDescriptor
    **A**Solves multi-step problems involving decimals and fractions independently; selects appropriate representations and strategies; interprets results in context
    **B**Solves two-step decimal/fraction problems reliably; selects appropriate operations with minor prompting

    **D**Solves simple decimal problems within 1 decimal place with significant scaffolding
    **E**Unable to solve word problems involving decimals or fractions

    LESSON 1.2 — Place Value to Thousandths

    Duration: 50 minutes

    Type: Concept Introduction

    Learning Intentions

  • I am learning to read, write, and understand decimals to thousandths
  • I am learning that thousandths are 10× smaller than hundredths

    • Success Criteria

  • I can explain 3.582 = 3 + 5/10 + 8/100 + 2/1000

    • I DO (15 min)

    Write 3.582 on the board.

    "3 ones + 5 tenths + 8 hundredths + 2 thousandths. 3 + 0.5 + 0.08 + 0.002 = 3.582."
    "Each place value is 10× smaller than the one to its left."
    "1 = 10× 0.1. 0.1 = 10× 0.01. 0.01 = 10× 0.001."

    YOU DO (15 min)

  • Write 4.582 in expanded form.
  • Write the value of each digit in 7.346.
  • EXTENSION: "What is 0.001 × 10? What is 0.1 × 10? What is 1 × 10?"

    • LESSON 1.3 — Comparing Decimals

    Duration: 50 minutes

    Type: Guided Practice

    I DO (15 min)

    "3.582 vs 3.528. Compare ones → same (3). Compare tenths → 5 vs 5 → same. Compare hundredths → 8 vs 2. 8 hundredths > 2 hundredths. So 3.582 > 3.528."
    "Key rule: Compare digit by digit from the left."

    YOU DO (15 min)

  • Compare using >, <, or =: 4.356 ○ 4.365
  • Order smallest to largest: 2.345, 2.534, 2.453, 2.543
  • EXTENSION: "Is 0.5 the same as 0.500? Explain."

    LESSON 2.1 — Decimal Addition

    Duration: 50 minutes

    Type: Explore

    I DO (15 min)

    "4.5 + 3.7. Jump: 4.5 + 3 = 7.5, +0.7 = 8.2."
    "Or column: line up decimal points."

    ```

    4.5

    + 3.7

    8.2

    ```

    "Same for 4.56 + 3.78: line up decimal points."

    ```

    4.56

    + 3.78

    8.34

    ```

    YOU DO (15 min)

  • 4.5 + 3.7 = ___ 5.6 + 3.8 = ___
  • 4.56 + 3.78 = ___
  • 7.234 + 2.345 = ___
  • EXTENSION: "4.567 + 2.345 = ? Where does the decimal point go?"

    • LESSON 2.2 — Decimal Subtraction

    Duration: 50 minutes

    Type: Explore

    I DO (15 min)

    "8.5 - 3.7. Column: line up decimal points."

    ```

    8.5

  • 3.7

    • ```

    "5 tenths - 7 tenths: can't! Regroup: 8 ones 5 tenths → 7 ones 15 tenths. 15 - 7 = 8 tenths. 7 - 3 = 4 ones. 4.8."
    "Think-addition: 3.7 + ? = 8.5. 3.7 + 4 = 7.7, +0.8 = 8.5. Answer: 4.8."

    YOU DO (15 min)

  • 9.5 - 4.7 = ___ (column)
  • 8.00 - 3.67 = ___ (add zeros to fill decimal places)
  • 7.654 - 2.345 = ___
  • EXTENSION: "Why do we add zeros to 8.00 when subtracting 3.67?"

    • LESSON 2.3 — Estimation for Decimals

    Duration: 50 minutes

    Type: Guided Practice

    I DO (12 min)

    "4.567 + 2.345. Round to nearest whole: 5 + 2 = 7. Actual: 6.912. Close ✓."
    "Round to nearest tenth: 4.6 + 2.3 = 6.9. Even closer ✓."

    YOU DO (15 min)

  • Estimate then calculate: 5.678 + 3.456 = ___
  • Estimate then calculate: 9.000 - 4.567 = ___
  • "Which gives a better estimate — nearest whole or nearest tenth?"
  • EXTENSION: "Estimate: 34.567 + 21.234 ≈ ?"

    LESSON 3.1 — Adding Fractions (Same Denominator)

    Duration: 50 minutes

    Type: Explore

    I DO (15 min)

    "3/8 + 2/8. Denominators are the same — 8. Add the numerators: 3+2=5. Denominator stays 8. 5/8."
    "Can it be simplified? 5/8 — 5 and 8 have no common factors (other than 1). So 5/8 is in simplest form."
    "What about 3/8 + 7/8 = 10/8 = 8/8 + 2/8 = 1 + 2/8 = 1 2/8 = 1 1/4."

    YOU DO (15 min)

  • 2/7 + 3/7 = ___ 4/9 + 5/9 = ___
  • 3/8 + 5/8 = ___ (simplify)
  • 5/6 + 5/6 = ___ (convert to mixed numeral)
  • EXTENSION: "What is 3/7 + 4/7 + 2/7?"

    • LESSON 3.2 — Subtracting Fractions (Same Denominator)

    Duration: 50 minutes

    Type: Explore

    I DO (15 min)

    "5/8 - 2/8 = 3/8."
    "7/8 - 5/8 = 2/8 = 1/4. Simplified!"
    "What about 1 - 3/5? 1 = 5/5. So 5/5 - 3/5 = 2/5."

    YOU DO (15 min)

  • 7/9 - 4/9 = ___ 5/8 - 3/8 = ___
  • 1 - 3/7 = ___
  • 5/6 - 4/6 = ___ (simplify)
  • EXTENSION: "1 - 3/5 = 2/5. Check: 2/5 + 3/5 = 5/5 = 1. What does this tell us?"

    • LESSON 3.3 — Mixed Numerals

    Duration: 50 minutes

    Type: Guided Practice

    I DO (15 min)

    "2 3/8 + 1 5/8."
    "Method 1 — add whole parts and fraction parts separately."
    "2 + 1 = 3. 3/8 + 5/8 = 8/8 = 1. So 3 + 1 = 4. Answer: 4."
    "Method 2 — convert to improper fractions first."
    "2 3/8 = 19/8. 1 5/8 = 13/8. 19/8 + 13/8 = 32/8 = 4."

    YOU DO (15 min)

  • 3 1/4 + 2 1/4 = ___
  • 4 5/8 + 2 3/8 = ___
  • 5 3/5 - 2 1/5 = ___
  • EXTENSION: "3 3/4 + 2 3/4 = ?"

    LESSON 4.1 — Common Denominators

    Duration: 50 minutes

    Type: Explain

    I DO (15 min)

    "1/2 + 1/4. Denominators: 2 and 4. The LCM of 2 and 4 is 4. Convert: 1/2 = 2/4."
    "1/2 + 1/4 = 2/4 + 1/4 = 3/4."
    "For 2/3 + 1/6: LCM of 3 and 6 is 6. Convert: 2/3 = 4/6. 4/6 + 1/6 = 5/6."

    YOU DO (15 min)

  • 1/3 + 1/6 = ___ (LCM of 3,6 = 6 → 2/6+1/6=3/6=1/2)
  • 1/2 + 1/3 = ___ (LCM of 2,3 = 6 → 3/6+2/6=5/6)
  • 2/3 + 1/4 = ___ (LCM of 3,4 = 12 → 8/12+3/12=11/12)
  • EXTENSION: "Find the LCM of 4, 6, and 8."

    • LESSON 4.2 — Adding and Subtracting with Related Denominators

    Duration: 50 minutes

    Type: Explain

    I DO (15 min)

    "2/3 - 1/4. LCM of 3 and 4 is 12. 2/3 = 8/12. 1/4 = 3/12. 8/12 - 3/12 = 5/12."
    "3/4 + 2/3. LCM of 4 and 3 is 12. 3/4 = 9/12. 2/3 = 8/12. 9/12 + 8/12 = 17/12 = 1 5/12."

    YOU DO (15 min)

  • 2/3 + 1/4 = ___
  • 3/4 - 2/5 = ___
  • 5/6 + 1/3 = ___
  • EXTENSION: "1/2 + 1/3 + 1/6 = ?"

    • LESSON 5.2 — Fractions in Context

    Duration: 50 minutes

    Type: Explain

    I DO (15 min)

    "Sam ate 2/5 of a pizza. Alex ate 1/4 of the same pizza. How much did they eat altogether? 2/5 + 1/4. LCM of 5 and 4 = 20. 2/5 = 8/20. 1/4 = 5/20. Total = 13/20."

    YOU DO (15 min)

  • "Sam ate 1/3 of a cake. Mia ate 1/4. How much cake is left?"
  • "A rope is 5m. Sam uses 1 1/2m, then 2 1/4m. How much is left?"
  • EXTENSION: "Sam has $20. He spends 3/5 on a book and 1/4 on lunch. What fraction of his money does he have left?"

    LESSON 7.1 — Connections: Fractions ↔ Decimals

    Duration: 50 minutes

    Type: Elaborate

    I DO (15 min)

    "1/2 = 0.5. 1/4 = 0.25. 3/4 = 0.75. 1/5 = 0.2. 2/5 = 0.4. 3/5 = 0.6. 4/5 = 0.8."

    YOU DO (15 min)

  • Convert to fractions: 0.75, 0.6, 0.375, 0.125
