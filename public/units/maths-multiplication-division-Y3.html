    A–E RUBRIC — Multiplication and Division (Year 3)

    Aligned to: AC9M3N03, AC9M3N04, AC9M4N03

    Criterion 1: Multiplication Tables (all to 10×10)

    GradeDescriptor
    **A**Recalls all multiplication facts to 10×10 instantly; any order; demonstrates proficiency with all related division facts
    **B**Recalls most facts reliably; occasional errors on 6×, 7×, 8×, 9×; uses known facts to derive unknown facts
    **C**Recalls ×2, ×3, ×4, ×5, ×10 fluently; ×6, ×7, ×8, ×9 with skip-counting or strategies; uses distributive thinking
    **D**Recalls ×2, ×3, ×5, ×10 with support; ×6, ×7, ×8, ×9 require skip-counting
    **E**Unable to recall facts; relies entirely on skip-counting or objects

    Criterion 2: Distributive Property

    GradeDescriptor
    **A**Applies distributive property to multiply: 6×7 = 6×5 + 6×2; uses to derive unknown facts; applies to mental computation
    **B**Uses distributive property with prompting; derives unknown from known facts
    **C**Beginning to use distributive property to break apart facts; uses with scaffolding
    **D**Uses partitioning with significant support
    **E**Unable to partition to multiply

    Criterion 3: Factor Pairs and Prime Numbers

    GradeDescriptor
    **A**Finds all factor pairs for numbers to 50; identifies prime numbers accurately; explains why some numbers are prime
    **B**Finds factor pairs reliably for numbers to 30; identifies prime numbers with minor errors
    **C**Finds factor pairs with scaffolding; beginning to identify primes
    **D**Finds obvious factor pairs with support
    **E**Unable to identify factors

    Criterion 4: Division

    GradeDescriptor
    **A**Divides using known facts; handles remainders; interprets remainder in context; uses inverse to check
    **B**Divides reliably; minor errors with larger divisors; remainder interpretation developing
    **C**Divides with known facts and skip-counting; remainder interpretation with scaffolding
    **D**Divides using skip-counting and objects; remainder identification requires support
    **E**Unable to divide without significant scaffolding

    Criterion 5: Problem-Solving

    GradeDescriptor
    **A**Solves multi-step word problems involving multiplication and division; selects appropriate operations; justifies reasoning
    **B**Solves one-step and simple two-step problems; operation selection mostly correct
    **C**Solves one-step problems with scaffolding
    **D**Solves simple problems with significant scaffolding
    **E**Unable to solve word problems

    WEEK 1 — ENGAGE

    Focus: Cold Task + ×6, ×7, ×8, ×9 tables

    5E Phase: Engage

    LESSON 1.2 — Strategies for ×6, ×7, ×8, ×9

    Duration: 50 minutes

    Type: Concept Introduction

    Learning Intentions

  • I am learning mental strategies to derive unknown facts from known facts
  • I am learning that 6×7 = 5×7 + 1×7 (distributive property)

    • Success Criteria

  • I can use known facts to work out unknown facts

    • I DO (15 min)

    "You already know ×5 and ×10. Use them to get ×6!"
    "6×7 = 5×7 + 1×7 = 35 + 7 = 42."
    "8×7 = 5×7 + 3×7 = 35 + 21 = 56."
    "9×7 = 10×7 - 1×7 = 70 - 7 = 63."
    "7×7 = 5×7 + 2×7 = 35 + 14 = 49."

    YOU DO (15 min)

  • 6×8 = 5×8 + 1×8 = 40 + 8 = ___
  • 7×6 = 5×6 + 2×6 = ___ + ___ = ___
  • 8×6 = 10×6 - 2×6 = ___ - ___ = ___
  • 9×8 = 10×8 - 1×8 = ___
  • EXTENSION: "What is 7×8?"

  • 7×8=___ 9×8=___ 6×8=___

    • WEEK 2 — EXPLORE

    Focus: ×9 table; ×3 and ×6 connections

    5E Phase: Explore

    LESSON 2.1 — The ×9 Table

    Duration: 50 minutes

    Type: Explore

    I DO (15 min)

    "9× table has great patterns!"
    "1×9=9, 2×9=18 (1+8=9), 3×9=27 (2+7=9), 4×9=36 (3+6=9), 5×9=45 (4+5=9)"
    "Pattern: in each answer, the digits add to 9 (until 10×9=90)."
    "10×9 - 1×9: 90 - 9 = 81. 10×9 - 2×9: 90 - 18 = 72. And so on."

    YOU DO (15 min)

  • 6×9=54 (5+4=9), 7×9=63 (6+3=9), 8×9=72, 9×9=81
  • "What is 10×9 - 3×9? What is the answer?"
  • EXTENSION: "If 9×7 = 63, what is 9×8?"

    • LESSON 2.2 — ×3 and ×6 Connections

    Duration: 50 minutes

    Type: Explore

    I DO (15 min)

    "×6 = double ×3."
    "3×7 = 21. Double = 42. So 6×7 = 42."
    "×3 is half of ×6. 3×7 = 21, 6×7 = 42. 21×2 = 42."

    YOU DO (15 min)

  • "If 3×8=24, then 6×8=___"
  • "If 6×5=30, then 3×5=___"
  • EXTENSION: "If 3×12=36, what is 6×12?"

    Duration: 50 minutes

    Type: Explore

    Learning Intentions

  • I am learning to find all factor pairs for a number
  • I am learning that factors come in pairs

    • Success Criteria

  • I can find all factor pairs for 12

    "1 and the number itself are always factors."

    YOU DO (15 min)

  • Factor pairs for 16: ___×___, ___×___, ___×___
  • "What is the largest factor of 12 that is less than 12?"
  • EXTENSION: "Find all factor pairs for 24."

    • LESSON 3.2 — Prime Numbers

    Duration: 50 minutes

    Type: Explore

    I DO (15 min)

    "A PRIME number has only TWO factor pairs: 1 and itself."
    "2: 1×2 → prime"
    "3: 1×3 → prime"
    "4: 1×4, 2×2 → composite"
    "5: 1×5 → prime"
    "6: 1×6, 2×3 → composite"
    "7: 1×7 → prime"
    "8: 1×8, 2×4 → composite"
    "9: 1×9, 3×3 → composite"
    "10: 1×10, 2×5 → composite"
    "1 is neither prime nor composite."

    YOU DO (15 min)

  • Is 11 prime? YES / NO. Prove it.
  • Is 13 prime? YES / NO. Prove it.
  • Is 15 prime? YES / NO. Prove it.
  • List the first 10 prime numbers.
  • EXTENSION: "Is 2 the only even prime? Why?"

    LESSON 4.1 — Division with Remainders

    Duration: 50 minutes

    Type: Explain

    I DO (15 min)

    "Division with remainders: 47 ÷ 6."
    "6×7=42. 47-42=5. So 47 ÷ 6 = 7 remainder 5."
    "Check: 6×7 + 5 = 42 + 5 = 47. ✓"

    YOU DO (15 min)

  • 53 ÷ 7 = ___ r ___ (7×7=49, 53-49=4)
  • 67 ÷ 8 = ___ r ___
  • 89 ÷ 9 = ___ r ___
  • EXTENSION: "What is the largest remainder possible when dividing by 7?"

    • WEEK 6 — ELABORATE

    Focus: Distributive property

    5E Phase: Elaborate

    LESSON 6.1 — Expanding: 6×(4+3)

    Duration: 50 minutes

    Type: Elaborate

    Learning Intentions

  • I am learning to expand: 6×(4+3) = 6×4 + 6×3
  • I am learning that the distributive property works in both directions

    • Success Criteria

  • I can expand 6×(4+3) = 24 + 18 = 42

    • I DO (15 min)

    "6×(4+3) means: 6 times the WHOLE group (4+3)."
    "6×(4+3) = 6×4 + 6×3 = 24 + 18 = 42."
    "Why? 4+3=7. 6×7=42. Same answer! ✓"
    "This is the DISTRIBUTIVE LAW. We distribute the 6 across the brackets."

    YOU DO (15 min)

  • 4×(3+2) = 4×3 + 4×2 = ___ + ___ = ___
  • 5×(4+3) = 5×___ + 5×___ = ___ + ___ = ___
  • 7×(5+2) = ___
  • 3×(6+1) = ___
  • EXTENSION: "Can you factor 24 + 16? Hint: 8×(3+2)?"

    • LESSON 6.2 — Factorising

    Duration: 50 minutes

    Type: Elaborate

    I DO (12 min)

    "Factorising is the reverse of expanding."
    "7×3 + 7×2 = 7×(3+2) = 7×5 = 35."
    "24 + 18 = 6×4 + 6×3 = 6×(4+3) = 6×7 = 42."

    YOU DO (15 min)

  • 8×3 + 8×4 = 8×(___+___) = 8×___ = ___
  • 5×4 + 5×3 = 5×(___+___) = 5×___ = ___
  • 32 + 24 = 8×___ + 8×___ = 8×(___+___) = ___
  • EXTENSION: "Factorise 50 + 35."
