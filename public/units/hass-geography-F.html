Unit Content

Features of Places — Geography Unit Plan

Unit Duration: 7 weeks × 3 lessons per week = 21 lessons

Year Level: Foundation

Curriculum Link: Australian Curriculum HASS v9 — Geography (Foundation)

Unit Big Idea: Places are all around us. Every place has special features that make it unique. We care for places because they are home to us and to living things.

Cold Task: Week 1, Lesson 1 — Draw and label the special features of a place you know well

Hot Task: Week 7, Lesson 1 — Draw and label two places you know, and tell how we care for them

Unit Overview

Learning Intention:

We are learning to identify and describe the features of places and how we care for them.

Success Criteria:

  • I can name at least 3 features of a place (e.g., trees, buildings, water, sky).
  • I can describe what makes a place special.
  • I can explain how we care for places.
  • I can ask questions about places I see every day.
  • I can describe how a place has changed or might change.

    WEEK 1 — ENGAGE: What is a Place?

    Lesson 1: Cold Task — My Special Place

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Assessment (Formative Pre-Assessment)

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Hook (5 min)Show a photo of a familiar place (e.g., school, park, beach). "What do you see? Is this a real place? Have you been somewhere like this?"Observe and share
    Introduction (5 min)"A place is anywhere you can see, touch, and be. Places can be big or small. Our classroom is a place. Your bedroom is a place. Australia is a place. The whole world is made of places!"Listen and nod
    Cold Task (20 min)"Draw a place you know well. Label at least 3 things you see there. Write or tell 1 sentence: What makes this place special to you?"Independent drawing and labelling

    Differentiation: Image word bank provided. Sentence starter: "This place is special because..." EAL students may label in home language first.

    Success Criteria Check: Student produces a drawing with at least 2 labelled features of a place.

    Lesson 2: Places Are Everywhere

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Engage (Hook)

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Places Walk (10 min)Take students on a brief walk around the classroom or school. "What places can you see? What features do you notice?"Walk and observe
    Places Game (10 min)Show images of 6 places: beach, forest, city, farm, desert, snowy mountain. "Have you been to any of these places? Which ones would you like to visit?"Raise hands, share
    Sort the Places (10 min)"Which places have lots of trees and water? Which have buildings and roads? Which have sand or snow?"Sort places into groups

    Success Criteria Check: Can sort at least 3 places into groups and explain why they belong together.

    Lesson 3: Our Special Place — The Place I Know Best

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Engage (Concept Introduction)

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    The Place I Know Best (10 min)"Close your eyes. Think of a place you know really well. It might be your backyard, a park, grandma's house, or the beach. What do you see there? What do you hear?"Close eyes, visualise
    Draw and Share (15 min)Students draw the place they imagined. Teacher circulates, asking: "What is this place? What can we see here?"Draw and share with partner
    Gallery Walk (5 min)Students stand up and walk around to see others' drawings. "Look carefully. What do you notice?"Gallery walk

    Differentiation: EAL students may talk through their drawing with a partner. NEP students may dictate labels.

    Success Criteria Check: Can share the name of a place and describe 1 feature they know about it.

    WEEK 2 — EXPLORE: Features of Places

    Lesson 1: Natural and Built Features

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Explore (Investigation)

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    What Are Features? (10 min)"A feature is something you can see in a place — like a tree, a building, a hill, or the sky. Today we are going to learn about TWO types of features."Listen
    Natural Features (10 min)Show images: mountains, rivers, beaches, forests, caves, rocks. "Natural features are made by nature. No person built them. Trees, water, sand, mountains, animals — all natural."Observe and name
    Built Features (10 min)Show images: houses, schools, roads, bridges, shops, stadiums. "Built features are made by people. We build houses so we have somewhere warm and safe to live."Observe and name
    Sort It Out (5 min)Give students picture cards (or show on board): tree, car, rock, book, mountain, shoe, river, shoe, sand, tent. "Which are natural? Which are built?"Sort into two groups

    Success Criteria Check: Can give one example of a natural feature and one example of a built feature.

    Lesson 2: What Can We See in Our Place? (Local Walk)

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Explore (Investigation)

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Before the Walk (5 min)"Today we are going on a places walk. Your job is to notice as many features as you can. Look up, look down, look all around."Prepare to observe
    Places Walk (15 min)Walk around the school grounds or local area. Students identify and name features they see. Teacher stops at 3–4 points to highlight features.Walk and observe
    Back in Class (10 min)"What did you see? Let's make a class list of features we found." Create a two-column list: Natural / Built.Contribute to class list

    Success Criteria Check: Can name at least 2 natural features and 2 built features found on the walk.

    Lesson 3: Describing Places

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Explain (Explicit Teaching)

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Describing Words (10 min)"We can use describing words to tell people about a place. Big, small, tall, noisy, quiet, green, sandy, rocky, wet, dry, hot, cold." Teacher shows images, models describing each one.Repeat describing words
    Model Describing (10 min)Show a photo of the beach. "This is a beach. It has soft, sandy, wet, blue, windy, sunny features." Model how to describe using features.Listen
    Describe Your Place (10 min)Students choose one place from their Cold Task and write or say 3 describing words for it.Write or say 3 words

    Differentiation: EAL students may draw features instead of writing words. NEP students use a word bank of 10 describing words to choose from.

    Success Criteria Check: Can use at least 2 describing words to tell about a place.

    WEEK 3 — EXPLORE + EXPLAIN: Places Around the World

    Lesson 1: Places Far and Near

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Explore (Investigation)

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Near Places (5 min)"Let's start close to home. What are some places near our school?"Name places nearby
    Far Places (10 min)Show images: Eiffel Tower (France), Great Wall of China, Pyramids (Egypt), Opera House (Australia), Amazon Rainforest (Brazil). "These places are far from us in Australia. Have you heard of any of these?"Observe and name
    Near vs Far Sort (10 min)Class sort: places that are near us vs places that are far away. "How can we tell if a place is near or far? (Distance, photos, maps)"Sort places
    Favourite Place (5 min)"What is a far place you would like to visit someday? Why?"Share wish-list place

    Success Criteria Check: Can explain the difference between a near place and a far place.

    Lesson 2: Amazing Places

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Explore (Investigation)

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Amazing Places Showcase (15 min)Show 5–6 amazing place photos: Uluru, Great Barrier Reef, Swiss Alps, Amazon rainforest, Antarctic ice, African savanna. For each: "What do you see? What is the weather like? What animals might live there?"Observe and discuss
    What Features Do They Have? (10 min)Create a class chart: PlaceNatural FeaturesBuilt Features. Fill in as a class.Contribute to chart
    Draw Your Amazing Place (5 min)Students choose one amazing place and draw it.Independent drawing

    Success Criteria Check: Can name one amazing place and describe at least 2 features it has.

    Lesson 3: Comparing Two Places

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Explain (Explicit Teaching)

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Compare Two Places (10 min)Show side-by-side: beach and forest. "What is the same? What is different? A beach has sand and water. A forest has trees and dirt. Both have sky and sunlight."Compare
    Venn Diagram (10 min)Draw a simple Venn diagram on the board (two overlapping circles). Label: BeachForest. Model filling it in together.Observe and contribute
    Your Comparison (10 min)Students draw two places and complete their own simple Venn diagram (overlap circle optional — can be side-by-side).Draw and compare

    Differentiation: EAL students may use picture cut-outs to compare. Gifted students: compare 3 places.

    Success Criteria Check: Can identify at least 1 similarity and 1 difference between two places.

    WEEK 4 — EXPLAIN: How Places Change

    Lesson 1: Places Change Over Time

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Explain (Explicit Teaching)

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Do You Know Any Babies? (5 min)"When you were a baby, you were very small. Now you are bigger and stronger. Just like you, places also change as time passes."Relate to self
    Photos Then vs Now (15 min)Show 3 pairs of photos: school building now vs older photo, local street now vs older, park now vs older. "Look carefully. What has changed? What is the same?"Observe and identify changes
    What Changed? (10 min)"In the old photos, there were fewer buildings. There were more trees. The roads were smaller. Places change because people build new things, cut down trees, or plant new things."Listen and discuss

    Success Criteria Check: Can identify at least 1 way a place has changed over time.

    Lesson 2: Our Place Yesterday and Today

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Explain (Explicit Teaching)

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Yesterday vs Today (10 min)"Yesterday you were in this place too. But yesterday the room might have been set up differently. The weather might have been different. Every day, places change a little bit."Think about yesterday
    Change Search (10 min)"What has changed in our classroom since this morning? What about since Monday?"Notice changes in the room
    Then and Now Drawing (10 min)"Draw our classroom as it was at the START of this week, and as it is NOW. What is different?"Draw then and now

    Success Criteria Check: Can draw one way their immediate place (classroom) has changed.

    Lesson 3: How Do We Know Places Change?

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Explain (Explicit Teaching)

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    How Do We Know? (10 min)"We can see that places change by looking at OLD PHOTOS, hearing STORIES from old people, and LOOKING AROUND US. Photos are like a time machine."Listen
    Source Introduction (10 min)Show: an old photo of the local area (from library, council website, or Trove), a story from an elder about a place, a current photo of the same place. Explain these are SOURCES — they help us learn about the past.Examine sources
    What Did You Learn? (10 min)"What is one thing you now know about our local area that you didn't know before, thanks to old photos?"Write or say one thing

    Success Criteria Check: Can explain one way a source (like an old photo) helps us know places change.

    WEEK 5 — ELABORATE: Caring for Places

    Lesson 1: Who Cares for Our Place?

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Elaborate (Application)

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Who Cares? (10 min)"Our place needs people to look after it. Who looks after our school? Our street? Our park?" Introduce: gardeners, council workers, rangers, cleaners, volunteers, YOU.Name carers
    Who Cares for Wild Places? (10 min)"Who looks after national parks, forests, and beaches? Rangers, Indigenous land managers, volunteers. They make sure plants, animals, and people are safe."Listen
    Draw the Carer (10 min)"Draw someone who looks after a place. Write or tell: What do they do to care for the place?"Draw and label

    Success Criteria Check: Can name at least 1 person who cares for places and describe what they do.

    Lesson 2: How Can We Care for Places?

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Elaborate (Application)

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Caring Actions (10 min)"We ALL can care for places. Putting rubbish in the bin. Turning off taps. Planting trees. Not picking flowers. Walking on paths, not on gardens."Listen and repeat
    Sort the Actions (10 min)Show image cards: dropping litter vs putting it in bin, wasting water vs turning tap off, breaking a branch vs leaving it alone, saving electricity vs leaving lights on. "Good for places or not good?"Sort into good/bad
    My Caring Promise (10 min)"Write or draw 2 things YOU will do to care for a place this week."Make personal commitment

    Differentiation: EAL students draw their commitments. NEP students use a visual checklist of 6 caring actions.

    Success Criteria Check: Can commit to at least 1 action to care for a place.

    Lesson 3: Looking After Our School

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Elaborate (Application)

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    School Care Walk (10 min)Another walk around the school — this time looking for things that need care: overflowing bins, dry gardens, papers on the ground. "What needs attention?"Walk and identify
    We Are the Carers (15 min)Class activity: pick up rubbish, water a plant, move to a seating area away from a fragile garden patch. Take action.Take action
    Share the Action (5 min)"What did we do to care for our school today? Why does it matter?"Share

    Success Criteria Check: Participates in at least 1 caring action for the school environment.

    WEEK 6 — ELABORATE + EVALUATE: Animals and Plants in Places

    Lesson 1: Living Things in Our Place

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Evaluate (Critical Thinking)

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Living Things Are Everywhere (10 min)"Our place is home to living things — plants and animals. They need places to live, just like us. Plants need soil, water, and sunlight. Animals need food, water, and shelter."Listen
    What Lives Here? (10 min)Show images of local plants and animals (or real specimens if available). Discuss: What lives in our school? In our suburb? In Australia?Identify local species
    Draw a Living Thing (10 min)"Choose one plant or animal that lives in a place you know. Draw it and label what it needs to survive."Draw and label

    Success Criteria Check: Can identify at least 2 things a living thing needs to survive in its place.

    Lesson 2: Homes for Living Things

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Evaluate (Critical Thinking)

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Every Living Thing Has a Home (10 min)"A habitat is a home for a living thing. Birds live in nests in trees. Fish live in water. Rabbits live in burrows underground. Insects live in the soil, on plants, or under rocks."Listen
    Match the Habitat (10 min)Show animal images: frog, dog, fish, bird, bee. "Where does each one live? What does its home look like?"Match animal to habitat
    Make a Habitat (10 min)Students draw an animal in its habitat. Label the habitat features that help it survive (water, food, shelter).Draw habitat

    Differentiation: EAL students use pre-drawn habitat outlines to fill in. Gifted students: draw a food chain from the habitat.

    Success Criteria Check: Can draw one animal and label its habitat features.

    Lesson 3: Protecting Living Things

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Evaluate (Critical Thinking)

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Why Do Living Things Need Protection? (10 min)"Sometimes places change in ways that make it hard for plants and animals to survive. Forests get cut down. Wetlands get filled in. Rubbish gets into oceans. Living things lose their homes."Listen
    What Can We Do? (10 min)"We can protect living things by: not littering, planting trees, keeping waterways clean, not disturbing nests or burrows, learning about native animals."Learn protection actions
    Protection Pledge (10 min)"Write or draw 2 things you will do to help protect living things in places."Make pledge

    Success Criteria Check: Can describe at least 1 way to protect living things in a place.

    WEEK 7 — EVALUATE: Hot Task and Reflection

    Lesson 1: Hot Task — Show What You Know

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Summative Assessment

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Reminder (5 min)"Today is your Hot Task. You will do the same task as Week 1 — but now you know so much more! Draw a place you know. Label the features. Tell how we care for places."Listen
    Planning (5 min)Students think about which place to draw and what features to label.Think and plan
    Hot Task (20 min)"Draw a place you know well. Label at least 3 features (natural or built). Write or tell 2–3 sentences: How do people care for this place? How do living things survive there?"Independent completion

    Differentiation: EAL students may use bilingual dictionaries. NEP students may dictate or present orally.

    Lesson 2: Cold Task vs Hot Task — What I Know Now

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Evaluate (Self-Assessment)

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Compare (15 min)Place Cold Task next to Hot Task. "Look at your Week 1 drawing. Look at today's drawing. What is different? What did you add?" Use green texta to mark growth.Compare work samples
    Growth Reflection (10 min)"What is ONE thing you know now that you didn't know in Week 1? What is ONE thing you do to care for places that you didn't do before?"Write or say growth
    Share (5 min)3–4 students share one thing they are proud of from their Hot Task.Share

    Success Criteria Check: Completes self-comparison showing growth in understanding of places and features.

    Lesson 3: Sharing Our Learning

    Time: 30 minutes | Type: Celebration

    PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
    Learning Gallery (10 min)Hot Tasks displayed. Students walk the gallery. Sticky notes: "I noticed your learning about..."Walk gallery
    Places We Love (10 min)"What do you love most about the places you know? Would you like to help take care of them?" Share as a class.Share
    Certificate (5 min)Award "Place Explorer" certificates.Receive certificate
    What's Next? (5 min)"What place would you like to learn more about? A faraway place? A place where animals live? A place in the past?"Share

    DIFFERENTIATION NOTES

    EAL Learners

  • Visual Word Wall: Permanently displayed. Key vocabulary: place, feature, natural, built, change, care, habitat, near, far, describe. Updated weekly.
  • Sentence Starters: "This place has..." / "I care for this place by..." / "One feature is..." / "This place is special because..."
  • Home Language Allowance: Students may use home language for brainstorming. English used for final published work.
  • Partner Support: Paired with supportive partner during discussions.
  • Image-Based Tasks: Use picture cards and sorting activities wherever possible.

    • Gifted Learners

  • Research Depth: Research a specific amazing place in detail — include location, climate, features, animals, and culture.
  • Creative Extension: Write a story from the perspective of an animal looking for a new home as places change.
  • 3-Place Comparison: Compare 3 places (instead of 2) using a Venn diagram.

    • Students with NEPs

  • Reduced Written Expectation: 1–2 sentences for written tasks.
  • Word Banks: Provided for all written tasks.
  • Oral Presentation Option: Students may present verbally instead of writing.
  • Extra Time: Additional time allocated for all tasks.
  • Scaffolded Templates: Pre-drawn Venn diagrams, partially completed word banks.

    • ASSESSMENT NOTES

    Formative Assessment (Ongoing)

  • Cold Task review (Week 1)
  • Observation during walks and sorting activities
  • Exit tickets (Lessons 2.3, 3.3, 4.1)
  • Self-reflection (Week 7, Lesson 2)

    • Summative Assessment

  • Cold Task (Week 1, Lesson 1): Pre-assessment drawing with labels. Establishes baseline prior knowledge. Used for growth comparison.
  • Hot Task (Week 7, Lesson 1): Post-assessment. Same task as Cold Task with additions about caring and living things. Assessed against rubric.
  • Growth Analysis (Week 7, Lesson 2): Students compare Cold Task to Hot Task using green/red texta method.
