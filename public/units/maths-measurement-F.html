UNIT AT A GLANCE

**Year Level**Foundation (Pre-Primary / Kindergarten)
**Strand**Measurement
**Duration**8 weeks × 3 lessons/week = 24 lessons
**Model**5E Instructional Model
**Big Ideas**Objects can be compared by length, mass, and capacity; language of comparison (longer/shorter, heavier/lighter, more/less); informal measurement
**Driving Question***How do we compare and describe how big, heavy, or full things are?*

AUSTRALIAN CURRICULUM v9 — CONTENT DESCRIPTORS

Foundation

CodeDescriptor
**AC9MFMM01**Identify and compare attributes of objects and use direct comparison to order objects based on length, mass and capacity
**AC9MFMM02**Use informal units to compare the mass and capacity of objects, and the length of objects, explaining that measurement uses a unit of the same size

BIG UNDERSTANDINGS

Students will understand that:

  • Objects can be compared by length (longer/shorter)
  • Objects can be compared by mass (heavier/lighter)
  • Objects can be compared by capacity (more full/less full)
  • Direct comparison means placing objects side by side
  • Informal units (hand spans, blocks, cups) can be used to measure

    Tier 2

    length, mass, capacity, compare, order, measure, unit, estimate

    COLD TASK

    Name: _________________________ Date: _____________ Year: _______

    Part A — Length

  • Which is longer? Circle: a pencil / a crayon
  • Put these in order from shortest to longest: (drawings of pencil, crayon, scissors, glue stick)
  • Draw a line LONGER than your finger.

    • Part B — Mass

  • Which is heavier? Circle: an apple / a feather
  • Draw something HEAVIER than your rubber.

    • Part C — Capacity

  • Which holds MORE? Circle: a teacup / a bucket
  • Draw something that can hold LESS than a cup.

    • Part D — Ordering

  • Order these from lightest to heaviest: (pictures of a leaf, a book, a chair, a rock)
  • Order these from smallest capacity to biggest capacity: (pictures of a thimble, a cup, a bucket, a bathtub)

    • WEEK 1 — ENGAGE

    Focus: Cold Task + Length comparison

    5E Phase: Engage

    LESSON 1.2 — Longer and Shorter

    Duration: 40 minutes

    Type: Concept Introduction

    Learning Intentions

  • I am learning to compare objects by length
  • I am learning the words: longer, shorter, longest, shortest

    • Success Criteria

  • I can compare two objects and tell which is longer

    • I DO (12 min)

    "Let's compare these two pencils. How can we tell which is longer?"
    "Place them side by side! Start at the same end."
    "This one sticks out further — it is LONGER. This one — SHORTER."
    "Longer means goes further. Shorter means doesn't go as far."

    WE DO (18 min)

    Sort the class:

    Cards with objects. Sort: LONGER / SHORTER.

    Pair activity: find two things in the room. Which is longer? Tell your partner.

    YOU DO (10 min)

  • Circle the longer: a ruler / a rubber
  • Draw two lines. Make one LONGER.
  • "Find two things on your desk. Which is longer?"

    • LESSON 1.3 — Ordering by Length

    Duration: 40 minutes

    Type: Guided Practice

    I DO (12 min)

    "Let's put these three objects in order from SHORTEST to LONGEST."
    "Compare each pair first. Then order: shortest, middle, longest."

    YOU DO (15 min)

  • Order these from shortest to longest: (pencil, crayon, scissors)
  • Order these from longest to shortest: (book, paper, table)
  • "Draw 3 lines. Make shortest → longest."

    • WEEK 2 — EXPLORE

    Focus: Mass comparison

    5E Phase: Explore

    LESSON 2.1 — Heavier and Lighter

    Duration: 40 minutes

    Type: Explore

    Learning Intentions

  • I am learning to compare objects by mass
  • I am learning the words: heavier, lighter, heaviest, lightest

    • Success Criteria

  • I can compare two objects and tell which is heavier

    • I DO (12 min)

    "Which is heavier — this book or this piece of paper?"
    "Books are heavier. Paper is lighter."
    "Heavier means feels heavier when you pick it up. Lighter means feels lighter."
    "We can compare by FEELING the weight — this is called direct comparison."

    WE DO (18 min)

    Sort activity: Objects to sort: book, pencil, rubber, scissors, glue stick.

    Hold each object. Sort into: HEAVIER / LIGHTER pairs.

    YOU DO (10 min)

  • Circle the heavier: a rock / a leaf
  • Draw something LIGHTER than a book.
  • "What is the heaviest thing you can find in this room?"

    • LESSON 2.2 — Ordering by Mass

    Duration: 40 minutes

    Type: Guided Practice

    YOU DO (30 min)

  • Order from lightest to heaviest: a rubber, a book, a chair
  • "Find 3 things on your desk. Put them in order from lightest to heaviest."
  • "Is a cup of water heavier than an empty cup? How do you know?"

    • WEEK 3 — EXPLORE

    Focus: Capacity comparison

    5E Phase: Explore

    LESSON 3.1 — More and Less

    Duration: 40 minutes

    Type: Explore

    Learning Intentions

  • I am learning to compare containers by capacity
  • I am learning the words: more, less, full, empty

    • Success Criteria

  • I can tell which container holds more

    • I DO (12 min)

    "A bucket can hold MORE water than a cup. A cup holds LESS than a bucket."
    "Full means as much as it can hold. Empty means nothing in it."
    "We can compare by POURING from one to another — this is direct comparison."

    WE DO (18 min)

    Pouring comparison: Water play. Pour between two containers. Which holds more?

    Sort: MORE / LESS pairs.

    YOU DO (10 min)

  • Circle what holds MORE: a bathtub / a thimble
  • Draw a container that holds LESS than a cup.
  • "What holds the most in our room?"

    • WEEK 4 — EXPLORE

    Focus: Informal units

    5E Phase: Explain

    LESSON 4.1 — Measuring Length with Informal Units

    Duration: 40 minutes

    Type: Explain

    Learning Intentions

  • I am learning to measure length using informal units
  • I am learning that units must be the same size

    • Success Criteria

  • I can measure a line using blocks

    • I DO (12 min)

    "How long is this table? Let's use blocks as our measuring unit."
    "Each block is the SAME size. We line them up end to end. Count: 1, 2, 3..."
    "The table is 12 blocks long."
    "Why same size? If we used different size blocks, we'd get a different answer!"

    YOU DO (15 min)

  • Measure your desk using pencils. About ___ pencils.
  • Measure your book using your hand span. About ___ hands.
  • "Why do we use the same size unit?"
  • EXTENSION: "Which is longer — 10 pencils or 10 rubber bands?"

    • WEEK 5 — EXPLORE

    Focus: Informal units for mass and capacity

    5E Phase: Explain

    LESSON 5.1 — Measuring Mass and Capacity

    Duration: 40 minutes

    Type: Explain

    I DO (12 min)

    "How heavy is this book? Let's use blocks. Add blocks until the scales balance."
    "The book is about 8 blocks heavy."
    "How much water in this cup? Let's use spoons. Count how many spoons fill the cup."

    YOU DO (15 min)

  • "Estimate how many blocks heavy your rubber is. Now measure."
  • "Estimate how many cups fill this container. Now count."
  • "Why is it important to use the SAME unit each time?"

    • WEEK 6 — ELABORATE

    Focus: Ordering; selecting units

    5E Phase: Elaborate

    LESSON 6.1 — Ordering

    Duration: 40 minutes

    Type: Elaborate

    YOU DO (30 min)

  • Order 5 objects from shortest to longest. Measure each.
  • Order 5 objects from lightest to heaviest. Explain your method.
  • Order 5 containers from least capacity to most. Prove it.

    • WEEK 7 — ELABORATE (Part 2)

    Focus: Peer games

    5E Phase: Elaborate

    LESSON 7.2 — Peer Games

    Duration: 40 minutes

    PEER GAMES

  • Longer or Shorter War: Draw two lines. Partner identifies.
  • Heavier or Lighter Sort: Sort object cards.
  • Capacity Race: Which container fills first?
  • Measure It: Use informal units to measure objects.

    Focus: Hot Task and celebration

    LESSON 8.3 — Hot Task + Celebration

    Duration: 45 minutes

    HOT TASK (20 min)

    Identical to Cold Task.

    CELEBRATION (25 min)
