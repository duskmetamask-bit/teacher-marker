    A–E RUBRIC — Multiplication and Division (Year 2)

    Aligned to: AC9M2N05, AC9M2N06, AC9M3N03

    Criterion 1: Multiplication Tables — ×2, ×3, ×5, ×10

    GradeDescriptor
    **A**Recalls all ×2, ×3, ×5, ×10 facts instantly and in any order; demonstrates proficiency with related division facts
    **B**Recalls most ×2, ×3, ×5, ×10 facts reliably; minor errors; related division facts mostly correct
    **C**Recalls facts with some prompting; uses skip-counting as backup; beginning to recall ×3 and ×4
    **D**Recalls ×2 and ×5 facts with skip-counting support; ×3 and ×4 require objects
    **E**Unable to recall facts; relies entirely on skip-counting or objects

    Criterion 2: Arrays and Area Model

    GradeDescriptor
    **A**Creates and interprets arrays to represent multiplication; connects array to area; explains rows and columns
    **B**Creates arrays reliably; beginning to connect to area; interprets given arrays
    **C**Creates arrays with scaffolding; uses skip-counting to find area
    **D**Creates simple arrays with support; counts all
    **E**Unable to create or interpret arrays

    Criterion 3: Division — Exact Division

    GradeDescriptor
    **A**Divides using known facts; relates division to multiplication; solves division word problems; uses inverse to check
    **B**Divides using known facts reliably; beginning to solve word problems; inverse understanding developing
    **C**Divides with skip-counting and known facts; word problems with scaffolding
    **D**Divides using objects and skip-counting; word problems require support
    **E**Unable to divide without significant scaffolding

    Criterion 4: Division with Remainders

    GradeDescriptor
    **A**Divides with remainders; interprets remainder in context; rounds up or down as appropriate
    **B**Divides with remainders reliably; beginning to interpret remainder in context
    **C**Divides with remainders with scaffolding; interpretation of remainder limited
    **D**Identifies remainder with significant support; interpretation not yet developed
    **E**Unable to divide with remainders

    Criterion 5: Problem-Solving and Reasoning

    GradeDescriptor
    **A**Solves multi-step word problems involving multiplication and division; selects appropriate operation; justifies reasoning
    **B**Solves one-step word problems reliably; selects operation with minor prompting
    **C**Solves one-step problems with scaffolding; operation selection requires support
    **D**Solves simple problems with significant scaffolding
    **E**Unable to solve word problems

    LESSON 1.2 — The ×3 Table

    Duration: 50 minutes

    Type: Concept Introduction

    Learning Intentions

  • I am learning the 3× multiplication table
  • I am learning that 3× connects to doubles: 3×4 = 2×4 + 1×4

    • Success Criteria

  • I can recall 3×1 through 3×10

    • I DO (15 min)

    "3× table: skip-count by 3: 3, 6, 9, 12, 15, 18, 21, 24, 27, 30."
    "3×4 = 12. Double 4 is 8. Plus one more 4 = 12."
    "Pattern: each answer is 3 more than the one before."

    YOU DO (15 min)

  • Complete ×3: 1×3=___, 2×3=___, 3×3=___, 4×3=___, 5×3=___, 6×3=___, 7×3=___, 8×3=___, 9×3=___, 10×3=___
  • 5×3=___, 7×3=___, 8×3=___, 9×3=___
  • EXTENSION: "What is the 10th number in the 3× table? How do you know?"

    • LESSON 1.3 — ×3 Patterns

    Duration: 50 minutes

    Type: Guided Practice

    YOU DO (30 min)

  • "What comes after 18 in the 3× table?"
  • "Is 17 in the 3× table? How do you know?"
  • "If 3×7 = 21, what is 3×8? How do you know without counting?"
  • EXTENSION: "Prove that every 3× answer has digits that add to 3, 6, or 9 (digital root)."

    • WEEK 2 — EXPLORE

    Focus: ×4 table; doubling ×2

    5E Phase: Explore

    LESSON 2.1 — The ×4 Table

    Duration: 50 minutes

    Type: Explore

    I DO (15 min)

    "4× is double the 2× answer!"
    "2×7 = 14. So 4×7 = 28. Double 14."
    "1×4=4, 2×4=8, 3×4=12, 4×4=16, 5×4=20, 6×4=24, 7×4=28, 8×4=32, 9×4=36, 10×4=40."

    YOU DO (15 min)

  • 4×3=___, 4×6=___, 4×8=___, 4×7=___
  • "Since 5×4=20, what is 4×5?"
  • "What is double 6×2? (12×2=24) → so 4×6 = ___"
  • EXTENSION: "If 4×8 = 32, what is 8×4?"

    • LESSON 2.2 — Connecting ×2, ×3, ×4

    Duration: 50 minutes

    Type: Guided Practice

    YOU DO (30 min)

  • Fill in the grid: ×2 / ×3 / ×4 for numbers 1-10
  • "What do you notice about ×4 and ×2? (×4 = ×2 doubled)"
  • "What do you notice about ×3 and ×6? (×6 = ×3 doubled)"
  • EXTENSION: "What is 4×12?"

    • WEEK 3 — EXPLORE

    Focus: Arrays and area model

    5E Phase: Explore

    LESSON 3.1 — Arrays

    Duration: 50 minutes

    Type: Explore

    I DO (15 min)

    "An array shows multiplication as rows × columns."
    "3 rows of 4 = 3×4 = 12."
    "4 rows of 3 = 4×3 = 12."
    "Same area — different orientation!"

    YOU DO (15 min)

  • Draw: 3×5=___, 5×3=___, 4×4=___
  • "Draw a rectangle with area 24 using two different arrays."
  • EXTENSION: "What is the area of a 6×6 array?"

    • LESSON 3.2 — Area Model

    Duration: 50 minutes

    Type: Explore

    I DO (15 min)

    "An array's TOTAL = area of the rectangle."
    "3cm × 4cm = 12cm². Each small square is 1cm²."

    YOU DO (15 min)

  • "A rectangle is 5cm by 3cm. What is its area?"
  • "Can you draw a shape with area 12? How many different shapes can you make?"
  • EXTENSION: "What is the area of a 10×10 array?"

    • WEEK 4 — EXPLORE

    Focus: Division with remainders

    5E Phase: Explain

    LESSON 4.1 — Division with Remainders

    Duration: 50 minutes

    Type: Explain

    Learning Intentions

  • I am learning that division doesn't always work out evenly
  • I am learning what a remainder means

    • Success Criteria

  • I can solve 17 ÷ 3 = 5 remainder 2

    • I DO (15 min)

    "17 ÷ 3. Can we share 17 between 3 equally?"
    "3×5 = 15. Share 5 each. 2 left over. That's the REMAINDER."
    "17 ÷ 3 = 5 remainder 2."
    "Check: 3×5 + 2 = 17. ✓"

    YOU DO (15 min)

  • 14 ÷ 3 = ___ r ___ (3×4=12, remainder 2)
  • 19 ÷ 4 = ___ r ___
  • 23 ÷ 5 = ___ r ___
  • EXTENSION: "What is the largest remainder when dividing by 4?"

    • LESSON 4.2 — Interpreting Remainders

    Duration: 50 minutes

    Type: Guided Practice

    I DO (12 min)

    "Remainder in context: 17 cookies shared between 3. 5 each, 2 left over."
    "What happens to the 2? Either: give to teacher, save for later, or cut in half!"
    "Context tells us what the remainder means."

    YOU DO (15 min)

  • "23 children go to camp in groups of 4. How many full groups? ___ How many left over? ___"
  • "19 apples packed into bags of 5. How many bags? ___ Any left? ___"
  • "If I have 25 and divide by 6, what is the remainder?"
  • EXTENSION: "How many 3s in 20? What is the remainder?"

    • WEEK 5 — EXPLORE

    Focus: All facts ×2, ×3, ×5, ×10; related division

    5E Phase: Explain

    LESSON 5.1 — Fact Fluency

    Duration: 50 minutes

    Type: Explain

    YOU DO (30 min)

  • ×2 grid: 1×2 through 10×2
  • ×3 grid: 1×3 through 10×3
  • ×5 grid: 1×5 through 10×5
  • ×4 grid: 1×4 through 10×4

    • LESSON 5.2 — Related Division Facts

    Duration: 50 minutes

    Type: Explain

    I DO (15 min)

    "If 3×4=12, then 12÷3=4 and 12÷4=3."
    "Fact family: 3, 4, 12."

    YOU DO (15 min)

  • From 4×7=28: write 28÷4=___, 28÷7=___
  • From 3×8=24: write 24÷3=___, 24÷8=___
  • From 5×6=30: write the full fact family
  • EXTENSION: "From 4×9=36, write all division facts."

    • WEEK 6 — ELABORATE

    Focus: ×4 and ×8 connections; halving

    5E Phase: Elaborate

    LESSON 6.1 — ×4 and ×8 Connections

    Duration: 50 minutes

    Type: Elaborate

    I DO (15 min)

    "×4 = double ×2. ×8 = double ×4 = double ×2 twice."
    "2×7=14. 4×7=28 (double 14). 8×7=56 (double 28)."

    YOU DO (15 min)

  • "If 2×6=12, then 4×6=___, 8×6=___"
  • "If 2×9=18, then 4×9=___, 8×9=___"
  • EXTENSION: "What is 8×7?"

    • LESSON 6.2 — Halving

    Duration: 50 minutes

    Type: Elaborate

    I DO (12 min)

    "Halving is the inverse of doubling. 16 ÷ 2 = half of 16 = 8."
    "Half of 24 = 12. Half of 40 = 20."

    YOU DO (15 min)

  • Half of 14 = ___ Half of 28 = ___ Half of 36 = ___
  • "If 4×6 = 24, what is half of 24? What is 4×3?"
  • EXTENSION: "What is half of 56?"

    • WEEK 7 — ELABORATE (Part 2)

    Focus: Mixed problems; peer games

    5E Phase: Elaborate

    LESSON 7.1 — Mixed Word Problems

    Duration: 50 minutes

    Type: Elaborate

    YOU DO (30 min)

  • "There are 6 rows of 4 chairs. How many chairs?"
  • "A farmer has 29 eggs. Packs into cartons of 6. How many cartons? Remainder?"
  • "How many groups of 3 in 27?"
  • "I have 20. Half it, then multiply by 3. What is the answer?"
  • EXTENSION: "How many different ways can you make 24 with multiplication?"

    • LESSON 7.2 — Peer Games

    Duration: 50 minutes

    PEER GAMES

  • Multiplication War: Draw two number cards, multiply. Larger wins.
  • Remainder Race: Division with remainders. Correct remainder wins.
  • Array Challenge: Build arrays from facts.
  • Fact Family Match-Up.
