Key Understandings

What students will understand by the end of this unit

  1. A fraction names equal parts — the parts must be the same size
  2. The denominator names how many equal parts the whole is divided into; the numerator names how many parts are selected
  3. The same fraction can look different (equivalent fractions) — they name the same amount
  4. Fractions and decimals are two different ways of writing parts of a whole
  5. Fractions exist on a number line — they have size and order
  6. You can only add or subtract fractions that refer to the same sized parts (same denominator)
Learning Intentions

We are learning to understand, represent and work with fractions and decimals.

Explain what a fraction is and why the parts must be equal
Read and write fractions — know what the top and bottom numbers mean
Shade or draw fractions of shapes
Find fractions of a collection (e.g. 1/3 of 12)
Show fractions on a number line
Find equivalent fractions (same size, different names)
Add and subtract fractions with the same denominator
Explain the connection between fractions and decimals (tenths)
Read and write decimals with tenths and hundredths
Order fractions and decimals from smallest to largest

Week 1 — Engage: Introduction to Fractions

5E: Engage
Lesson 1: Cold Task and Launch
60 min Assessment
Hook (10 min) Read The Doorbell Rang aloud. Turn and talk: What happened to each person's share every time the doorbell rang? "That's what this whole unit is about."
Cold Task (35 min) Students attempt the 10-question pre-assessment independently. Teacher circulates and observes only — does NOT scaffold or prompt.
Debrief (10 min) Normalise not knowing. Preview the 8-week journey. "By Week 8, you'll do this exact same task again. And you will be amazed at how much you've learned."
Lesson 2: Equal Sharing — What Makes a Fraction?
60 min Explicit
I Do (15 min) Read The Doorbell Rang pp. 1–15. Model folding paper circles into halves. Key concept: "For something to be a fraction, the parts must be EQUAL in size." Introduce numerator (top = how many parts) and denominator (bottom = how many equal parts).
We Do (25 min) Students fold paper circles: Round 1 — 2 people (halves). Round 2 — 4 people (quarters). Round 3 — 3 people (thirds). Discuss: what changes when more people share?
You Do (15 min) Draw and shade rectangles and circles to show fractions. "My friend says this shape shows halves:" (show unequal pieces) — agree or disagree and explain why.
Differentiation: EAL/D: vocabulary cards (numerator, denominator, equal, whole). Gifted: Can you draw a shape that shows 5/8? NEP: Focus on halves only.
Lesson 3: Naming Fractions — Reading and Writing
60 min Guided
I Do (15 min) Build fraction vocabulary wall: halves (1/2), thirds (1/3), quarters (1/4), fifths (1/5), sixths (1/6). Key rule: "When the numerator equals the denominator, we have ONE whole. 4/4 = 1."
We Do (25 min) Fraction strip exploration. Partner activity: Partner A points to a fraction strip piece; Partner B reads it aloud. Whiteboard check: teacher calls out fractions, students write and hold up.
You Do (15 min) Write fractions shown by shaded diagrams. Draw own diagrams for given fractions. "I ate 3/8 of a pizza. My friend ate 5/8. Together, did we eat the whole pizza?"

Week 2 — Explore: Fractions of Shapes

5E: Explore
Lesson 1: Area Model — Fractions of Shapes
60 min Explicit
I Do (15 min) Model: divide rectangles and circles into equal parts. Show that equal AREA is required — not just equal number. Circle divided into thirds, sixths. Key: "Sixths make a half — 3/6 = 1/2."
We Do (25 min) Grid paper activity: students draw rectangles (3×2, 4×2, 3×3 grids) and divide into halves, thirds, sixths. Partner check: "Is my partner's shape correctly divided into equal parts?"
You Do (15 min) Shade 3/4 of each shape (rectangle, circle, cross). Tick shapes correctly showing 1/3. Cross ones that do not — explain one cross.
Lesson 2: Multiple Representations
60 min Guided
I Do (12 min) Show the fraction 3/4 in four completely different ways: (1) Rectangle diagram, (2) Number line, (3) Collection of counters, (4) Words. Key question: "What stays the SAME? What CHANGES?"
We Do (25 min) 5-station rotation: (1) Pattern blocks — if hexagon = 1 whole, what fraction is each shape? (2) Number lines — mark halves, thirds, quarters. (3) Drawing — show 3/5 three different ways. (4) Word problems. (5) Sorting — same-amount fractions together.
You Do (15 min) Show 2/3 in three different ways. "Which representation do you find easiest? Why?" Challenge: can 1/4 be placed closer to 1 than to 0 on a number line?
Lesson 3: Comparing Fractions — Which Is Bigger?
60 min Guided
I Do (15 min) Which is bigger: 1/2 or 1/6? Test with fraction strips. Key insight: "When we cut something into MORE pieces, each piece gets SMALLER." Golden Rule: For unit fractions — the bigger the denominator, the SMALLER the fraction.
We Do (25 min) Fraction strip compare activity: 1/4 vs 1/3, 2/4 vs 1/2, 3/6 vs 1/2, 2/3 vs 3/4, 5/6 vs 4/5. Build strategy anchor chart: (1) Use fraction strips, (2) Think about size of piece, (3) Compare to 1/2 benchmark.
You Do (15 min) Compare using >, <, =. Order fractions smallest to largest. "I have 1/3 of a chocolate bar. My friend has 1/4. Who has more? Explain."

Week 3 — Explore: Fractions of Collections & Number Lines

5E: Explore
Lesson 1: Fractions of a Collection
60 min Guided
Engage (5 min) Re-read The Doorbell Rang — 12 cookies shared between 4 children. Work out the maths: 12 ÷ 4 = 3 cookies each. Each child gets 3/12 = 1/4 of the total.
I Do (12 min) Model with 12 counters: find 1/3 of 12 (divide by 3 = 4). Key connection: "Finding 1/3 of something = dividing by 3." Model non-unit: 3/4 of 20 — find 1/4 first (5), then count 3 groups (15).
We Do (25 min) Partner counter activity: find 1/4 of 16, 1/3 of 15, 2/3 of 12. The Doorbell Rang problem set: 12 cookies shared between 2, 3, 4, 6, 12 people — record cookies per person and fraction of total.
You Do (15 min) Find: 1/3 of 18, 1/4 of 24, 1/6 of 30, 2/3 of 15. "There are 20 students. 1/4 are wearing hats. How many?" "Sam says 1/5 of 25 is 10. Is he correct?"
Lesson 2: Fractions on the Number Line
60 min Guided
I Do (15 min) "Numbers don't jump from 0 to 1. That world is fractions." Divide 0–1 into halves, then quarters, then sixths. Notice: 2/4 and 1/2 are at the same point. Extend to beyond 1: 5/4 and 7/4 on 0–2 number line.
We Do (25 min) Floor number line activity: each student places a fraction card. Partner number lines: stack strips showing halves, thirds, sixths — record equivalences. "Which fractions line up?"
You Do (15 min) Place fractions on a number line from 0–1 and 0–2. "I placed 1/3 halfway between 0 and 1. Is this correct? Explain." Challenge: where would 1/3 go on a halves-and-quarters number line?
Lesson 3: Fractions Flip Book (Formative Artifact)
60 min Independent
I Do (10 min) Model flip book construction. Each page includes: diagram of shape, fraction name, number line placement, fraction of a collection example.
You Do (35 min) Students create personal Fractions Flip Book independently: (1) Halves, (2) Thirds, (3) Quarters, (4) Fifths, (5) Sixths, (6) Back — "My two favourites." Teacher circulates, conferences, assesses on 1–3 scale.

Week 4 — Explain: Equivalent Fractions

5E: Explain
Lesson 1: What Are Equivalent Fractions?
60 min Explicit
I Do (15 min) Show 2/4 = 1/2 using fraction strips — exactly the same length. Analogy: "It's like calling me Yuki or calling me 'the teacher' — different names, same person." Build: 1/2 = 2/4 = 3/6 = 4/8. "Every time we double both numerator and denominator, we get equivalent fractions."
We Do (25 min) Fraction wall activity: find all fractions equivalent to 1/2, 1/3, 2/3. Gallery walk — 4 posters with sticky notes. Order fractions using the wall: 2/6, 1/2, 3/4, 1/3.
You Do (15 min) Write two equivalent fractions for 1/3, 1/4, 3/4. Fill in blanks: 1/2 = ___/6 = ___/8. "My friend says 3/6 and 1/3 are equivalent. Do you agree?"
Lesson 2: Finding Equivalent Fractions — The Pattern
60 min Explicit
I Do (15 min) "Multiply top and bottom by the same number": 1/2 × 2/2 = 2/4, × 3/3 = 3/6. "Why does this work? Because we're multiplying by 1 — 2/2, 3/3." Simplifying: 12/16 → divide by 4 = 3/4.
We Do (25 min) Whiteboard rounds: find equivalent fractions, simplify. Missing number puzzles: 1/3 = ___/9 (answer: 3), 2/5 = 6/___ (answer: 15). Partner quiz: write 5 equivalent fraction puzzles to swap.
You Do (15 min) Find three equivalent fractions for 1/4, 2/3, 3/5. Simplify: 6/8, 4/6, 10/15. Fill in: 3/4 = ___/12 = 9/___.
Lesson 3: Concept Check Quiz + Consolidation
60 min Assessment
Quiz (20 min) Concept Check covering Weeks 1–4: represent fractions from diagrams, find fractions of collections, place on number line, identify equivalent fractions, explain denominator role, order fractions.
Consolidation (25 min) Flexible grouping: Group A (Strong) — peer teaching, explore unlike denominators. Group B (On Track) — practice page. Group C (Needs Support) — teacher-led small group with fraction strips.
Reflection (10 min) Exit ticket: "One thing I understand well: ___. One thing I'm still confused about: ___. My goal for next week: ___."

Week 5 — Explain: Adding and Subtracting Fractions

5E: Explain
Lesson 1: Adding Fractions — Same Denominator
60 min Explicit
I Do (15 min) The Doorbell Rang: Sam ate 2/6 of cookies, then 1/6 more. "2/6 + 1/6 = 3/6." Rule: ADD the numerators, KEEP the denominator. "Why? The denominator names the SIZE of the pieces — sixths are sixths. Only the numerator changes." Model: 2/3 + 2/3 = 4/3 = 1 and 1/3.
We Do (25 min) Fraction tile activity: build 2/5 + 2/5, 1/3 + 1/3. Partner challenge: build a problem where the answer is 3/4. When answer > 1, write as mixed numeral: 7/6 = 1 and 1/6.
You Do (15 min) Add: 2/5 + 2/5, 3/8 + 4/8, 1/3 + 2/3, 5/6 + 4/6. "Sam eats 1/4 of cookies, Jay eats 2/4. What fraction altogether? Is there any left?"
Lesson 2: Subtracting Fractions — Same Denominator
60 min Explicit
I Do (15 min) "Ma had 3/4 of a pizza. She ate 1/4. How much is left?" Model: 3/4 − 1/4 = 2/4. Same rule — SUBTRACT numerators, KEEP denominator. Key: 1 − 3/4 = 4/4 − 3/4 = 1/4 (convert whole to fraction form first).
We Do (25 min) Diagram-first approach: draw fraction diagrams, then write equations. Problems: 4/5 − 2/5, 5/6 − 3/6, 5/6 (whole) − 5/6 = 0. Partner: "Sam had 5/8 of cake. After the party, only 2/8 left. How much was eaten?"
You Do (15 min) Subtract: 4/5 − 2/5, 7/8 − 3/8, 5/6 − 5/6, 1 − 3/5. "I had 3/4 of water bottle. I drank 1/4. How much left?"
Lesson 3: Problem-Solving with Fractions
60 min Guided
I Do (10 min) STAR problem-solving strategy: Search (find numbers + question), Translate (write maths sentence), Answer (solve), Review (does it make sense?). Model: "Sam has 5/8 of pie. Gives 2/8 to brother. Gets 1/8 back. How much?" → 5/8 − 2/8 + 1/8 = 4/8 = 1/2.
We Do (20 min) Partner problem set: 11/12 of chocolate bar left; Maya ate 3/12; Sam ate 4/12 — how much left? Jay ate 2/6 pizza at lunch, 1/6 at dinner — more or less than half? Recipe needs 3/4 cup flour; added 1/4 cup — how much more needed?
You Do (20 min) Students choose 2 of 4 problems including multi-step fraction problems. Extension: describe the full Doorbell Rang cookie-sharing sequence in fractions.

Week 6 — Elaborate: Introducing Decimals

5E: Elaborate
Lesson 1: What Is a Decimal? Tenths
60 min Explicit
I Do (15 min) "Decimals are another way to write parts of a whole." Show 10-column grid: "Each part = one tenth = 1/10 = 0.1." Build table: 1/10 = 0.1, 2/10 = 0.2, 5/10 = 0.5, 10/10 = 1.0. "The decimal point separates the WHOLE part (left) from the PARTS (right)." Place value: ones | . | tenths.
We Do (25 min) Grid colouring activity: shade 3/10 columns → "What fraction? What decimal?" Shade 10/10 → "What fraction? Decimal? Whole number?" Whiteboard place value chart practice. Match fractions to decimals card activity.
You Do (15 min) Write as decimal: 4/10, 9/10, 1/10, 10/10. Write as fraction: 0.6, 0.2, 0.8, 1.0. Shade grid for 0.7. "Is 0.5 the same as 1/2? Show how you know."
Lesson 2: Tenths on a Number Line and Place Value
60 min Guided
I Do (15 min) Divide 0–1 number line into 10 equal parts = tenths. Label: 0, 0.1, 0.2... 1.0. "Where is 0.5? Right in the middle — the same as 1/2!" Extend to 0–2: place 1.3. MAB blocks: long rod = 1 whole, small cube = 0.1. Build 1.4: 1 rod + 4 cubes.
We Do (25 min) Number line placement: 0.3, 0.7, 1.2, 0.5, 1.8, 0.9. MAB building with partners — build and read: 2.3, 1.7. Order decimals from smallest to largest and justify.
You Do (15 min) Place on number line: 0.2, 0.8, 1.5, 0.5. Order: 1.3, 0.7, 1.0, 0.4, 1.9. Write in words: 0.3, 1.6, 2.9. Challenge: what decimals come between 0.5 and 1.0?
Lesson 3: Converting — Fractions ↔ Decimals (Tenths)
60 min Guided
I Do (12 min) Conversion table: 1/10 = 0.1, 3/10 = 0.3, 7/10 = 0.7, 10/10 = 1.0. "What about 1/2? Denominator is 2, not 10." Make equivalent: 1/2 = 5/10 = 0.5. Common conversions: 1/5 = 2/10 = 0.2, 2/5 = 4/10 = 0.4.
We Do (25 min) Conversion whiteboard rounds — teacher calls a fraction, students convert and hold up decimal, and vice versa. Match-up card game (20 cards). Mini investigation: which fractions with denominator 2, 4, 5, 10 can be written as decimal tenths? Which can't?
You Do (15 min) Convert to decimal: 6/10, 1/2, 3/5, 9/10. Convert to fraction: 0.4, 0.9, 0.5, 0.2. Order: 1/2, 0.3, 3/5, 0.8. "I say 3/5 > 0.5. Is this true?"

Week 7 — Elaborate: Hundredths and Ordering Decimals

5E: Elaborate
Lesson 1: Hundredths — The Next Column
60 min Explicit
I Do (15 min) Show 10×10 grid (100 squares): "Each tiny square = one HUNDREDTH = 1/100 = 0.01." One column (10 squares) = 10/100 = 1/10 = 0.1. Build place value: ones | . | tenths | hundredths. Model: 0.34 = 34/100, 0.07 = 7/100, 1.25 = 1 whole + 2 tenths + 5 hundredths. Key: 0.50 = 0.5 — trailing zero doesn't change value.
We Do (25 min) Hundredths grid colouring: shade 34 squares → write as fraction and decimal. Shade 50 squares → "Is there another name for this?" (1/2, 0.5). Place value chart: build 0.45, 1.23, 0.09, 2.50. Decimal dictation — teacher reads "three tenths and seven hundredths", students write 0.37.
You Do (15 min) Write as decimal: 45/100, 7/100, 3/10 and 9/100. Write as fraction: 0.23, 0.08, 0.90, 1.45. "My friend says 0.50 is bigger than 0.5. Are they correct?" Write 1/4 as a decimal.
Lesson 2: Ordering and Comparing Decimals
60 min Guided
I Do (15 min) "Common mistake: students think 0.9 > 1.2 because 9 > 1." Compare ones FIRST → then tenths → then hundredths. Compare 0.45 and 0.5: same ones, tenths 4 vs 5 → 0.45 < 0.5. Trick: write 0.5 as 0.50 — now compare easily.
We Do (25 min) Number line placement race: teams order decimal cards. Rounds: 0.2, 0.8, 0.5, 0.1 | 1.3, 0.9, 1.1, 1.7 | 0.45, 0.4, 0.54, 0.5 | 0.09, 0.9, 0.19, 0.90. Whiteboard comparison: hold up >, <, or =.
You Do (15 min) Compare: 0.7 ○ 0.3, 1.2 ○ 0.9, 0.45 ○ 0.5, 0.60 ○ 0.6. Order: 0.3, 0.03, 1.3, 0.31, 0.13. Between 0.4 and 0.5, name three decimals. Find the error: 0.9, 0.91, 0.19, 0.09 ordered incorrectly.
Lesson 3: Peer Assessment + Decimal Review
60 min Independent
Peer Assessment (25 min) Swap Week 6/7 workbook pages. Check: Are diagrams divided equally? Are fraction-to-decimal conversions correct? Write one specific praise and one specific suggestion.
Review Task (25 min) Individual: Write as decimal (3/10, 47/100, 1/4, 1/2). Write as fraction (0.9, 0.35, 0.06, 1.25). Place on 0–2 number line: 0.5, 1.25, 0.75, 1.9. Order: 0.6, 0.06, 1.6, 0.16, 0.61.
Reflection (10 min) Journal: "What I understand about decimals: ___. What I'm still working on: ___. One connection between fractions and decimals: ___."

Week 8 — Evaluate: Consolidation and Hot Task

5E: Evaluate
Lesson 1: Fraction Gallery and Consolidation Games
60 min Guided
Gallery Walk (20 min) Flip Books from Week 3 displayed around the room. Students add sticky notes: GREEN (something I notice), ORANGE (question), PINK (connection to another concept).
Consolidation Games (30 min) 4 rotations: Fraction Snap (matching game), The Doorbell Rang Maths (reenact with counters, write fraction equations), Decimal Place Value Race (dice game), Equivalent Fraction Bingo.
Reflection (5 min) "Without looking at your notes, tell your partner three things you know for sure about fractions and decimals now that you didn't know in Week 1."
Lesson 2: Connecting It All — Unit Synthesis
60 min Guided
Concept Map (20 min) Whole-class concept map built on board: "Fractions and Decimals" in centre. Connections: Fractions ↔ Division (fractions of collections), Fractions ↔ Number Line, Fractions ↔ Equivalent, Fractions ↔ Decimals, Fractions ↔ Addition/Subtraction.
Doorbell Rang Maths Retelling (20 min) Read The Doorbell Rang one final time. STOP after each doorbell ring. Pairs: write new fraction per person, subtraction equation, place on number line. Final question: "At start: 2 people each get 1/2 (6 cookies). At end: 12 people each get 1/12 (1 cookie). Write the full sequence. What pattern do you see?"
Personal Concept Map (15 min) Independent: create own concept map with at least 6 key terms, 5 connecting lines with labels, 2 examples. Extension: include a diagram or worked example.
Lesson 3: Hot Task + Cold → Hot Comparison + Celebration
70 min Assessment
Hot Task (30 min) IDENTICAL to Cold Task from Week 1. Students work independently and silently. Collect all Hot Tasks.
Cold → Hot Comparison (25 min) Return Cold Task alongside Hot Task. Students compare with coloured pens: GREEN circle = right in both, BLUE = wrong in Week 1, right in Week 8, ORANGE = still tricky. Write sticky notes: "Before Week 1, I thought... Now I know... I'm most proud of..."
Celebration (10 min) Each student shares one "I'm most proud of" statement. Display both tasks side by side: "Look How Far We've Come." Final Doorbell Rang read: "Grandma arrives with more cookies. What fraction does each person get now? Tell me — because you're mathematicians now."

EAL/D Students

  • Pre-teach key vocabulary with visual dictionary cards before each lesson
  • Sentence frames: "This fraction shows ___ because ___"
  • Bilingual anchor charts; allow home language for brainstorming
  • Concrete materials (fraction tiles, pattern blocks) before symbols
  • Accept oral explanation in place of written where appropriate
  • Minimum 20 seconds wait time after questions
  • Fraction wall always available as reference

Gifted / Advanced Students

  • Extend to comparing fractions with unlike denominators
  • Investigate improper fractions and mixed numerals beyond scope
  • Challenge: If 1/3 = 2/6 = 3/9, what is the pattern? Write a rule.
  • Introduce percentage as a third way of naming parts of a whole
  • Problem-posing: create your own Doorbell Rang style problems
  • Extend decimals to thousandths; connect to measurement (km, kg)
  • Open investigation: Which fractions can be written as terminating decimals?

Students with NEPs

  • Consolidate halves and quarters before introducing thirds, fifths, sixths
  • Physical cookie-cutting (paper circles) to build equal-parts concept
  • Fraction wall always available as reference tool
  • Focus on Parts A and B of each lesson — not expected to reach extension
  • Modified success criteria: 3 "I can" statements rather than all 10
  • Pre-lesson micro-session: preview the concrete model
  • Reduce denominator range: halves, quarters, eighths only if needed
  • Peer support pairing with a student who has mastered the concept
