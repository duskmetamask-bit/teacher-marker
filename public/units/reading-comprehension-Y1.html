Learning Intention

We are learning to retell a familiar story in sequence and demonstrate understanding of literal and inferential meaning..

  • I can retell a story with a beginning, middle and end.
  • I can identify the main character, setting and problem in a story.
  • I can answer questions about what happened and why.
  • I can make inferences using clues from the pictures and words.
  • I can connect what I read to my own experiences.

Week 1 — INTRODUCTION TO THE STORY

Lesson 1: Cold Task (Assessment)

35 min Assessment
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- ---------------- ---------
Hook (5 min) Show cover of *The Tale of Peter Rabbit*. "What do you notice? What do you think will happen?" Share predictions
Read (15 min) Read *The Tale of Peter Rabbit* aloud. Show illustrations. Listen, follow, observe
Task (15 min) Cold Task (same as Hot Task): Draw and write the story of Peter Rabbit. Show the beginning, what happens, and how it ends. Tell me what you understood. Independent retell
DifferentiationSentence starters available. Visual sequence cards.

Lesson 2: Reading Peter Rabbit (I Do)

35 min Explicit Teaching
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- ---------------- ---------
Read (15 min) Reread pp. 1–15. Pause to think aloud: "Peter is disobeying his mother. I think she'll be angry." Track and respond
Mini-Lesson (10 min) Introduce story elements: CHARACTER (who), SETTING (where), PROBLEM (what's wrong), EVENTS (what happens), RESOLUTION (how it ends) Take notes in reading journals
Share (10 min) "Which character do you like best? Why?" Turn and talk. Partner discussion

Lesson 3: Story Mapping (We Do)

35 min Guided Practice
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- --------- ---------
I Do (10 min) Model drawing a story map for *Peter Rabbit* using the 5 elements Observe
We Do (15 min) Together, fill in the story map on the board: Who? Where? What went wrong? What happened? How did it end? Contribute answers
You Do (10 min) Complete your own story map for *The Tale of Peter Rabbit* Independent work

Week 2 — STORY SEQUENCE

Lesson 1: Beginning, Middle, End (I Do)

35 min Explicit Teaching
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- ---------------- ---------
Read (10 min) Read *Peter Rabbit* again. Track events on a BME chart. Track events
Mini-Lesson (10 min) BEGINNING: Peter disobeys and goes into the garden. MIDDLE: He gets chased, lost, nearly caught. END: Peter escapes and goes home sick. Label BME
You Do (15 min) Draw 3 pictures showing B-M-E of the story with labels Sequential drawing

Lesson 2: Sequence Language (I Do → We Do)

35 min Explicit Teaching + Guided
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- --------- ---------
I Do (10 min) Show transition words: FIRST, NEXT, THEN, AFTER THAT, SUDDENLY, FINALLY. Use in a sentence about Peter Rabbit. Copy words
We Do (15 min) Together, retell *Peter Rabbit* using the sequence words. Stand up and act it out! Physical retelling
You Do (10 min) Write 5 sentences about Peter Rabbit using the sequence words Written practice

Lesson 3: Retell Practice (You Do)

35 min Independent
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- --------- ---------
You Do (30 min) Use picture cards to practice retelling *Peter Rabbit* to a partner. Use sequence words. Peer practice
Share (5 min) 2–3 students retell for the class Performance

Week 3 — CHARACTERS AND SETTING

Lesson 1: Meet the Characters (I Do)

35 min Explicit Teaching
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- ---------------- ---------
Read (10 min) List characters: Peter, Mrs. Rabbit, Flopsy, Mopsy, Cotton-tail, Mr. McGregor Identify
Mini-Lesson (10 min) Character traits chart: Peter (curious, brave, disobeys) / Mrs. Rabbit (caring, strict) / Mr. McGregor (angry, protective of garden) Match traits
You Do (15 min) Draw a character web for Peter Rabbit. Add traits and a quote from the story. Character analysis

Lesson 2: Where Is the Story Set? (I Do → We Do)

35 min Explicit Teaching + Guided
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- --------- ---------
I Do (10 min) Show garden pages. Describe: vegetable garden, gate, greenhouse, toolshed. Use senses: what you see, hear, smell. Visualise
We Do (15 min) Together, describe the setting using senses. "I see tall lettuces... I hear birds..." Contribute
You Do (10 min) Draw Mr. McGregor's garden and label what you can see there Creative work

Lesson 3: Character Profile (You Do)

35 min Independent
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- --------- ---------
You Do (35 min) Create a character profile for Peter. Include: Name, What they look like, Their personality (3 traits), What they want, What they do in the story. Written and drawn character study

Week 4 — LITERAL COMPREHENSION

Lesson 1: What Did It Say? (I Do)

35 min Explicit Teaching
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- ---------------- ---------
Read (10 min) Read a page from *Peter Rabbit*. "What did the words actually say?" Recall
Mini-Lesson (10 min) Literal questions: What is Peter's name? Where did he go? What did he eat? Model finding the answer in the text. Practice locating answers
You Do (15 min) Answer 5 literal questions about *Peter Rabbit* with evidence from the text (write the page number) Written comprehension

Lesson 2: Finding Clues (I Do → We Do)

35 min Explicit Teaching + Guided
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- --------- ---------
I Do (10 min) "Where in the text does it say Peter was scared?" Find the evidence together. Search for text evidence
We Do (15 min) Find evidence for 5 questions together. Write the page number and the words that prove your answer. Guided practice
You Do (10 min) Complete 3 more literal questions with evidence hunting Independent work

Lesson 3: Answering Text Questions (You Do)

35 min Independent
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- --------- ---------
You Do (35 min) Complete a literal comprehension worksheet about *Peter Rabbit*. Use the book to find evidence. Independent reading and writing

Week 5 — INFERENTIAL COMPREHENSION

Lesson 1: What Do You Think? (I Do)

35 min Explicit Teaching
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- ---------------- ---------
Read (10 min) Read pp. 10–20. "Why do you think Peter ate all the carrots?" "Why was Mrs. Rabbit worried?" Think and discuss
Mini-Lesson (10 min) Inferential thinking: Use clues from the story (pictures and words) + what you already know = your inference. Example: Peter runs fast → He must be scared. Learn inferencing strategy
You Do (15 min) Make an inference: Why was Peter crying when he was in the jacket? Write your inference with evidence. Written inference

Lesson 2: Reading the Pictures (I Do → We Do)

35 min Explicit Teaching + Guided
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- --------- ---------
I Do (10 min) Show an illustration of Peter hiding. "What do his face and body tell us?" Analyze visual clues
We Do (15 min) Look at 3 pictures and infer: What is Peter feeling? What might happen next? Why is he in that position? Partner analysis
You Do (10 min) Choose one picture and write what you can infer from looking at it Written response

Lesson 3: Making Inferences (You Do)

35 min Independent
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- --------- ---------
You Do (35 min) Make 3 inferences about *Peter Rabbit*: 1. Why did Peter disobey his mother? 2. Why was he so tired at the end? 3. What do you think Peter learned? Written inferential comprehension

Week 6 — MAKING CONNECTIONS

Lesson 1: Text to Self (We Do)

35 min Guided Practice
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- --------- ---------
I Do (5 min) "Have you ever disobeyed a parent or teacher? What happened?" Share a personal story. Listen
We Do (20 min) Turn and talk: What connection can you make between Peter's story and your life? Draw or write about it. Personal connection
Share (10 min) Share connections with the class Class discussion

Lesson 2: Text to Text (I Do)

35 min Explicit Teaching
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- ---------------- ---------
Read (15 min) Read *The Gruffalo* (or display cover). Compare to *Peter Rabbit*. Both have: a character who goes somewhere scary, a problem, an escape. Compare stories
Mini-Lesson (10 min) Venn diagram: What is the same? What is different? Characters, setting, problem, resolution. Note similarities/differences
You Do (10 min) Complete the Venn diagram for the two stories Diagram completion

Lesson 3: Comparing Stories (We Do)

35 min Guided Practice
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- --------- ---------
We Do (20 min) Complete a comparison chart: Peter Rabbit vs The Gruffalo — both have a small character, a scary setting, they trick someone, happy ending. Group work
You Do (15 min) Write 3 sentences: "Peter Rabbit and the Gruffalo are the same because..." Written comparison

Week 7 — DEEP UNDERSTANDING

Lesson 1: Reread and Think (I Do → You Do)

35 min Guided → Independent
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- --------- ---------
I Do (5 min) "Good readers reread stories to understand them better." Show how to mark text with thinking prompts: ? = question, ! = wow moment, ★ = favourite Observe
You Do (30 min) Reread *Peter Rabbit* with a thinking marks sheet. Mark questions, favourite parts, and wow moments. Independent reading

Lesson 2: Reader's Theatre (We Do)

35 min Performance
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- --------- ---------
I Do (5 min) Assign roles: Narrator 1, Narrator 2, Mrs. Rabbit, Peter, Mr. McGregor. Practice reading with expression. Role assignment
We Do (25 min) Perform *Peter Rabbit* as a readers theatre. Focus on expression and following the story. Group performance
You Do (5 min) Reflect: Which part of your performance worked best? Self-reflection

Lesson 3: Retell Mastery (You Do)

35 min Independent
PhaseTeacher (I Do)Students
------- --------- ---------
You Do (30 min) Practice retelling *Peter Rabbit* using all we have learned: sequence words, character traits, setting details, your inferences. Record on iPad. Independent practice + recording
Review (5 min) Listen to your recording. What did you do well? What could you improve? Self-assessment
