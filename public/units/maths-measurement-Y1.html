COLD TASK

Name: _________________________ Date: _____________ Year: _______

Part A — Length

  • Measure this line using blocks:

    • (draw a 12-block line)

    Length: ___ blocks

  • Which is longer? How do you know? (a pencil vs. a crayon — just observe)
  • Order from shortest to longest: (pencil, scissors, book — drawings)

    • Part B — Informal Units

  • Measure your desk using pencils. About ___ pencils.
  • If you measured the same desk using paper clips, would you get MORE or FEWER? Explain: _______________

    • Part C — Mass

  • Circle the heavier: a chair / a book
  • Draw two objects. One should be LIGHTER than a cup.

    • Part D — Capacity

  • Which holds MORE? A thimble / A cup
  • "A thimble holds less than a cup." True or false? Explain: _______________

    • Part E — Problem

  • Max measures his desk in pencils and gets 6 pencils. Sam measures the same desk in books and gets 3 books. Who used the BIGGER unit? How do you know?

    A–E RUBRIC — Measurement (Year 1)

    Aligned to: AC9M1M01, AC9M1M02

    Criterion 1: Length — Measuring with Informal Units

    GradeDescriptor
    **A**Measures length using informal units placed end-to-end with no gaps or overlaps; records measurements accurately; explains importance of consistent units
    **B**Measures reliably; minor gaps/overlaps; beginning to explain consistency
    **C**Measures with scaffolding; gaps or overlaps; beginning to understand consistency
    **D**Measures with significant support; units not placed correctly
    **E**Unable to measure length using informal units

    Criterion 2: Length — Comparing and Ordering

    GradeDescriptor
    **A**Compares and orders objects by length; uses shortest/longest accurately; justifies reasoning
    **B**Compares and orders reliably; comparative language mostly correct
    **C**Compares two objects with scaffolding; orders three with support
    **D**Compares with significant support; language limited
    **E**Unable to compare by length

    Criterion 3: Mass

    GradeDescriptor
    **A**Compares mass of two objects using balance scales or direct comparison; describes difference using heavy/heavier/heaviest; estimates reliably
    **B**Compares mass reliably; heavier/lighter used correctly; estimation developing
    **C**Compares with scaffolding; beginning to use comparative language
    **D**Compares with significant support
    **E**Unable to compare by mass

    Criterion 4: Capacity

    GradeDescriptor
    **A**Compares capacity of containers; uses more/less/full/empty accurately; estimates capacity
    **B**Compares capacity reliably; estimation developing
    **C**Compares with scaffolding; capacity language developing
    **D**Compares with significant support
    **E**Unable to compare by capacity

    Criterion 5: Mathematical Reasoning

    GradeDescriptor
    **A**Explains why consistent units are important; justifies comparisons; makes and checks estimates
    **B**Explains unit importance with prompting; estimates checked
    **C**Beginning to explain; estimates with support
    **D**Limited explanation
    **E**Unable to reason about measurement

    • WEEK 1 — ENGAGE

    Focus: Cold Task + Measuring with informal units

    5E Phase: Engage

    LESSON 1.2 — End-to-End Without Gaps or Overlaps

    Duration: 50 minutes

    Type: Concept Introduction

    Learning Intentions

  • I am learning to place informal units end-to-end with no gaps or overlaps
  • I am learning why this is important for accurate measurement

    • Success Criteria

  • I can measure a line correctly using blocks

    • I DO (15 min)

    "Measuring properly means placing units END-TO-END. No GAPS between them. No OVERLAPS — they don't stack on top."
    "Watch: I place each block right next to the last one. End to end."
    "This desk is 12 blocks long."

    YOU DO (15 min)

  • Measure this line using counters (no gaps, no overlaps):
  • Draw a line. Measure it in blocks.
  • "What would happen if there were gaps between the blocks?"
  • EXTENSION: "Measure the same line with pencils. Why might you get a different answer?"

    • WEEK 2 — EXPLORE

    Focus: Consistent units and comparing

    5E Phase: Explore

    LESSON 2.1 — Does Unit Size Affect the Answer?

    Duration: 50 minutes

    Type: Explore

    I DO (15 min)

    "Same desk. Measured in pencils: 6 pencils. Measured in books: 3 books."
    "The desk didn't change — but the NUMBER changed because the UNIT changed."
    "Smaller unit → MORE units needed. Larger unit → FEWER units needed."

    YOU DO (15 min)

  • "If I measure a table in paper clips, will I get a bigger or smaller number than measuring in pencils? Why?"
  • Measure a pencil in cm cubes. About ___ cubes.
  • Measure the same pencil in cm rods (2cm each). About ___ rods. Which is more accurate?
  • EXTENSION: "What happens if you use mixed-size units?"

    • LESSON 2.2 — Comparing Lengths

    Duration: 50 minutes

    Type: Explore

    YOU DO (30 min)

  • "Who has the longer pencil — you or your partner? How can you prove it?"
  • Order your desk items from shortest to longest. Measure to check.
  • "Can you compare two objects without measuring? How?"
  • EXTENSION: "Can you estimate the length of the room in giant steps?"

    • WEEK 3 — EXPLORE

    Focus: Mass and capacity

    5E Phase: Explain

    LESSON 3.1 — Comparing Mass

    Duration: 50 minutes

    Type: Explain

    I DO (15 min)

    "We compare mass using a BALANCE SCALE."
    "If the scale tips down on one side, that object is HEAVIER."
    "If the scale balances, the objects have the SAME mass."

    YOU DO (15 min)

  • "If object A balances object B, what does that mean?"
  • "If object C tips the scale down lower than object D, which is heavier?"
  • Order: a book, a rubber, a pair of scissors. (Estimate first!)
  • EXTENSION: "Is the mass of an object the same on the moon?"

    • LESSON 3.2 — Comparing Capacity

    Duration: 50 minutes

    Type: Explain

    I DO (12 min)

    "To compare capacity: fill one container, pour into another. If there's space left over, the second is bigger."
    "Or: fill both to the same level and compare."

    YOU DO (15 min)

  • "A cup holds less than a bowl. True or false?"
  • Find two containers. Which has greater capacity? Prove it.
  • "Can you order 3 containers from smallest to largest capacity?"
  • EXTENSION: "What holds more — 5 cups or 1 jug?"

    • WEEK 4 — ELABORATE

    Focus: Choosing appropriate units

    5E Phase: Elaborate

    LESSON 4.1 — Choosing the Right Unit

    Duration: 50 minutes

    Type: Elaborate

    I DO (12 min)

    "Would you measure your desk in paper clips? It would take a LONG time — lots of paper clips!"
    "Would you measure your room in rulers? You'd get a tiny number — maybe 10 rulers!"
    "We choose a unit that makes sense for the SIZE of what we're measuring."

    YOU DO (15 min)

  • Which unit for a pencil: paper clip / book / desk?
  • Which unit for a room: pencil / ruler / desk?
  • Estimate, then measure: the length of the room in giant steps.
  • EXTENSION: "What is an appropriate unit for measuring the length of a football field?"

    • WEEK 5 — ELABORATE (Part 2)

    Focus: Estimation and problem-solving

    5E Phase: Elaborate

    LESSON 5.1 — Estimation

    Duration: 50 minutes

    Type: Elaborate

    I DO (12 min)

    "Before measuring, ESTIMATE. Have a guess. Then measure to check."
    "My desk: I estimate about 8 pencils. Actual measurement: 7 pencils. Close!"

    YOU DO (30 min)

  • Estimate then measure: your book (in pencils).
  • Estimate then measure: your chair (in hands).
  • Estimate then measure: the door (in footsteps).
  • Record: Estimate / Actual / Difference.
